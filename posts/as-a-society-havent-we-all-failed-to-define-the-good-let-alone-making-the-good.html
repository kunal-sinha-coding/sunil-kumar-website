India to Bharat · Essay #034

As a society, haven’t we all failed to define the good, let alone making the good?

1. Dissatisfaction with Present: We’re in a state of Perpetual Purposelessness! Thanks to the materialistic influence of the Western cultures, we have obviously forgotten and are totally ignorant of the age-old wisdom of India that is Bharat, of the 4-Purusartha, universal human Strivings and 4-Ashram, stages of Life:

1st Strive to get established in ‘power of goodness’, commitment to ‘Righteous, ethical conduct’, viz. ‘Dharma’ and our respective duties, Swadharma, kartavya-palan – during first 20-25 years of learning, education, growing-up, Brahmacharya-ashram – not mere 3R’s.

2nd and 3rd Strive thru’ Dharma to ‘Earn’ & ‘Enjoy’ following an occupation and vocation as per our respective inherent mindset, tendencies, Samskaras and Swadharma, – as a Householder, Grihastha-ashram.

The 4th human Striving, whether we are now aware of it or not, is to attain the highest human Goal of Perfection, Freedom, Nirvana, etc. – Vanaprastha and Sannyas-ashram, akin maybe to today’s Senior Citizens. Ignoring our common Goal, God-wards, we’re now in a state of ‘Perpetual Purposelessness’. Education gives us no long-term vision!

2. Vision for Future: We all must understand and appreciate our duty, Swadharma, to imbibe the spirit of our Constitutional Fundamental Duties which inter alia enjoin on us, to:

# Value and Preserve the Rich heritage of our composite culture.

# Promote harmony and brotherhood …

# Cherish and follow the noble ideals that inspired the national struggle for freedom (love for our nation, tyaga, seva, etc.)

# Develop scientific temper, humanism, and the spirit of inquiry and reform.

# Strive towards excellence in all spheres of individual and collective activity; Protection of environment; safeguard public property and to abjure violence …

The USP of the composite culture of India that is Bharat needs to be appreciated based on our Vedantic worldview that as Self we’re all One and so we must evolve for ourselves a self-development action plan, for self-study, reflection, life-skill practices to awaken Viveka-buddhi and build powers of mind to walk-the-talk towards higher long-term goals for good of samaaja.

We will learn to direct all our ‘thoughts-motives-choices-work-Vocation’ as our highest worship, supported by prayers, meditations, etc. of our respective religious beliefs – this process will expand and purify our mind to selflessness and to experience oneness with all.

3. To know of India and the essence of India that is Bharat, the best source is Vivekananda, whose inspiring words in English inspired the Freedom fighters as stated by Mahatma Gandhi, Pt. Nehru, Tilak, Subhas Bose and many others. Vivekananda was declared as our National Youth by PM Rajiv Gandhi in 1985. His words of Inspiration at Parliament of Religions in 1893 at Chicago, USA, on Education, Hinduism, Indian culture, Karma Yoga, India and our problems, etc. maybe expressed as Practical Vedanta – Serve God in Man; Work as Worship.

Whatever be our belief or our nationality, race or religion, it is universally accepted that more and more goodness, love, care, concern for each other and righteousness, excellence in work, working for good of others is the way towards highest Perfection! In a nutshell we can evaluate goodness based on the principle that more and more unselfishness is good, better, best and more and more Selfishness is not!

Religion is not just BELIEF; it must inspire a scientific and academic enquiry into Truth, based on a refined mind, intellect and character, earned thru’ selfless work done in a spirit of Karma Yoga for good of Samaaja – for only a pure mind can appreciate the idea of highest Perfection and awaken Viveka and powers of mind to walk-the-talk.

4. Vivekananda: Religion is realization; not talks, not doctrine not theories, however beautiful they may be. It is being and becoming, not hearing or acknowledging; it is the whole soul becoming changed into what it believes.

# It is a privilege to serve mankind, for this is the worship of God; God is here, in all these human souls. He is the soul of man.

# The difference between God and the devil is in nothing except in unselfishness and selfishness.

5. Action plan for Implementation:

As citizens we must discuss and explore how within our colonies and school communities, we can promote awareness and commitment to our obligatory constitutional Fundamental Duty.

In what way can we all, irrespective of our faith and religious beliefs, work with each other, in small study-circles, say of five-each aka ‘pancha-parmeshwar’, to study, explore, discuss, reflect to get intellectual conviction in our common Goal, and evolve short-term and long-term action plans to produce the ‘good’, selfless responsible, peaceful, useful citizens, who strive to imbibe the spirit of:

# Commitment to our respective duties, Swadharma, kartavya-palan, as now mentioned in GOI mission Karma Yogi; and to our “Fundamental Duties (Art. 51A of Constitution)”

# Spirit of striving for quality and excellence and Working in a spirit of service, for the good of society – “Yogaha Karmasu Kaushalam”; “Atmano Moksartham Jagad Hitaye cha”, which are the motto of “IIT K” as well as of the “Academy for training of civil servants at Mussoorie and of GOI Mission KYi

# Spontaneous feelings of love, care and concern for each other’; ‘whole world as our family’; G20 Global vision of “One World. One Family. One Future.”

Essence: As enlightened and Senior citizens our duty is to inspire and Mentor society God-wards by helping individuals and societies to get established in practices to imbibe Righteousness, work in a spirit of worship for good of all, and engage in occupation in keeping with our inherited tendencies – this purifies and expands our Mind and we remain on the critical path of Righteousness.
