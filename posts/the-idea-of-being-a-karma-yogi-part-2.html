India to Bharat · Essay #153

The idea of being a karma yogi: Part 2

We talk of right and justice, but these are mere baby’s talk; might and mercy guide our conduct. Whereas Might is just selfishness, where we try to take maximum advantage of whatever power we’ve got; for a Karma-yogi, Mercy is heaven itself; even justice and right should stand on mercy.

Self-restraint is a manifestation of greater power; all outgoing energy following a selfish motive is frittered away; but if restrained, it will result in development of power. This self-control will tend to produce a mighty will, a character which makes Christ, Buddha. Let the foolish try to rule mankind, work for selfish ends, money, name, fame; but a Karma Yogi always tries to work towards higher and higher unselfishness.

To work we have the right , but not to the fruits thereof. Do your duties to the very best but leave the fruits alone; why care for results? If you wish to help a man, never think what his attitude should be towards you. If you want to do a great, good work, just do your best without worrying over results. The ideal man is he who, in the midst of the intense activity finds peace and silence. He has learnt the secret of restraint, self-control. His mind is calm and yet he is intensely working all the time. This is the ideal of Karma-Yoga.

Begin from wherever I am now : Take up the duties and works as they come; whether it is to Rule, teach, just sweep the floors, matters little. Gradually learn to be more unselfish every day. Almost without exception, in the beginning, our motives may always be selfish; gradually this selfishness will melt by persistence, till at last we can do really unselfish work.

The fruit : As we struggle through our duties, we shall become perfectly unselfish; and the moment the sweeper or the ruler attains to that unselfishness, the infinite divine potential power, goodness, knowledge within, which is ever ours, will be manifest and we will attain the highest human goal of Perfection and Ananda. This is the fruit of striving to be a Karma Yogi.

Our duties and values will differ depending on many factors, vocations, nation, community, state of life, set of circumstances, etc. For example, “Resist not evil,” non-resistance is the highest moral ideal. If we attempt to put it fully into practice, the whole social fabric would fall to pieces, the wicked would take possession of our properties and our lives! This may be a highest ideal; yet to teach only this, would condemn a vast portion of mankind.

Not to hate ourselves is our foremost duty. To advance we must have faith in ourselves first, in our status, duties, families, profession, and then in God. He who has no faith in himself can never have faith in God. One man does not resist because he is weak, lazy, and cannot, not because he will not; the other man knows that he can strike an irresistible blow if he likes; yet he not only does not strike, but blesses his enemies. So, we must always be careful about what we really mean when we speak of this non-resistance and ideal love.

Only if we’ve the power of resistance, and yet do not resist, are we doing a grand act of love; If we cannot resist don’t deceive yourselves that we are motivated by the highest love. Arjuna became a coward at the sight of the mighty against him; his “love” made him forget his duty towards his country and king. That is why he was called a hypocrite; asked to stand and fight! Such is the central idea of Karma-Yoga.

It is very easy to say, hate nobody, love all, resist not evil. The Karma-Yogi understands the ideal of non-resistance but before reaching it, our duty is to resist evil. Work, resist, fight; only when we gain power to resist, will non-resistance be a virtue. Work, duties, always means resistance. Resist all evils, mental and physical; and when you have succeeded in resisting, then will calmness come.

If you desire wealth, go after it, plunge into the struggle for wealth, or else your mind will be running day and night after money; this is hypocrisy and will serve no purpose. Plunge into the world, and when you have suffered and enjoyed all that is in it, will calmness, renunciation come. So, fulfill your desire for power and everything else, and after you have fulfilled the desire, will come the time when you will know that they are all very little things; but until you have fulfilled this desire, until you have passed through that activity, it is impossible for you to come to the state of calmness, serenity, and self-surrender.

Serenity and renunciation may be great; but first we must fulfill our own goals. That is a surer way of progress than taking up ideals, which we can never hope to accomplish; but let the ideal remain at the back our minds as we first meet our own! All people in any society, are not of the same mind, capacity, or of the same power to do things; they must have different ideals, and we have no right to sneer at any ideal. Let everyone do the best he can for realizing his own goals and ideals. Nor is it right that I should be judged by your standard or you by mine.

Our duty is to encourage everyone in their struggle to achieve their ideals and goals, while they strive at the same time to make those as near as possible to the highest ideals.
