India to Bharat · Essay #143

Appreciation of our social systems & duty of govt and schooling

Govt. & Schooling have the Obligatory Duty today to ensure that all of us are made aware of the wisdom of our forefathers. During the last century we were under the shackles of British rule. They had painted us as a backward, superstitious, idol worshipping, divided, caste ridden, society. The foreign rulers, their preachers and we, their-copycats, had no knowledge of our thousands of years of civilization and so, no love for it; and we lost no opportunity to look up to their ways, while joining them to look down upon our social evils, ways of living, worship, etc.

Divided or United? Following criticisms heaped on our systems, our schools, especially the then most elite and prestigious Convent schools, also taught and highlighted all that was good there and bad here. We the educated, especially those from cities and convent schools, have been in leadership positions in govt. and society and we’ve all grown up, including teachers and the taught, to look down with scorn, on our religious attitudes, festivals, family-values, our social milieu, etc.

We’re thus a divided society nowadays, between those that wish to follow and respect our Sanatana Dharma or Hindu ways and the angrezi -elite who were in power so long!

Who is our Role Model? Among the many good things that the govt. after independence has achieved, the greatest was to declare Vivekananda as the National Youth Ideal and to direct us to respect our composite culture and ideals of Gandhiji. However, till now the govt. and the education ministries have failed to include their teachings in mainstream educational processes. The new Govt. now in power, also holds Vivekananda as their ideal; and, Gandhiji has also affirmed that his love for the nation grew a thousand-fold, and also those of the freedom fighters, after studying his works.

Our present PM had stated when he was Gujarat CM, that universities must teach Vivekananda’s thoughts on Karma Yoga, Bhakti Yoga, Raja Yoga, Gyan Yoga; and the PM has now directed that 46 lakhs govt. servants may be trained to be Karma yogis; these ideals represent the essence of all religions and represent the History of our spiritually oriented civilization.

India is a unique nation: Our USP and focus is Spirituality; not matter, not more and more of this or that. Our whole society was geared to inspire and help everyone towards the highest Goal. Our varna ashrama was a well-planned social system so that in and thru’ our vocation based on our inherent tendencies, we may focus on fulfilling our duties thru’ ethics, dharma, to remain focused on attaining God-realization; not on promotion in status or desiring for more and more.

Look how scientific and logical are the thoughts behind the designing of the social systems established by our forefathers; today, we may question and judge them good or bad, but let’s appreciate their rationale: –

i) People strive for 4-major goals: Dharma (Ethics), Artha (Money), Kaama (Desires), Mukti (Bliss/Ananda/Salvation/ Freedom/Heaven/Perfection/ Happiness, etc.)

ii) People pass thru’ 4 stages: Student > Householder > Retired > God-focused.

During studentship they’re required to get established in Dharma besides acquiring knowledge and skills. As Householders they create wealth and fulfill their desires thru’ Dharma. During Retirement they help society and focus on God-realization. Finally they focus on God realization and Mentor society.

iii) People who are committed to their duties are of 4-types based on their inborn tendencies:

  • Brahmin (Mentors/Role-models/Advisors of society). Their focus is on God-realization and duty is to guide people towards Dharma and God-realization. They’re men of wisdom as they have satiated, to the maximum extent their desires for pleasures and wealth.
  • Kshatriya (Administrators, Protectors). Their focus is on Dharma and duty is to learn from Mentors and establish Dharma in society so that as householders people earn and enjoy thru’ dharma. They’re not expected to waste energies on artha and kaama.
  • Vaishya (Entrepreneurs, Result producers). Their focus is on Artha , businesses, wealth creation, farming, trading, etc.; not on kaama.
  • Shudra (Result producers, Skilled/unskilled). They’re the work force, labor, and are free to fulfill their desires, Kaama.

iv) 5 th type is of those who don’t follow societal norms; who are not committed to their duties.

It must be noted that one can attain god-realization from any stage. Therefore, the idea is not to rise vertically. The goal is to fulfill our duties thru Dharma; to keep growing in quality and excellence in work. The goal is not to get promoted to higher status; not to get more money, bigger house, etc. but to rise and grow towards God-realization.

Thus, man is placed in a family, circumstances, situations, knowledge, skills, in harmony with his tendencies, that are best suited to take him on the optimum and critical path to God-realization!

Ours was perhaps an ideal system but its traces still linger! So, though it may not be practical today; it can certainly give us food for thought.

Modern Scientists Vs Ancient Rishis: In contrast to our aspiration for the highest spiritual quest of God Realization the Modern elite, the IQ-scientists are busy looking at matter – exploring, enjoying, discovering the variety of “known” objects with Names, Forms, that the universe consists of! We’ve yet to realize that there is an infinite variety of “known” and therefore no one can ever know it all. So, go within and discover the Conscious-Life, Spirit within us; the One-Knower, and knowing him by reuniting (Yoga) with pure Consciousness, we can know it all; if we so wish then!
