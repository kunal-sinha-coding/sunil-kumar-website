India to Bharat · Essay #090

Reflections & understanding of the idea of caste based on an understanding of the purpose of work & life

To appreciate the idea of caste, it is necessary to first understand the essential purpose of Work and Life based on a healthy, universal spiritual worldview of Reality, in comparison with the modern so called scientific thinking based on a limited study of external, Objective universe of ‘matter’. We blissfully ignore the Subjective aspects relating to the ‘knower’, the ‘Consciousness’ that makes us living-beings, Jivatman. We ignore the Biblical advice: to seek first the Kingdom of God within, and his Righteousness and everything else will be added unto us.

According to modern thinking, based on Western ideas, our daily work and profession is meant for earning a living, money, promotions, status, etc. According to universal as well as our own Vedantic wisdom, the reality is that our high or low status, rich or poor, worker or master, are irrelevant!

What should matter is: In and through our work and life are we on the path of ‘dharma’, Righteousness, to the ultimate goal and purpose of human life? Are we committed to kartavya-palan, fulfilment of our respective duties? Are we striving to work selflessly for the good of society as a whole? If we are, then we are much higher in our journey towards the goal of life, than a selfish person of high status, indulging in selfishness, ‘corruption’, etc.

In fact, the 25 years of growing up is to ensure that thru’ Education, Religions, Governance, we get established in commitment to our respective duties, kartavya-palan. This fulfilment of Duties leads to Purity of Mind, Chitta-suddhi, and the more the mind is purified through fulfilment of our respective Duties, the more does the Mind become amenable to control towards higher and better.

Thus, we can appreciate that as long as we are full of desires for Artha and Kaama it is better, we remain as result producers, offering excellence in work and vocation and we focus on fulfilling our duties to the best of our abilities.

If we run after status, leadership positions, and continue running after Artha and Kaama instead of Righteousness, then it leads to pollution of mind not purity of mind!

The Indian work-ideal, aka Karma Yoga, asserts that our daily work and living is our best, if not the only opportunity to walk either towards or away, from the highest goal of life. Of course, most people today are not even aware of or interested in the higher dimensions of work and life because schooling is not interested in guiding the young to be good, be righteous!

In India, it was the responsibility of Teachers and Preachers as Mentors of society, to guide the young to ‘know’ and build the ability and powers and purity of Mind to get committed to kartavya-palan and selflessly engage in a vocation for good of society.

Therefore, the attempt of the srestha of society was to try and evolve a social system where people could engage in work and duties where they could excel, based on their inherited tendencies and aptitudes, and learn the necessary knowledge and skills while growing up, without undue effort and time. The idea was to be good and engage in work, likely to be most helpful in our path to perfection; whereas today the tendency is to go after work, which can give more money, power, and status – making the good people is nobody’s responsibility!

Modern society led by Western ideas is still grappling with the problem of how to identify, educate, select, and place the right people in the right positions of responsibility. Historically all societies, ancient or modern, including Indian caste idea, had a thumb rule to solve this perplexing issue – go by status and seniority of birth; follow in the footsteps of your family. Was it such a bad rule, considering that it is time tested and is still followed and remains the most common yardstick globally, even today. Exceptions prove the rule and the 80:20 principle applies even today!

Whether we like it or not, the fact is that people are born unequal. Why? Does the law or principle of ‘as we sow, so we reap’ finishes at death? Or is it a continuous process of birth and death, till we realize the Kingdom of God within and become Perfect!

Not one in million understands what the idea of caste is. There is no country without it. The plan in India is from caste to reach to the point of no caste; to raise everybody to the highest level thru’ selflessly striving to be committed to their respective duties based on our respective inherent tendencies, mindset, Samskara.

What is this highest ideal that every man must reach to attain heaven, Perfection, salvation, mukti, nirvana? Absence of worldliness; abundance of wisdom; free from selfishness; lives and works to acquire and propagate wisdom and power of love; good and noble; spiritual and moral – committed to righteousness.

The whole society is to be raised to this ideal by engaging in a vocation based on our inherent samskara so that we may attain chitta-suddhi and build the ability to walk-the-talk.
