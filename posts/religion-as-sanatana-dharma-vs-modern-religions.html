India to Bharat · Essay #088

Religion as Sanatana dharma vs modern religions!

1. Sanatana Dharma as Religion simply means righteous conduct, i.e., those universal and eternal human values and commitment to our respective duties, that when imbibed and followed lead to purity of Mind; thereby enabling us to awaken powers of discrimination and build powers of Mind to know and walk-the-talk towards our highest Perfection, Freedom, Mukti. Purpose of Saints, sages, mentors, is just to inspire us to be committed to our respective duties, kartavya-palan. Righteousness is the essence; faith, belief in this or that religious way or gods is secondary!

Essence of all Religions and Sanatana Dharma has been expressed as: ‘Each soul is potentially divine. The goal is to manifest this divinity within by controlling nature, external and internal. Do this either by Work, or Worship, or Meditation, or Philosophy – by one, or more, or all of these, and be free’. ‘This is religion. Doctrines, or dogmas, or rituals, or books, or temples, or forms, are but secondary details’ – Vivekananda.

2. Complex Modern Ideas of Religion : Religion is a range of social-cultural systems, including designated behaviors and practices, morals, beliefs, worldviews, texts, sanctified places, prophecies, ethics, or organizations, that generally relate humanity to supernatural, transcendental, and spiritual elements —although there is no scholarly consensus over what precisely constitutes a religion. Different religions may or may not contain various elements ranging from the divine, sacredness, faith, and a supernatural being or beings.

Religious practices may include rituals, sermons, commemoration, veneration of deities or saints sacrifices, festivals, feasts, trances, initiations, matrimonial and funeral services, meditation, music, prayer, art, dance or public-service.

Religions may have sacred histories and narratives, which may be preserved in sacred texts, symbols and holy places, that primarily aim to give life meaning. Religions may contain symbolic tales that may attempt to explain the origin of life, the universe, and other phenomena; some followers believe these to be true stories. Traditionally, both faith and reason have been considered sources of religious beliefs – Wikipedia.

3. The purpose of work, life, religions, education is just to inspire us to strive to be Righteous. Thus, all systems e.g., parenting, growing up, education, schooling, daily work, vocation, social customs must be aligned towards inspiring us to get established in the habit and discipline of “daily self-development practices”, to develop powers of mind to awaken viveka-buddhi and self-control and will-power to acquire commitment to righteousness and to our respective duties. Only such a mind can enable us to walk-the-talk towards the good, better, Best. The spiritual practices, needed to walk-the-talk towards “Perfection”, may be described variously, which we may call Religion. Today, we must learn to focus more and more on those universal Self-development practices needed for building powers of mind. We must learn to appreciate that mythology, scriptures, are different and we may draw our inspiration from our respective faith but allow others to their respective faith; instead of dividing societies into My-Vs-Your God/Religion.

4. Science of a human-being: Man is a Living-being, Jiva+Atman, Body-mind complex Enlivened by Consciousness. The Kingdom of God is within us.

# The goal of mankind is to manifest this divinity; to befriend the Consciousness within. Seek you first the Kingdom of God within each of us, thru’ Righteousness; thru’ love thy neighbour as thy Self; thru’ service of God in man; thru’ work and vocation as our highest worship; Practice seeing God in all; Practice seeing and worshipping Mother/Father/Teacher/Atithi as God; etc.

# How? By striving to purify our Inner nature, our Mind, thru’ righteous conduct and thru’ fulfilment of our respective duties and thru’ pouring good thoughts thru’ our respective ‘prayers’, worship, self-reflection, etc.

# Only a purified mind becomes amenable to control towards the higher dimensions of life; towards becoming good, better, Best.

# All Religions are essentially asking us to realize Consciousness in and thru’ our daily work and vocation, supported by our respective ways of worship, meditation, scriptural knowledge.

5. How do we become men of Wisdom, Mentors, Shrestha, of society? There are just four minimum qualities to be acquired by each of us, irrespective of who and what we are, to understand and grow in wisdom! These four attributes are: i) power of discrimination ( viveka ) ii) ability to focus on the higher and the essentials and ignore the non-essentials iii) Set of six-Virtues and Self-control disciplines, finally iv) the intense desire to seek Perfection, Freedom, Peace, Bliss.

The Six-disciplines are: i) Practicing Control of Mind (Improve Quality of Thoughts; Reduce Quantity of Thoughts by keeping busy on planned Important goals and Direct Thoughts towards good, better, best) ii) Practicing Controlling the Sense organs iii) Practicing Self-Contentment iv) Practicing Forbearance v) Practicing and building intellectual conviction in the highest Goal of life, Sraddha, Faith vi) Equanimity, evenness, calmness of mind.

6. Who is Accountable to make us Mentors, Shrestha? Parenting, schooling, preaching must be made responsible and accountable to inspire us to acquire, imbibe these disciplines during 25 years of growing up. Anyone who acquires these attributes will enjoy its benefits. Aside from these there are no other requirements or restrictions or beliefs in this or that God or Religion.
