India to Bharat · Essay #224

Welcoming 2019, Bharat’s message to India:

1. Ancient Indian civilization is our role model asserts Gandhiji in his Hind ‘Swaraj’: He had succinctly observed that under the impact of the British rule, India is turning into an ‘irreligious’ country, away from God. He likened modern civilization to a ‘mouse’ ‘gnawing’ our people while apparently ‘soothing them’! He critiques Lawyers, Doctors, ‘false education’ of Macaulay.

Gandhiji therefore envisioned that Education must place before students the ideal of Bharat that defines ‘true civilization’ as that where education should instill that ‘mode of conduct which points out to man, the path of duty’. Gandhiji says that education and schooling must instill what he identifies as our core values and ideals: –

  • Attaining ‘mastery over one’s mind’ since ‘self-mastery’ leads to moral behavior
  • curb on unnecessary competitiveness
  • focus on rural life
  • preference for small scale technologies and decentralized polity
  • emphasis on ancestral profession (i.e. we should choose a vocation based on our inherent tendencies, skills, etc.; and, a work within our character and competence, doing which we refine our samskaras and remain on path to ‘Perfection’)
  • limits to self-indulgence of luxuries and pleasures (simple living high thinking)
  • moral control of ‘sages’ over the ‘kings’ (‘Wisdom’ as guide to ‘authority’; those in power to have Vivek to know and Will to do right)

Gandhiji himself admitted that at present, modern India is moving away from our ancient traditional values!

Our Challenge: As India, we have so many political parties and opposition parties each trying to divide society and disbursing freebies for numbers. We also have divisive religions who believe and teach that their way is the only true way. Similarly, we have many other differences, languages, social customs, etc. How can we bring all citizens together as one Bharat, as one people walking towards a common goal?

So, what are the uniting ideals of Bharat? It is to scatter broadcast the highest fundamental truth, and the core of every religion, that behind every living being is the same divine Spirit, Self, Rooha, Noor, Atman, Kingdom of God. And that, realizing our divinity is the common, universal goal not just of a Bharati, but of all humanity.

All religious practices, all goodness and virtues, when sincerely practiced lead ultimately to the awakening to our spiritual reality and oneness as ‘spirit’, expressing itself as love for all equally, whatever may be the outer differences.

How do we imbibe this ideal of Oneness? Bharat gives the answer: By serving people with the idea that we are serving God in man. Serve man as God. This way we raise our daily work to a stage of worship. Doing our duties itself becomes the highest worship. It becomes meditation with eyes open.

Focus of Schooling, politics, governance, religions , must therefore be to spread and teach the ideal of our ‘oneness’ as spirit, that unites (not divides) all citizens.

Who is the Role Model of India that is Bharat? All freedom fighters led by Gandhiji, including Subhas Bose, Nehru, Indira Gandhi & particularly her mother, who was ardently spiritual, were inspired by Vivekananda and his love for the nation and his proclamation that the USP of India was and shall be the same as the spirit of tyaga and seva of Bharat. It was Indira as PM who nominated Vivekananda as the National Youth icon and it was Rajiv Gandhi who declared the Birthday of Vivekananda as the National Youth Day on 12 th Jan 1985, and GOI celebrates it as such, ever since, by discussing his vision of Bharat.

Let all political parties come together to transform India into Bharat as envisioned by Vivekananda, Gandhiji and our freedom fighters.
