India to Bharat · Essay #176

Nuggets for Self-introspection

Many of us are under lockdown with time on our hands. Here is a principle to ponder over: “An effect is never different from its cause”; but “cause is unaffected by the effect”. The ‘sun is the cause; all activities are the effect’. Based on this scientific principle, here is a nugget of wisdom to mull over during our daily work and life:

Our God, by whatever name we call it and whatever be our understanding of It, is really omnipotent, omnipresent, omniscient. Our respective name, form, idea of an all-powerful intelligence, ‘God’ may be different but we generally agree that It is all powerful, all pervasive and all knowing. For most of us, consciously or unconsciously, this is our universal idea of God. We, in India that is Bharat refer to God as Brahman, pointing towards the idea of ‘greater than greatest’.

If we really appreciate our idea of God, we will realize that God has to be one with all of us and the whole creation. Science expresses this idea through the example of the one Sun. The same sun is common to us all. We eat the same sun in our food; the sun digests it; we wear sun in our clothes. All of us and all our actions come from the sun; it is the sun which is one with us by its life and light.

It follows that if we can practice performing our day-to-day activities with this awareness, then our daily actions, our work itself, becomes a worship!

Consuming food is a daily activity and our daily necessity. Generally, we consider ourselves as the doer and agent of all our activities. However, with this Knowledge of God, ‘greater than greatest’, we can transform this daily action itself, into an act of worship, by remembering and reflecting on the idea, which is an ultimate truth, that: –

Whatever we ‘eat’ is Brahman. Whatever we ‘offer’ is Brahman. The ‘process’ of offering itself is taking place in and through Brahman. The ‘stomach’ to whom the food is being offered and which digests the food for us is Brahman. I who am offering is Brahman. And of course, one who is conscious of this wisdom during this process of activity offering attains oneness with Brahman.

This is a verse used by many for reflection before meals; ‘eating’ as an action being a process common to us all, without exception. However, ‘eating’ is only symbolic for an action and so, the same idea is equally applicable to all our actions, whatever they be. Thus, all my actions, work, are being done by Brahman, as an offering to Brahman, as individuals and as a society; and so, they must be ethical, virtuous, of excellent quality and for good of society.

In fact Food itself is called Brahman, because we are nothing but the food we consume! And, food includes all out thoughts, words, deeds also, that we take into ourselves! Let’s reflect over this nugget of wisdom of India that is Bharat.
