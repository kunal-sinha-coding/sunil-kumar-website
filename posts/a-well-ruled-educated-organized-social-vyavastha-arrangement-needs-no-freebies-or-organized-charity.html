India to Bharat · Essay #039

A well ruled, educated, organized social Vyavastha, arrangement, needs no freebies or organized charity!

1. Education and religions must be a process of cultivating Karma Yogi’s who nurture, not exploit societies! Large scale focus on charity and political parties offering subsidies and freebies to win votes are certainly needed when natural disasters occur! Otherwise, if the society is well governed, why should there be any need for large scale organized charities or freebies!

Today, if religions, devotees, society and governance are all busy mainly in charity, offering subsidies, freebies, it is obvious that schooling, growing up, social norms, governance have all miserably failed to produce the good who can be mentors and guide society towards goodness!

Don’t we realize that in democracy political parties win elections not by being good and giving good governance but by fault-finding and, pleasing the monied and giving freebies to people to ‘buy’ support. We need to realize that we need organized charities only because people and Vyavastha, systems, are not focused on Righteousness! If and when and where the individuals, rulers and administrators are educated to be righteous, aka Karma Yogi, then religions, devotees, politicians don’t have to organize ‘giving’.

2. Education must somehow help inculcate and imbibe the universal wisdom of all religions: I can secure my own good only by doing you good. There is no other way. It is only by doing our duties that one attains to one’s own good. Doing our duties and good to others is the one great, universal religion. Why should we do good to the world? Apparently to help the world, but really to help ourselves; because selfless fulfilment of duties purifies the mind!

3. The idea of God arises from the difference between an “Inert-matter” and “Living-beings”. Matter is that which doesn’t move on its own, unless and external force is applied. A stone moves, if say a boy kicks it. The force is inert because it’s point of application, magnitude, direction is determined by the boy whose body is matter; but because he’s a living-being, Jiva-atman, the “consciousness” gives him free-will/freedom of choice and the boy suffers the consequences of his actions. Behind motion of all matter is consciousness, and identified with that, we’re all one.

4. So, religions and education must teach that only when we’re righteous and fulfil our duties, we purify our mind, i.e. reduce ego and expand towards selflessness. So, we must imbibe the idea, you are God, I am God, man is God. It is this God manifested who is doing everything in this world. Is there a different God sitting high up somewhere, but not in our hearts? Purpose of work and life is to regain (religion), reunite (yoga), rejoin in oneness with whole world as our family!

5. Our time-tested “ varna-vyavastha ”, i.e. ‘Family based parenting, occupation, vocation’ was based on the Universal Wisdom of our Oneness as “Consciousness” and both Religions and education was a process of “Swadhyaya and Sadhana”, Self-reflections and Mind management. All V yavastha was an organized idea, a societal arrangement where the majority of people occupied positions in harmony with their joint family’s inherent, innate tendencies, skills – ‘character and competence’, and readily accepted their situation and each focused-on learning and doing their respective duties, from childhood! Thus, governance had no need to indulge in pleasing the masses but ensured that education produced Karma Yogis and Mentors of Samaaja.

6. Modern idea of religions and education isn’t focused on inculcating and imbibing commitment to kartavya-palan thru’ engaging in a daily process of self-study/self-reflection and daily Self-development practices for managing and directing the ‘Mind-thoughts-desires’ God-wards, in and thru’ our daily work and occupations. If growing-up is not concerned with identifying and evaluation based on commitment to duties, discipline, character-competence, then with freedom of choice people tend to focus on ‘Artha-Kaama’ (money-desires) and remain unconcerned with ‘Dharma-Moksha’ (Righteousness-God wards).

7 . Karma Yoga is excellence in action. It is selflessly working for good of samaaja. It is fulfilment of all our duties. It is ability to focus on doing our duties to best of our ability. It is to remain calm and respectful even if results are not as we expected. It is knowing what exactly our duty at a particular time, place or role is, and just doing our best. It is Selflessness. It is the life skill of working in a way that guarantees long term peace prosperity fulfilment for all.

Samaaja literally means, ‘same as God’. Our parents are our creator and we are one with them and we exist only because of them. Similarly, we all are one with each other and one with God! Only a purified mind can experience the divine consciousness, the self within and realize that as self we’re one and eternal….

We’ve to learn and practice to be aware of thoughts flowing in our mind. Realize I am the observer of my thoughts. So, I must choose that which helps me Befriend the Atman, within. I have to manage my mind and responses; improve quality of thoughts and direct them God wards. Otherwise, my mind, ‘my past thoughts-desires-habits’, ‘Samskara’, will manage me! Leading a disciplined timetable based planned life for long-term good and focused on Important things, helps build willpower, self-control, and viveka-buddhi and we gradually learn to be the boss of our mind, not its slaves; as we all are!

8 . Conclusion – Karma Yoga as a compulsory subject: Preachers and teachers should help students get established in the habit of self-development practices to build powers of mind to be able to walk-the-talk. They should be held responsible and accountable to inculcate, guide, and help imbibe and evaluate based on commitment to constitutional fundamental duties, universal values and our respective duties, Kartavya-palan.
