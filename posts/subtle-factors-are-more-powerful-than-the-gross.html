India to Bharat · Essay #058

Subtle factors are more powerful than the gross

The Healthy Worldview of Reality is that we’re all one, interconnected, interrelated, and interdependent. This is the holistic worldview of “Oneness” as opposed to the worldview of “separateness”. Who am I? ‘Am I’ not an integral part of Nature? Why then ‘I am’ not a subject of schooling? Isn’t it really, really surprising that while scientists are busy exploring nature and the world out there, they haven’t bothered to know about themselves!

Schools teach us all kinds of subjects relating to the universe, but funnily enough, they somehow exclude ‘me’ from it, even though I am an integral part of nature, the ‘object’ being studied! And, I am also the ‘knower’, the observer, the ‘subject’, who does the studying! How is it that this curious and amazing fact has never dawned on us?

The Upanishads tell us: ‘All this is but Brahman’; ‘All this has That as its essence’; ‘That is the Reality’; ‘That is the Self’; That thou art’. Just as the foam, ripple, wave, bubble are all different modifications of one sea but they all are nothing but water; non-different from sea, though among themselves they interact in the form of separating, coalescing!

Only when we are taught and learn to live by this worldview of “Oneness”, can we grow to have love, care, concern for more and more, till we attain the actual experience of our oneness with the whole creation; and this is mankind’s goal. To attain to this experience of “Oneness” we’ve to take care of the ‘subtle’ factors, the “Subjective” as opposed to the external, objective, where we’re now focused.

Subtle Factors? When we talk of subtle factors it implies our ‘worldview’, nazariya, the subjective, inner subtle thoughts. Our present behavior, manners, character, conduct are seen by people in our external actions, but in reality, they are governed by our own previously acquired ‘worldview’ which are not visible. Our worldview includes our choices, actions, motives, intentions, ideals, values, virtues, morals, ethics, etc. which manage and govern our outer actions.

Our Thoughts and Responses: The subtlest ‘Building-blocks’ of our life are our ‘thoughts’ that are continuously forming our personality, based on our Responses and Choices, and become our world-view, beliefs, etc. and then govern our personality. Like it or not, we’re enslaved by our Responses and worldview that we’re continuously forming; and so, it is very important to be aware of the subjective impressions that we create in our mind by our responses and actions.

People as resources or Human-beings? The very fact that today we’re treating human being as ‘resources’ implies that our intention is not to help their growth per se but to use them like any other material resources. While development of both gross and ‘subtle’ factors of our personality are important and need to be harmoniously developed, today we need to pay far more attention to the inner or ‘subtle’ aspects, which we’ve totally ignored.

Managing our Responses: During schooling and training programs we need to practice and learn how to manage our Responses. The first step before responding is to pause, to stand apart as a witness and, just become aware of thoughts, emotions, feelings arising in our mind. We can then, before responding, use our Conscience, Viveka, and ask ourselves: is it legal; contractual; ethical; will it give me long-term peace and joy? What if my family knew? What if everyone knows; newspaper publish? We can then use our Creative Imagination to choose a Win-win response for long-term good over short term. And, build the Will power not to react but respond positively based on the win-win choice.

Principles to be reflected upon that education can help us explore and imbibe:

  • Work, serve, interact, with each other seeing God in them
  • Work done in a spirit of service leads to material prosperity and spiritual Perfection
  • Strength and inspiration for excellence come from the God within, thru’ prayers, meditation, holy studies, unselfishness
  • Serve others not only with material things but also by showing respect to them in recognition of the kingdom of god within them
  • Fulfillment of duties with a spirit of detachment is excellence in action
  • Equanimity of mind is the best of all yogas
  • Purity of mind governs external action; we need both internal purity and excellence in action
  • By material sciences we overcome obstacles and attain prosperity; by spiritual sciences we are inspired to lead an ethical life and attain immortality.
  • The essence of leadership in any field is self-control, the ability to respond on win-win basis
  • Only by mutual cooperation, respect and fellow-feeling can all of us attain the highest good, both material and spiritual.
  • He is a true leader who is ready to sacrifice his life for the good of others
  • Wherever there is righteous ethical action, there alone is prosperity, victory, welfare of people and relentless justice.
  • Unlimited bliss and peace come only to those who see God in themselves and serve God in every being.
  • True happiness is never found in the finite; it is found only in the infinite when we experience our oneness with the whole creation.
