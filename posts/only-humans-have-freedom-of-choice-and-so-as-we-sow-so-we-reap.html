India to Bharat · Essay #044

‘Only humans have Freedom of Choice and so, as we sow, so we reap’

1. Who is responsible for our Birth? Why in a particular lineage, place, religion, era? Certainly, I will not choose to be born poor, sickly! To study this issue, we can perhaps start by defining the idea of God as ‘He who is responsible for those things for which you and me are not’!

2. Why’re we different from our very birth? It is our greatest misfortune, that modern Religions, based on mere belief, bereft of reasoning, have failed to sit together and give a logical universally acceptable answer for mankind, to this basic question. The result is that Religions and preachers are no longer leaders and role models of society! Modern society rests on a stupid idea, supported by foolish preachers and politicians, that people are born equal with a clean state; that this is the one and only life; so, ‘ feel free to grab what you can’ ; there will be no other opportunity!

Society has rejected unreasonable religiosity and belief-based ideologies and thrown them out of mainstream discourses – and we’re now led by money and political manipulators. We’re all now in a state of perpetual purposelessness with no men of wisdom to guide and inspire us to be good! Let us evolve for ourselves a logical explanation, based on the plan of Creation as espoused by all religions:

3. Scientists agree, we live in an intelligent Universe. We realize that the entire nature and all living beings, are being nurtured by a cosmic intelligence, which maintains the entire universe in a harmonious order, Rtm, governed by two simple all-embracing principles: ‘everything is changing’, Jagat and ‘things come and go’, sansar ! In this grand cosmic-play only we human beings have freedom of choice; we’ve no script to adhere to, and no role assigned to us. Are we really free? Are we competent to be free?

4. We alone appear to have Freedom of Choice: We’ve the freedom to write our parts and play the role of rich, poor, good, bad, teacher, administrator, trader, worker, efficient, inefficient…We are in a way, getting more and more freedom to choose our vocation, spouses, do whatever we wish to do … within societal norms of course. However, we are told to be good and do good; and, there’s a fine print too, which says, treat others as ourselves; the whole creation is our family! Why?

5. We’re governed by the principle of ‘as you sow, so shall you reap’, what Newton discovered, action is equal to reaction, the ‘law of cause and effect’ governs us! We’re all equally the children of one cosmic principle, and are born equal, innocent and full of all virtues and goodness. However, with our freedom of choice, curiosity, inquisitiveness, impulses, we grow up making different choices and get entrapped in the consequences of our own choices and are today what we’re, based on our own actions. So, we must first of all stop blaming others and learn to be fully responsible for what we’re; we’re not just our choices; but also, reapers of the consequences of our own choices!

6. A mother loves all children equally whether they are dull, intelligent, talented, good or bad, but doesn’t feed them equally! She nurtures them depending on their need, and guides their growth in their respective areas of interest, character and competence, with love. Even when she chastises them or even punishes them, it is out of love; never out of hatred.

Similarly in God’s kingdom we’re all His children, here to enjoy the universe and only after we’re done with pleasures and feel satiated, in one or in many lives, we strive to go home from where we came. This is expressed as reunite (Yoga) and regain , Latin of Religion, our Oneness with the Creation and Creator . Of course, we’re free to make our choices but our guides, mentors, guru, god-men keep reminding us that happiness, peace, fulfilment that we seek thru’ all our actions can be guaranteed only when we are established in commitment to righteous conduct, to our respective duties, ethical conduct, etc.

If our choice was to be rich and famous but we’re poor and infamous; these are the long-term consequences of our choices for short-term results. And so, if we are not very happy with what we now are, we have the freedom of choice to start struggling and striving to make different choices. And slowly but surely, we will overcome the earlier consequences and learn to awaken our Vivek to make long-term good choices.

7. The Creator who is responsible for our Birth, wants us to come back to Him, after enjoying the creation and, this is the Goal of all human beings. So, he decides where in which family, circumstances, is my best chances to bear the consequences of my choices and continue to grow towards experiencing our oneness with the whole Creation. And really, He alone is Responsible for our birth; it is not a mere accident; not luck. He alone has ability and intelligence to ‘analyze’ all our actions and determine what is for our good, using all our previous actions. And out of love for us, He gives us the Birth in the Family/caste which is the Best possible plan for our spiritual growth towards Oneness with more and more till we feel one with the whole.

8. This in a nutshell is the plan of creation as expressed by all religions. This universal message is well expressed in the pithy statement: “Through Dharma ‘Earn’, ‘Enjoy’ attain Mukti/Freedom”, aka Perfection, Heaven, Nirvana, Salvation, etc. It is the duty of elders to produce enlightened citizens, and Mentors of society.
