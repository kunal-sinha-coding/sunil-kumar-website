India to Bharat · Essay #132

Reflections on rise and fall of thought – belief, worldview, nazariya, soanch

Today we’re in a position to immediately access all the ‘wisdom’ of all philosophers, sciences and religions! And so, to inspire us to be good, the educated must be made aware of what the best people have thought on some big-issues of our lives, such as why we’re here, where’re we going, the idea of virtues, of god, life, death and thereafter, purpose of creation, etc. What makes us living? Life? Then what exactly is life? Who is supposed to look and research into life?

We needn’t be slaves to narrow ideological beliefs pedalled by self-centered preachers of faiths who have lost our trust; and so, stand banished from the mainstream. We need no longer confine ourselves to their divisive belief that ‘my-god-my-book’ alone is the truth, which they now preach behind closed doors and which, continues to be responsible for genocide, terrorism, etc.!

To explore the idea and purpose of life we can start with its USP, i.e., death; which really is the main fact of life and what happens after death is the focus of all ‘thinking’. We can observe how life begins, but science and modern religions don’t know why we’re uniquely different, even at birth. We can find a medical cause of death, but not why at a particular time and manner.

Interestingly, Sanskrit words for ‘Body’ and this ‘Creation’ help understand its ‘swabhava ’, nature. They’re ‘ shareera’, ‘ jagat’/‘samsar ’, respectively meaning, that which ‘ deacays ’, that where things ‘ change ’ and, that where things ‘ come and go ’; that is, they come into existence, decay, and go out of existence.

When we look at various ideas of death, we are struck by a mysterious fact, that though death is an end of existence; no culture or religion or worldview, accepts death as the end! Why? Why do we wish to continue existing? Why do we bury the dead and believe that on a Day of Judgment our deeds will be reviewed by someone, God? Why do we bring the idea that we should do good, to have a better life, … after death!

Doesn’t all this point to a logical conclusion, that death is not the end but there is life after death ? Thus, the only explanation seems to be, ‘do good’ because there is life after death. What is the Day of Judgment? According to the principle of ‘ as we sow, so we reap ’, every moment must be the Day, i.e., whenever we are happy and fulfilled that is ‘Heaven’ and when we’re in pain and suffering we’re actually in ‘Hell’! And this principle also explains why we’re not equal but unequal and different at very birth; obviously based on our good or bad deeds. So, try to be good; it is for your own good!

Why do some ‘burn’ others preserve or ‘bury’ the body? It is our worldview that decides. They burn the body, who believe or know that there is no permanent connection between the body and the life/Consciousness which had made the body alive! And, body-brain has no ‘record’ of our deeds; and death is the end of this individual. They bury the body who believe that the body and its record of deeds will somehow survive for review on the Day.

Science should ask how and on what basis will our deeds be Judged, after death? Where is the record in the brain after death? And this exploration can lead to the idea that we’re not just body-brain! The biggest scientific discovery was and is, that we’re body-brain- mind consciousness . While body-brain are matter; mind is extremely subtle matter but being in proximity with Consciousness, appears as the ‘apparent-knower’ and ‘I am’ idea emerges. Mind is the subtlest matter, so subtle that it is just like a field where we experience flow of thoughts-feelings; though it is actually pure-singular Consciousness that is the one and only ‘knower’, ‘subject’ which enlivens the mind.

On death it is our mind-consciousness that survives and remains suspended, pending the ‘Judgment’. This mind is extremely subtle matter which carries a record of all our deeds. It is sort of a bridge between ‘matter’ and ‘consciousness’. And, in fact it is based on this record that we’re being continuously judged. All our prosperity, joys, pain, sufferings, sorrows, are a direct result of the principle of as we sow, so we reap , continuously being recorded in the conscious-mind; therefore, all exhortations to be good and do good, etc.

Unfortunately, modern sciences and the educated study only nature, the measurable world of matter but we collectively have no idea how the body becomes alive, even though a living being is called Jivatman! So, what is this Life, Consciousness, Atman, Spirit, Rooha, Kingdom of God within us?

Mind-brain-consciousness: The scientists are now trying to explore the idea of consciousness which the vedic scientists have directly experienced and tell us, that ‘knowing’ is the USP of ‘life and consciousness’. Thus, the ‘knower’ is separate from the ‘known’, which is matter. As a living-being, I am ‘Body’ which is ‘observable-matter’ enlivened by ‘Life/Consciousness’. So, to explore life/consciousness we have to go inwards to study ourselves as the ‘knower’, not outwards!

Evolving our Collective Thought: Modern society is bereft of mankind’s spiritual wisdom because of the immaturity of narrow minded preachers who enforce ‘belief’ but disallow open academic discussion of their ‘version’ of the ‘thoughts’. And so, unfortunately, we’re focused only on ‘earning’ and ‘enjoying’!
