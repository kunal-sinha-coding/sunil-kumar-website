India to Bharat · Essay #048

An ode to the asuric culture we’ve adopted – on this International Day of Poetry

We wonder and wonder, why our bureaucracy, our schools and teachers, our elders, and you and I are shy of saying that at least the educated. if not, all citizens should Imbibe the spirit of FD!

What’s the harm in agreeing that the educated be committed to Swadharma and be Karma-yogis!

We keep looking for the lost tribe of the Shrestha, but alas they are rarely found!

The Shrestha, the gentle-men, the good and noble persons, are now an extinct breed!

Rich or poor, worker or ruler; whatever were their status or vocation,

They were found once in Bharat and were the role models and elite of society.

In and thru’ their daily working and living, they were committed to their swadharma;

They were the karma yogis, rulers or workers, they had but one fault!

They never focused on ‘Artha’ , kama’ but strove towards a vague, unknown idea of illumination!

Now we’re here in the 22nd Century and the Asurs have taken reins of our land,

They now call it India and the Asuric scientists focus only on what they can see and enjoy.

Artha and Kaama are now in fashion, no more running after the invisible!

Knowledge now means knowing of the infinite variety we see in the world;

Not of that One, He who sitting within really enables us to see!

Evaluation is based only on paper-degrees, not by one’s character; be you a thief.

No more do we have Vidyarthis burning for Vidya that leads to humility, illumination, freedom!

No more Acharyas, role models of righteous conduct; but we’ve teachers, preachers, galore

We bemoan, O where are our forefathers who believed that childhood was meant to get established in our Swadharma, be disciplined, learn to strive to be a Karma yogi.

No more striving to awaken Viveka! Why struggle to build will-power, self-control, strength and powers of mind to walk the ‘right’ path! There’s no right; no-thing as duty or as illumination!

Finally, we’re no more slaves driven to be good, better, best; we’re free to do what we wish!

Vision of our Forefathers: As elders and as academia, it is our role and responsibility to help students, imbibe the spirit of our Constitutional Fundamental Duties, Art. 51A, and inspire and help them to evolve for themselves an Action Plan for Self-development, so that the educated strive for excellence in their vocation and work as Mentors for good of samaaja.

Dissatisfaction with the Present: Modern education and public governance has failed to curb galloping selfishness, consumerism, greed, exploitation, and wide spread corruption among the educated and the political leadership.

Society, without proper systems of character-education, training, recruitment, promotion etc. and nurturing the young as leaders, is bound to be ineffective and will be unable to deliver results for good of samaaja.

Agreed First Steps: The GOI has now declared their intention to train government servants as Karma Yogis and the New Education Policy talks of Human Values. So, the first steps:

i) Each of us must evolve for ourselves an action plan for Self-development:

ii) We must reflect and determine a Benchmark for ideas of the highest and best, of excellence, of perfection, for determining the direction and goal of growth and development?

iii) A Benchmark: Do I have love and peace within myself? Am I growing in apanapan with more and more? Am I growing in commitment to my duties? Am I working for good of more & more? Am I growing in spontaneous feelings of love, care, concern, for others?

iv) How do I train my mind and manage my unwanted thoughts to expand and grow?

Let’s Reflect on: Who am I? Who are ‘we’? Who is the Institution? Who is Samaaja? What are our basic duties? What systems and steps are needed to imbibe ethics, scientific temper, spirit of excellence, in our individual, societal, Institutional, work-culture?
