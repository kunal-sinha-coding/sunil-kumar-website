India to Bharat · Essay #114

The idea of Hindutva – Should it be detested or discussed and understood

Hindutva defies all attempts at analysis: The ideas and ideals, the systems and societies, the thoughts and sentiments which have centred round this name are so varied and rich, so powerful and so subtle, so elusive and yet they’re so vivid that it has taken forty centuries, if not more, to mould it as it is. It is the resultant of countless actions of prophets and poets, lawyers and law-givers, heroes and historians, now conflicting, now commingling, now cooperating!

# Hindu-sthan meaning the land of Hindus, the first essential of Hindutva must necessarily be this geographical one. A Hindu is primarily a citizen either in himself or through his forefathers of ‘Hindusthan’ and claims the land as his motherland. In America (around 1920) the word Hindu is generally understood of an Indian, without any religious implication.

# Had the word Hindu been left to convey this primary significance only, which it had in common with all the words derived from Sindhu then it would really have meant an Indian, a citizen of Hindusthan as the word Hindu does.

# Our land is so vast and yet so well-knit, so well demarcated from others and yet so strongly entrenched that no country in the world is more closely marked out by the fingers of nature as a geographical unit beyond criticism, as also is the name Hindusthan or Hindu that it has come to bear. The first image that it rouses in the mind is unmistakably of our motherland.

# Hindutva is not a word but a history. Not only the spiritual or religious history of our people but a history in full. Hinduism is only a derivative, a fraction, a part of Hindutva. Hindutva is not identical with what is vaguely indicated by the term Hinduism, which generally means religious dogma or creed. ‘Hinduness’ would have certainly been a better word than Hinduism as a near parallel to Hindutva.

# Hindutva embraces all the departments of thought and activity of the whole being of our Hindu race. To understand the significance of this term Hindutva, we must first understand the essential meaning of the word Hindu itself.

# ‘The land which is to the north of the sea and to the south of the Himalaya Mountain is named Bharata. All those on this side of the Indus who claimed the land from Indus to the seas, as the land of their birth, felt that they were directly mentioned by that one single expression, Hindustan, the land of Hindus.

# Religion is a mighty motive force; and, so is plunder: But where religion is goaded on by rapine and rapine serves as a handmaid to religion, the propelling force that is generated by these together is only equalled by the profundity of human misery and devastation they leave behind them in their march.

# Before the sword of Islam of Peace, Arabia ceased to be what Arabia was; Iran annihilated; Egypt, Syria, Afghanistan, Baluchistan, Tartary; nations and civilizations fell in heaps! India had to face Arabs, Persians, Pathans, Baluchis, Tartars, Turks, Moguls. Day after day, decade after decade, century after century, the ghastly conflict continued and India single-handed kept up the fight morally and militarily. Nothing can weld peoples into a nation and nations into a state as the pressure of a common foe. Hatred separates as well as unites.

# In contrast to the sword, Buddhism had made the first and yet the greatest attempt to propagate a universal religion. ‘Go, ye monks, to all the ten directions of the world and preach the law of Righteousness! But truly, it was a law of Righteousness. It had no ulterior end in view, no lust for land or lucre quickening its steps.

# In this prolonged furious conflict our people became intensely conscious of ourselves as Hindus and were welded into a nation to an extent unknown in our history. It must not be forgotten that we have all along referred to the progress of the Hindu movement as a whole and not to that of any particular creed or religious section thereof; all suffered as Hindus and triumphed as Hindus.

# The enemies hated us as Hindus and the whole family of peoples and races, of sects and creeds that flourished here had a common issue, to defend the honour and independence of Hindustan and to maintain the cultural unity and civic life of Hindutva and not Hinduism alone, but Hindutva, i.e., Hindu dharma, righteousness, that was being fought out on the hundred fields of battle.

# Civilization is the expression of the mind of man; of what man has made of matter; at its best it is the perfect triumph of the soul of man over matter and man alike. It is the story of its thoughts, literature, social institutions and Hindus have succeeded in preserving their culture through millenniums.
