India to Bharat · Essay #037

Essence of Vedas & religions is to treat work as ‘worship of God in man & creation’?

1. Everyone accepts the validity of the Vedas! But isn’t it strange that no one has ever claimed the authorship of the Vedas! Had there been an author there was bound to be some mention somewhere. We know from our experience today, how people claim authorship, patents, etc. for everything. Why is it that no one has ever claimed the authorship of the Vedas?

2. Another very profound fact is that the Vedas have remained unchanged! Not many of us may know that every letter, every syllable, and the duration and rhyme for its chanting, is permanently fixed!

We know from our experience that people love to change tunes, words, pattern of chanting, etc. but surprisingly every intonation and word of the Vedas has remained fixed! If the ancient Rishis visit us today, they will find no change in it.

To imagine that any human being could have developed and written it down and that these have remained unchanged for thousands of years and are chanted today as they were chanted in the beginning of creation shows that they are eternal.

3. We have never realized how huge are the Vedas! They’re immensely large, beyond all imagination and were never written down but transmitted verbally – that’s why Shruti, ‘that which is heard’, is another name for the Vedas, ‘to know (that which is beyond intellect!)

In fact, most of the Vedas are now lost to us yet whatever fraction of it remains, takes 245 hours for chanting from beginning to end. This cannot be the creation of any one single human being.

Moreover, if different people have written it at different times, then there are bound to be contradictions. And even if one person had written it, we know that people are not consistent in their views, and keep changing and revising their views.

4. Surprisingly, there are no contradictions in this huge ocean that the Vedas are! Of course, at a superficial level or read through translations but without proper knowledge of rules of grammar, logic, and preparation and training, people may mistakenly see some!

5. Vedas reveal unknown facts: There are certain statements made in the Vedas, which are being discovered by scientific minds with all the instruments at their disposal only, now. The simplest example is that there is a mention in the Atharva-Vedas of seven continents, whereas, the 7 th , i.e., Antarctica has been found only in 1911. The Vedanta is the essence of the Vedas, and asserts that Consciousness alone Exists eternally!

6. Are you a sincere seeker? Do you seek to know God, our highest Goal? If we really reflect on the above points, we will come to the conclusion that no human being could have invented or written the Vedas; they cannot be of human creation and must be divine in origin. As Parents and as educated Leaders and Teachers of society, we must realize our duty to be Mentors of society by studying for ourselves the profundity and the essence of the Vedas as given in the following universal statements, which are the essence of all the Vedas and all the Religions:

1 st Step: The seeker is told “Pragyanam Brahman: Consciousness is God”. The great infinite truth you are seeking is Consciousness! Brahman, aka God, literally signifies divine, conscious, infinitude, omniscience, omnipotence, omnipresent, etc.)

We as educated, sincere, honest Parents and Teachers are the Mentors of society. As Mentors it is our duty to understand, imbibe and then spread this wisdom. It must be our duty to discuss, debate, question, study, explore and reflect on the idea of what exactly is God! The Vedas assert and explain that God is Consciousness; and it seems logical to us today that God cannot be finite Matter and our Bodies, brain etc. are all matter, so Life or something like it must be Consciousness. Brahman literally signifies, greater than…. Infinitude; as the singular Knower!

2 nd Step : As Mentors we must reflect and ask where is this Consciousness? We ourself will realize and negate all “objects”, as they are observed reality. Vedas then clarify: It is not in any temple etc. The Supreme Truth, Consciousness, is right within your innermost self. Consciousness within you is present everywhere – “That Thou art, Tattvamasi”.

The Bible says, “ Love thy neighbour as thyself ” . Why? ‘ The neighbour is loved, because he is your own Self. The same spirit, divine Consciousness, is in all of us. “ Living-being ” , Jiva-atman, is ‘ Body-mind ’ enlivened by ‘ Consciousness ’ ; Each soul is potentially divine. Jesus teaches, ‘ The kingdom of God is within you; seek you first the kingdom of God and his righteousness and everything else will be added unto you ’ . ‘ Ana-al-Haq ’ in Islam means ‘ I am the Truth ’ .

Surprise of surprises! How can I, we, all, be God?

3 rd Step: The Vedas explain, When I separate myself from all ‘matter’ then I experience “Ayam Atma Brahman”; ‘This Atman in me is Brahman’ . Our body-mind is just dead matter, so Life, Consciousness within is God, aka Brahman.

4 th Step: Now I have to realize “Aham Brahmasmi, I am Consciousness”. In essence “I am Brahman”, I realize it when I stop looking at “names-forms”, ‘naam-roopa’, thru our limited mind!

Once I realize I am Brahman; then I expand to realize, I am all that Exists, Sarvam khalvidam Brahman.

Consciousness being infinite cannot be defined, but it can be expressed as “Sad-Chid-Ananda”, ‘Unchanging Truth-Consciousness-Bliss”. Before birth and before awakening from Deep Sleep, we’re all ONE as Consciousness. As long as I identify as body-mind, I am a Jiva-atman. Thru’ ‘Righteous conduct’, and ‘selfless work as worship’, aka Karma Yoga, I purify and expand my mind to Re-gain/Re-unite (Religion/Yoga) and Realize my Oneness and be in Rtm, Order, with the whole creation.
