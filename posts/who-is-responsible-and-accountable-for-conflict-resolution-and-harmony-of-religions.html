India to Bharat · Essay #081

Who is responsible and accountable for conflict resolution and harmony of religions?

Religion is a modern, Western word which divides people, and we talk of My God, Religion, Scripture, Prophet vs Yours. Mere Belief in our respective faith and religions is necessary and helps in the beginning of our journey God wards. However direct experience, realization of the truth of the Creation and God as Creator is the ultimate test. So, the more mature preachers, seekers, followers, must also be ready to discuss, explore, reflect, know, other viewpoints and realize that, that infinitude of an omnipotent God cannot be encapsulated into any particular, finite belief system!

2. Bharat’s civilizational ideal is of harmony of religions! So, Education, Religions, Governance must ensure that every citizen understands, appreciates, and can sincerely affirm:

# “The whole creation is our family”. “All Creation is God”. “Serve God in man”. “Work is Worship”.

# “God is in every particle of the world”. “The world is in God”. “The world is God”. “God is separate from the world, but the world is not separate from God”. It is impossible to discern God and the world and whether it is one with or separate from God or not”.

# I understand the Truth that the body dies, but not the soul (jivaatman), which is born and reborn again and again till it achieves perfection, by becoming one with the whole Creation, God, from whence it appeared”.

# I accept that whatever we do bears consequences, as we sow, so we reap (in this life or another).

# I accept that all human beings as soul are one and interconnected and made out of love, even beneath the hate and the killings”.

# I shall strive to build powers of mind to walk-the-talk of Righteous conduct, commitment to kartavya-palan, our respective duties; duly supported by our respective Prayers (Bhakti Yoga), Works (Karma yoga), Meditations (Raja yoga), Knowledge (Gyan yoga).

3. The problem: While some view religion as a source of conflict, it cannot be denied that religion can also play a significant role in conflict resolution and peace building. Modern systems have allowed petty, narrow-minded, Self-centered leaders to contribute to conflicts and violence using their authority to promote hatred for the ‘others and extremist, fundamentalists, and exclusionary ideologies.

4. The Solution – Ministry for Harmony of Religions and Minority Affairs : It should be made the responsibility of the Academia and the Shrestha of every political party and religion, to strategize how to highlight the importance of promoting religious leaders who hold moderate and inclusive views and ensuring that they have the support and resources necessary to promote peace and nonviolence not just within their communities but with other political parties and religious communities.

Academicians from different religions have the potential to act as promoters of peace and serve as moral authorities and facilitators, fostering unity and harmony. Religious leaders should be made responsible and accountable to prevent extremist ideologies and promote peaceful messages, encouraging forgiveness, tolerance, acceptance, by promoting dialogue, reconciliation, and social service and addressing the underlying causes of violence.

5. “The Islamic perspective on faith as a catalyst for peace – conflict resolution” : A scholarly paper suggests scholars need to revive and disseminate some fundamental but neglected teachings of Islam, which emphasize the importance of eradicating violence and conflicts, e.g.,

# The need to honor and respect all humans, regardless of their religion, race, or skin color.

# The affirmation of religious differences among humans occurred based on the will of Allah.

# “If thy Lord had so willed, He could have made mankind one people; but they will not cease to dispute”.

# The preacher’s responsibility is to remind people, not forcing them to admit and believe in God, neither to question the disbeliefs of the unbelievers.

# Islam claims that the Muslim community should be “just” even to non-Muslims, “O ye who believe! Stand out firmly for God, as witnesses to fair dealing, and let not the hatred of others to you make you swerve to wrong and depart from justice. Be just: that is next to piety: and fear God, for God is well-acquainted with all that ye do”.

# Islam encourages its followers to engage in dialogues with non-Muslims. “Invite (all) to the Way of thy Lord with wisdom and beautiful preaching; and argue [dialogue] with them in ways that are best and most gracious; for thy Lord knoweth best, who have strayed from His Path, and who receive guidance”.

# Acknowledge religious pluralism and honor differences of opinion among human differing in background, culture, religion, race and ethnicity

# Avoid three harmful behaviors warned by the Qur’an and common human ethics viz. Extremism: being too extreme in translating and implementing regulation or decision. Chauvinism (ta’asub): accepting only own thought or view from particular group as the best, while others must be refuted or side-lined. Fanaticism: reacting blindly in following or implementing particular action, stand or own approach without considering other judgment”.
