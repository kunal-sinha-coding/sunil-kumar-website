India to Bharat · Essay #059

Creative power of Thought – it is ‘Shabda’ that creates the world of names and forms

1. It is Shabda that projects the external world: God’s thought creates the whole world. Our thoughts create our world which exists only in our mind. So, it is good to see how this happens!

Both the Vedas and the Bible tell us that, in the beginning was the Word, Shabda , and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. The same was in the beginning with God. All things were made by him; and without him was not anything made that was made. In him was life, and the life was the light of men. And the light shinest in darkness, and the darkness comprehended it not… and the Word was made flesh.

2. Shabda is a sound produced by us from “within” ourselves: It is different from sound as dhwani, produced by objects striking against each other, which may have no universal specific meaning. Whereas sound as “ Shabda ” has a universal, specific meaning, and emerges as spoken Words from within us, out of pure Consciousness, Life, Spirit, Atman, Kingdom of God within. Brahman being the cause it is aptly called Shabda-Brahman .

Words are just words and sound, which issue forth when spoken and instantly fade away; but it is words that reveal the meaning and thru’ words we cognize that which the word represents, the object, the meaning, the thought, feelings, emotions.

Shabda is produced by us in response to the emotional feelings, mental movement or an idea which by our will-power, seeks outward expression and emerges as Shabda. Shabda is spoken for hearing of others by the speaker, who is only a manifester , but the Shabda connected with its meaning is eternal and is other than the speaker!

3. How do the speaker/hearer cognize the meaning of Shabda? At any moment the Jiva is subject to innumerable influences from all quarters of the universe, but only those reach our consciousness which attract our attention. That aspect of mind which cognizes is termed Shabda, Naama , name; and that aspect in which the mind has taken the vritti of the outer object to cognize it, is termed Artha, Rupa , form.

When an impression of anything, any ‘object’ is presented to manas, to perceive it, the manas is formed into the same shape of the outer thing. This is a mental- vritti , mental-modification, exactly corresponding to the outer object. The mind as a vritti represents the outer object/thing. The outer is gross object projected by Cosmic Consciousness, shakti, mind of God, while the inner vritti, mental impression, is the subtle-object, enlivened within us, by the same Consciousness!

Our mind, functioning as manas , inputs those impressions and conveys it to buddhi . Before uttering, the Shabda sound is motionless in a seed form. It then comes into general motion associated with input function of mind, manas. Then it comes to the Buddhi function of mind, which ‘cognizes’ based on the earlier vritti, and then gives it the Shabda, ‘words’ to be spoken before the Shabda can emerge as uttered speech, vaikhari, as sentences, varna (i.e., ka,kha, ga, gha…).

4. Cognition: For perception to take place, besides the inner vritti as object in the mind, there has to be another part of the mind which cognizes this very vritti; thus, the infinite mind divides itself into two aspects, and becomes itself the perceiver of the perceived vritti. Perception is thus consequent on the simultaneous functioning of mind as subject-object, as cogniser and cognized.

The object perceived is “Artha”, which literally signifies ‘to get’, ‘to know’, ‘to enjoy’, from the root ‘ri’. The mind in the form of mental impression, vritti, is also Artha as a reflection of the outer thing/object. Mind is thus both subject and object; knower and the known by itself.

Thus, if we speak the good, holy words, mantra, repeatedly, the meaning behind the mantra gets revealed. Greek word ‘logos’ also means thought and word combined. Just think: this creation is just cognition of Names and Forms in the mind and by the mind itself! Without the mind which distinguishes, identifies, reveals, creation is meaningless!

5. And therefore, importance of thinking good: We are continuously creating vritti, impressions, samskars, in our mind. And, infinite series of these vrittis form our characteristic and leads to who and what we’re, our worldview, our destiny, etc. Thus, purifying, refining, these vritti is what spiritual practices, yoga, Religions inspire us to do, by pouring good ideas, prayers, etc. So, when the mind thinks at length, of virtuous, holy thoughts, it is transformed into the likeness of that thru’ continuous devotion. By allowing our deity, our ideal of worship to occupy our mind for long, by constant vritti, the mind becomes as pure, as all pervasive and all-knowing, as the ideal of God, of pure Consciousness itself.

This is the fundamental principle behind all schooling, religions and all extortions ‘to think, to be, and to do’ good. Shouldn’t schooling help us learn how mind functions and inspire goodness?
