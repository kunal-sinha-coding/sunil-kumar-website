India to Bharat · Essay #106

Do we need educated political animals or Karma Yogi

Bharat was the world leader throughout history because our leaders were men of wisdom who had created social systems, educational processes, entrepreneurs and workforce which were all focused on being and creating Karma Yogi! In contrast, today all system focus on creating, clever, greedy, power and status seeking political animals, in all walks of life.

From childhood and growing-up, our citizens learnt from their parents, elders, leaders the importance of treating our daily work and vocation as worship. Bharat’s way was to ensure that citizens were established from childhood, not in learning this or that, but first of all, citizens must imbibe in their character the Commitment to universal humane values, and to our respective Duties, Swadharma and Kartavya-palan.

Children, professionals and leaders learnt, and were inspired to practice self-development processes to build powers of mind so that they could be karma yogis, committed to do their duties regardless of the results!

Society, families, elders all ensured that we learnt that performing righteous actions in conformance of our respective Swadharma is our responsibility and we shall be judged and evaluated for leadership on our commitment-ability.

Growing up and schooling convinced us and inspired us to strive to build the will power, self-control and Viveka to know our Swadharma and build the strength to do our duties, irrespective of its consequences. Whether we were successful or failure, we were to be judged, whether our actions were in conformity with ethics and morals.

We learnt that our personal emotions and feelings shouldn’t come in the way of our doing what is ‘right’ and for long term and common good.

This was the training to be a Karma Yogi; to do what is right regardless of the consequences.

However unless we strive to awaken our vieka-buddhi, to know what is right from childhood, and strive to build powers of mind and self-control to do what is right, we cannot be a karma yogi, all of a sudden, merely because I may win votes or pass some exams! Striving to be a political leader or administrator has to start from childhood, before we become clever, educated adults who want to grab money or power or promotion!

Of course, it is not Easy: Obviously, doing the right thing is not always easy! On the contrary it is certainly difficult and it can cause miseries and grief to our loved ones and the path of moral or ethical conduct is not simple; it will provide numerous ethical dilemmas. It is like ‘walking on a bed of thorns’; ‘walking on a razor edge’; and, ‘a road less traveled by’!

Nevertheless all of us have to learn, strive, struggle, to grow towards the ideal and obviously a very few of us succeed and really only these very few are really fit to our Mentors, Governors, Administrators, Policy makers – Leaders, the Shrestha who can be role models of society.

What is Hinduism? Hinduism simply says, “Go ahead and do whatever you wish”! Really! Joke? Aren’t we, the majority of us, always striving to do that, in any case!

Only later, when we’re ‘ready’, will we realise that every action has its consequences! And, then we will ourselves struggle to be a Karma Yogi.

So, purusartha, ‘striving’, call for action, for fulfilling our Swadharma, with intellectual conviction in its importance and courage is the real essential message of Krishna’s advice; not to a lazy, corrupt, inefficient citizen; but to Arjuna who has achieved excellence.

Thus as the workforce and result producers we’ve first to attain excellence in action; only then do we have the passport to be a Karma Yogi, deserve a leadership status!

Alas the educated India, our teachers and leaders have even failed to achieve worldly excellence that a common illiterate Indian, a housewife, craftsmen, traders, skilled and unskilled workers strives and achieves! And the same Indian achieves excellence abroad; the Westerners are striving for excellence in work and are thus more ready to be a Karma Yogi!

Do we want Political Animals? Or Karma Yogis and citizens who are committed to what Hinduism aka Sanatana Dharma stands for, Leaders and Academia who can commit themselves to producing Citizens who are committed to universal righteous ethical conduct and our respective duties.

What is a political animal? Perhaps, one who has decided to take sides, “right or wrong”; and, defend it to best of his abilities for various reasons, good or bad. And we all play this role in our daily life for various reasons. Political animals perhaps are those whose profession, like Lawyers has become to take sides, for ‘fees’, and those in politics of democracy who see their role as Opposition, not of Cooperation for common good!

What about modern Media or even Historians, etc.? They’re like a judge. Evidence, fact, is placed. There are certain rules to follow to evaluate the facts. Weigh the evidence and present the facts. We don’t argue a point of view. Our duty is to place what you believe as the truth. Don’t we now openly see how Media and historians are all biased and may not be fair and just and, for this or that reason write or have to write ‘stories’ or ‘His-story’, history, based not just on facts as they are, but based on biases, opinions, prejudices, etc.

Our failure as governance and Academia: Who if anyone, is focused on creating and selecting men of good character as our leaders who are really capable or interested in selflessly working for long-term common good of all? Can people ever be totally fair and just and that too without any character-building from childhood or character-evaluation?
