India to Bharat · Essay #144

Why aren’t we taught the wisdom of our most ancient history?

1. Why aren’t we proud of our culture and united as a Nation? Let’s look at some reasons that our governance and schooling has failed to unite us and imbibe in us, pride and love for our nation, and its values and ideals?

Firstly, democracy thrives on dividing and not uniting us! The parties in power for many years were still idealizing modern 3R’s, science and western ideals and neglected our indigenous ideals that schooling is meant for character building and so must establish us in commitment to our respective societal dharma and individual Swadharma so that as householders we earn and enjoy thru’ ethical and righteous conduct. Now a party has come to power which will hopefully harmonize both the modern and ancient ideals of our culture, e.g. Ram-rajya; Yoga; Cleanliness, purity – external and internal; and, now Karma Yoga, etc.

Secondly, we’ve allowed religious leaders to dwell on differences and have failed to mobilize them to instill in us the common ideals, e.g. Fulfill your duties; work as worship for good of society; care and concern for all equally, etc. And, while minority schools are allowed to teach their idea of my-god vs your god, our ancient ideal is not taught anywhere, i.e. One God, but many religions or paths to it, e.g. Work and Vocation as Worship also leads to the same Highest goals, which religious promise.

Another reason is that neither our government nor schooling are focused on building pride in our composite culture and the ideals of our freedom fighters as specifically ordained in our Fundamental Duties.

2. What should the govt. now do? It must be the duty of the govt. to work with Academia, all Teacher training colleges, institutes, and schools to decide what are our National Values and Ideals and how can schooling and religious/spiritual leaders/NGO’s work together to instill in the young, awareness, conviction, pride in our common values and ideals. The ideal of being a Karma-yogi should be made an integral part of schooling.

3. Teach ‘Our-story’; not ‘His-story’ written by foreigners: Why shouldn’t history be taught based on our own values and ideals? History is taught in all schools so that we may learn some lessons from our past; but we must remember that ‘his-story’ is written by victors. And so, it will always be prejudiced! We have been brought up reading history as written by our conquerors and rulers, who had no love for our ancient culture and wisdom and in order to impose their religion and rule over us, they deliberately suppressed all that was good in our ancient history.

Now that a new government is in power, at least now, we should ask the question: What is the source to learn ‘our-story’, history of our own ancient civilization? Not ‘his-story’ written by copycats of Western historians.

4. Our-story: The wisdom of our forefathers was that they wrote the history of our civilization not merely based on the story of kings and their battles, but they emphasized words of wisdom and moral and ethical lessons, glorifying the character of those whose life and teaching are worth emulating for future generations. And, they wrote it in the format so that our-story will be of relevance through thousands of years. Thus, our epics like Ramayana, Mahabharat, are the history of our civilization and contain lessons for all mankind, so that people may learn and follow good values and ideals and remain on the critical path to the highest human goal of Perfection.

Similarly, the Upanishads are the history of how our forefathers thought on the fundamental issues of life. They reveal the wisdom of our oneness and inter-dependence of our relationship with God and Creation.

These then are the history of our ancient culture. They express, and are the Truth of truths, not based on belief or hearing from someone, but on actual seeing and experience of many historians.

5. A Lesson from Our-story: A holy man’s dog was in constant distress because of its fear of bigger jungle creatures; and so, the holy man took pity and turned it into bigger and bigger animals, till he promoted it into a tiger. Whereupon it was ready to pounce on the holy man himself!

Lesson: Do not promote people to levels beyond their character and competence. Today we say, people are equal; but we don’t have a system of educating, recruiting or promoting people with good character as our administrators and political leaders. In the garb of democracy, those leaders get elected who are focused on grabbing money and power by corrupt means! Our political leaders don’t ‘cherish and follow the noble ideas which inspired our national struggle for freedom’. And they’ve failed to teach themselves and our citizens ‘to value and preserve the rich heritage of our composite culture’ as the Fundamental Duties of our Constitution enjoins on us.

Our ancient ideal has been, only by doing well the duties of our present circumstances, birth, status shall higher duties come to us; provided they’re necessary for our growth towards our highest goal and purpose of Work and Life. So, let’s first fulfill our present duties! If we treat people as lower or higher based on where they stand on the pyramid, there is bound to be a mad cut-throat race to rise higher; and people tend to rise beyond their character and competence.

Conclusion: The lesson from Our-story contained in our Epics is to be a Karma Yogi, i.e. in and thru’ daily work and vocation let’s remain on the optimum path to realize the Ultimate purpose and Goal of Life, i.e. to once again “Regain” and “Reunite” (Religion and Yoga respectively) my Oneness, with the whole-creation.
