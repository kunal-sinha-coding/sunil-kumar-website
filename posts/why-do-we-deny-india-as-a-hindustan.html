India to Bharat · Essay #212

Why do we deny India as a Hindustan?

1. This is how we the educated Indians think and argue: Maybe Hindus were very great in the past. Maybe our ancient Vedic ideas, social systems, teachers, kings, rulers were much better than the corrupt politicians and inefficient administration of today, but so what. Are we not progressing? Today the Westerners are superior in every way. Look at their well-maintained clean cities, discipline, punctuality, civic sense; they have conquered the globe with their science, technology and sheer hard work. They wanted to be achievers and have achieved. We’ve no option but to follow their ways. What have our puja and religiosity given us excepting to make us fatalistic, weak, servile. This is how we think and we’re not wrong either.

2 . Do we, therefore, give up our identity? Let us first reflect on: Why are we, what we have become? Is it because of our religion and beliefs? Who is responsible for other countries marching ahead in leaps and bounds whereas we are neither as good as the West nor as spiritual and prosperous as Bharat was? Could it be because we are continuing with a century old archaic education system which is now totally outdated, focused only on memorizing, IQ, 3R’s, marks and degrees, creating babus and not on creating enlightened citizens who have love for Bharat and its Bharatiya heritage which had once made us great. Our education is not focused on enabling us to stand on our own feet; It is creating unemployable Graduates with no useful skills or creativity, no entrepreneurship and no problem-solving initiatives. It is also not a man-making, nation-building and character-building education to create responsible and accountable citizens! We need lions, not foxes!

3. Modern Schooling: Students are not taught the basic features and uniqueness of Bharat that the purpose of life is to realize and manifest our unlimited potential for being good and perfect. And, excellence in our vocation is our only opportunity to either go towards (Sur) or away (Asur) from being perfect. It is therefore extremely important to ensure that education helps us choose a vocation where we can excel and work for good of society and helps us awaken Viveka and get established in self-development processes to be committed to our duties so as to walk the path to Perfection. It is our misfortune that those who governed us and have being drafting our education policy arrogated the right to themselves to deprive Indian citizens of our spiritual heritage.

4. We the educated elite are responsible for what India now is, good or bad: We are the elite minuscule minority. We get educated in the best of schools and colleges, not the government schools meant for the masses; and the moment opportunity arises we prefer to go abroad, on scholarships or own money, depending on our marks and means! Since independence, we have ruled the nation and to put it crudely, we the elite, have been utterly selfish and self-centered and arrogated to ourselves all kinds of privileges and perks, besides shamelessly looting the wealth of the nation.

5. The educated have given us a corrupt, inefficient leadership , and therefore, we’ve had no moral authority or initiative to insist that public servants and citizens be men of sound character, integrity, committed to duties, etc. We’ve miserably failed to produce the educated people with the spirit of kartavya-palan and tyaga and seva, based on the time-tested traditions and wisdom of Bharat, which were the ideals of our freedom fighters, mostly born in the first decade or two of the 20 th Century, who had love for the nation and readily sacrificed themselves for the good of Bharat!

6. We’re the parasites, enjoying the fruits of their sacrifices: The fault is not wholly ours because whether we are brilliant or dull in studies, we have to be cooped up in classes for hours and for many years of schooling, memorizing and learning irrelevant information! We’re denied that wisdom, discipline, work ethics, entrepreneurship, skills, creativity which had made us great and glorious. With classroom cocooned schooling we have become unemployable graduates looking for jobs, unable to stand on our own feet!

7. Our secular historians, education and governance are practicing ‘Negationism’, denying us the wisdom of Bharat and Vedanta, as expressed in the Bhagvad Gita, while denying the historical religious persecution, atrocities, massacres, and criticisms heaped upon Hinduism that we’ve been subjected to by our foreign conquerors for centuries.

It was because both materially and spiritually we were very rich and a cultured civilization that over centuries, we’ve suffered invasion, deliberate destruction, loot and plunder by first the greedy Muslim invaders and then by a greedy money-loving-trading company, both without scruples or principles. Together they destroyed our ideal of ‘simple living and high thinking’, as rolemodeled by Gandhiji, but given up by his inheritors. The occupiers destroyed our native gurukuls, vaidyas, caste system, which kept people educated and engaged in vocations appropriate to their inherent character and competence, so that they could remain on the narrow road of ethics and morals, dharma, to attain peace, happiness, prosperity, the highest spiritual goal of mankind. Today money-making and enjoyment have become our only goals and the greedy, our leaders!

We the educated willy-nilly have to accept all their criticisms because education and governance have deliberately kept us in ignorance of our Vedantic wisdom, in spite of Gandhi, Nehru, Indira, Rajiv asking us to study Vivekananda to know and have love for our spiritual heritage. We allow minorities to teach in their schools the “separatist” ideology, that “my religion alone is the only right way”, leading to what is being called ‘clash of civilizations’, but somehow we’re afraid to teach our own Vedantic worldview of “oneness, the very foundation of India that is Bharat.

We need our spiritual wisdom once again. Let it come to us through all religions, all scriptures, and all the Gods, old and new!
