India to Bharat · Essay #123

What should be ‘Quality of Mind’ of Mentors, Policy-makers, Administrators so that they may guide us towards the higher goals of man?

We’re what we’re based on our worldviews: We know people are not equal, though modern worldview is that they’re! How do we differentiate between them today? Based on IQ, marks, degrees; on status, position, vocation, rich or poor; belonging to my-or-not-my religion, family, caste, group, etc.

All these differentiations are based on “external” factors; whereas the reality is that we are what we are, based on our worldview that we’re continuously creating by the quality of thoughts that we entertain in our mind. Our thoughts, actions, choices, motivations, intentions, desires, remain in our mind as ‘memories’, ‘samskaras’; conscious or deep within; and these thoughts-actions are continuously forming our worldview, refining or polluting it, based on the quality of our thoughts-actions. The external behavior and actions are just the effect, their cause is our stored up ‘memories’, samskaras.

What is a healthy Worldview and idea of good? There are two broad worldviews: i) We’re all ‘Separate’, and so, idea of narrowness, limitedness, selfishness; me and mine! ii) We’re all ‘One’, and so, idea of good and better is to strive to be more and more generous, all-inclusive, unselfish! All virtuous, ethical, spiritual actions arise from this worldview of ‘oneness’.

We’re all one, interconnected, interdependent, interrelated, unlimited, is thus the healthy worldview of Creation. So, the very purpose of life, vocation, education must be to inspire us to continuously grow towards feeling of love, care, concern for more and more, thru’ selflessness, righteous conduct, worship, etc. till we actually experience the reality that we’re one with the whole Creation; and this is the highest and best goal of man.

This worldview of ‘oneness’ is our common “North” and the yardstick to decide and differentiate people, including the idea of religions, into good, better, best. Selfishness of the mind has to be transformed into unselfishness thru’ gradual, continuous practice of virtuous conduct. And, of course it is not at all possible to change our worldview easily; but the ‘direction’ towards which we are to develop must be clear in our worldview.

Differentiating people based on Quality of mind, worldview: The Creation can be considered as a mixture of three qualities or ‘guna’, viz. Sattva, Rajas, Tamas; Calm-Active-Dull. So, depending on the ‘thoughts’ that people intake as ‘food’ into our mind (and also what we ‘eat’), people can be differentiated as being predominantly sattvic, rajasic, or tamasic.

The predominantly Sattvic people have the mind-quality that manifests itself as selflessness, purity, knowledge, harmony, goodness, joy, nobility, contentment. Rajasic people are likely to be predominantly full of passion, action, energy, ambition, which leads to attachment, longing for fulfilling goals, desires. Tamasic will generally be lazy, dull, prone to impurity, darkness, violence, cruelty, content with routine, work.

Thus, those with Sattva will be the best as Mentors, policy-makers, statesmen, advisors and role models of society. It is their obligatory duty to mentor, teach and guide those with predominance of Rajas-Sattva to Administer societies fairly and justly, and to create systems and the environment where the Entrepreneurs and the Workforce who are the only result-producers and wealth-creators, can be as materialistic as they wish.

Of course, it is the obligatory duty of the Mentors and Administrators to ensure that the schooling and vocation of every person is focused on creating systems that promote ethical, righteous conduct and establishing the young in the daily habit of self-development practices, prayers, meditation, reflection, worship, etc. which help transforming the mindset gradually from Tamas to Rajas to Sattva.

Society has to be continuously mentored, monitored, inspired, to be good, better, best towards the feelings of love, for all till we experience oneness with the whole Creation. Thus, Sattvic and Sattvic-rajasic people are responsible to inspire and mentor people to grow towards being more and more sattvic.

Unfortunately, modern religions have divided people on their ‘belief’ that ‘my religion’, ‘my god’ alone is true, instead of inspiring all people to become virtuous imbibing the natural, universal values of truth, ahimsa, righteous conduct, peace, love for all equally, working for good of all, etc. And so, religions have been thrown out of mainstream systems. Religions ‘believe’ and propagate that we’ve only one life; that ‘belief’ in their God and ‘belonging’ to their religion alone is the way to the ‘Best’. They ‘believe’ that no matter how virtuous or good you are unless you ‘belong’ to their religion you cannot attain the ‘best’. Similarly, political parties and leaders in power are more interested in their self-interest.

Sattva, Rajas, Tamas are qualities of mind and we’re identifying ourselves with our mind because we’re in ignorance of our divine Self. The game and purpose of life is to continuously strive to refine and purify the quality of our mindset, thru’ ‘virtuous thinking and conduct’, till it is refined and purified thru’ ethical conduct, prayers, selflessness, etc. so as to be free from all samskaras, vrittis, modifications-of-mind. And, a pure mind, totally merges into Oneness with Creation; because the pure mind is now free even from Sattva quality of ‘thoughts-desires’ and therefore it is no longer propelled into selfish actions!

The problem now is, who will judge who? So, we decide all ‘are equal’, The result? We’re not led by those with predominance of Sattva, but are led by those with predominance of Tamas-Rajas. They’re now are our role models and everyone strives to rise to levels beyond their character and competence and money and power obtained by any means are generally our aspiration!
