India to Bharat · Essay #021

Who has the authority to direct ‘religions, preachers & teachers’ to work-together to inculcate spirit of ‘mission karma yogi’, ‘work as worship of god in man’!

1. The vision on education of Vivekananda and Sir Syed Ahmed Khan was a link between traditional and modern education. Both were great thinkers and reformers as well as pioneering figure in the cultural and spiritual history of the Indian people. They embrace education, which for them signifies ‘man-making’ and ‘scientific temperament’, as their very mission of the life in different aspects.

The former was declared as National Youth icon in 1985 by GOI; and the latter founded The Aligarh Muslim University, 1877, and Mahatma Gandhi called him ‘the prophet of education’. Their educational ideas got practical shape in the form of Ramakrishna Mission and Aligarh Muslim University (AMU). Both reformers saw education as a constructive, practical process of character building and believed that the sense of dignity rises in man when he becomes conscious of his inner spirit; and that is the very purpose of education.

They propagated the essence of truth of religions: Love thy neighbour as thyself. Kingdom of God within. Brotherhood of men. Unity of humanity. Harmony of religions. Righteousness & Work as Worship of God in man as the Religious-path. Supremacy of spiritualism over materialism of the west. They believed that Moral and religious values are being undermined; Fundamental principles of civilization are being ignored but conflicts over secondary ideals, manners and habits are pervading. Disregard for everything old is the fashion of the day.

Both reformers were seeking the solutions of all these social and global evils in the transformation of man through moral and spiritual education, founded on the firm ground of our own philosophy and culture; highlighting the dire need of awakening man to his spiritual self; harmonizing the traditional values of India with the new values brought through the progress of science and technology.

2. We’ve failed as a society “to be and make mentors” who can inspire us to imbibe & inculcate spirit of work as worship, ‘fundamental duties’, worldview of oneness, etc.

# Shouldn’t we as a time-tested spiritual society and as governance, academia, educational institutes, etc. wonder why has modern society removed, ignored, rejected the authority of religions, preachers and philosophers from mainstream academia?

# Isn’t it because we along with preachers and heads of religions have failed to imbibe the spirit of Kartavya-palan, equality, and selflessness in our citizens?

# Haven’t we failed to prove our bona fide because of our utter failure to either ‘come together as mentors of society’ or ‘unite and inspire people towards a long-term vision’?

# Haven’t our politicians and leaders failed to understand or implement our age-old wisdom of Dharma-Artha-Kama-Moksha; that Growing up must be a process to establish the young in commitment to Dharma – righteousness; kartavya-palan; work and vocation itself as our highest worship; etc. to produce enlightened citizens.

3. Now that GOI has awakened to the need to train all govt. servants as ‘karma yogi’, how can we all help ‘to be & make’ karma yogis?

# “Mine and yours are spoken of by petty, narrow-minded people; for the large-hearted noble souls, the whole world is our family” – “Worldview of Oneness & Subjective Focus”.

# Today, as a Google interconnected global society we need “to be” and “to make mentors” who can guide us towards becoming good, better, best; give clarity on the long-term and the higher goals towards which we may direct our efforts; and build viveka-buddhi and powers of mind, self-control, inspiration and will to walk-the-talk!

As a “Global Spiritual Family of Devotees” irrespective of those who spread hatred as “My vs Your God, Religions”, it is our obligatory duty to plan how we can work in our respective areas to be and make Karma Yogi and create an Online Platform for their training!

T he govt. brochure on the Mission Karma Yogi, KYi, explains its meaning thru’ “ Atmano moksartham jagad hitaye cha ”, i.e., KYi means to “Realize your Divine Self by working for good of all”. And “ Yogaha karmashu kaushalam ”, i.e., Work when done selflessly as a duty, Kartavya, without desire for its fruits, leads to union with our Divine Self). And during G20 countries Meet, the motto was “One World. One Family. One Future.”

The idea is that as life, self, consciousness, ‘atman’, spirit we’re all One. Since we’re all engaged in “work”, if we are taught and learn from childhood to treat our daily work as our highest worship, seeing God in man and in Creation, then it becomes ’Karma Yoga, i.e., Work as Worship; and a religious path, to realize our Oneness with all. Of course, our respective Prayers, Meditation, Knowledge all become aids to build powers of mind, self-control, etc. to practice the idea of work as worship.

These are the universal time-tested ideals of all religions! We believe, it becomes our duty a s Teachers, spiritual Devotees and Volunteers of all religions to discuss together how we can help communicate these ideals in our respective educational Institutes, Communities, Colonies, Areas of influence, etc.

In spite of our idea of Karma Yoga, of work as worship, why are our institutes, organizations, not world class? If we study how some, like the long-lasting Corporations, Industrial houses, or say, Delhi Metro (DMRC) remain world class; we find that the simple reason is that the success depends upon excellent Codes of Ethics – Institutional, Environmental, Societal, Individual Ethics, and the role of Leaders as Mentors, not mere ‘boss’.
