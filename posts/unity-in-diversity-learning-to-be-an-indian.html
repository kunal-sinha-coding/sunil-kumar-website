India to Bharat · Essay #018

Unity in diversity – Learning to be an Indian

“I have inherited a great philosophy, and I am part of that universal consciousness which is called Hinduism. I am an eternal joker and making human beings laugh is my religion” – Raj Kapoor.

1) We the wise men of religions and academia, have allowed politicians to divide us! We have failed to scatter broadcast thru minority and mainstream schooling and Preaching the essential religious ideal of selfless commitment to duties – that spirituality is our ability to adjust, adapt, accommodate to externals, while internally keeping our thoughts flowing God-wards; just as that ‘universal consciousness’ aka God, accepts us all, with all our good and evil differences, while nudging us to be and do good in and thru’ our daily living!

2) In Discovery of India, Nehru says: ‘India must break with much of her past and not allow it to dominate the present … lessen religiosity and turn to science’. In contrast, Dr Radhakrishnan says: ‘Whatever may be your social program, whatever revolutions you may bring about in economic and politics, unless you have the dynamic inspiration of religion, you will never succeed. Ask, says he, ‘whether you’re going to reduce human beings to mere political or social creatures or would you give them some inner sanctity which nothing outward can touch?’

3) At least now, it is our duty to ensure that Education and Preaching must help and inspire us to learn and explore the USP of Indian civilizational culture as distinct from the Western modern religious cultures which focus on ‘Artha-Kama’ and claim that their scripture alone is true and their God, as the only true God.

As the educated we must re-discover the USP of Indian culture termed variously as Bharati, Indian-ness, Hinduism, Hindu civilization, Vedic culture, Hindu-sthan, Sanatana-dharma, etc. focused on ‘Dharma-Artha-Kama-Moksha’, following our respective religious and God ideals.

We need the best of our ideal focused on ‘harmony’ and the best of Western ‘sciences and work efficiency’. India still survives as an entity, even though thru the ages, it has remained divided and ruled by around 20,000 tiny, small, big kingdoms with various Religions, sects, castes, social norms, food habits, etc. etc. because of the foundation of our Vedantic culture that shows logically our Oneness as Consciousness, that makes us Living-beings, Jiva-atman.

Indian culture wants us to first get established in Righteousness, Dharma, kartavya-palan thru’ daily ‘reflections and self-development practices’, Praxis following our respective Religion. Only then, seek to Earn & Enjoy so that all work and life is directed towards realization of our universal, common goal of Freedom, Perfection, Heavenly Bliss…

4) It is incumbent on us as elders and as Indians to explore and re-discover for ourselves what is the USP of India, the ideal of “Indian-ness”, so that we can ensure that politicking, teaching and preaching are together focused on helping us to realize the vision of both external prosperity and inner peace, in and thru’ our daily work.

We must be inspired to imbibe the best of our civilizational culture of selfless kartavya-palan to purify our mind and realize the truth of our ideal of ‘the whole world as our family’. If the best, instead of becoming spiritual, become self-centered political animals, then society will continue becoming narrow, petty-minded and selfish, as we now see…

5) In this universe (uni – verse; one Song) of Unity in Diversity there must be different Religious ways to different people; just as in a family the mother treats and feeds each member differently! Different people need different ways of worship:

a) for the Intellectuals we’ve Scriptural and philosophical seeking for the truth of Truths.

b) for the Devotional we’ve prayers, worship, singing … and love for their respective ideal.

c) for the Calm we’ve Meditation etc. according to our respective religious ideal….

d) for ALL Work itself becomes Religion, irrespective of who and what we’re or do, and worship of God in man and in all creation is the universal religion of Work.

5) Since everyone works, Quality and Excellence in whatever WORK we do, and selfless fulfilment of Duties is the IDEAL towards which all Parenting, Preaching and Teaching must be focused!

6) Obvious common sense is that Religion isn’t and can’t be narrow. It is catholic, sympathetic, broad-minded in outlook. Patriotism is not merely love of the land in which we are born; it is respect for the universal values and ideals by which we are sustained. Man has a spiritual dimension. Our development material and spiritual, can take place in various ways. So, the cardinal feature of Indian tradition is that we should have respect for all religions and various ways of worship. It is Indian and not merely Hindu. Indian-ness is based on the firm idea that as spirit the whole world is our family.

7) Modern religions and all schooling need to teach the spirit of constitutional Fundamental Duties of harmony of religions and brotherhood, as also directed by the highest court to avoid growth of fanaticism!

Academia must learn and teach the basic reality that every single moment we’re at ‘work’ and that work isn’t just a vocation for earning but work holistically includes every ‘action’ and its simultaneous consequences – action and reaction are equal and opposite! And every action or work holistically also includes each and every “intentions, motives, words, choices and actions”, every moment! And the simultaneous ‘consequences’ of each ‘work’, gets recorded in our mind as ‘subtle impressions’ (samskaras) and then becomes the cause of subsequent choices-actions as well as our characteristics, habit, attitudes and makes us whatever we’re!
