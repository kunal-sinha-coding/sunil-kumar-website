India to Bharat · Essay #142

We’re the “Knower” – Even our body-mind is a thing to be ‘known’ like anything else!

Mankind’s ultimate purpose? Though, I may be only interested in living well, fulfilling my dreams, it is good to be aware of the highest ideals. So, let’s reflect on the profound experience of our forefathers:

Learn to Differentiate the “Knower from Known”; “Observer from Observed”; “Subject from Object”; “Seer from the Seen”. The entire Universe can be divided into two: Living-beings/Jiva-atma and Non-living/Matter. When we speak of the Universe, we only mean that portion of existence that is observable , albeit limited by our mind, which we can see, feel, etc. And, this alone is being studied today by us.

Aping the West, we’re busy exploring the wonders of science and technology and the comforts and entertainment it can offer. Of course, this creation is for us to know, enjoy, suffer; but is that all its purpose? We haven’t yet realized the purpose and plan of creation, that our forefathers had already discovered and placed before us. And, without any effort to discover the purpose of creation and of our own life, we’re busy trying to know and enjoy the known.

Questions for Reflection: What is the cause of our sufferings? How do we maximize happiness? What is the purpose, Goal of Life; Role of work; How to attain the Goal, etc.? shouldn’t the young, the academia, elders, wrestle with these basic human issues instead of mere marks, degrees, money!

Who is the ‘Knower’ different from the ‘known? Is there something beyond this portion of existence that we can observe? Beyond the world of our minds? Our Rishi-Scientists reflected and questioned: the observable objects being infinite, can we ever know it all? And realized that the infinite can never be known! So, they meditated and thought: Why not first of all explore and discover the “Knower”, i.e. ‘myself’; which is not only always with me, but is nearest to me!

A very surprising phenomenon: Scientists are busy exploring nature which is matter , but they never study themselves; even though we’re also nothing but nature, matter. The moment we start studying ourselves, we will realize that though the body is matter, I am not matter; I am a living-being, i.e. “I am the Knower”, but my body is just matter, the ‘known; ’ why? Because I can observe the body. Also think: we use ‘I’ for my-Self; but I say ‘my-body’; not I-body. Why? Because, intuitively we are separating our Self, the knower, from our own body, the known! ‘I am That I am’.

‘Knower’ is always separate from things ‘known’: We must reflect and realize this profound truth of truth. So, if I am the knower of my own ‘body’, it logically follows that I am separate from the body; I am the ‘subject’, ‘knower’ not an ‘object’ to be ‘known’. This is a profound discovery of our ancient Rishis! What about our mind, known or knower?

Reflect; am I not aware of thoughts and feelings arising in our mind? So, wonder of wonders, my mind thoughts-feelings are also ‘known’ and ‘I am’ separate; I and I alone am the ‘knower’ as life, consciousness, in living-beings. I am the Atman, Spirit. This body-mind complex of mine is matter just like other parts of creation! I am separate from my body-mind just as I am separate from other things in creation. Reflect on another profound logical discovery – there cannot be two ‘Knowers’, infinite-regression! There can be but One, and one alone knower! aka One God!

Our Vedic Rishis had also first explored the “known” objects but quickly that there was no way we could ever know the ‘infinite’! That in spite of all efforts, miseries remain; and do what we may, ultimately, we all die but the world continues! So, they focused on asking, exploring, meditating, seeking to know the answer to a basic Question: What exactly are we, why’re we here, what is the purpose of life, if it ends in death. They lived a simple life without any modern distractions, so they had time to discuss, think deeply; whereas we’re too busy in enjoying the ‘known’!

So, Bharat went inwards and lo and behold, discovered the One Subject, Knower, Observer; the Consciousness, Life, Spirit, Atman within us all. And realized that, That Knower is one, and One alone, and that knowing the Knower, everything else becomes known; if at all we still wish to know then, the issues of this known-world, which seem unimportant after knowing the One!

USP of Bharat: Why do we all struggle/work? Going inwards, Bharat discovered the answer to the basic purpose of creation and evolved the ideals of Dharma, Swadharma, Self-development processes, Karma-yoga, Social and Institutional systems, i.e. Hindu-way-of-life or Sanatana-dharma , i.e. righteous or ethical way of living. And, therefore, the Vision and Mission of Bharat became, get educated in Dharma. Then strive to earn and enjoy thru’ your respective Swadharma. This will purify our mind and keep us on the critical path to realize our oneness with the ‘Knower’ within us; and, with the whole Creation, aka Freedom, Nirvana, god-realization, heaven, etc.

It is up to us, elders and leaders of society, to persuade govt. and schooling to educate and inspire the young to learn and practice being a Karma-Yogi, in and thru their daily work, living, vocation.
