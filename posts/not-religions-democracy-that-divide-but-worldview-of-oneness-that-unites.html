India to Bharat · Essay #206

Not religions & democracy that divide but worldview of oneness that unites

Selfish, narrow-minded Preachers & Politicians divide people

We’re in the midst of Electioneering and we see and hear of intimidation of voters, bribery, illegal use of huge amounts of ‘black’ money, corruption, violence, conflicts. A s good men we’re appalled at the antics and the abusive public discourse by the political animals who’re supposed to be the srestha , the elite, the role models for society to emulate.

No wonder that these political animals have totally failed to lead society towards greatness; instead, they have led people to galloping selfishness and widespread corruption. It should now be very clear that firstly, democracy has a divisive and polarizing influence and is not designed to bring people together. And, secondly, without ‘good’ people at the helm, society is bound to go on deteriorating. We have to question, what guarantees have we that democracy or any of its reformed measures will last unless it is based on first developing the goodness in man.

Understanding the uniting spirit of Bharat

There are three basic entities in our experience. Firstly, the ‘Jagat’, i.e. that which changes, e.g. nature, world, universe, cosmos, all that is inert matter. Secondly, the Jivatma, i.e. all living beings that have life, consciousness, including plants and animals. Finally, the unknown, the invisible cause, or God, the Omnipotent, Omniscient, Omnipresent, power.

Though in our day-to-day interactions and transactions, we know and feel that these are three separate entities, Vedanta, invites us on an adventurous inward-journey to understand the reality that though they look different, both Jagat and Jivatma are not at all different from the Unknow God or Brahman (i.e. ‘greater than’ or unlimited or infinite), but are one with it!

What is “my roopa” , i.e. Swaroopa?

Swaroopa refers to that unchanging, basic substance or form without which that object cannot exist, and which remains with it at all times. For example, ‘gold’ is the swaroopa of all gold ornaments, irrespective of their individual names or forms, which may keep changing. Similarly, swaroopa of jivas, living beings, is Brahman! We are one with it, Uni+verse !

I am an integral part of Jagat!

An important point to be noted here is that when we discuss ‘Jagat’ we deal with everything, but somehow exclude ourselves from it! Science and we haven’t bothered to know ourselves while we have been very busy in trying to know the Jagat and Brahman. When we strive to explore our own nature, we shall come to know ‘who I am’!

What do I gain from this inward journey?

Fulfillment, satisfaction, contentment, a realization that everything is obtained and there is nothing more to be known or gained; and, we remain perpetually happy, blissful, fulfilled, and peaceful. This is our inherent, deep inner desire for being fully free, independent and blissful, ‘mukti’ , which is the ultimate human goal. This feeling of freedom from all grief, of poorna-ananda, which is not dependent on any external factors, which is really our own inherent nature, our swaroopa , is what we are all really seeking in life, in and through all our activities. Study of ourselves will show us that our basic inherent nature being ananda-swaroopa, divine, we can never be really happy, fulfilled or peaceful till we ourselves experience this divine ananda and, oneness with all. And, this realization results in empathy, compassion, spontaneous feelings of love, care and concern for all, etc. The ultimate goal may not be my immediate goal and I may not be interested in it at all at this stage. Nevertheless, this is the ultimate desire and goal of all life.

I and I alone can undertake this inner-journey

It is not a matter of faith, believing in something or someone, following or belonging to this or that sect or religion. I alone have to be convinced, undertake the inner journey, realize and experience. And, till I do so, I will never be really fulfilled. Only if I study and I myself realize this oneness; can I ever be really fulfilled.

Not knowing my own swaroopa is my fundamental ignorance and problem. The more I start understanding my swaroopa the less can situations, events and others disturb me, and the more I remain happy and helpful in all conditions in all my interactions with aspects of the world and other people.

Who are competent to undertake this Inner journey?

What qualifications, competencies, degrees do I require? Do I need to be a Pundit, a scholar, study the 3R’s or Sanskrit and the Vedas, etc.? Do I have to become a monk? Renounce pleasures? Do I have to be a Hindu? Do I have to give up my religion?

No; No; No

Irrespective of who and what we are, there are just four minimum qualities to be acquired to realize the fruit of the study. These four competencies or attributes, or characteristics are: 1) Power of discrimination, discernment, long-term vision, clear-sightedness ii) Knowing and striving to focus on the essentials, on the higher, the better and the best, while withdrawing from the lower iii) Virtues and Disciplines: e.g. growing ability to manage our mind and sense-organs, and directing them towards the highest ideal of perfection, forbearance, sraddha, and balance/evenness of mind. These are to be cultivated thru practice and thru’ prayers, worship, study of scriptures, etc. according to our respective religions and faiths and, iv) Burning and Intense desire to become good and better towards the best, seeking everlasting peace and bliss.

All actions are motivated by desire, and desire alone. Therefore, if we sincerely desire to become good and better towards the best, all other competencies are easily acquired; so, cultivate good habits! Anyone who sincerely strives to acquire these attributes will enjoy its benefits.
