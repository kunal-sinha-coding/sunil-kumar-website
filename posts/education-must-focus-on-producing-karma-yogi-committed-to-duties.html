India to Bharat · Essay #105

Education must focus on producing Karma Yogi, committed to duties

The idea, ideal and practice of Karma Yoga , to become a Karma Yogi is necessary in mainstream systems of education and governance as it helps harmonise ‘work-excellence’ with ‘inner-bliss’ and leads to the higher goals of life.

Threefold aspects of k arma : Modern society, education and vocation deal only with the external aspects of our daily karma , work, but blissfully ignore the inner aspects of work, which is also termed karma , i.e., the inner effect of our karma (which includes our every ‘thought-word-action’) as impression on our mind, samskara , which enslave and gradually, thruogh repetition, become our habit and character and ultimately become our fate, also termed, karma !

So, present ‘ karma -as-action’, is influenced by the previous ‘ karma -as-habit’ and becomes our fate, karma -as-destiny/character/disposition/worldview and “we-reap-as-we-sowed-the-seeds” of “thoughts-words-deeds”. So we need ‘extra’ effort to change self-acquired habits and fate; and, striving to be a Karma Yogi , is a guarantee for success.

While the world wishes that our work must be good and we must all strive for perfection in whatever we do, modern world and schooling doesn’t tell us how to develop the ability and inspiration to excel in work and be committed to kartavya-palan !

Karma Yoga asks us to strive to treat our daily work and duties as our highest worship, and as prayers and meditations with eyes open; and to achieve it, it implores us to get established in the habit of daily reflection and self-development practices so as to:

i) Build strength and powers of mind, self-control, will-power, concentration, etc. to excel in work;

ii) Awaken powers of discrimination, Viveka-buddhi , to choose the ‘long-term good’ over the ‘immediate or short-term pleasures and goals’; and,

iii) Harmonise external work-excellence with Inner-peace and bliss to remain on the path to Highest Perfection, our common “North”.

Obligatory duty of Religions & Academia: The idea, ideal and practice of Karma Yoga in mainstream schooling, and as an essential teaching of all religions, will inspire the modern world also to believe and teach that to get and maximise prosperity, happiness together with inner-peace; we must continuously strive for:

# Excellence and improvement in work and relationships

# Commitment to universal values of righteous conduct, love, peace, truth, non-violence

# Living, earning, enjoying through ethical means

# Excellence and improvement in work and relationships

# More and more caring and loving

# Knowing and fulfilling all our respective duties, Swadharma

# Becoming more and more selfless

Excellence in work for common good is of course a universal ideal! The motto of a college in USA is, “Non Ministrari sed Ministrare”, i.e. not to be served but to serve; not to be ministered unto, but to minister!

Wisdom of Church is that God’s love is expressed through good works, explained with a simple rule: “Do all the good you can, by all the means you can, in all the ways you can, in all the places you can, at all the times you can, to all the people you can, as long as ever you can”. There will always be worthy debates about good and whose definition of good one follows!

To discuss the good, we need to reflect on the ‘best’, our common North! Facing death fearlessly, Socrates is asked, ‘how shall we bury you and with a smile he replies, ‘you must catch me first, the real me; as to the body, do with it as you do to others!’

Karma Yoga takes us on the critical path to know ‘the real me’ our North, that the Greek philosophers refer as ‘know thyself’; Jesus refers as ‘Kingdom of God within’; and we say each soul is ‘potentially divine’, is a Jivatman .

Religions must teach and spread the message that fulfillment of our duties and excellence in work is the path to our highest goal and the passport to enter the world of religions. Belief or belonging to this or that religion; or pray or worship to this or that God or in this or that way is secondary.

Modern society has yet to explore, intellectually recognise and discover this universal goal of man that is attainable through Karma Yoga . Religions have to learn to stop dividing societies and learn to unite and work with academia to create Karma Yogi who harmonise external and inner efficiency; and, this should becomes a part of modern civilisation and the priestly culture!
