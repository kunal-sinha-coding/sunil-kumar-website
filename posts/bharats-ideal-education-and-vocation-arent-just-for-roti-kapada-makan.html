India to Bharat · Essay #042

Bharat’s ideal – Education and Vocation aren’t just for roti-kapada-makan!

1. The history and culture of mankind’s most ancient civilization are based on the Vedantic concept and the ideal that man, nature and God are not different but are ‘one’, interconnected, interdependent, interrelated, integrated whole .

Modern ‘scientific’ society still holds on to the false worldview of ‘separateness’; it is yet to recognize the healthy worldview of ‘ oneness of creation’ as the ancients ‘saw’ for a reality. And therefore, we see ever-increasing selfishness, profiteering, environmental pollution, and constant erosion of natural resources and of essential social, moral and spiritual values.

2. We’ve to appreciate the essence of the plan of creation and our role in it; and so, the sages and rishis, who are the forefathers of all mankind, have designed all our social, educational and vocational systems, so as to educate, inspire and convince us, that the very purpose of our vocation and life is to ‘ Regain our oneness with more are more’, till we actually ‘Regain’, ‘Reunite’ our oneness with the whole, by learning to ‘work’ as karma-yogis for the satisfaction of ‘needs’, not ‘wants’ of society.

This exactly is really the essence of all Religions, which in Latin literally means to ‘Regain’; and similarly, Yoga literally means ‘Union or Reunite’ with the whole as that alone can give us the fulfilment we really seek.

Of course, identified with our body-brain-mind complex we’re different! It is this Truth of truths that asserts that as consciousness, we are all one integrated whole . This grand profound spiritual wisdom is really discovered by the very first Rishis, Seers who inhabit the creation, during their deep meditations and it is they who have recorded their discoveries in the Vedantic foundation of mankind’s wisdom.

3. Religions have so far been dividing societies but now Education and religions have to come together to restore and sustain the universal and eternal values oriented towards unity and integration of people-with-people and people-with-creation. Schooling must give us the conviction that the purpose of vocation and daily life is not just for living and enjoyment, but it has to help us grow in feelings of oneness with more and more till we experience our oneness with the whole creation.

4. It is unselfishness and love, care, concern for each other, that gives rise to the variety of religious cultures and religions. The essence of all religions and their teachings of virtues, ethical and righteous conduct, of charity, of compassion, etc. is based on the fundamental basis that man and man and man and creation are one ; and, therefore if I harm anyone, I alone am being harmed as there is really no ‘other’! Therefore, ‘as we sow, so we reap’.

Thus, it is selfishness and unselfishness that define and differentiate between good and bad . It doesn’t matter how intelligent we’re or how many degrees or how religious we’re or what is our status in society!

5. Religions have lost touch with their duty of making unselfish men and are meandering in the forest of ideologies, ideas of gods, scriptures, rituals; and so, we of the modern societies have no idea of who are the learned men of wisdom who we should follow! Somehow, we have created a culture that believes that politicians and public servants are our best of men and our role models! Though we all very well know, they aren’t!

Throughout schooling we’ve been given no clarity on who we’re, why are we here, where we are going. And so obviously, we’re blissfully ignorant, and have no societal ideal of the aim and purpose of creation; role of our vocation and life; and we’ve received not training to be more and more unselfishness and rise to be Mentors!

6. Education for Selflessness; not IQ: Are you becoming more and more selfless? This is the real test and evaluation of how good or bad a person really is! How do we be more and more unselfish?

Everyone can certainly be educated as per modern schooling but how should we educate and identify those who are fit to be Mentors and leaders of society? What can we learn from the ancients?

Without the personal life of the teacher as Mentor, no inspiration for self-development takes place. In Bharat therefore, education was always given through men of ‘ tyaga and seva ’. The students did not pay for education. Logic was taught as a subject and the pupils had to have a fixed daily routine of prayers, meditation, Self-study Swadhyaya . It meant study of the Self, of the divinity that creation is, of how to live together in peace and harmony performing our respective Swadharma.

Discipline included control of the senses, practice of austerities, and living a life of dedication and service to elders. Students were taught self-control to curb desire, anger, envy, covetousness. They were to practice forgiveness and commitment to their duties. They were to be modest, self-confident and devoid of pride. Selflessness, self-control, endurance, desire for freedom, etc. were the requisites of a good pupil, fit to be Mentors.

7. Education for becoming a Mentor: It has to be recognized that as long as our heart is not pure it is difficult to inculcate selflessness; and that one who hankers after petty joys and sense pleasures, can rarely cultivate the powers of concentration necessary for greatness. 10-20 years of schooling must identify and nurture those as Mentors who get established in self-development processes to strive to grow in feelings of oneness with more and more and are thus fit to be leaders of society in whatever vocation they may be.
