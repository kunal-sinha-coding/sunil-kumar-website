India to Bharat · Essay #038

Academia & religions must unite to help us inculcate & imbibe practical Vedantic ideal of ‘work as worship of god in man’ into everyday work life

In this modern world of AI & Google, knowledge of all Religions of the world is available to all of us on our fingertips! We can no longer allow Preachers to divide and spread hatred for the ‘other’!

Education, religions, governance must now work together to plan how to imbibe goodness and selflessness. We get more than enough of theoretical knowledge thru’ academia, but unfortunately, we’re not taught how to imbibe the spirit of working selflessly for good of all! If we’re inspired and taught to imbibe the ideal of work as our highest worship, we get opportunities day and night to practice the highest truths and will strive to be a karm yogi, to attain the highest perfection of God-realization.

For most people there is a difference between Work and Play. But if we do what we love, then work becomes play, and we never have to work for another day in our life. That is why Krishna asks Arjun not to abandon the work that is best suited to his Swa-bhava, Swa-dharma, innate, inborn tendencies, nature, even if it has defects in it, rather to work according to his natural propensity! Benefits of following our Swadharma far outweigh its defects. And the foremost benefit is that it provides a comfortable and natural path for one’s purification and elevation to the highest goal of Perfection.

Merely running away from the responsibilities of life is of course not good, but merely doing our duties is just better but not the best! Like Arjuna, we all have to learn to work and fulfil our duties, but without any motives or expectations in a state of action lessness. We can practice reaching this state amidst the flow of the world by detaching ourselves from events and outcomes, and simply focusing on doing our duty. This is just as water flowing under a bridge enters from one side and flows out from the other. The bridge is neither the recipient of the water nor its distributor; it remains unaffected by its flow.

Likewise, the karm yogis do their duty, but keep the mind unaffected by the stream of events. They do not neglect putting in their best efforts in doing their duty, as an act of worship to God, but they leave the final outcome in his hands, and are thus contented and undisturbed with whatever happens – Having two children, one child prays to have plentiful rains, while other, not to send rains; and the wise parent reflects, “God alone knows what is best. Let him do what he feels is best.” Such acceptance of the will of God brings detachment from outcomes, despite being immersed in the incessant stream of events in the world.

Surprisingly, modern education totally ignores the practical wisdom of the Vedas and Vedanta; nor does it talk of how to assimilate the wisdom of compassion, love, and ‘be good and do good’ of Buddha.

While India should be as progressive as any nation, shouldn’t it at the same time be faithful towards our traditions – aka Karma Yogi; “whole world as our family’; Worldview of Oneness & Subjective Focus. History should have highlighted that while all conquerors and looters have killed, fought, and chased the dream to attain universal “Political” &/or “Religious” empires, none has succeeded, and world is divided into many nations and religions are divided into many religions, faiths, sects…

Plan of Nature seems to be to maintain unity in diversity; solidarity of the human race social as well as religious with scope of infinite variation. Splitting of religions into sects is the preservation of religion by frustrating the tendency to rigid sameness … Unity will come by the Fusion, Harmonizing, the essence of different religions into one grand philosophy.

Of course, rituals, scriptures, mythologies, will be many’ but the principle of work and duties as our highest worship can be accepted and from childhood, we can be educated to imbibe the ideal of worship of our mother, father, teacher, Atithi, and nature, seeing God in man and nature… thus we can grow up learning to gradually transform our self-centered mindset towards selflessness and learn to work in the spirit of worship of God!

Isn’t this the most practical, universal idea of God, worship, and Religions? Shouldn’t Academia, teachers, parents and preachers investigate, discuss, reflect and appreciate the need to learn and imbibe the wisdom that the moment I have realized God sitting in the temple of every human being, the moment I stand in reverence before every human being and see God within every being, that moment I am free from all bondages, everything that bound me vanishes and I am free. Those who strive to imbibe this ideal become fit to be Mentors of society.

Now that GOI has introduced the idea and the need for Govt. servants as Karma Yogis, Mission “KYi” and placed before us the G20 Global vision of “One World. One Family, One Future.” we have to plan how we may bridge the gap between academia and religions, thru sincere, selfless, spiritually mature Devotees of different religions as teachers, parents as mentors of society.

“Do not stand on a high pedestal and take five cents in your hand and say, ‘Here, my poor man,’ but be grateful that the poor man is there, so that by making a gift to him you are able to help yourself. It is not the receiver that is blessed, but it is the giver”.
