India to Bharat · Essay #196

Shouldn’t schooling inspire us to be seekers of truth?

1. Both Vedanta and Science both are seeking the truth of Truths : Science seeks truth in the external, objective world of infinite variety and in their seeking they always look for a unity behind this variety. On the other hand, Vedanta, i.e. essence of spiritual wisdom, is the science that seeks for truth in the subjective; in the inner world of thoughts, feelings, ideas, and tries to see the unity, the change-less, the one-constant, behind both the external and the inner ever changing variety! It proclaims the truth that as spirit we are one, interconnected whole!

2. Religion is Out, Science is In : It is the fashion now to treat Science as the only true religion. And, religion has now become a dirty, communal, idea that has given rise to mass scale killings, crusades, fanaticism and now terrorism. Why has religion become a cause of conflict? The reason is the worldview and belief that is preached, i.e. that “my-prophet-my-scripture-my-religion, is the only truth; and, this gives rise to the idea that it is my religious obligation to convert others to my religion. This idea of limiting an infinite idea of God to a finite time and a holy-book is designed to divide and create conflicts! It is not a seeking of truth but is a mere belief. Because of this narrow and limiting idea of what religion is, it is now no longer a part of schooling! The reality is that most of the top scientists, leaders and people are also deeply spiritual; nevertheless, we’re told that religion is not a public affair; it will divide people, and so on!

3. Our Ignorance : As academia and governance, we have ignored that fact that the much bigger purpose of a religion is to make people aware of the higher purpose of work and living and inspire them to practice leading an unselfish, virtuous life. How to develop powers of mind to follow righteous and virtuous conduct and walk-the-talk towards the goal of life, is an essential aspect of all religions which is now missing from schooling.

Our ways of worship may be different in different religions, but a daily routine of self-development practices like prayers, meditations, reading of inspiring passages, etc. are common practices to develop ability for inner-governance to manage quality of our thoughts and direct them towards the highest perfection. Schooling therefore, must provide one-hour daily for exploring, studying, practicing, as per our respective traditions. Only daily practices can keep us on the critical path to the highest goal of life.

4. Rigid control over search for Truth : It was because of the rigid control of church over even scientific inquiry and thinking that the Royal Society established in Oxford in the 1660’s excluded all questions of religion and politics and their motto became Nullius in verba: ‘Nothing in words’, or nothing on authority of the church! The great scientist Newton himself was in search of eternal truth and for him there was no separation between science and spirituality; they were parts of the same truth and were equally avenues to God.

During the time of Newton there was a controversy between Arius and Athanasius schools of Biblical thought and the latter, church-accepted view was that Christ was equal or ‘consubstantial’ with God. Newton held that there is only one invisible God, which corresponds to the Vedantic worldview also.

5. Bharatiya Vedantic culture of ‘whole world as our family’ is based on the highest truth that each one of us are, in our own inherent unchanging nature, already one with and equal or ‘consubstantial’ with God. Vedanta goes on to explain logically how we’re all one with God and asserts that the highest purpose of man, Jivatman, is to realize his oneness with God. Since God is all pervasive, full of love, bliss and all virtues; therefore, being equal to or being made in the image of God, would imply that the ultimate purpose of man ought to be to ultimately become selfless and thereby experience this oneness with the whole creation.

Our problem is that instead of being aware of this profound truth and reality, we’re entrapped by our ego and we believe that I am this limited creature with its likes and dislikes! Of course, intellectually our ego and mindset refuse to accept the idea of our infinitude; we love to hold on to our limited identities and that is what life is all about. Unless, we start practicing being virtuous like God, we can never experience our infinitude, in the waking state.

6. Let us look at a profound, mysterious, fact of our daily experience! What happens in deep sleep? Why don’t we discuss it with each other? We don’t discuss because somehow, intuitively we know that our common experience is that we knew nothing; nevertheless, we wake up refreshed! Why? In deep sleep we are one with all; we’re one with the whole creation! We daily do experience ‘oneness’ but are unable to talk of it, because in deep sleep we are without our only instrument of perception, our mind-ego! From this deep sleep state experience, we can infer that when I am unattached to my limited mind, I am free from all problems; I am one with my blissful infinitude. So, the question is: how to detach ourselves from our selfish-limited-ego-mind? And, reflection will show, it is only by trying to be unselfish and virtuous in waking state!

7. Conclusion : Thus, Schooling must first focus on establishing us in a daily routine of prayers, worship, reading of scriptures, etc. according to our respective faiths, to make us unselfish, make us virtuous, make us compassionate, merciful, forgiving, etc. since these are the very qualities of that omnipotent, omnipresent, omniscient, one God.
