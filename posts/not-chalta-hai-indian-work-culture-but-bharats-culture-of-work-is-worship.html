India to Bharat · Essay #192

Not chalta-hai Indian work culture, but Bharat’s culture of work is worship

1. Work is not just for ‘artha & Kaama’, ‘earning & enjoying’: Modern schooling and materialistic life-styles have failed to inspire us to inculcate the spirit of ‘tyaga and seva’, the twin ideals of Bharat, and the clarity and conviction in the knowledge that our daily ‘work and living’, is our greatest opportunity, to go either towards, or away from health, happiness, success and prosperity, aka ‘Perfection’, the goal and purpose of all human life.

2. Understanding the Purpose of Work: Bharatiya samskriti asserts that man is potentially divine, full of Ananda, Perfect. He is unable to fully experience his blissful divinity and Perfection because of the wheel of karma set into motion by his thoughts-words-works, i.e. karma. Thus, his mind is not calm, stable and peaceful, but is being constantly disturbed by agitations caused by his work, which sets into motion the wheel of cause-effect-cause-effect…, the wheel of causation, resulting in actions and reactions. And so, man is a slave of these karma continuously being created and stored in our mind. Collectively these karmas are our samskaras, our mindset, our worldview, our likes, dislikes, knowledge, skills, behavior, character, and so on. We are what our samskaras are.

Until man makes deliberate, planned, sustained, long-term self-development efforts to refine, purify, efface the samskaras, man cannot be compassionate, selfless and virtuous; he cannot express the divine qualities and virtues, be Perfect and experience ananda.

Once we are clear that our daily work is our opportunity to fulfill the purpose of life, i.e. to attain Perfection, we will strive to work in an appropriate manner and in a vocation where we can excel. It is therefore important that schooling should teach us the right attitudes at work and help us choose that work and vocation thru’ which we can remain on the path to Perfection.

3. Right Attitudes at work:

To work alone is our right and under our control, but the external results are not under our control and so we have no right to them

Let not the results of actions be your motive for work

Always perform your work and duties that are to be done with a calm mind, unattached to results

Those who are impelled by results cannot avoid being miserable

Having giving up attachment to the fruits of actions, even though engaged in action, we’re free of the clutches of the law of cause and effect

Whereas when we work under the sway of desires and expectations of fruits, we are captured by law of cause and effect and get bound and enslaved

From detachment with fruits, we remain on the path of perfection, freedom from bondage to samskaras

When we’re not attached to fruits, we’re not pulled here and there under influence of desires, and can do our work and duties with a calm mind.

Calm, peaceful, stable, focused mind is the goal of life, is perfection in yoga.

Desire thru attachment and aversion is the root cause of agitated and disturbed mind which cannot focus on work. So, as desires lessen so do restlessness and misery.

Learn to do your best and just do your best, dedicating the work to your ‘Ishtam’, favorite form of ‘God’.

Work when done with full attention, without attachment or desire for its fruits is to be practiced. Our task is to do our best. Doing our best is in our control. The results are not in our control and so, let’s focus on the task, not results.

It is only good unselfish karma that leads to purity of mind and only a pure mind is amenable to control. An impure mind is led astray by desires.

Question: If there is no desire how can there be any action? Won’t daily work and life cease?

Answer: See a mother undergoing all hardships for her child. In her child’s happiness and misery lies hers. She does so because of her love and attachment, but still it is not love for herself, but for another person. This shows it is possible to work for others!

Keep the example of a mother working and toiling for her child, a child who can give her back nothing in return. This is the right attitude to work.

Summary: This is the twin ideal and vision of Bharat – Realize your highest potential and manifest and experience your divinity by working in a spirit of worship for good of all.

If we are convinced on the truth that each soul is potentially divine, then we are in ‘sur’, harmony, with the vision of Bharat, ‘the whole world is our family’; no one is a stranger, ‘atithi devo bhava’. Thru’ study, self-introspection and inquiry acquire an intellectual conviction in the knowledge of the goal of life and the role of our daily work and living in remaining on the path to Perfection. Learn thru’ practice to concentrate the mind on the task in hand, treat our work as worship of God in man.
