India to Bharat · Essay #085

Besides 3R’s we need to learn the fact that ‘each being is potentially divine’

Can mere ‘marks/degrees’ help people learn to love and help each other? The Outcomes of schooling, parenting, and religious preaching must be a changed behaviour towards good, better, best. Knowledge and religions are of use only if they make us behave righteously.

Without clarity and conviction in the aim and purpose of work and life we cannot get the inspiration to strive towards Righteousness, which alone purifies the mind, chitta-suddhi , and enables attaining the highest and best. And, without daily reflection, open intellectual discussions, supported by daily Inner-Governance/Self- development practices, it just isn’t possible to get a long-term vision and the inspiration to learn and consciously strive towards the higher goals of work and life.

We need to create Central Boards of Education & Religion to ensure that citizens imbibe the spirit of Righteousness, of selflessly working for long-term good of whole society, the idea of “whole Creation as our Family”; “love for each other”, etc. aka KYi. We must not allow politics and religions to continue dividing people by talking of “differences” and teaching “hatred” for others.

A compulsory subject: It must be a compulsory part of mainstream education to reflect, study, explore, discuss, and teach the scientific fact, reality, and the truth that a living-being, i.e., a Jivaatman, is just a Body-mind complex which is just Inert-Matter. It becomes a living-being only as long as it is enlivened by consciousness of the Self, aka Spirit, Rooha, Atman, Kingdom of God.

This is the obvious essence of a living-being, as presented by the Upanishad and presented in Veda-anta, literally the ‘end’ of Vedas, i.e., its essence. This is not religion!

Why should this scientific truth, a fact, be discussed? Why should we be selfless; love and help each other? Because ‘Learning and practicing seeing the Atman in all beings is the best medicine to overcome aversion or hatred in our heart!’

As long as we cherish any hatred for each other, we cannot have anything to do with righteousness, or being good or being educated or being religious or God loving.

All religions teach, do not seek to injure anybody. Never censure anybody. The phenomenal world is constituted of good and evil, and there will never be any change on this plane. But he who clings to hatred, aversion, and fear, cannot reach God, but becomes more and more the slave of his uncontrolled emotions.

Therefore, we must get an opportunity to introspect, study, know, be convinced in the wisdom that “He who perceives all beings in the Self alone, and the Self in all beings, does not entertain any hatred on account of that perception”. This is the message of all Upanishads!

# “That which is Consciousness alone and that which is all-pervading, eternal, all-full, which is of the form of bliss, ananda, and which is indestructible, is the only true Brahman, Infinite Consciousness, God.

# Whether the body perishes now or lasts the age of moon and stars, what matters it to me having Consciousness alone as my body? What matters it to the sky in the pot, whether the pot is destroyed now or exists for a long time?

# Mankind is bound by ‘mine’, but he is released by ‘Not mine’. Mankind should abandon all the thoughts relating to externals and so also with references to internals; having given up all thoughts, you should rest ever content in your soul.

# Like the butter hidden in milk, the pure Consciousness resides in every being and that ought to be constantly churned out by the churning rod of the Mind.

# Those who are scholarly in arguments about Brahman but are without the Righteousness pertaining to Brahman and who are greatly attached to the world, are certainly born again and again (in this world) through their ajnana , ignorance.

# Arise! awake! approach the great men of Wisdom and learn. Like the sharp edge of a razor is that path, so say the wise, hard to tread and difficult to cross.

# That which cannot be expressed by speech, but by which speech is expressed, that alone know as Brahman and not that which people here worship. That which cannot be apprehended by the mind/ perceived by the eye/ heard by the ear/ but by which, they say, these are apprehended, that alone know as Brahman, and not that which people here worship. (It is God, Consciousness, Brahman, within each being, that enlivens the Mind and Sense-organs and empowers us to become a Jiva-atman)

# People. like children, are immersed in ignorance in various ways. Performing rituals but without commitment to Righteousness for chitta-suddhi, and without giving up attachment to pleasures, they flatter themselves, thinking we have accomplished life’s purpose. These performers of rituals fall from heaven, misery-stricken, when the fruit of their rituals is exhausted.

# As flowing rivers disappear in the sea, losing their names and forms, so a wise man, freed from name and form, attains the purusha, Infinite Consciousness, greater than the Greatest.”

Let’s not entertain aversion for anybody. Aversion gives rise to anger. Do not get upset by either censure or praise. What do they mean? Do not cling; learn to be impersonal and yet sympathetic and helpful. We ourselves are so full of faults and insufficiency; never seek fault in the other person or group. Do not allow hatred to rise in your heart. As long as we have hatred for ‘others’ we’re an atheist, and nothing but that, whatever we may pretend to be and to believe, and to whatever institutional religion we may happen to belong outwardly.

Seeing the Atman in all beings, and all beings in the Atman, one attains the highest Brahman.
