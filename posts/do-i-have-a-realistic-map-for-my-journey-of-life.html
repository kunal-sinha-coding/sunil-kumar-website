India to Bharat · Essay #210

Do I have a realistic map for my journey of life?

A child is very curious: Am I curious? Do I wonder about this vast creation of ours? Do I wonder about myself and what I am doing in this cosmos? Do I have any idea of the holistic big picture, a healthy worldview of what this creation is all about? Have I understood where I am, why am I here, the direction towards which I am going, and much more important, the direction where I should be going, and the goals and the milestones on the way, and how to get there?

Schooling and bureaucracy kill creativity and curiosity: Professional courses and training programs in government or in private sector, religions, and Education thru’ syllabus, textbooks, subjects, exams, etc., all focus on IQ, marks, degrees and on teaching but not on discovering; giving secular knowledge for the job-markets. We come out as obedient sheep-like devotees; babus and munims.

Human-beings alone can think: We can think of many tomorrows. We not only see and feel, we are inquisitive, curious, we want to know, we can learn to read, discuss, question, seek answers to mysteries of the people, things, around us. We have the ability to envision a future, fix our goals, and then plan how to get there. We have to first look at ourselves and discover our strengths and weaknesses and prepare ourselves to be able to undertake this journey.

A good and true map is a necessity to a good, healthy, happy, successful, peaceful, prosperous life. Various people, religions, and science have various ideas and beliefs about the creation as a whole, and a utopia of the ultimate goal of all life. We are free to investigate always keeping in view that our idea may or may not be true unless I personally ‘see’; ‘finite can never know the infinite’.

A Vedantic worldview of ‘oneness’: Bharatiya samskriti is based on the foundation of Vedanta, i.e. ‘essence of Vedas’. Let us, therefore, look at how Vedanta tries to explore the idea of creation. It suggests that the entire creation can be considered as three separate entities, although when I explore and I investigate, I discover that there is but one eternal, blissful, reality! All human goodness and virtues of all religions, stand on this basic foundation of ‘oneness’!

The Oneness has to be individually experienced: The 3-entities are ‘Living beings’; ‘Matter’ and ‘Unknown entity or One God’. Vedanta is not based on any one God, person or Scripture, but is based on the direct experience of an infinite number of sages and seers over the ages and even today, who have experienced their oneness and have tried to express the idea in the Upanishads. They tell us that it is the One God which is appearing to exist as living beings and nature for a time. That One God is of the nature of infinite existence-consciousness, bliss or ananda. Life is a journey towards this experience of oneness and the good are those who are in ‘sur’, in harmony with the idea of creation; they’re literally the Bharati, i.e. striving for highest perfection.

In fact, we’ve forgotten our real identity. The purpose of ‘earning’, ‘working’, ‘enjoying’ is to regain our lost identity with our ananda-swaroopa, blissful-form. It is One God who is Omnipotent, Omnipresent, Omniscient, Infinitely-existing-all-pervasive-Intelligence, Conscious-Energy, which appears to be the grand-master governing all happenings, without being visible, which we refer to as God, Allah, Paramatman, Brahman, Bhagvan, OM, etc.!

In and thru’ our life we’ve to know, feel and experience for ourselves the reality that we the living-beings, nature, and the Unknown Creator are interconnected, interdependent, interrelated. In fact, we’ve no independent ‘existence’ apart from the Unknown God. ‘Existence’ itself being an intrinsic property of the Unknown. We’re just like the waves arising out of the sea for some time and reemerging back into it.

Vedanta suggests that just as the waves arise from the sea and merge back into it, in the same way a living-being (Jivatman) arises from God, or say the sea of God, because of its ‘ego-sense’, ‘me-not-mine’ ideas and identifying with ego-sense it assume a separatist worldview, a name and form.

Freedom of choice : A human-Jivatman alone has the freedom of choice to do or not do something. With freedom of choice comes concomitant responsibility and accountability – as you sow so shall you reap; law of cause-effect, karma. A human is now busy seeking happiness by fulfilling its desires. As it acts, the consequences of its thoughts-actions get impressed in its mind and it gets enslaved in its own created mind-cocoon.

Refining the mind : The goal and purpose of life is to know and then strive to actually realize and experience our oneness with the whole creation. The purpose of religions, education and their self-development strategies is to show us how this mind-cocoon can be refined, purified, expanded, made transparent so that we experience “poorna-ananda”, total-fulfilment and experience the infinitude of God’s love, compassion, oneness with whole creation.

This knowledge has first to be heard . We then have to reflect upon it in deep silence, question it, explore it and get an intellectual appreciation. If we’re convinced, we’ve to act upon it by developing all human virtues, values and gradually move from selfishness to less-self, to unselfishness.

Importance of ‘work’ : Work here implies all our thoughts, motives, intentions, words, actions. Thus, it follows that education and training must ensure that we’re committed to our duties and to quality and excellence in our vocation. This is the very basic requirement expected from any citizen. It is all the more expected from the academia, public functionaries, and above all from the leadership of any sector and at any level, for it is they, who set the tone, which others follow.

Summary : Education, Religions, Governance must focus on communicating and creating people and systems conducive to ‘responsible and enlightened citizens who are convinced and committed to “Knowing and doing the right things” as the only guarantee for both happiness and prosperity.
