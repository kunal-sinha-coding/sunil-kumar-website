India to Bharat · Essay #033

Understanding our common, universal, ultimate Goal.

1. ‘The whole world is full of the Lord, open your eyes and see Him’. ‘Each soul is potentially divine. The goal is to manifest the divinity within’.

‘All the books contained in the Upanishads have one task before them – to prove the following theme: ‘Just as by the knowledge of one lump of clay we have the knowledge of all the clay in the world, so what is that, by knowing which we know everything in the universe?’

‘In the Atman, they found the solution. Know this Atman alone.’ ‘The debt which the world owes to our Motherland is immense. Taking country by country, there is not one race on this earth to which the world owes so much as to the patient Hindu, the mild Hindu’ – Vivekananda.

Biblical statement, ‘Seek you first the Kingdom of God of within and his righteousness and everything else will be added unto you’.

Know Thyself; I am That I am: The Shruti (that which is heard; the Vedas), are the direct perception of selfless, pure Seers, Rishis who ‘see’, during their tapas, meditations, the Truth of Brahman, God, i.e., ‘Sad-Chid-ananda’, ‘Eternal Existence-Consciousness-Bliss’. Shruti tells very clearly that to know our Swaroopa (i.e., our real unchanging form as distinct from the changing body-mind), is the ultimate purpose of life!

Living or dead we all wish for a state of perpetual bliss, a state which is totally free of all traces of sorrows. The ultimate Goal is expressed variously as Salvation, Nirvana, Perfection, Moksha, Heaven, etc. Many do not believe in God or the afterlife and hold death as the end; so, ‘eat drink and be merry’. Many believe in God but not in rebirth and hold that we have only one life to enjoy/suffer the consequences of our deeds followed by heaven/hell and then a day of judgement! The Buddhists believe in rebirth and ‘to be and to do good but for them shoonya, nirvana, is moksha. The worldly people hold that to live happily itself is moksha.

Some believe that heaven is moksha; though we know of stories of how some have to get out of heaven for this or that reason. So, the joy of heaven can only be a temporary stage. Even being in the company of God cannot be moksha, of which we know of many instances. None of these are the true form or Swaroopa of moksha. Another name for moksha is ‘bhooma’, infinite.

Fear comes from the other, feeling of separateness! Thus, shruti makes it very clear that getting rid of dwaita-bhaava, feeling of duality, i.e. that there is anything other than myself, is moksha. Whether it is fear or sorrows, the jiva will experience it when he sees anything as separate from himself. One fears only something other than himself. When there is absolutely nothing separate from myself, why should I become afraid?

According to shruti, moksha is the real Swaroopa of the Jiva. The real experience and meaning of moksha are “eternal existence and never decaying bliss, ananda. We must however remember that this is being said of the formless Swaroopa of the Jiva, which he experiences only in deep sleep, Sushupti, when unattached to body-mind!

2. We’re ignorant that this is an ‘Intelligent Universe’! And so, our birth, ‘rich or poor’, ‘here or there’, etc., and our experiences every moment, are not mere coincident or accidents but they’re the logical ‘judgment’ of what we deserve every moment to moment, good or bad, based on the accepted universal principle of law of cause-effect, ‘as we sow, so we reap’.

Through 20-25 years of learning, schooling and growing-up we must discuss, explore and get intellectual conviction that our real Swaroopa, changeless, unchanging form, is one, singular Consciousness! Our Swaroopa can never be the ever-changing body-mind with which we’re ignorantly identifying ourselves! To once again Re-gain (Yoga & Religion) our Oneness with our Swaroopa is the purpose of work and life. This is termed Freedom, Mukti, of nature of eternal, never decaying bliss, Ananda.

The conviction has to be made firm that my own Swaroopa is one with Brahman. ‘I am myself Brahman’, this realization must radiate through me. When we practice acting out of this conviction, then our mind, which was always being pulled outwards through the senses, now can get attracted inwards towards the atman. So, we can say that Shruti (Sravana), tarka (manana or reflection) and anubhava (experience) – all three are necessary for the realization of Atman.

Because we identify with body-mind, we’ve sraddha in our respective past world view, knowledge, direct perception and our inference, and so we’ve doubts! When we reflect upon and understand the arguments and logic of shruti, then we can firmly determine that the Jiva is one with Brahman – to keep the mind focused on the truths determined by reflection, is the intellectual conviction to be attained.

This Brahman-gyana is acquired through ‘listening-reflection-and acting out of the Vedic conviction that I am Brahman’. Mind has to be prepared and equipped with good tendencies, samskaara, etc. over a long period of practice to attain purity of mind, chitta-suddhi, thru’ Righteousness, selflessness, etc.

We have to reflect upon the subtlest of truths, which is beyond any past experiences and beliefs and use all our reasoning to appreciate the logic of the arguments given to grasp the spirit of what is heard; avoid unnecessary arguments and instead try to reconcile and grasp what the shruti is trying to communicate.
