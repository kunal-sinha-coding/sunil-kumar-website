India to Bharat · Essay #023

Learning mind management to be a Karma Yogi – Commitment to Righteousness & Kartavya-palan is the purpose of education & religions!

‘Knowing as Education’ & ‘Believing as Religion’ cannot ever be the Fruit of being educated or devotees, or followers, of this or that religion or God!

Even though UNESCO pillar of Education asks schooling to teach ‘Learning the Treasure within’; ‘Learning to be’; ‘Learning to Live together’, unfortunately modern Education ignores this aspect of Education and teaches nothing but only Learning ‘to know’ & ‘to do’!

What is the Purpose of Rituals & Worship in any Religion? It is to Purify the Mind by dwelling on holy thoughts; and to learn to control and manage the restless mind towards our respective ideals of Worship. Worship, i.e., ‘Upasana’, literally means ‘sitting near devotedly’. It implies a method; a ‘Mind-management’ & Self-development process, a ‘Life-skill’, to ‘Know’ and control the flow of our thoughts, i.e. our ‘Mind’, towards a worldview of ‘love thy neighbour as thyself’, i.e., more and more, spontaneous feelings of love, care, concern for each other.

All ‘Worship’, including any ‘work’ or ‘action’, irrespective of that action or “my vs your god or religion”, is in its essence nothing but a process of creating a ‘habit’ and learning to manage our mind and control it towards whatever we desire; be it ‘bad or good’; ‘cheating or teaching’; ‘leading or misleading’.

When we use the word Worship, Upasana, it implies gradual control of Quality, Quantity, and Direction of Thoughts towards the higher, long-term good; towards the God of our worship; an approach towards realizing Perfection, Heaven, Nirvana, Freedom from slavery to our limited selfishness and experiencing Utopia; One, infinite blissful Omniscient idea of God.

During educational and growing up processes we must be made aware of and given an intellectual conviction in the evident reality that “my” continuously changing “Body-mind-intellect- individuality”, “ roopa ”, that gets born and disappears with death, cannot be “my” real changeless Identity – because “that which Exists can never cease to exist”! And behind change, there has to be a changeless, “my” real Swa-roopa , i.e., my real ‘roopa’, form, the unchanging identity as “Conscious-Existence” that is ever fulfilled and which prompts me always to seek peace, bliss, fulfilment, and to live!

Education must be a self-development process , a Sadhana, to learn to manage, control, and calm the mind and turn the ever external oriented, extremely restless mind, “inwards”, to know itself. Schooling & growing up must inculcate in us a habit of daily personal time for Reflection & Practice. Only then can we learn to identify with “our” Swaroopa and realize that love for each other makes each of us more peaceful, blissful, fulfilled!

To reflect on our changeless Swaroopa, with our ever-changing Mind is really very difficult and needs continuous practice, focus, and a burning desire! We must realize that not just growing up, we need life-long practice to improve “Quality of Thoughts” flowing in our mind and directing them “Inwards”, to reflect, know and realize our Swaroopa!

So, schooling needs to make us learn and get established in the habit of regular and daily self-development practices.

What is the idea of Worship, Upasana? Focusing the mind on a sacred, holy ‘symbol’, ideal, prayer, personality; not allowing any other unrelated thoughts; continuously keeping the mind flowing towards the ideal, is Upasana. It is a ladder to gradually stabilize the mind by pouring holy, good, positive thoughts, to create new holy impressions, samskara, memories towards realization of our ideal. Upasana leads to purification of mind, and it is easier than concentrating on formless idea of infinite God, directly. It gradually purifies the mind and our thoughts flow towards the God ideal because we’re conscious that the symbol signifies Omnipresent God.

Merely doing our prescribed familial, individual, societal, vocational duties, Kartavya-palan, also yields good results; however, if done selflessly without desires or expectations, it becomes karma yoga and leads to our highest ideal.

Selfless kartavya-palan, without expectations of any rewards other than satisfaction for fulfilling our duties, becomes Work as Worship and Service of God in man and creation. Now GOI has directed that all Government Servants be trained to be Karma Yogi. And the Global G20 Vision of Oneness as proposed by GOI gives an inspiration to learn to work as Worship, Upasana.

The meaning of Upasana is to keep the mind in equilibrium; flowing of holy thoughts focused on one ideal, ‘work / vocation’ as worship’; ‘fulfilment of duties’ as worship; not allowing the mind to run here and there expecting ‘fruits’ of work. Profits, wages, wealth accrue to us as compensation for service of society; ‘Fruits, profits’ need not be the motivation to work!

During growing up and education we must learn of our Swaroopa thru’ daily, continuous ‘ shravan and manan’ , ‘hearing/learning and deep reflections’, to be convinced of our Ideal. This ‘hearing’ is Upasana and as we reflect, we get intellectual conviction; and thru’ consciously, and continuously striving to act out of the conviction of our Swaroopa, it purifies and becomes our habit.

Conclusion: All our present human thinking is based only on our own limited beliefs, memories, samskara, impressed in my mind by my own choices-actions and its consequences, as embedded in our limited Minds. However, if we learn from a young age that identified with our Swaroopa, we’re one with all and God, then we get time to explore, debate, enquire this ideal of Oneness; the truth of truths. Our basic human ignorance is to think ‘I am this Body’, based on my limited Mind. But as we continue with our Upasana, we purify our mind and this helps us to strive to selflessly strive to be a Karma Yogi and walk towards realizing our Swaroopa.
