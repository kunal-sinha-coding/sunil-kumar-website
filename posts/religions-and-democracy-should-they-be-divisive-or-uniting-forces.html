India to Bharat · Essay #200

Religions and Democracy – Should they be divisive or uniting forces?

1. Wisdom of Bharat: Modern religions claim their scripture and Prophet alone to be true and their God as the only true God. Of course, they also believe their God to be omnipotent, omniscient, and omnipresent. Obviously, an infinite God can never be bound and expressed in a finite book and time of history and therefore, this narrow, and divisive mentality is bound to create conflicts as it has done throughout history and is still the major cause of most of the wars, conflicts, and is now appearing as terrorism! Similarly, the very idea of democracy divides society into warring and conflicting parties and we have leaders of Opposition! Wisdom suggests that since people are different, we’ve different religions whose common purpose is to inspire man to strive to be good, better, best; and, we’ve different political parties whose common purpose is to make India great.

2. Education, Religions, Politics, Governance must unite: ‘Unity is strength’, is commonsense and therefore, once elections are over, all elected members have to come and work together for planning, governance, and execution of plans for good of society, which they’re not doing now!

How and why will they work together unless they’ve been educated to be good and enlightened citizens? So, education and religions must learn to work together, to create the good, committed to quality in work and to their obligatory duties. Democracy needs responsible citizens who can become selfless leaders; and we need political leaders of cooperation not opposition. We need leaders who have the wisdom and selflessness to work not for ‘myself’ and ‘my party’ but for good of society as a whole.

3. Common ideals of Religions which Schooling must brainstorm & instill in the young:

  • Are we in a state of perpetual purposelessness? Or do we all have a common, universal, eternal goal towards which we all are struggling?
  • If we reflect, we will know that in and thru’ all our activities we are all struggling to “regain” our lost happiness, literal Latin meaning of Religion; similar to yoga, i.e. “to unite” us with our lost happiness.
  • Therefore, the universal and common message of all education and all religions must be to inculcate in us the simple wisdom: “Strive to ‘Earn’ and ‘Enjoy’ through ‘Righteous, ethical conduct’ to remain on the critical path to becoming good and better towards the “Best”, i.e. ‘Perfection’, Heavenly-bliss, Nirvana, Salvation, Freedom, “perpetual-bliss”, “Ananda”.
  • We can never be really fully happy, peaceful or fulfilled till we experience Ananda, which is our inherent unchanging, blissful nature, i.e. swaroopa, as distinct from happiness which comes and goes, being dependent on things outside us.
  • This clarity on our common goal can then inspire us to continuously cooperate and together struggle to harmonize and direct all our work, thoughts, choices, motives and activities towards experiencing our inherent blissful nature, ananda-swaroopa!
  • Our education and our western, materialistic worldview, and our busy work-life, all deprive us the time or opportunity to explore and discover what our forefathers have thought on the most momentous questions of life – “By honors, medals, titles no true man is elated. To realize that which we are, this is the honor for which we are created” – Angelus Silesius
  • Our modern education has denied us the opportunity and time to go through and understand logically the wealth of profound knowledge and wisdom available to all of us as children of Bharat, i.e. the knowledge of the infinite, omnipresent divine consciousness, which is beyond the comprehension of finite minds, and is the universal and eternal spiritual wisdom for all.
  • We owe it to our children and to our forefathers to know the reasons for the sustainability of Bharat’s heritage and about its foundational roots and thoughts, so that we can pass on this wisdom to the next generation of mankind.
  • As the educated elite and elders of society, it is our responsibility to learn the roots of our culture so that we may give a higher direction and purpose to our life. Schooling must help us read and know of our own roots and foundations. Let’s then have sraddha in this time-tested wisdom, the foundation of our most ancient civilization which can guide us all even today.
  • The entire scheme of Bharat’s heritage is based on this central idea – the oneness and divinity of man, life, and of all creation. And therefore, the whole focus of Indian culture was to create systems that would make people aware of their potential, their divinity, and their oneness with all creation and help develop purity and powers of mind to manifest their potential and grow towards this oneness, through every movement of life.
  • This scientific and Bharatiya worldview of ‘oneness’ is needed today to guide man towards its higher dimensions, bring harmony amongst religions and manage, what is being called the conflict or clash of civilizations, terrorism, exploitations, etc.
  • We were hoping that modern one-man, one-book-religions, systems and technology would finally give us back our lost happiness; but there are no signs of it, yet.
  • Students must discuss subjective issues, and reflect upon fundamental questions like: Who am I? Why am I here? Where am I going? How to get there? And then seeks for answers within.
  • We all understand that a living-being is potentially divine, in accordance with the biblical proclamation, the ‘Kingdom of God’ is within us. Therefore, schooling must give us an intellectual conviction in the idea that each living-being is potentially divine and therefore, we must ‘strive first for righteousness and everything else will be added unto you’.
  • From the above idea it follows that work in the spirit of worship as service to living-beings, seeing God in man, becomes our highest worship. Schooling must give us this wisdom.
  • In the objective world we now judge ourselves by our modern standards of knowledge, living and enjoyment. However, with a subjective focus we will ask the questions which our forefathers reflected upon: What is that by knowing or getting which, everything is obtained and there is nothing more to be known or gained and by acquiring which we will be perpetually happy, blissful, peaceful and fulfilled.
  • Even the UN is now introducing a Human Happiness Index in our measures of development. Therefore, schooling must brainstorm and discuss issues like: Are we becoming good, better and happier? And, then we will realize that we need to first discuss and decide upon a common concept of the ‘highest and best’ towards which all of us can then direct our energies.
  • And, it is this question that schooling must deal with and intellectually argue and show that as the ‘subject’, we are directly responsible for creating our own destinies and only identified with the real ‘subject’, the Self within ourselves, can we really, not only regain our lost happiness, but can also experience our oneness with the whole creation, and be at peace.

4. Conclusion: Parents and Leaders have abdicated their responsibilities. Teachers now have the power and authority to create good citizens who will become good leaders who can provide good governance. Teachers may absorb the universal principles and essence of religions and their idea of daily Self-development practices to discipline and guide the mind towards the higher dimensions of work and life. If we can provide one-hour daily then teachers, with the help of many sincere NGO’s working in the field of value education, etc., can establish students in daily Self-development practices so that they get an intellectual conviction in the power of goodness, and can appreciate that “it is a privilege to serve mankind, for this is the worship of God; God is here, in all these human souls. He is the soul of man”; “Religion comes from intense self-sacrifice. Desire nothing for yourself. Do all for others. This is to live and move and have your being in God. The difference between God and the devil is in nothing except in unselfishness and selfishness.”
