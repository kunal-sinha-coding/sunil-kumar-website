India to Bharat · Essay #031

Criticisms of caste we all know – but why does it still survive? It maintains, Rtm, order, harmony in samaaj!

1. Vision: The holistic plan of creation and our Goal of Life isn’t ‘Artha-Kaama’ as the Western cultures and modern schooling see it, but it is getting established in commitment to respective-Duties, Swadharma and Kartavya-palan, so as to be able to remain on the path of ‘Dharma-Moksha’, the highest goal of Perfection, Freedom, Oneness – being in perfect Rtm with creation by adhering to Dharma, Righteousness.

The focus during growing up shouldn’t be just on 3R’s, but on establishing the young in ‘Dharma’, Righteousness, spirit of Karma Yoga, etc. thru’ a daily routine of Self-development practices, so as to awaken viveka-buddhi and develop the spirit of detachment and simple living and high thinking, building powers of mind, will-power, self-control, etc. so that they get inspired to work for good of samaaja – which literally means equal to Paramatma, aka Brahman, God, with a burning desire to be in Rtm, following their Dharma and respective Swadharma.

2. Freedom: Freedom, Equality, Liberty, aren’t free gifts given to all! Real Freedom is not in doing what we want to do ; but in not having to do what we want to do ! Given freedom and equality, without commitment to Righteousness, etc., will always have a tendency to lead to greed for Artha-Kaama; as we now see! Freedom and equality, Oneness, have to be earned thru’ Swadharma. And Equality and Freedom mean to be in Perfect Rtm with the whole Creation fulfilling our Swadharma.

3. Dissatisfaction with Present: Don’t we see today, how Freedom and Equality have led to people without character becoming our political masters, policy makers, administrators, teachers! We can perhaps now realize the wisdom of Vedantic Rishis who could foresee that freedom and equality tends to push people beyond their level of character and competence, to their level of incompetence!

Today, the first 20 years of growing up and education is wasted in learning and memorizing vast irrelevant 3R’s knowledge and then finding a job, vocation, career, etc. which has nothing to do with what we studied!

4. The idea of Varna, caste divisions in society , is an attempt to bind people within their levels of familial background, occupational, vocational, work path, so that each person is guided from childhood in keeping with their inherited tendencies, knowledge, skills, character and competence.

Only after Vidya i.e., education that leads to Freedom, can a person be committed to kartavya-palan and to love, care and concern for all others equally. Vedanta accepts that people are different with different desires, attributes, goals, samskaras, and therefore need different types of education, vocation and self-development practices appropriate for their growth towards Perfection, from wherever they are now.

People are different because of the different samskaras they’re continuously acquiring thru their ‘choices, motives, thoughts, words, deeds’; and are born in different circumstances, based on law of Cause-effect; as we sow, so we reap. As a thumb rule, we may generally consider people of four types:

i) Mentors (who are seekers of ‘highest wisdom, Mukti’ and therefore best suited to be the guides, teachers, policy-makers in society)

ii) Administrators (who with their strength of mind and long-term vision are best suited to establish ‘dharma’, fairness, justice, rule of law and righteous conduct in society)

iii) Entrepreneurs (who focus on artha and are responsible to create wealth and resources for society and provide opportunities to the workforce; they facilitate production and distribution of resources)

iv) Workforce (who focus on kaama, provide skilled and unskilled labour and are the main result producers, facilitated by the above)

The purpose of education and religions is ‘subjective’, i.e. to help us attain inner purity of mind in and thru’ our daily work life and therefore one must engage in vocation which enables chitta-suddhi, purity of mind, selflessness, expansion of mind towards Oneness with all.

5. Why Caste? This is how Vedanta explains the idea : Any nation, samaaja, needs all four types of people, just as a person has Head, Hands, Middle part, and Legs and while all have their natural defects and have to respect each other, it is the Workforce, that nourishes everyone and it is commitment to our respective Dharma, Righteousness, that maintains Rtm, Oneness.

Dharma is equivalent to Creation, Paramatma, Truth, Rtm, Satt-chid-ananda, ‘Unchanging Existence-Consciousness-Bliss’ , God. Application of Dharma and our respective Swadharma, keeps everyone including the knowers of wisdom, bound ‘on the critical path to the highest Truth’. All people, castes, are under control of Dharma. In all places and at all times, even the weakest person has the faith that he shall emerge victorious against the stronger person, even though that may be the ruler himself, because Creation maintains perfect Rtm based on our commitment to Dharma/ Swadharma.

Though we may think people as higher and lower either in the caste/varna or any modern systems we’re all One because the same One all-knowing God, Sad-chid-ananda, is seated in the heart of everyone and is everywhere and gives birth to people in different varnas according to their samskara acquired by them through their own karma. God lays down the dharma, through the Veda, in accordance with the acquired guna, characteristics of Sattva-Rajas-Tamas, ‘Calm-active-inert’, for different varnas. As long as people follow their dharma/swadharma, they are protected by dharma itself.

The samaja, remains in Rtm, Order, based on this varna-arrangement, where each fulfil their respective duties, and work unselfishly for good of samaja. If education doesn’t establish us in dharma, bad qualities like attachment, greed, etc. affect us; we ignore dharma and then there is misery in the world. Irrespective of race, nationality, ‘my vs your’ God/religious ideas, education must establish us in commitment to our respective dharma/swadharma.
