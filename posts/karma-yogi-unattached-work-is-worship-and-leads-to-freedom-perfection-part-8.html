Karma Yogi – Unattached Work is Worship and leads to Freedom, Perfection (Part 8)

To acquire freedom from stress and miseries and be full of peace and happiness is our universal Goal, but such freedom cannot be found here, as long as we’ve samskaras; which we continuously create by our ‘thought-word-action’, Karma. Law of Karma means the inevitable law of cause-effect, which applies throughout the universe.

Till our mind is full of Samskaras, perfect equilibrium, or what is called, peace that passeth all understanding, Mukti, Ananda, cannot be had in this universe, nor in heaven, nor in any place where our mind and thoughts can go, senses feel, or we can imagine.

There’re two ways to attain freedom from miseries, withdrawing to forests for hard tapasya or Karma-yoga, since we all work and create Karma 24×7. So, Schooling and Religions must ensure that we’re taught how to work without creating new Samskaras. Karma yoga is a way to refine and purify our Samskaras just as any Religious worship. Thankfully, Vivekananda has presented the ideal of Karma-Yoga, in inspiring English which all can hear/read, reflect, imbibe!

Why are all people and universe working ? For what? For freedom from miseries. And, unknowingly, for merging our finite identity into the infinite; for, there is no happiness in the finite; infinite alone is freedom, Ananda, Bliss . So, unconsciously as it were, we all are always trying to expand, to be free of all limitations, away from bondage. We don’t want to be limited by anyone! W ork is inevitable, it must be so; so, we must learn and practice working in the spirit of karma-yoga to attain to freedom, ‘Perfection’.

Karma-Yoga is the method of doing work in a way that is most beneficial. It says, work incessantly, but give up all attachment to work. Don’t identify with anything. Hold your mind free. Misery comes through attachment; not through work. If we don’t identify, we aren’t miserable. If I think ‘I and mine’, misery comes.

Therefore, enjoy the beauty of all Names-Forms in the world, but don’t identify with any of them. Whenever we say mine , misery comes. Don’t think of even my-child/family. Thru’ practice destroy this tendency for selfish-attachment. Then work.

Non-attachment is the basis of all Religions, Yoga. If we don’t link our mind with the objects that we observe, we are non-attached. You may be on a throne, perfectly non-attached; another may be in rags but very much attached.

Basic Questions for Reflection? Where is this binding link of attachment, I and mine? In those things or people out there? Or only in my-mind ? Where does this Attachment, Law of Karma, Gravitation exist? They’re only in our-mind; they’re the manner, in which our-mind grasps a series of phenomena of names-forms in the world. Attachment or Law of Karma exist only in our-mind; it is only a subjective experience of how our-mind observes the objects!

When we speak of the universe, we only mean that portion of existence which is observable albeit limited by our mind – the universe of the senses, which we can see, feel, touch, hear, think of, imagine. This alone is under law; but beyond it, existence is free and cannot be subject to law, because causation does not extend beyond our minds.

Look at those slaves to duty! Duty is ever on them. They come home and think of the work for the next day. It is living a slave’s life, at last dropping down in the street and dying in harness, like a horse. This is duty as it is understood.

The only true duty is to be unattached, to work as free beings : Be at rest, be free, and work. Whatever you have to do, give, do by all means, but not as a slave. Be not compelled; idea of doing under compulsion builds attachment. Why should you have any compulsion, yet do what is to be done. This kind of work-in-freedom is a very hard thing to attain, but we must fix and strive towards the ideal!

Resign everything unto God: For those who believe in God there is a much easier way. Give up the fruits of work unto the Lord; just work and help, seeing God in Creation. Whatever you do, see, feel, hear, is for Him. The highest men do not seek to get any name or fame from their knowledge. See the Vedic Rishis they have left their ideas to the world; they put forth no claims for themselves and established no religions, school, systems in their name.

The highest men are calm, silent, unknown : They remain in isolation but when they think and give ideals, their true thoughts penetrates through mountains, oceans, and travels through the world. The highest cannot work, for in them there is no attachment. But everyone else has to work. So, remember, we only help ourselves in this gymnasium of the world; it is a privilege to help.

Karma Yoga is the attaining, through unselfish work, of that freedom which is the goal of all human nature. That which is selfish is immoral, and that which is unselfish is moral. Buddha said, I don’t care of various theories about God. Don’t just believe in this or that God; reason it out for yourself, then do good and be good. And this will take you to freedom, Truth of truths.
