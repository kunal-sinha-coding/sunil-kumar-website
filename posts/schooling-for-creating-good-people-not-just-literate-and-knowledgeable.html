India to Bharat · Essay #189

Schooling for creating good people not just literate and knowledgeable!

1. Challenge of Education – Making good people: Education today has to perforce focus only on marks, marks, because that is what parents and society demand! And, yet society expects good people to emerge from schooling. The Academia has to wake up to the reality that we first need the good. Only thereafter, we need ‘marks’.

The Academia has to assume leadership and has to insist that schooling will provide one-hour daily to establish children in self-development practices to ‘know’ and build strength to struggle to become good, better, best.

The Academia has to focus on giving the young awareness of the spirit of our composite culture and to instill the spirit of quality; of kartavya-palan; of work as worship; of compassion; of love, care, concern for all, equally, as enjoined on us in the Fundamental Duties Art. 51A. Academia has to take this responsibility, to make the good, since politicians and bureaucracy are concerned more with short-term results for their vote banks and do not have the long-term vision.

Sincere people have to come together to inspire teachers, parents, elder citizens to strive to be mentors and use the 12 years of schooling to imbibe in the young the worldview of oneness and the spirit of striving for the highest human perfection, in and thru’ daily work and life.

2. Traditional Wisdom: Society needs good teachers, administrators, traders, entrepreneurs, farmers and a vast skilled and unskilled work force. The good are those who are inspired to be on the path of dharma while earning and enjoying. The very purpose of schooling is to make good persons. The young are to acquire Vidya, i.e. that which leads to humility; to freedom, the highest human goal. They are to be trained for a vocation in accordance with their inherent tendencies and skills, within their character and competence, where they could excel and work for good of society. Only a good person established in practice of dharma, when given knowledge will use the knowledge for the good of society.

3. Teachers as Acharyas: The young as well as the masses learn by imitating their elders. The maxim, ‘whatever the leaders do that the people follow’; the values and ideals of the teachers and leaders becomes the norm for society. Accordingly, our teachers were called Acharya, i.e. persons of righteous ethical conduct; of tyaga and seva; of character. And, people learned from their conduct.

4. Who is responsible for making the Good? Religions have failed to make good people who can work together for good of society. Religions have now become narrow, divisive, ‘ritual’ based, teaching that only their way is right way, leading to communal fights; and so, they are no longer a part of mainstream.

It is now the responsibility of the academic universe to extract, understand and teach the self-development processes, the essentials of all religions, to the young, throughout schooling. Schooling should provide time, space, environment to engage and establish the young in daily self-development practices to develop powers of mind, concentration, will-power, self-control, etc. through prayers, meditation, self-introspection and study-of-Self to reflect on who we are; why are we here; where are we going; how to get there; etc.

5. Teachers trained as Mentors can now be requested to make the Good: How we behave, feel and think, how we conduct ourselves, the choices we make when facing ethical dilemmas, etc. are determined by the state of our mind, our samskars ; we’re enslaved by our own self-created samskaras.

Teachers as Mentors can create a nexus of the good within their locality so that we’ve more volunteers and mentors who can interact with the children and inspire students and the community to strive to become good, better, best, following their swadharma (duties, ethical and righteous conduct), in and thru’ their daily pursuits for ‘earning and ‘enjoyment’ to attain the ‘best’, ultimate purpose of life.

6. One-hour daily for Self-development Practices: To change our characteristics, our mind-set, we have to work on our Mind – ‘I alone’ am responsible to change; to refine or pollute ‘my mind’! Therefore, there is a need to introduce in schools say, one-hour daily, to enable teachers and students: –

To explore, discuss, question, and try to get convinced that it is worthwhile to be ethical.

That it is worthwhile to struggle to know and to do what we know to be right. That being ethical is good for us in the long run. That we must struggle to build self-control and will power to manage our responses and refrain from doing what we know is not right. That it is not just worthwhile but necessary to engage in daily Self-development practices to learn to improve the Quality of our thoughts and learn to manage our responses and our thoughts and Direct our thoughts towards “higher, better; highest and best”.

We need to strive to know and get gradually convinced in the ‘highest and best’. Only if schooling can inspire us when we’re young, will we undertake the daily struggle to be ethical, and to harmonize our daily thoughts, words, work and actions, towards the higher and better.

7. Striving for the “highest and best”: The daily struggle to be good, better, best is the ideal of all Religions as well as of the Composite Culture of “India that is Bharat”. The Fundamental Duties of Art. 51A of our Constitution, enjoins on us to imbibe the spirit of our composite culture. The ‘Code of Professional Ethics for School Teachers’ created by National Council of Teacher Education, NCERT, AIPTF, AISTF, and The Supreme Court of India have also directed us to know our composite culture.

The essentials of our composite culture and the essence of all Religions are similar. All those striving for the highest illumination are “Bharati” (i.e. striving for the highest Perfection), whatever be their race, nationality, religion, caste; they’re in “SUR”, in Rtm, with nature, where Rtm means Righteous ethical conduct. Those who so strive are the Srestha, fit to be our teachers and leaders; those who don’t so strive are really the ‘ Asur ’!

8. Purpose of Schooling is to establish us in good practices: Teachers as Mentors can inspire us to strive for the ‘highest’ common goal of all life thru’ the habit of i) Daily Swadhyaya, i.e. Self-study to know my-Self, who am I, why I am here, where have I come from, where am I to go? How to get there? Etc. ii) Daily Sadhna, i.e. Having determined the desired goal, sadhna is the striving to build the ability and following the means to achieve the goal. Common Goal of our strivings are: Sukh, shanti, Ananda; Swastha; Health, Happiness, Success; Balanced life.

At a higher level our common goal is to know that each soul is potentially divine; A living-being, Jiva-atma, is body as matter which is enlivened by Consciousness. The goal is to manifest this infinite potential within by controlling nature, external and internal (Mind). The mind is being ‘reformed/refined or polluted’ continuously by our thoughts-actions, selfless or selfish. We can facilitate and accelerate the process of refining the mind by Self-development practices, Sadhna and Swadhyay a .

9. Self-development processes: Work itself is an opportunity to attain the highest thru’ Kartavya-palan, Quality work in spirit of seva-bhava for good of society. Devotion and worship of our “Ishtam”, i.e. favorite form of God, according to our respective Religion, Faiths. Meditation according to our respective religion and faiths. Knowledge/Philosophy, self-study, debate, discuss, study of self.

This is the essence of all religions. Doctrines, or dogmas, or rituals, or books, or temples, or forms, are but secondary details. All actions must lead to reducing ego, refining the mindset and realizing our Self.

10. Summary: Becoming Good, better, Best – Are you in Sur? If not you’re an Asur! Religions and Democracy divide us into yours and mine. UNESCO wants education to teach us to “Learn to live together” & “Way of Peace”; but schooling doesn’t teach us how and so, we remain ‘divided’ and not ‘together’.

Schooling must teach us to see the ‘whole world as our family’. Schooling must establish the young in daily sadhna and swadhyaya , Self-development practices to purify the mind; get inspired in practice of Dharma to attain the Goal of Life
