India to Bharat · Essay #140

Vast knowledge gives only scholarship – Wisdom is in just knowing the one!

Why Care for each other? Why do all good men and all religions ask us to love, care, have concern for each other and implore us to be virtuous, good, value based, be ethical, follow Righteous conduct, be a Karma-Yogi, etc.?

God is Omnipresent and therefore can be One alone! It has been the experience of countless seers and sages that we’re all One integrated whole. Somehow, I now see myself as separate from the whole and so, I tend to be concerned with my good alone. If we’re One, as seers tell us, then intentionally or unintentionally, if I hurt anyone, actually I am hurting only myself. Therefore, we’re asked not to hurt anyone, because there is no other, there is no second! The universal principle of ‘action and reaction’; of ‘as we sow, so do we reap’, applies. Therefore, only by selflessly working for good of all, we remain in harmonious tune with the truth that we’re all really One. And so, we should struggle to gradually cut down on selfish actions thru’ self-less actions.

Implication of an Omnipresent God: After the Incarnations, Prophets and God men come the saints and holy men who bring the message of our spiritual unity to the common man. It is the silent work of these holy men that rejuvenates, nourishes the soul, and enables mankind to maintain its direction God-wards, loving and caring for each other.

In the midst of terrorism, materialism, greed, corruption, the seers and their spiritual wisdom are like the oases. It is the seers who tell us and, all religions and theists believe that God is Omnipotent, Omnipresent, Omniscient. Science is also now veering to the idea of a united, intelligent universe. How do the seers express this idea of Oneness and, what does it really mean in practical daily life?

Vedantic Wisdom in a Nutshell:

  • Advaita: There’s no second; One alone is.
  • Therefore, it follows that, ‘That’ alone exists. Existence is itself That, God.
  • Know the One and you will know it all
  • Advaita: There’s no second; One alone is.
  • Therefore, it follows that, ‘That’ alone exists. Existence is itself That, God.
  • That (Brahman) thou art
  • Consciousness (knowing, awareness, experiencing) is That, Brahman
  • This Atman itself is Brahman
  • My Swaroopa (my unchanging form) has to be God, Consciousness, That
  • Purpose of Religion/Yoga is to help us ‘Regain’ Oneness/Reunite with That
  • In and thru’ daily working and living Regain/Reunite with That
  • Each individual is potentially That; because That alone exists;
  • But I don’t realize that I am That; so, what is to be done to know that I am That?

1st step: Hear, reflect, discuss and know that the final Goal of work and life is to know that ‘I am That’.

2nd step: Reflect, discuss, introspect, get an intellectual conviction in the Goal and the Self-development processes needed and commit to daily practices to build powers of mind to walk-the-talk to the Goal, e.g. prayers, meditation, worship, etc. in accordance with our respective Religions, faiths.

3rd step: Strive to be a karma-yogi – in and thru’ daily life, strive to act unselfishly for good of all. This gradually purifies the mind. And, a pure mind can manifest our potential Godliness and realize the truth of truths – I am That I am.

Spiritual knowledge gives us Strength: Vivekananda says, “Strength, strength is what the Upanishads speak to me from every page. This is the one great thing to remember, it has been the one great lesson I have been taught in my life; strength, it says, strength, O man, be not weak. Are there no human weaknesses? – says man. There are, say the Upanishads, but will more weakness heal them, would you try to wash dirt with dirt? Will sin cure sin, weakness cure weakness? Strength, O man, strength, say the Upanishads, stand up and be strong; ‘Abhih’, “fearless”.
