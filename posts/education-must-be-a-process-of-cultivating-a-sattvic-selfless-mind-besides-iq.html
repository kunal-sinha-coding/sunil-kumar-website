India to Bharat · Essay #040

Education must be a process of cultivating a sattvic, selfless mind, besides IQ

A powerful story in Mahabharat, of a Sannyasi, simple housewife and a butcher, illustrates that whatever may be our status, wealth, vocation – ‘Leader or Laborer’, no duty is ugly or impure, and it is not status, wealth, IQ-Marks-Degrees, but only the consciousness with which we perform our duties, whatever be our occupational duties, that determines its worth!

The housewife did not practice any austerities but did her duties with devotion and dedication; the righteous butcher then explains to the Sannyasi that we all have our respective swadharma, based upon our past karmas and competence. If we discharge our natural duty, renouncing the desire for personal gain and rising above the fleeting happiness and misery coming our way, we will purify ourselves; and doing the prescribed duties and not running away from them, the soul gradually evolves from its present gross consciousness to divine consciousness. The lecture the butcher delivered is known as the Vyadha Gita of the Mahabharat. This message is applicable to Arjuna because he wanted to run away from his warrior dharma, thinking it is painful and miserable; and a lesson to us all, that by doing our occupational duties based on our inherent tendencies, in proper consciousness, we worship the Supreme, and easily attain Perfection!

When our prescribed vocational, occupational duties are in accordance with our inherent nature, it is natural to our personality and in tune with our disposition and tendencies. Hence, we enjoy it, our mind is comfortable, and we work intuitively, just as artistes lose themselves in their art!

If we abandon our duties thinking them to be low, and take up another’s duties unsuitable for our nature, we struggle against the innate inclination of our personality. This was exactly Arjun’s situation. His Kshatriya nature was inclined to military and administrative activities. Events drove him to a situation where it was necessary to participate in a war of righteousness. If he were to shirk from his duty and withdraw from the battlefield to practice austerities in the forest, it would not help him spiritually, for even in the forest, he would not be able to get away from his inherent nature. In all likelihood, he would gather the tribal people in the jungle and become their boss.

The lesson is we must choose our vocation carefully; fulfil our duties; and keep striving to work selflessly for good of all!

2. Without character, imagine the problems inherent in IQ, AI, internet, computers: AI is inherently biased; entirely based on what data has been fed into it! Suppose there are two sides to a story, and you feed only one side of it into your AI system. It will tell you things based on only that side of the story! Things are never so cut-&-dried in this world. Everything is nuanced. ‘To err is human, to forgive is divine, but to really mess up things, you need AI! People now recognize how IQ, freedom of choice, selection by Votes, give rise to chalaaki, not character!

It’s Not at all Easy to change our Character! So, traditional Systems of schooling and growing-up was to get established in the habit of daily self-reflection and self-development practices needed to transform the quality of our respective minds from Tamas to Rajas to Sattva, to awaken viveka-buddhi, discriminative-intellect, and imbibing the values and life skills to build powers of mind to know and then walk-the-talk.

Indian wisdom suggests that far more important than “knowledge, IQ, Degrees, etc.” is the sincerity by which we strive to grow in character from “Tamas (inert, lazy, unrighteous) thru’ Rajas (active, ambitious) to attain a predominantly Sattvic (calm, peaceful, righteous) quality of mind”! Thus, education, governance, parenting, must be a process to inspire us to selflessly fulfil our duties, working for good of others, helped by respective prayers, worship, meditation, etc.

Only a Sattvic mind is more amenable to Righteousness, Commitment to Duties, etc. and is fit to be a teacher and a leader who can inspire society towards a common, universal idea of highest and best goal and purpose of life, a “North”, “Perfection”, “Heaven”, Freedom from all bondages, etc., howsoever we may understand it!

While the Universe doesn’t need the help of any human being to manage it, each human being has to be disciplined, trained, educated so that he may use his freedom of choice wisely to think-act and grow towards good, not bad and selflessness, not selfishness!

Growing-up must give us the intellectual conviction in the power of goodness and must recognize that the Universe is always in perfect Order, Rtm, harmony, where each of us, every moment do and get, exactly what we deserve, ‘heaven or hell; sukh or dukh’ based on whether our thoughts-actions are selfless or selfish!

No work is low or high; no soul is superfluous in God’s creation. His divine plan is for the gradual perfection of all living beings. We all fit into his scheme like tiny cogs in the giant wheel. And he does not expect more from us than the character and competence we’ve so far acquired.

Actions are unavoidable and act we must. Our choice is to act selflessly, simply doing our duties to the best of our abilities; and we must gradually learn to act without the sense of Doer-ship and with the attitude of detachment to fruits of actions; recognizing that any action done as a Doer, binds us with consequences of actions in terms of Sukha/Dukha whereas real bliss, peace is to be desireless and free of bondages of sorrows that are concomitant with joy.
