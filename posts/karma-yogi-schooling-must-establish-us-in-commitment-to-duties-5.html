Karma Yogi – Schooling must establish us in commitment to Duties (5)

Promotion to higher Status Or more Unselfishness & Character? One great danger in our nature is that we never examine ourselves! We think we’re fit for higher status; even if we’re, we must first show that we’ve built the Character, Competence, Commitment to our Vocational, Citizenship, Human Duties. Only if we fulfill duties of our present position will higher duties come to us if at all they’re needed for our growth towards ‘Perfection’.

Less and Less of lower-self : The very idea of organised institutions and society has thus been developed, consciously or unconsciously where, only by practicing self-control and limiting ‘selfishness’, we open the way to growth of ‘unselfishness’ leading to unlimited expansion of mind towards love and feelings of oneness for more and more of Creation!

We know that the greatest work is done only when there is no selfish motive to prompt it. Duty is seldom sweet. It is only when love greases its wheels that it runs smoothly; it is a continuous friction otherwise. When we begin to work earnestly in the world, nature gives us blows right and left and soon enables us to find out our position. Yet it is work through the sense of duty that leads us to work with love, without any idea of duty; when work will become worship – nay, something higher – then will work be done for love sake.

The ideal of work and duty, whether it be in the form of Swadharma, Ethics, Charity, Love, is the same as in every other Religion or Yoga; the object being the diminishing of the lower selfish self, so that the real, higher, unselfish-infinite-Self, may shine forth. Only when we give up frittering away of energies thru selfishness, that the soul can manifest its divine goodness and potential.

When we’re inspired by our respective Religions, parents, teachers, to fulfill out duties, Swadharma, gradually our selfishness gets converted into selflessness; less and less self.

Without love , how could parents do their duties to their children; husbands to their wives; and vice versa? Do we not meet with cases of friction every day in our lives? Duty is sweet only through love, and love shines in freedom alone. Isn’t it slavery to be a slave to desires, anger, jealousies and a hundred other petty things that must occur every day in human life? In all these little roughness’s that we meet with in life, the highest expression of freedom is to forbear. Due to our own irritability, jealousies, tempers, we blame each other; we think we’re asserting our ‘freedom”, whereas it is slavery to our senses.

Mother as Role model of Karma Yogi: The position of the mother is the highest in the world, as it is the one place in which to learn and exercise the greatest unselfishness. The love of God is the only love that is higher than a mother’s love; all others are lower. It is the duty of the mother to think of her children first and then of herself. But, instead of that, if the parents are always thinking of themselves first, the result is that the relation between parents and children becomes the same as that between birds and their offspring which, as soon as they are fledged, do not recognize any parents.

The Purpose of Religions: It is not to fight among each other claiming my way is the only way, but to inspire and show us how-to live-in society being good, following our respective duties, faiths and religions. Our religious paths may be different, but commitment to all our respective duties and working for good of the whole Creation is a must. Then, there are certain universal human values which we all are expected to follow, e.g. love, peace, non-violence, truthfulness, and righteous-virtuous-, ethical-conduct.

Just as the basic purpose of schooling is to educate; of apprenticeship is to provide vocational skills; of work is to produce results; of Religions must be to provide the:

Highest and best Ideal; Show us how to reach the Ideal; Inspire us to struggle and strive towards the Ideal of being good, better, best in and through our respective ways of worship and commitment to all our duties.

Role of Schooling: Once we appreciate the Ideal, we can harmonize our education, vocation and work towards the highest and best Ideal. Thus, Academia and Religions must work together to provide, from a common platform of Schooling:

The “Knowledge” of the highest and best ideal so that we may have conviction in the ideal, Goal of Life; Importance of “Devotion” towards the highest; Various “Self-development practices” like prayers, meditation, worship, rituals, etc. to prepare the mind to walk-the-talk towards the ideal. And,

How to “Work”, i.e. Ethical, Virtuous, Righteous Conduct, and excellence in work and relationships so that in and thru our daily work, vocation, relationship, working and living, we may continue on the critical path towards the Goal of Work and Life.
