India to Bharat · Essay #199

Appreciating what religion is not & what ‘religion’ & ‘being religious’, is:

1. What Religion is Not: Religion is not just belief in my-God, my-Holy scripture, my-worldview, etc. Religion is not just belief in images, symbols, holy words. Religion is not just belief in Rama, Krishna, Allah, etc. Religion is not just Puja, Arati, Rituals, Pilgrimages, clothes we wear, food we eat or not eat, etc. ‘Belief’ is not religion. Each religion advises us into a different set of beliefs, scriptures, holy men, holy symbols and so on. We believe in our respective belief, not based on any experience, but only because we have been told to believe in this and not in that. All these are of course good and helpful supports which can lead to the realization of the truth but are not the truth.

We follow our respective religious tradition, rituals, belief, merely as a routine, to avoid scolding or fearing that not doing it could be bad! Traditions have made all these sacred and holy; they are different pathways but certainly, they are not the truth, the unchanging Reality, which is our common and ultimate goal and purpose of being religious.

2. ‘Being Religious’ means excellence in work, not poor work culture: Excellence in work for good of society and commitment to duties is the first sign that we’re progressing in our religion. Inefficiency, procrastination, poor and shoddy work is a sure sign that we’re far from being a religious person. Inefficiency and inactivity are not at all the sign of being religious. Activity always means resistance. Resist all evils mental and physical. An efficient illiterate worker, cobbler or a sweeper is far more advanced and nearer to the realization of the highest truth than an educated, inefficient, self-centred preacher or leader.

Bharat is certainly all for material prosperity and does admire the inventive genius, enterprise, organization, elevation of the common man of the West. However, Bharat’s vision of the welfare of man is holistic – physical, mental, spiritual. Unfortunately, perhaps in the name of secularism and minority appeasement we have totally discarded and thrown the ‘spiritual’ aspect of man, which inspires us, in and thru’ our daily work and life, to ‘know’ and ‘strive’ to become good and better ‘towards the best’.

Schooling is the best place where the students can discuss, explore, study, and acquire clarity and intellectual conviction on the ultimate and common goal of all mankind, irrespective of their religion, nationality, vocation, caste, high or low status. Students must be inspired to strive always to fulfil their duties to society, organization, community, family self; and, for quality and excellence in work.

3. Our Challenge – Unite the best of Bharat and the West: It is true that India had focused on spirituality and lost to the brute strength and bloodshed of others. However, the west has lost out on spirituality while advancing materially. Our challenge today is to find how to attain material prosperity without harming spiritual life.

Bharat has its own inherited USP which clearly distinguishes it from the rest and like it or not, the focus is the ‘spiritual’. Unfortunately, imitating the rest, we have lost awareness of the spiritual aspect common to all mankind and are focused only on the ‘material’, the external ‘objective’ word which is available to sense-perception. And, therefore schooling must give us back the awareness that only excellence in work and work for good of all, can give us both material prosperity and our ultimate goal of peace and fulfilment.

Without this grand ‘spiritual’ vision of Bharat, all of us, poor or rich, have become totally self-centred and inefficient, instead of being a leader in world affairs as Bharat remained throughout history.

4. Schooling must imbibe in us the essential principle of Religions: Religion, education, work have but one Common Purpose to make us aware of our unlimited potential and how to manifest it in our daily work and life. The wisdom of Bharat asserts that each man is potentially divine, perfect, and our daily work and living itself is our only opportunity to manifest our full potential by working in the spirit of service and worship for good of society. This idea of service of God in man is the essence of Bharatiya culture and Buddha’s teachings.

Therefore, schooling must create unselfish people; it must inspire us to be pure and holy, and help others to be holy also. It must give us the conviction that the gist of all worship is to be pure and to do good to others. It must make us aware that ‘He who sees God in the poor, in the weak, and in the diseased, really worships; otherwise his religion and worship are but preliminary’.

Though the expertise of religion, education, vocation or profession may appear different, the long-term goal, purpose and the higher ideal of all these is and must be the same, i.e. to make us grow and manifest our unlimited potential for success, perfection, peace and happiness, in and thru’ our daily work and life.

Whereas narrow, petty-minded preachers may continue teaching that ‘my-God/Scripture’ is the only truth, it is the responsibility of Schools and Colleges to educate us to understand and appreciate the essentials of all religions – Not rituals and beliefs but ‘Serve man as God; this world is His universal form. He alone is religious who is moved to pity for all creatures and exerts himself for them even at the risk of incurring personal damnation. Be ready to serve God, no, not God, but his children – the poor and downtrodden, the sinful and afflicted.

4.1 Daily Self-development Practices: Schooling must give us the conviction “I can secure my own good only by doing your good. There is no other way, none whatever”. And for being able to do it, education and religion have the responsibility of making us aware of our potential and establish us in the daily, regular habit of time-tested rituals and self-development practices of our respective religions, e.g. prayers, self-introspection, reflection, self-study and contemplation of life and teachings, etc., so as to become aware of the purpose of work and life and build strength and powers of mind, e.g. self-control, will-power, powers of concentration, etc. to ‘know’ and do the ‘right’.

Only thru daily reflection can we imbibe the conviction that even the least work done for others awakens the power within; even thinking the least good of others gradually instils into our heart the strength of a lion. Without this awareness, we remain self-centred foxes!

5. CONCLUSION – We the Academia & the educated have the Responsibility to create systems of education and governance so that we can scatter broadcast the highest spiritual wisdom to the masses, and guide them towards the highest truth! Unfortunately, imitating the west, we the best have become materialistic and secular, weaned away from spirituality!

Because we have abdicated our responsibility, we have got narrow minded preachers who may be very good but who cannot claim from the bottom of their heart, as Vivekananda says: after so much austerity, I have understood this as the real truth – God is present in every being; there is no other God besides that. He who serves living beings serves God indeed. We the best of us call ourselves religious but living in the midst of God, we can’t see God, but seek him in temples, symbols and images. We project Him in the form of an image, while all the time He exists in the temple of our body. We are lunatics and this is the great delusion. This is the end of religion – to worship everything as God – every form His temple.
