India to Bharat · Essay #025

The educated person, Pandit, is ‘One who has Panda’ i.e., knowledge of our Swa-roopa’ – so what is our Swaroopa?

Principle of existence : We know that ‘we all, and this Creation’ Exists. We also know the basic Principle that, “that which exists can never Cease to Exist; and that which doesn’t exist can never come into existence”!

If we are given the knowledge and the opportunity to reflect, we can become aware that we all “exist” and can realize that ‘Existence’ is our ‘real-unchanging Identity,’ i.e., our real, true, unchanging ‘Swa-roopa’, not the ever-changing ‘body-mind, form-nature-name’!

And if we recognize our Swaroopa as ‘Existence’, then we can easily understand that as ‘existence’ we’re all ‘One’ with each other; and so, we will naturally be ‘Selfless’ and good to each other!

If we aren’t made aware of our identity, Swa-roopa, as Existence, but identify with this ever-changing ‘Body-mind’, then we are bound to be Self-centered, as we all are!

Our Fundamental Ignorance and thence our problem is to continue thinking that ‘I am this self-centered-changing-body-mind’ which ‘didn’t exist’; ‘gets born-exists-dies-disappears’.

So, as educated men and particularly as academicians, religious preachers and devotees, we must question, reflect, discuss, study and try to discover ‘my-unique, unchanging, distinct, form-nature’, Swa-roopa, that I am always! And then be able to look at the world with my Selfless, righteous, Swaroopa, not thru’ “selfish-body-mind”

Our traditional wisdom: This is how we’ve analysed our Swa-roopa: We all exist. We are all Conscious beings. We all seek to be fulfilled, be happy, and avoid pain and miseries. So, Existence-Conscious-Bliss itself must be our Swaroopa – both logic and science assert that, that which Exists can never cease to Exist. Ignorance of our Swa-roopa, is the fundamental ignorance of modern societies.

# Being totally ignorant of our Swaroopa, who, why and where I am; I am aware only of me, my-body, ‘Shareera’, and what I ‘want’. I am nor concerned with what it means to “exist” or how to live and work to be in my Swaroopa, “Blissful and Fulfilled”!

# If made aware our Swaroopa, told how to realize our Swaroopa, and inspired to learn and practice the self-development processes etc. then we can gradually become “Aware and Conscious” and seek to Re-gain (purpose of education and religions) our “Blissful and Fulfilled”, state of “Poorna-ananda”.

# We do get a glimpse of Bliss of our Swaroopa, every day, but only in “Deep Sleep”, where I am detached from body-mind and so know nothing of that experience of Bliss when I was ONE with SELF, because my Mind, my instrument of perception, is Not my Swaroopa! We all strive to live and work to Re-gain (Religion) that happiness, that is my Swaroopa!

# As “I” grow up “I” have learnt to believe that this Shareera is me and I am this Shareera body and I wish to be Blissful. At Birth “I” had a “Body”, Shareera’, and a “name” was given to me. So, what is my real Form and Nature? Shareera literally means, that which decays! So, body is designed to die!

The purpose of Parenting, schooling, religions, academia, is to give us during growing up the wisdom of our Swaroopa – the divine Self, Spirit, Kingdom of God within, Atman, Life, which is our eternally Existing, unchanging nature, i.e. Swaroopa.

# Growing up must be a preparation to learn to practice Worship of Mother, Father, Teacher, Atithi (stranger guest) as God and “Work as Worship of God”; Righteousness, selflessness, etc.

# Education and Vocation must be a process to build Discriminative-intellect and determination to walk-the-talk. We must understand the necessity and importance of Viveka-Buddhi to overcome the ignorance that I am this body-mind, and strive to realize our eternal, changeless self.

Schooling must encourage Knowing & Fulfilment of Duties, Kartavya-palan, without attachment to results, focusing on the present actions, learning to maintain a calm, balanced mind, undisturbed by expectations of consequences, good-bad results. The whole focus of education must be to reflect and get convinced of our Oneness in our Swaroopa and striving to realize our Swaroopa and attain perpetual peace and bliss through Righteous, ethical conduct; not on mere knowing!

Once we “know”, then we must take the Sankalpa, firm-resolve, during schooling and getting a degree, and be determined to follow Righteousness! We must Imbibe & Inculcate this Samskara, mindset, of keeping promises, resolving to act thru Viveka-Buddhi, giving up ‘Vikalpa’, wavering ‘yes-no’, of mind!

Schooling mut help and teach us to be Saral, straightforward, follow universal Dharma and our respective Swadharma, learn to be and make Mentor!

‘Practice’ Awareness, being in our Swaroopa; not in body-mind: Practice not identifying with feelings and thoughts flowing in the mind’; just being a witness, observer! Just observing the body-mind show hunger, anger! I am the observer. I am awareness. Just watch. Be aware of hunger, anger!

We can Practice being ‘aware’; practice not reacting; practice transforming reaction into Response–ability (responsibility) … learn to Respond with calmness.

We can practice being aware of anger, just watch anger. Don’t ‘react’ in anger. ‘I am aware of the pain’. Practice being ‘Aware’; ‘I am the host – thoughts etc. are guests! Learn to ‘sit silently’, just witnessing. Practice not judging; not blaming; just be aware. Over time we can learn to be just Aware. Remember our Swaroopa – I am the One, changeless Existence-Consciousness-Bliss, our Real SELF, just “IS”, I am Existence per se. All ‘doing, thinking’ is on surface; background is one Consciousness Existence.
