India to Bharat · Essay #014

Education must shift from mere IQ-knowledge to Stoic ‘reasoning’ to control ‘wants, desires & emotions’

In this intellectual age, besides knowledge as IQ and information, we need the philosophy of stoicism to appreciate the necessity of developing our reasoning capacity.

The word stoic means a mental attitude of indifference or unaffected by emotional experiences of pain or pleasure, fortitude, patience, or resignation. However, Stoicism was a living philosophy followed by the Kings and rulers when the Roman Empire was at its peak, and then it was a surprisingly friendly and compassionate system.

The Roman Emperor Marcus Aurelius 121-180 CE is known as a wise and philosophical king whose reign is the only period in history when the happiness of the people was the sole object of governance. With his death, the formal 400-year history of Stoic philosophy ended and began the fall of the Roman Empire. The reward of stoic virtue is now, in this life, not after death. It is not religion. It is an ancient philosophical system which shows how we may live virtuously, ethically.

The book ‘Meditations of Marcus Aurelius’ tells us that the emperor was severe to himself, indulgent to the imperfections of others and just and beneficent to all mankind. He detested war as a disgrace and calamity of humans, but when war was necessary, he was ready. He helped the poor, opposed slavery, founded philosophical schools, orphanages, and hospitals and was ready to dispose of his assets instead of raising taxes.

He left short personal notes, his reflective Meditations, which are still very popular, read by people from all walks of life to regulate their personal lives in businesses and governance and to learn how to reason like a Stoic, irrespective of our religious affiliations, based on Reason to lead a joyful life!

It deals with the seemingly elusive human quest for happiness; what it truly is, and what we must do to be happy. It deals with concepts of happiness, virtues, vice, body, Mind and how to apply them in our attitudes towards politics, business, environment, social and personal life.

Life, it says, isn’t necessarily lonely, poor, nasty, brutish, or short. These aren’t just matters of fact but are based on our formed mental attitudes and opinions. And since we’ve formed them, we alone have the power and freedom to change our opinion of anything! This is not a denial of the facts per se, but comes from a shift in values, our perspective.

I have always had the freedom of choice to change my opinions! We feel poor because we’ve no money; nasty, lonely when we lose our health or loved ones, to tragedy. But these feelings of poverty, loneliness, grief rest on the beliefs, opinions, thinking that money, health, relationships are important Values that give life its meaning.

He questions: What would happen if we realised that the values that give meaning to life weren’t these? What would it be like for us if the things that really give meaning were not these things, but that which we could never lose, and if somehow, we came to know of it with certainty?

The Stoicism of Marcus is still relevant today because it is deeply rooted in friendly rational discussions, dialogue, and arguments.

It is not dependent on any god, religious faith, authority or divine revelation but instead is founded on a method that invites each of us to discover and to explore nature on our own terms to discover the happiness and freedom we all desire and deserve. Stoicism ideas for Reflection:

Consider that everything is opinion, and opinion is in our power to change. To change or to persist is our freedom of choice.

Retire into yourself (a voyage into the mind – the locus of rationality. Reason is what makes us fully human – humane). The rational principle that rules our lives is content with itself when it does what is just and so secures tranquillity.

(In a single world community of rational individual souls (which is the seat of human reason) sharing a common world Soul (Logos), any sort of unjust activity by us becomes an irrational assault on every member of the human race. Really, it is an act of war against the unity of the whole, because we’re united together thru reason.)

Our duty is to live in conformity with the 3-ruling principle, native intelligence that lies at the heart of human capacity to Reason. They’re:

We’re social beings (interconnected and interdependent)

We must never yield to the demands of the body

The mind is where our true nature lies, i.e. Logos, the common universal soul. This Duty is common to all because it comes from a universal shared intelligence, at the heart of the natural world, the universe.

This is the tranquillity and serenity derived from cooperative peace.

Common people seek retreats in their houses, seashores and hills for themselves. But we’ve the power to choose to retire deep into ourselves, for our soul is the quietest and is free from all troubles of the mind; tranquillity is nothing else than the good ordering of the mind. A person is just when the reasoning part of the mind takes precedence over emotions and desires – our emotions and physical desires should always be mediated by reason.

We must then give to ourselves this retreat to our soul, to renew ourselves, remembering the 3 principles. And we will return free from all discontent with the things to which we return.

What is good? The ruling principle that directs human nature comes to us directly from the ruling intelligence of nature responsible for the capacity of reason and logic because it comes from the same 3-principle, which are flawless.
