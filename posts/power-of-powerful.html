India to Bharat · Essay #214

Power of powerful

1. Our National motto categorically asserts: Truthfulness alone triumphs, and never untruthfulness. Following truth, we remain on the critical path to mankind’s highest goal of Perfection – Mundaka Upanishad. Our Constitution says “India that is Bharat”, and yet the history and culture of Bharat doesn’t form an integral part of modern India and its schooling!

2. We’ve fallen into the trap of Negationism! What is this new word? It means the denial of known facts, not just a reinterpretation. It has evolved from the movement to deny the genocide; omit the holocaust from the history of Germany. And, we know that “History” is nothing but the version of the ‘story’ of facts, interpreted by successive Victors or parties in power and therefore we’ve Negationism or Revisionist, by those who come to power later. Men, even Supreme Court Judges, will always have their prejudices and History is written by men. We’ve grown up knowing history as written by our conquerors the Moghuls, Britishers and those in power since independence.

3. “Strive for the highest thru’ righteous conduct” is the essence of our foundational history as depicted in the Upanishads. Our culture enjoins on us to strive for the highest perfection, following ethical conduct in and thru’ our daily ‘earning’ and ‘enjoying’. Its profound ideas, focusing on inspiring us to strive for the highest have been elaborated thru’ histories in our historical epics Ramayana and Mahabharat, as recognized in the original copies of our Constitution of India. Unfortunately, as Indian citizens, those in power have denied to us our foundational Vedantic culture, in our schooling. The result is we grow up as self-centred divisive society in a state of perpetual purposelessness.

4. Why are we discussing Negationism? Look at we the modern educated Indians. We put Bapu on a pedestal as Father of Nation. He was the very epitome of Bharatiya culture, after discarding his suited-booted Indian and lawyer persona. He was a man of God, with Ram-naam ever on his lips. He openly admitted his Hindu faith and prayed openly and believed passionately in Hinduism’s ideal of Ishwar-Allah tero-naam and did his very best to unite us in One God. He was not ashamed of being a Hindu. He wasn’t a secularist who kept religion away from politics or his daily work and living. He wasn’t an atheist nor leftist, nor someone who in the name of science looks down upon Hinduism, as was the fashion then, but none follow him today!

5. I ndia was becoming an irreligious nation, lamented Bapu. He is our role model of the idea that being religious, irrespective of any religion, means not just believing in some God or scripture or belonging to a religion, but in practicing righteous conduct in our daily life. The question is: Is your daily behaviour based on the principles of ahimsa, satya, tyaga, seva, love, which are the universal principles of righteous conduct according to any religion?

6. We’re talking of Negations’, because, since independence, we’ve failed to make this message of oneness, of unity of all religions, a part of our schooling and governance. We prohibit teaching of the ancient wisdom and worldview of ‘oneness’ as summarized in the Gita and works of Vivekananda, while at the same time we’re allowing minorities to teach their religion in their schools. Modern secular Hindus believe more in atheism and science and look down upon Hinduism as mere superstition, cow worship, puja of stones, trees, many gods and goddesses.

7. Like the blind leading the blind , the unfortunate repercussion is that today’s leadership is either ignorant or uncommitted to our ideal of “satyameva Jayate” and therefore, we’ve failed to ensure that only those who follow this ideal should be our teachers, political leaders, administrators, businessmen and workforce!

8. Points for Reflection: Fearlessness, truthfulness, commitment to duties, are demanded of everyone but more so from those who are educated and govern us. Who is responsible for creating a society where truth takes a back seat? Fearing what have been made totally ignorant of our own wisdom?
