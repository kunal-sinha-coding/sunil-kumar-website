India to Bharat · Essay #141

Bharat has clarity on good-better-Best: India is still confused, clueless!

What is good? We all believe that it is good to be good and we do try to be good, better, best, in our own way; but what is good? We’ve differing ideas on what ‘good’ or ‘best’ mean. Similarly, do more IQ, money, power, status, vocation, make us better? Higher? How do we decide? Or, who can really decide? Selfish, prejudiced people like us who have a very short-term vision? Scientists? Political animals?

Modern ideas and systems are being created by those well-meaning people who have insufficient clarity on the highest and best goal, i.e. the very purpose of work and life! Thus, our ideas of right or wrong; good or bad; higher or lower; etc. remain vague and undefined without the benchmark of a common ‘North’. Consequently, we’re not sincerely committed to being ethical; and, character is no longer a quality for promotion, selection; and, who will judge and how?

Bharat has a clear vision of ‘North’ and how to get there: Our common ‘North’, the highest Goal of all mankind, is Freedom, Mukti, aka Perfection, God-realization, Salvation, Heaven, etc. And, from a young age we have to get established in self-development processes, to awaken Viveka and build powers of mind to know and be committed to our respective duties, Swadharma. Then earn and enjoy thru’ dharma/ethics so as to remain on the path of good, better, Best.

Freedom is the ultimate goal to be attained; it is the end result of discipline, self-control, struggle to do the right; whereas western society who had a culture of slavery, now foolishly talks of freedom as a right and, even a child is free to indulge in its whims and fancies!

Bharat had clarity that society can be best led by those who are educated and inspired to consciously, deliberately, intelligently, strive and struggle to “ walk towards North ” following ethical and righteous conduct. Those who do so are in ‘Sur’, i.e. in tune or harmony with Creation.

Only those who have been established in goodness and are sincerely striving to remain committed to their respective Swadharma, righteous conduct, etc. can be good as rulers, administrators, professionals, result-producers,

Bharat believes that those who are self-centered and hungry for money and power, are the “Asur”, not yet ready to be in leadership positions. They need guidance, control, discipline to remain ethical; not freedom to do as they wish.

Bharat respected Spiritually mature people as role models and Mentors of society and, had created societal systems where people engaged in vocations in accordance with their character and competence. Simple living, spirit of tyaga, seva, karma-yoga were our ideals.

Ideas of India, in contrast: Today, Asuric ideals like comfort, money, power, ‘freedom to choose and do what I want to do’, are the new norms and civilizational status symbols. India foolishly believes, aping the West, that educated people like us and political leaders, whom we elect or the administrators whom we select based on IQ, are totally impartial, fair, unprejudiced rulers who will selflessly work and look after our interests!

In spite of all our sciences and education our best men, the politicians and preachers, divide people into this and that political parties and religions, and cannot agree on a program for developing the nation. Individually and as a nation we’ve still not arrived at a common idea of good and how to be good! And, unless we first fix the ‘Best’ how can we ever grade ‘good or better’ or even, ‘worst, worse, bad, so-so’?

So, education must engage us in knowing, discussing, debating various ideas to arrive at National and mankind’s values and ideals on good, best, higher, lower. Today we leave it to religions and brush ‘goodness’ under the carpet.

God as the Best-goal: S pirituality is the very USP of India that is Bharat! God is a commonly accepted idea of the highest and best. We may or may not believe in God and may have differing ideas of God, but we can perhaps define ‘good’, dharma, etc. as the optimum path and ways of living and working which takes us towards our respective ideas of God. And the role of religions is to show and inspire us to acquire the ability to walk to God. Today we’ve many Religions and ideas of God. M any of us and Religions consider God as separate from us and worship him. Some consider that we’re an inseparable part of God.

All religions believe that God is all powerful. If it is true that God is all powerful, and most of us believe in it, then the logical conclusions has to be that God has to be Omnipotent, Omnipresent, Omniscient. If God is Omnipresent then Existence itself is God. All that exists in Creation has to be God alone. God somehow is all this creation. Nothing else but God exists! So, whether we believe in this or that God or no God, if God is Omnipresent, it follows that we’re all in God; we’re all nothing but God! This is what has been realized, experienced, discovered by a countless of seers and sages and is expressed in the Upanishads. And, Bharatiya culture holds that all religions sincerely practiced lead to one God.

Immature Religious preachers and Politicians divide not unite us: Perhaps, it is now up to the mature followers of different religions in academia to look at the common ideas of good, best, of God, etc. from all religious and put it in an academic, rational way, free from ideology so that we’re all inspired to build powers of mind, discrimination, self-control, etc. to remain on the path of good, better, best.
