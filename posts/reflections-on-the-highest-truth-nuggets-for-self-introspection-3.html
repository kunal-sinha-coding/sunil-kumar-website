India to Bharat · Essay #174

Reflections on the highest truth? Nuggets for self-introspection – 3

1. Exploring the idea of truth of Truths: All religions and faiths claim to show us and guide us towards an abstract idea of the ‘highest Truth’, whatever it may be. Many of us call this truth of Truths simply as God, without any idea of what actually it means! Many of us refuse to accept this idea of God. They also have no idea of what is it that they’re denying! Unfortunately, we ignore the wisdom of Rishis and Sages but readily accept scientists as our modern preachers and Gods. But, saints and mystics, preachers and devotees of all religions and faiths, never give up; they’re always bursting to tell us about this unknown, maybe unknowable Truth! Let us explore!

2. What was there before this creation? Here’s a profound poetic Vedic reflection: “There was neither non-existence nor existence then. There was neither the realm of space nor the sky which is beyond. What stirred? Where? In whose protection? There was neither death nor immortality then. There was no distinguishing sign of night nor of day. That One breathed, windless, by its own impulse. Other than that, there was nothing beyond. Darkness was hidden by darkness in the beginning, with no distinguishing sign. Poets seeking within their heart with wisdom, found the bond of existence and non-existence. One arose through the power of heat. Desire came upon that One in the beginning, that was the first seed of mind. Who really knows?

Who will here proclaim it? Whence was it produced? Whence is this creation? The gods came afterwards, with the creation of this universe. Who then knows whence it has arisen? Whence this creation has arisen – perhaps it formed itself, or perhaps it did not – the One who looks down on it, in the highest heaven, only He knows or perhaps even He does not know.”

3. Because all creation come from the unknown God, He alone is our ultimate nourisher and supporter. Whatever I am and do is possible only and only if and as long as ‘Life’, Consciousness, this “I” exists. So, what is this “I”? This “I” is Existence itself! To illustrate, look at the sea and the infinite waves that arise, disappear, arise, and so on. Waves can say “I am wave A”, “I am wave B’’, and so on. The Truth is that each “I am” is nothing but Sea, sea water. Similarly, “I” is same everywhere our names, forms, are nothing at all different from existence, from sea. I am the Sea, though now I think I am a wave. Imagine sea as Consciousness, as God. So, we’re Existence as Consciousness! But now my-ego-mind makes me think “I am – so and so”. Thus, “I” remains as an Existence, witness to all our thoughts-actions. He is our ultimate abode; our shelter and our dearest friend. What the ocean is to the waves, God is to the entire creation. So, He alone is our origin, our dissolution, our very foundation, the treasure-house for enjoyment and the eternal Seed. God thus being the ultimate cause of creation, the whole creation is an effect and as such is never different or separate from God; but God is unaffected by the effect, i.e. we the creation, waves, bound by our ego!

4. Truth for Reflection: Therefore, sages say, whatever you do, whatever you eat, whatever you offer as a sacrifice (for good of others), whatever you gift away, whatever austerities you practice, do it as an offering for the glory of God. When we think we act for me and mine, I limit myself to this limited wave. Each time we act unselfishly, follow the ideal of self-denial and don’t pander to our ego, we move towards feeling and experiencing our oneness with the whole creation, aka Mr. God. Constantly keeping our mind on this idea, is a great help towards peace, happiness and prosperity for all.
