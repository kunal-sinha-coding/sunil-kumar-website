Knowing our mind and managing our thoughts (2)

People are not equal – Why? Differences in us are based on the constitution and quality of our mind that we’re continuously either refining or polluting by the impressions that are being left on it by the quality of our own thoughts-words-actions. Based on the predominant quality of our impressions we can be broadly classified into three: Sattvic; Rajasic; Tamasic, i.e. Calm/peaceful or Active or Dull. All of us are a mixture of these 3-qualities and at different times we exhibit these qualities in varying proportions.

For all mankind the purpose of education, working and living is to gradually rise to the predominant state of Sattvic, which alone can propel us towards our highest Goal; happiness and fulfilment that we strive for. It is up to each one of us to rewrite the constitution of our mind by continuous practice and focus on the ‘higher’ and learning to be aware of and managing the flow of thoughts in the mind. This is the plan of creation for all beings irrespective of who, what, where are we from.

Power of Thought: If matter is powerful, thought is omnipotent. Bring this thought to bear upon your life, fill yourselves with the thought of your almightiness, your majesty and your glory. Doing is very good, but that comes from thinking…. Fill the brain, therefore, with high thoughts, highest ideals; place them day and night before you; and out of that will come great work -Vivekananda .

We are what our thoughts are; On the quality of our thoughts, depends the quality of our life. Sow a thought, reap an action. Sow an action, reap a habit. Sow a habit, reap a character. Sow a character, reap a destiny.

The mind is built to look outside through the five sense organs. By its very nature it is restless like a monkey – Drunk with ‘Desires’; stung by scorpion of ‘Jealousy’; and also possessed by devil of ‘Pride’. Therefore, very difficult to control! The more the control we’ve, the greater as a person we’re, whoever and whatsoever we maybe.

Though I am the creator and recorder of my ‘thoughts-feelings’, I am only their ‘knower’, totally separate and independent; and so, I have the freedom to choose an appropriate response. And, I don’t have to be their slaves; I can learn to choose ‘rightly’, by practicing becoming aware of thoughts as they arise; and by learning to manage our thoughts.

Thought management: We’ve to develop the habit of being aware of the flow of the thought in our mind and learn to pause before response – responsi-bilty! Flow of thoughts have three aspects – Quantity, Quality and Direction.

Quantity: Higher the frequency and number of thoughts, the more is the mind disturbed. Thoughts are a load on the mind. We don’t have to carry the load of unwanted thoughts by learning how to stop creating unwanted thoughts. Following a time-table and keeping busy with planned activities is the best method for keeping the quantity of thought under check.

Quality: Pour good thoughts to weed out unwanted thought. Holy company, holy books, prayers, worship, excellence in work, fulfilling our duties – swadharma, unselfishness, working as a Karma-yogi, etc. etc. are the best method for improving the quality. As we think so we become; therefore, we’ve to be very careful of the thoughts we entertain, the company we keep and the books we read.

Direction: Mind is one track in nature. It is very difficult to change the direction of thoughts. By entertaining the opposite thoughts, we can change the track. When angry or hateful, think of love and goodwill, think of the divinity. Continuous exposure to good thoughts gives a new direction to thought-flow. Discipline is a tool for mastering the mind. Once we resolve to be disciplined we start fighting the unwanted thoughts. Learn not to be a slave to your thoughts – be the Master.

Fixing a routine, setting-up a time-table, planning short-term and long-term goals, adhering to your routine, etc. are time-tested ideas for practice. It may be difficult in the beginning but we’ve to keep on trying; never mind failures. Gradually we learn to control our mind. Discipline forces the mind to become our slave.

Best to keep direction towards the Best: Bharatiya culture insists that we must always try to fix our short and long-term goals in a way that keeps us Godward, i.e. our life remains directed towards the highest and the best, i.e. manifesting the infinite potential, divinity, perfection within.

And so, schooling must establish us in Swadharma, personal ethics, for only then are we equipped to strive for artha and kaama , wealth and pleasures, thru’ righteous conduct. Modern society, without the benchmark and inspiration of the ‘best’ during schooling, remains in a state of ‘perpetual purposelessness’, involved in scientific and technological wonders of matter, entertainment and materialistic comforts!

What is freedom? Doing what we, i.e. our mind, wants to do is slavery to our past tendencies; it isn’t freedom. Freedom is in not having to do, what the mind wants to do! The more freedom we give to our mind, the more difficult will it get to control. Unwanted thoughts are our real unseen enemies. Constant practice and detachment from unwanted thoughts is the only method for preparing the mind for higher direction and success in any field.

Mind wants enjoyment; we want success. Mind will not give up easily. Only repeated thoughts, repeated habits can overcome past tendencies and impressions. There is no short cut.
