India to Bharat · Essay #223

We Indians are funny people! Why don’t we place the highest truth before our very own people?

1.Our ancestors, our own forefathers, have discovered some of the highest truths of mankind . Why are we as academia and leaders, afraid of placing it before our people?

2. Our sages posit, and present the worldview of ‘oneness’ ; of ‘ the whole world as one family’ ; of realization of this ‘oneness’ as the very purpose of life, work, education! This ‘oneness’ is also apparent from the universal ideal of “love one another”; from “latest scientific discoveries” and the very term ‘universe’, meaning ‘one united-verse’. Our seers address all mankind. They inspire all to strive to know and grow towards love and compassion for all creation, following their own vocation and way of worship, at their own pace.

3. The very creation and its living-beings are obviously a highly complex, conscious, and ‘intelligent design’. All this infinite variety just couldn’t appear and happen all by chance! So, from the very beginning of creation, and concomitantly with creation itself, all the very first living-beings and men, needed to know millions of things, including a ‘religion’ to guide and inspire them to be good! How else would they live, communicate, create languages, survive, know that it is good to be good? And, the ancient seers and sages were the mentors and guides of all mankind, before modern organized ‘separatist’ religions and civilizations as we know of them now, arrived.

4. Today, it must be our duty and responsibility as the srestha , i.e. the educated elders, teachers and academia, to become aware of the highest truth left to us by our forefathers. Only then can we compare it in our own mind with what we already have within us and, with what modern science and knowledge is giving us.

5. We may well fall below the highest ideals left to us by our Rishis, Prophets, Saints and Sages! Yes, we all do have a multitude of defects, but the sages tell us that if there are defects in us if we make mistakes, know that it is not from your real nature that they arise. They come from your previous thought-action, for want of higher ideals. In your own pace and way, you are all growing not from ‘untruth to truth’, but from ‘lower truth to higher truth’.

6. So, the sages console us saying don’t you worry, but first do have an idea! If a man with an idea makes a thousand mistakes, know for certain that the man without an ideal will make fifty thousand. So, go ahead and place before yourself the highest and best ideal known to mankind, our ‘oneness’ with creation. For it is better to have an idea, and the higher the better. And this ideal we must hear about as much as we can, reflect upon it, think of it till it enters into our hearts, into our brains, into our very veins, until it tingles in every drop of our blood and permeates every pore in our body.

7. Unfortunately, due to criticisms heaped upon us by our conquerors, who believed in their ‘separatist’ ‘one prophet-one-scripture’ religions, we the educated in governance, have no faith in the wisdom of our own forefathers due to the 3R’s education given us by the British. Funnily, we allow the conquerors to teach their ‘separatist’ belief but are afraid to even acknowledge our own worldview of ‘oneness’; of ‘the whole world as our family’ as written on the gates of our own Parliament. It was also proclaimed by Vivekananda at Chicago in 1893, who is now our National Youth Icon, declared by Indiraji and Rajiv Gandhi of the previous political party, acknowledged by Gandhiji, and also by the present political ruling party!

8. And yet, as governance we’re still afraid to teach this profound wisdom that binds the whole creation in love. Why are we still afraid?

9. We tell the students, ‘You must be literate; get high marks; get a degree – and that becomes their only goal; they are even ready to cheat and adopt wrong means to fulfil that temporary and short-term goal. Can we blame them? Do we evaluate people on the basis of marks and degrees or character?

10. Have we shown them how to develop powers of mind, fearlessness, and strength? Have we established them in daily practices to improve their minds and work for good of society? Drilled into them the importance of character, the importance of doing the right action?

Are we the Srestha, good role models?
