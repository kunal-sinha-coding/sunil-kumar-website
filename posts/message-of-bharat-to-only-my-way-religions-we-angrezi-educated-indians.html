India to Bharat · Essay #163

Message of Bharat to ‘only-my-way’ religions & we angrezi-educated Indians

1. Who is Responsible & Accountable to Nurture the Good? Are modern Religions and Education really useful?

Hasn’t modern schooling failed to focus on character and nation-building?

Haven’t religions failed to work with academia to produce good and useful people by inspiring and establishing them in daily self-development practices according to their respective faiths and life skills?

Haven’t they together failed to produce enlightened citizens and men of character who love the nation and have the selflessness to fulfill their swadharma and work together for good of all people?

Haven’t they together created self-centred citizens, more interested in their narrow worldview of ‘Separateness’, of ‘me and mine’, of ‘my-god, my-religion’ vs ‘yours’?

Haven’t religious preachers and teachers, who’re responsible to produce the good, failed miserably to create spiritually mature followers who have love and concern for all equally?

If they can’t unite and work together, are they really useful in the modern era of science and reasoning?

Shouldn’t all minority and mainstream schooling be taught compulsorily the worldview of ‘Oneness’ which can lead to all religions uniting to teach: ‘may all beings be happy, peaceful, blissful’; ‘love thy neighbour as thy own self’; ‘seek you first the kingdom of god within’; ‘be established in righteous conduct’; ‘work is worship of god in man and creation’ , etc. ?

Is the policy to allow minorities to teach their idea that ‘my-religion’; ‘my-god’ alone is true while not teaching our composite culture of ‘oneness of all’, in both minority and mainstream schooling, leading to communal riots, fighting, etc.?

2. The Panacea:

Shouldn’t we make religious preachers and their religious heads along with teachers and administrators of all schools and colleges, learn to unite and work together to produce the good during schools and colleges?

Shouldn’t we make them directly responsible and accountable for teaching the wisdom and composite culture of India and creating enlightened citizens?

Shouldn’t we make both academia and religions responsible to create citizens committed to their swadharma; and, guiding them in appropriate vocation, in accordance with their character and competence, and then allowing only those who are sincerely striving to serve society in and thru’ their vocation, for leadership positions, in society.

Shouldn’t they now focus on improving the quality instead of quantity? Shouldn’t they spread the message that it cannot be God’s idea to kill others because ‘our religion is in danger’ or to protect ‘my God; my religion’? God cannot provoke its naïve followers to kill and riot?

Shouldn’t academia and religions now realize that the fruit of religion is the ability to have love, care, concern, equally for all?

Isn’t this the USP of Bharat, though India with its modern schooling may have forgotten it?

If a religion criticizes other religions; or, if a narrow-minded religious preacher teaches hatred for others; it is nothing but fanaticism; not religion. For no real saint, Prophet or Master, can rise by criticizing, hating, killing others!

Don’t we see how modern one-god, one-master, one-scripture Religions have divided people into ‘my-god vs your god’, creating unnecessary conflicts, crusades, persecutions? How can academia now help?

3. All religions must now focus on Imbibing Values: NEP 20 has now, for the first time, specifically included five-universal values of all religions, e.g. truth, peace, non-violence, righteous conduct, which all students have to strive to imbibe. It is time for religious preachers to offer to work together with academia, to show them how during 12-15 years of studies we can inspire students to imbibe these values and how we may select only those who have the character to work for good of society for leadership positions in politics and governance, especially for becoming teachers, professionals, businessmen.

4. Our composite culture? Aurobindo: That which we call the Hindu religion is really the eternal religion, because it is the universal religion, which embraces all others. If a religion is not universal, it cannot be eternal. A narrow religion, a sectarian religion, an exclusive religion can live only for a limited time and a limited purpose. This is the one religion that can triumph over materialism by including and anticipating the discoveries of science and the speculations of philosophy.

It is the one religion, which impresses on mankind the closeness of God to us and embraces in its compass all the possible means by which man can approach God. It is the one religion, which insists every moment on the truth which all religions acknowledge that He is in all men and all things and that in Him we move and have our being. It is the one religion, which enables us not only to understand and believe this truth but, to realize it with every part of our being. Other religions are preponderating religions of faith, but the Sanatana dharma is life itself; it is a thing that has not so much to be believed as lived.

4.1 Gandhiji: I have ventured to tell English and American missionaries that if they could have refrained from ‘telling’ India about Christ and had merely lived the life enjoined upon them by the Sermon on the Mount, India instead of suspecting them would have appreciated their living in the midst of her children and directly profited by their presence… Faith does not admit of telling. It has to be lived and then it becomes self-propagating. He says: On examination, I have found Hinduism to be the most tolerant of all religions known to me. Its freedom from dogma makes a forcible appeal to me inasmuch as it gives the votary the largest scope for self-expression. Not being an exclusive religion, it enables the followers not merely to respect all the other religions, but it also enables them to admire and assimilate whatever may be good in the other faiths.

5. It is now for angrezi-elite Indians to understand and include character building in education!
