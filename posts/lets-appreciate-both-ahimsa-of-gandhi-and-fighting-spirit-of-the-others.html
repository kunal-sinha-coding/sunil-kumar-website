India to Bharat · Essay #117

Let’s appreciate both Ahimsa of Gandhi and fighting spirit of the ‘others’

As educated apolitical group we are not here to take sides but should appreciate both the ideology of ‘Ahimsa’ as embodied by Gandhi and the necessity of ‘fighting for our rights’ as embodied by many others like Madan Lal Dhingra, Lala Har Dayal, Subhash Bose, Vinayak Savarkar, Bhagat, et al. Surprisingly, even historians have not emphasized the contribution of these young Indians, many of whom stayed at India House, London, in the early 1900’s, where Vinayak, a born leader, also landed and soon, became a leader of this motley group, while he was just around 24-26 years old!

We’ve so far been over-selling the ahimsa, aspect of the freedom movement but have certainly totally ignored, deliberately, the contributions of those who also loved India that is Bharat, and have certainly sacrificed and suffered equally, if not more!

And, Vinayak is a perfect example of those who passionately loved India that is Bharat, from a very young age, and sacrificed his life for the good of the nation, but for political reasons has been vilified for the last so many years.

Now that society is becoming more and more polarized, it is good to know who is Mr. Vinayak and of his passionate, selfless, love for India that is Bharat and his spirit of ‘tyaga’ and ‘seva’, which are the twin ideals of Bharat. But alas! They’re not the ideals of India of today! Nor are they the ideals of the present political animals who vilify, malign, degenerate, belittle him and the contribution of those like him.

An incident, in the historical background of London and Europe during early 1900, gives an idea of who Vinayak, born in 1883, was. From the very school days, he fell in love for Bharat and led his school friends in the protests for freedom movements led by Tilak, Gokhale, Aurobindo.

He also became an inspiring speaker and with his intelligence and voracious love for reading and learning started writing articles in newspapers and speaking to even adult groups. It was this that got him a scholarship, and he came to India House, London.

He also researched the “Mutiny of 1857” and declared it as the first War of independence, to counter the British tale of Mutiny of 1857. He also read of Mazzini and his revolutionary ideas for Italy, of his sacrifice and devotion.

Madan Lal Dhingra was a jovial fun-loving youth staying at India House, whose father and two brothers were Doctors in Amritsar. Listening to Vinayak, he got so much inspired to help free India, that he openly shot the PS to the Secy. of State for India, a Britisher, in London, and so was hanged. He made a statement in the Court, extracts of which were widely reported in the press:

“If it is patriotic for an Englishman to fight against the Germans if they were to occupy this country, it is much more justifiable and patriotic in my case to fight against the English, responsible for the murder of 800 million Indians in last 50 years and taking away 100 million Pounds every year from India to this country.

Just as the Germans have no right to occupy this country, so the English have no right to occupy India, and it is perfectly justifiable on our part to kill the English polluting our sacred land. I am surprised at this hypocrisy, the farce from you who pose as champions of the oppressed… You can pass death sentence…”. He was hanged in front of large crowds, but hardly any Indians!

Winston Churchill exclaimed, Dhingra’s last words are the finest ever made in the name of patriotism. Most of the then servile Indians including his family boycotted him, of course! In Jail, Vinayak met Dhingra with tears streaming down both their cheeks. Before the hanging, Vinayak told Madan, I have come to have darsan of a great patriot and martyr, and Madan touched his feet with tears of joy and gratitude.

Vinayak then fought with the authorities to get Dhingra’s voice published so that he did not go down in history as a violent and misguided lunatic that the Indian community and his own family were portraying him as. Daily News then published his statement which Police had confiscated, where he said:

“A nation held in bondage with bayonets is in a perpetual state of war. Since open battle is rendered impossible, I attacked by surprise…As a Hindu, I feel that a wrong done to my country is an insult to God. This war of Independence will continue…The only lesson is to learn how to die and the way to teach it is by dying ourselves.” Should we continue to vilify those who loved India and were ready to sacrifice themselves for what they believed in?

It was only in 1976 that Dhingra’s mortal remains were repatriated to Amritsar and a memorial built for him, by the govt. led by Indira Gandhi.
