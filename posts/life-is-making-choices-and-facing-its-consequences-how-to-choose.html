India to Bharat · Essay #050

Life is making choices and facing its consequences – how to choose?

Every moment all living beings make choices; however only human beings have the widest freedom of choice. All other beings generally react to situations unconsciously by force of past habit, needs, circumstances. Usually, we make choices based on our needs, desires, goals, etc. Choices are innumerable! They’re between – short-term vs long-term; individual vs family; family vs society; self vs others; etc. etc. Each one of us, in our own way and time, and thru’ our individual desires and goals, good and bad, are invariably making choices and moving towards something or other!

Awakening Viveka : Since we’ve the freedom of choice we become responsible for the consequences of our choices. It is therefore absolutely necessary that we first learn to choose wisely and then build the will-power to act on them. And, this is not an easy but an extremely difficult task; and this is the real purpose of all education, work and living!

Responsibility really implies the ability-to-respond , not just react. This ability to pause, reflect on the consequences of our choices, and then use our free will to respond consciously and responsibly to make ‘right’ choices is a life-skill that has to be learnt and practiced from childhood; and, throughout schooling and college. Bharat holds that only those who have this Response-ability and Viveka-buddhi, powers of discrimination, are really the educated who can be role models, teachers, leaders of society.

Only with proper education and self-reflection, can we learn to awaken our Viveka-buddhi, and the very first step is to study, explore, discuss, reflect upon and fix our highest and best goal, the benchmark which alone can help us make right long term good choices. Without first knowing the highest how can we really judge any action!

Bharat’s ideal of Schooling was to inspire us towards ‘Right’ choices and Righteous action: The main purpose of parenting and schooling was to get conviction and strive to develop powers of mind to walk-the-talk towards our common universal goal, the goal of all mankind, the highest Truth, aka Perfection. All our systems including the much-maligned idea of Varna or caste were designed to lead us towards the highest, doing our respective duties, Swadharma. Our ideal of schooling was to get established in ethical, righteous conduct during growing up so that we can strive to attain ‘Perfection’, in and thru’ ‘earning’ and ‘enjoying’.

The purpose of Vidya is not knowledge for sake of knowledge, exams and degrees like today; but to prepare us for righteous action, whatever be our status or vocation; and, it is our duty as human beings to learn to choose and act rightly, to be a Shrestha, the good, and attain Perfection.

Thus, the basic choice, according to Indian insights, is between that which takes us towards or away from Perfection. The first step in resolving dilemmas, conflicts, is to understand and keep in view the highest and best goal, for only then we have a benchmark of higher and better to struggle towards.

Indian philosophy is termed darsan , experiencing: The idea is not just to talk and philosophize but to do whatever is necessary to experience the highest Truth. Similarly, Religion is not just to believe and have faith in some ideology, god, philosophy or scriptures, but to attain Truth.

Indian psychology is focused on chitta-suddhi , i.e., how to improve quality of our mindset, our worldview and quality of our thoughts, desires, goals, and direct them towards that Truth.

All our Epics, our idea of arts, music, theatre, philosophy, psychology, education, religion, vocation, etc. are not just for enjoyment or separate theoretical subjects for mere study; they’re all designed to take us on the critical path of ethics, kartavya-palan, Swadharma, human values, righteous conduct, towards Truth.

Our Epics show the ‘right’ choices when faced with Dilemmas between :

i) Things we equally like: Arjuna chose to fight for justice, dharma, even against his own family and elders.

ii) Things we equally dislike: Yudhishtir chose to tell an untruth to Dronacharya for sake of the higher truth, to save dharma from adharmic forces.

iii) A thing we like and a thing which will hurt me: Rama had the choice of letting go of his beloved Sita or following his Raja-dharma.

iv) Two things both of which will hurt me: Nachiketa had the choice of annoying his father or letting him incur sin.

Viveka-choices: He who protects dharma is protected by dharma. For a higher cause we may have to sacrifice and accept the consequences for the greater good. A man in authority must be ready to forsake even his dearest for the sake of his duty, responsibility, and larger interest of society. If one finds faults with his elders, he does have to suffer their wrath, however, if our intentions are sincere and advice right, one certainly gains in the end.

Viveka is defined as the ability to choose that which takes us towards Perfection. We’ve to learn to choose higher knowledge and long-term good over pleasures; that unselfishness is always more paying. Thus, schooling, parenting, society have the obligation to help us build strength and powers of mind to be committed to ethical, righteous conduct; to be a Karma yogi. All this is true both for individuals and organizations.
