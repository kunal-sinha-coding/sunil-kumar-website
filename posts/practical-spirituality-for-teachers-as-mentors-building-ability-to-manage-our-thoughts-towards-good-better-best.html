India to Bharat · Essay #017

Practical spirituality for teachers as mentors – Building ability to manage our thoughts towards ‘Good-better-Best’

Teachers, parents – elders and bosses can only teach, Inculcate and Inspire! However, without daily reflections and practice, Praxis, Abhyasa Yoga we may just ‘believe’ but won’t be intellectually convinced to strive to build the mental ability’ to ‘Know’ and ‘Walk-the-talk’ to really imbibe, assimilate and change our habits and values towards the higher dimensions of work and life.

Considering that good habits and values are best formed when young, society needs elders and teachers of righteous conduct, acharya, role models, the shrestha who can inspire us all TO BE & DO GOOD. Teachers must therefore be educated and trained to be & make mentors.

Praxis – Practice : We need to get established in self-reflection and practice, ie Sadhna Sampatti, ie ‘wealth of human values’; equivalent ideas are Abhyasa Yoga; Sadhna-Swadhyaya.

Praxis is needed as an essential aspect of schooling to ensure we imbibe righteousness & goodness also. These universal life skill practices are totally missing from modern ideas of schooling and academia and even preaching, with obvious consequences!

“Learning To Know” + “Learning To Be”: To change our past habits, worldview, values, beliefs, etc. isn’t at all easy! Mentors can inspire, suggest, help us adopt some inner governance, self-development, life skills, and practices to awaken discriminative intellect, Viveka-buddhi to be able to distinguish and choose the long-term good over between the short-term pleasurable and then strive to build mental abilities of will power, self-control, concentration, commitment to selflessness etc to remain committed to Kartavya-palan.

Principle of cause-effect: From a young age we must understand the importance of powers of goodness and the fact that with every “selfless-action, we ‘purify’” our mind, as we do when we fulfill our duties, and with all our respective prayers, worship, etc. And only a pure mind can help us strive towards our ‘Perfection’, ‘Oneness with all as Self’ within! Can governance, education, preachers work together to help!

The conviction that the universe is governed by the unfailing cause-effect principle; that each intention-action has its consequences, can give us the inspiration to strive to awaken our discriminative intellect (Viveka-buddhi). Only awakened Viveka can help us ‘know’ and ‘choose the long-term good’ over short-term pleasurable; only then we’re inspired to strive to build the powers of mind to make right choices and be inspired and enabled to walk-the-talk.

‘Happiness + Prosperity for all’: Modern education in schools, colleges, professional and governance institutes, etc train and teach various subjects, even philosophy, but build and evaluate only our IQ. Schooling has no action plans to build EQ, SQ, Viveka, etc. to make us or evaluate us as good human beings by helping us be ‘Perfect’. Mentors can focus on helping us to:

‘Build powers of Mind; Respect our mother, father, teachers, strangers; Know and build commitment to all our various duties; Imbibe the spirit of quality in Work as Service (worship of God in man and in all creation); Strive towards more and more Selflessness and less and less of self-centeredness; Imbibe the spirit of Love, Peace, Ahimsa, Righteous conduct, Truth, etc.; Love thy neighbour as thyself – Why, because as Self, Consciousness, Life we’re all one; worldview of oneness (whole world as our family) & subjective (mind & self) focus’.

Refining our mind-set : There are only two basic processes for refining our minds and imbibing values – repetitive thoughts, words, actions, constant practice; and learning to focus on the essentials and with drawl from the non-essentials – Practice. These self-development processes for refining our mind have to be undertaken regularly, daily, continuously, at all times, in every moment, in every choice that we make, and for our whole life. The broad methods for improving our mind-set, for self-development:

i) Treat Work as worship, i.e. meditation with eyes open: Work is not just for roti, kapada, makaan ; it is for purification of mind so that the power and bliss within can be experienced. Our daily work becomes a path to perfection through fulfillment of all our duties; quality and excellence in whatever we do; and working for the good and well-being of all.

ii) Prayers: Prayers are the pouring of good thoughts in the mind , to reform and refine the existing impressions. It is talking to God, who is our nearest and dearest, in our own language and making him our partner, with whom we can share all our problems. Gradually we can realise that the more unselfish our prayer, the better. Since we are all one in ‘life’, we must learn to continuously pray: May all beings be happy, peaceful and blissful.

iii) Meditation: Meditation, Self-awareness, living in the present, mindfulness, etc is a process of calming the mind and listening to God, our idea of Perfection. Constant remembrance and repetition of brief prayer or holy name of God, while dwelling on his form, qualities, life and teachings, is one of the best methods of purifying the mind and developing powers of will, concentration, discrimination, strength, fearlessness. The mind acquires the quality of that on which it dwells. If we keep the mind on the highest and best, we attain the Perfection, which is our real unchanging nature, our Swaroopa, Consciousness .

Not mere ‘Belief’ but conviction : Teachers as mentors are needed to give us the intellectual conviction in the power of goodness and that it is our obligatory duty to study, explore, and get established in the spirit of selfless kartavya-palan as the necessary first steps to walk-the-talk to realise the universal goal of “Best, Perfection, Freedom”.
