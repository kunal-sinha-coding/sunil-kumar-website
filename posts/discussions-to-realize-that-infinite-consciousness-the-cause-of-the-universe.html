India to Bharat · Essay #135

Discussions to realize that infinite consciousness, the cause of the universe

1. We now know of That as the Creator, only through shruti, i.e., heard from Vedic Rishis, Prophets, Scriptures. We the educated wish to know the ‘real unchanging form’, Swaroopa, of That, the infinite omnipresent, omniscient, omnipotent entity which is well known as God.

2. Direct perception of That is seemingly impossible with our finite minds, because the mind is itself only conscious because of That. Thus, there’s no way we can ever know That infinite.

3. So, how to know That? The only way is to seek guidance from the ‘words’ of those pure selfless god-men! Without help from statements of sages there’s no other way; till we ourselves purify our mind thru’ ethical conduct and realize That. While the Rishis try to describe the indescribable through words and tell us where and how to look, it is our duty to try our utmost to understand God by our limited mind itself, even though the infinite is beyond the grasp of a limited mind!

4. And, this questioning , discussing, seeking to understand and realize That highest and best ideal thru our mind and intellect, is itself a spiritual path like prayers, worship, etc., which purifies the mind and makes it so transparent that, That Infinite Consciousness shines thru us, and we ourselves realize That, as the Rishis have.

5. It was the best of men who took up this grand quest and, it is now for us the educated, those of highest intellect, teachers, scientists of the modern era, to get interested in undertaking this search to know the unknowable, along with study of mathematics and sciences. This is the great and courageous task before the best of us.

6. Meanwhile let’s see the various logical ways in which the identity of That has been discussed by those who have realized That, to understand the idea of God, since realizing him is said to be our highest Goal and our search can proceed only from the known, i.e., the Creation before us!

i) Swaroopa: Let’s start our logical search to know That, by appreciating the idea that the Swaroopa of a thing means that essential, unchanging, inherent nature, ‘stuff, material’, which remains integral with it at all times, even though its Name and external Forms may appear changing. For example, the Swaroopa of ice, snow, steam is apparently water, but their Names and Forms are changing, so water can’t be their unchanging Swaroopa! When we use our scientific knowledge, we realize that actually H2O is their Swaroopa, their inherent unchanging nature, in all the states.

ii) Similarly, Clay is the stuff of all pots; Gold is the integral material, Swaroopa, of all gold-jewelry, with changing names and forms; wood is the Swaroopa of furniture, etc. And, we drink sherbet and find it sweet; we infer that the ‘cause ’ of the sweetness is sugar; so, whenever we see the ‘ effect’ , sweetness, we can conclude that the ‘ cause ’ is sugar. Name and cube form of sugar are no longer visible but yet, we recognize the subtle cause , sugar.

iii) Again, from observation we see that the Material-cause, e.g., Clay, Gold, Wood, cannot be transformed on its own into effect, e.g., pots, ornaments, furniture. For this, a separate cause is required which is referred to as the efficient-cause. For example, to convert clay into pot etc. there is the pot-maker; for gold into jewelry, goldsmith; for furniture, carpenter; they all have ‘intelligence’! Moreover, to make the pot, jewelry, etc., we need various instruments. These are the supporting causes .

7. Fundamental principle of Cause-effect: We intuitively think that there is, or must be, an invisible cause for the coming forth of Jagat and Vedanta shows that this jagat is an ‘ effect ’, of which That God, is the ‘ cause ’; Jagat is the manifested, vyakta , form of the avyakta, unmanifest God.

8. So, lets analyze the Big Question: What is the Swaroopa of Jagat? That infinite Consciousness, God, is the Material-cause, Efficient-cause as well as the Supporting-cause of Jagat, which is ever changing!

i) The form in which we directly perceive the Jagat cannot be its Swaroopa; without the basic stuff or ‘material’, the changing names and forms cannot exist.

ii) So, That is the basic stuff, which while holding on to its Swaroopa, is appearing in different names and forms, which we call Jagat.

iii) To Know anything in the jagat, we need three things, i.e., the triad of ‘Knower’, ‘process of Knowing’, ‘object to be Known’. The entire manifested Jagat is perceived, ‘seen’, based on this triad. We perceive the different forms in Jagat, but we are not able to directly perceive the basic stuff, which is taking different forms; in fact, it just cannot be perceived, because all that we see in this jagat are nothing but various modifications, names and forms of the same material-cause, i.e. That infinite Consciousness. So, there is no triad for knowing!

9. This projected universe, Jagat, is the Effect of which the ultimate and only Cause is That, and so, through the effect, I can try to understand the Cause. Man has been made in the image of God. To help us know God intimately, God Itself has limited Itself and has come and stands before us as this Creation!

Only a person, who can see, experience and visualize all the objects and things at one and the same time, can be depended upon for giving the right guiding principle; and such a person can only be That infinite God and the Vedas are his words as are the revealed scriptures. It is not possible for any one scripture or person to have the knowledge of the vast universe. Depending on our partial knowledge and experience we can only theorize about a very small part
