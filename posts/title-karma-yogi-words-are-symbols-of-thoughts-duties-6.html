India to Bharat · Essay #149

Title: Karma Yogi: “Words are Symbols of Thoughts” & “Duties” (6)

1. Thought behind our Duties: Words are but symbols of Thought! Symbols are of greatest help. From time immemorial symbols have been used by all religions. We can’t think but in symbols of Names and Forms/shapes. Thus, everything out there in the universe appears as but symbols in our minds; and the One infinite God is the essence behind it! And, the Consciousness of the same God is within me, as ‘Life’ due to whom I am and I am aware of the universe! Thus, the universe, work and duties exist, only to point our attention towards the essence, the One God; and so, we’re to fulfill our duties selflessly to attain the Essence!

There never was an idea/thought without a corresponding word or a word without a corresponding idea; ideas and words are in their nature inseparable. Language is not the result of a seminar or convention where people discussed and agreed to represent certain ideas by certain words! Every thought in the mind has a name/form as its counterpart. (Noam Chomky, Noble Prize holder found that Languages cannot ever be created anew; they have to exist eternally!)

This is called in Sanskrit philosophy Nâma-Rupa, Name-Form. We need to know the relation between Thought and Word and what can be achieved by the power of the Word. In every religion the power of the word is recognised, so much so that in some of them creation itself is said to have come out of the word. (In the beginning was the word and the word was with God…). The whole universe is for us; to help take us beyond Names & Forms, to God, the Unity of the Uni-verse. The One song; which we call Om!

The external aspect of the thought of God is the Word, and as God thought and willed, He created; and, creation came out of the Word. I am talking to you. I am not touching you; the pulsations of the air caused by my speaking go into your ear, they touch your nerves and produce effects in your minds. You cannot resist this. What can be more wonderful than this? One man calls another a fool, and the other lands a blow on his nose. Think of the power of Thoughts and Words! They are a great force in higher philosophy as well as in common life. Day and night, we manipulate this force without thinking and without inquiry. To know the nature of this force and to use it well is also a part of KarmaYoga.

2. Don’t look down on Menial work and lower Caste: Vyadh-Gita from Mahabharat contains one of the highest flights of Karma Yoga and Vedanta. It is the teachings of a “Butcher to a Monk”, the lowest teaching the highest who thought he was far superior in status, wisdom, Spirituality! The Sannyasin felt astonished; ” With such profound knowledge, why are you just a butcher doing such filthy, ugly work? “My son”, no duty is ugly, no duty is impure. My birth placed me in these circumstances and environments. In my boyhood I learnt the trade. I am unattached, I try to do my trade/householder duties well; I do all I can to make my parents/family happy. I know no Yoga; nevertheless, all that you have heard and seen has come to me through the unattached doing of the duty which belongs to my position”.

Secret of work: “Let the end and the means be joined into one”. When you are doing any work, do not think of anything beyond. Do it as worship, as the highest worship, and devote your whole life to it for the time being.

Thought Lesson: Do all your duties with cheerfulness, whole-heartedness and become illumined: Karma Yoga asserts that the right performance of duties of any station in life, high or low, without attachment to results, leads us to the highest realisation of the perfection of the soul. It is the worker who is attached to results that grumbles about the nature of the duty which has fallen to his lot; to the unattached worker all duties are equally good, and form efficient instruments with which selfishness and sensuality may be killed, and the freedom of the soul secured.

We are all apt to think too highly of ourselves : Our duties are determined by our deserts to a much larger extent than we are willing to grant; “as we sow, so we reap”. Competition rouses envy, and it kills the kindliness of the heart. To the grumbler all duties are distasteful; nothing will ever satisfy him, and his whole life is doomed to prove a failure. Let us work on, doing as we go whatever happens to be our duty, and being ever ready to put our shoulders to the wheel. Then surely shall we see the Light!

This world is perfect, i.e. perfectly fitted to meet its ends: We help ourselves, not the world. Our duty to others means helping others; doing good to the world. Why should we do good to the world? Apparently to help the world, but really to help ourselves. Always do try to help; that should be our highest motive; but if we consider well, we find that the world does not require our help at all! We cannot deny there is much misery, so helping others is the best thing we can do; but in the long run, we find that we only help ourselves; we get moral exercise. This world is neither good nor evil. It will go on beautifully well without us. We manufacture our world for ourselves by our thoughts-words-actions, Karma.
