India to Bharat · Essay #032

Newton, Aristotle and scientific harmony between all religions!

Is the modern man more scientific and rational than Isac Newton? Modern man believes more in science, which deals with matter and can be perceived by our sense organs. The reality is that many of the top scientists are also deeply spiritual, and philosophy, science and spirituality are all seeking an ultimate truth which lies behind the gross, material world in which we live.

Newton was in search of eternal truth and for him there was no separation between science and spirituality; they were parts of the same truth and were equally avenues to an unknown and unknowable God! They were the keys to the true knowledge of the universe. Newton was evidently a philosopher in the ancient traditions of Vedic Rishis, a sincere and relentless seeker after true reality of Creation/Creator, not mere belief.

In his treatise on ‘gravitation’, Newton had suggested that the analogy between Divine faculty and our own is greater than has formerly been thought by Philosophers. Newton himself wanted to be closer to the divine and just as he had mastered mathematics, philosophy and science he devoured all kinds of Biblical studies then available, and had written notes running into 850 pages, besides many other documents on his thinking on the Creator and his creation!

Newton’s discovery of universal gravitation was a further proof of the divine plan, and he declared that God was everywhere within his creation. He was the Universal Ruler. Science accepts that, ‘that which Exists can never go out of Existence’; ‘that which doesn’t Exist, can never come into Existence’!

As Newton states, gravity is not inherent in matter but has been created by some unknown agent and he says we do not know what the CAUSE of gravity is except that it requires the mediation of something else, which is not material! And he says that, that Cause was ‘very well skilled in mechanics and geometry’. Scientists are searching for a unified theory of physical forces and some of the topmost scientists are also now suggesting that rather than big bang, accidental creation from matter, this is an ‘intelligent universe’. This is a reminder to the modern man, as it was in the Vedic as well as Newtonian age, that the universe had a divine foundation.

In his seeking after the truth behind the universe, Newton also found that ‘there was some universal ‘ether’ or spirit (Consciousness; Life or something like it!) that created such forces as gravity and while he kept getting closer and closer to the heart of the mystery, but in the end, he would have realized that it was beyond the power of human intelligence to venture much further – Infinite Consciousness is to be experienced by expanding to be One with creation; it isn’t a object to be known!

In fact, this is exactly what the Vedas say: Who really knows? Who in this world may speak of it? Whence this creation, how was it engendered? Even the gods (were) subsequent to the (world’s) creation; so, who knows whence it arose’.

Is there ‘intelligence’ behind force? Is there a divine plan behind the order in the universe? The First of the Laws of Motion discovered by Newton is that all bodies, things, ‘continues in its state of rest, or of uniform motion in a right line’ unless it is affected by an ‘external force’.

The scientist in Newton led him to conclude that ‘God was everywhere within his creation’ and that ‘He is the Universal Ruler’, and the ‘unknown agent behind the cause of force of gravity’. In fact, from Vedanta also we can state that just as behind every motion there must be a force, behind every force there must be that divine intelligence ruling over and governing that force; and in this way we may harmonize the learning of both Vedanta and the greatest scientist. That is why the Vedic Rishis had deified all natural forces and linked them to a deity, Sun-god, Wind-god, Earth-god, river-god and same applies to a Jiva+atman, living-being, body as matter, enlivened by Consciousness and so, worship as God our mother, Father, Teacher, and even Atithi, stranger!

A young divine in 1692 asked Newton whether his discoveries outlined in the Principia Mathematicia are in a way confirming the workings of divine providence in the universe. Newton confirmed, saying that ‘when I wrote my treatises about our system, I had an eye upon such principles, as might work with considering men for the belief of a Deity and nothing can rejoice me more than to find it useful for that purpose.

Some scientists believe that Newton stripped the universe of divine causation and rendered it wholly mechanical. Nothing could be further from truth. Newton insisted that the universe could only be understood as the work of a creator, and that its orderliness (Rtm in Sanskrit signifies cosmic Order, Oneness, in Uni+verse!) was the result of a divine intelligence (Consciousness).

Throughout his life Newton had been a sincere seeker after truth and from his writings published and unpublished, it seems clear that he possessed some vision of a primitive church – whose principals were, simply to love God and to love your neighbour, the foundation of true Christianity, but as a scientist, he was not taken in by the various doctrines and dogmas of the Church, and that is how science grew in the West, as the real search for truth, bereft of the forced authority of the church.
