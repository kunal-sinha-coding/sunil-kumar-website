India to Bharat · Essay #087

Religions & education must evaluate & promote based on spirit of ‘Tyaga and Seva’

Vivekananda has highlighted that the national ideals of India are “renunciation and service”, “Tyaga and Seva”. It is therefore necessary to understand this idea and ideal and realize that schooling, religions, governance, must be made responsible and accountable to ensure that only those who imbibe this spirit of tyaga and seva become our leaders in politics, administration, and as professionals and businessmen.

Our idea of history as education is not just to learn events of life and times but history and epics must educate, guide, and inspire us to strive to be good, better, best. Rama and Buddha were both Prince and they both became great because of their sprit of tyaga.

Mahabharata is full of such examples of people sacrificing their own interests, their own opinions, and ideas, for the sake of accommodating others, for the sake of preserving peace and harmony. We have the glowing examples of Krishna, Bhishma and Karna, who constantly put themselves below and behind others for the sake of the greater good of greatest numbers!

In fact, Rama’s glowing example shows us that tyaga is needed even in married life; and in many ways, tyaga is more required in a married life, than in monastic life; since as a householder one must sacrifice one’s own comforts and accommodate many other people in married life. In fact, if we reflect, we will find that daily life is full of tyaga! A mother, and a newly married woman leaving her family and entering a new household are best examples of tyaga and seva.

Welfare of any family, any society, anywhere in the world, depends on people sacrificing their self-interest for the sake of the larger good, for the sake of the good of others. Selflessness is universally accepted to be better than self-centeredness! We cannot serve others unless we learn to renounce our self-interest for the sake of others.

Practical Vedanta – ‘Shiva jane jiva seva’: Vivekananda says, “Man was loving God and had forgotten all about his brother man. The man who in the name of God can give up his very life, can also turn around and kill his brother man in the name of God. That was the state of the world. They would sacrifice the son for the glory of God, would rob nations for the glory of God, would kill thousands of beings for the glory of God, would drench the earth with blood for the glory of God. This was the first time they turned to the other God – man. It is man that is to be loved. It was the first wave of intense love for all men – the first wave of true unadulterated wisdom – that, starting from India, gradually inundated country after country, north, south, east, west.”

Service of man as God was the lesson which Vivekananda learnt from his Master, and it is this which he has propagated, and it has now become the essence of the GOI Mission Karma Yogi and is quoted in GOI brochure “Atmano moksartham jagat hitaye cha”, Strive for the highest Perfection/Freedom working for the good of Creation.

Practice serving man seeing God within him. If we continuously look upon others as divine, we begin to look upon ourselves as divine. This is the immense utility of Serving man as God. If we develop a habit of serving others with the idea that God is residing in them, gradually we ourselves will start feeling the presence of God within us. Thus, a very common act of serving others gets transformed into a powerful spiritual practice.

This may be a visualization in the beginning. But, when the imagination becomes a habit, then transformation of personality happens. Now, when we work for good of others in a spirit of serving the other person seeing God in him, feeling that God is residing in his heart and receiving our service, can we be callous towards that person? A deep sense of humility and respect towards that person automatically wells up within us.

This is the inspired idea with which Vivekananda lifted our daily work itself and presented Karma Yoga, fulfilling our duties for the long-term good of all, as the highest worship, for the present age. He says: “It is our privilege to be allowed to be of service to others, for only so can we grow. The poor man suffers that we may be helped by serving; let the giver kneel down and give thanks, let the receiver stand up and permit. See the Lord back of every being and give to Him.”

For Vivekananda the life of Buddha had a special appeal: “I have more veneration for that character than for any other – that boldness, that fearlessness, and that tremendous love! He was born for the good of men. Others may seek God; others may seek truth for themselves; he did not even care to know truth for himself.

He sought truth because people were in misery. How to help them, that was his only concern. Throughout his life he never had a thought for himself. How can we ignorant, selfish, narrow-minded human beings ever understand the greatness of this man? Surely he was the greatest man who ever lived. He never drew a breath for himself.”

Be good and do good; that is all of religion. Sacrificing oneself for others is being good. Serving others is doing good. So, Tyaga and Seva is all of religion! This is a very compact formula given by Vivekananda.
