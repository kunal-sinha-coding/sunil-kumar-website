India to Bharat · Essay #222

The idea of success – reflections on the National Youth Day

On 12 th January, 1985, the birthday of Vivekananda, the then youthful PM declared that hence forth India will celebrate the birthday of Vivekananda as the National Youth Day, ‘ as it was felt that the philosophy of Swamiji, and his ideals for which he lived and worked, could be a great source of inspiration for the Indian youth’.

Why Vivekananda? Rabindranath Tagore says, “If you want to know India, study Vivekananda. In him everything is positive and nothing negative.”

Subhas Chandra Bose says, “Swamiji harmonized East and West, Religion and Science, past and present. And that is why he is great. Our countrymen have gained unprecedented self-respect, self-confidence and self-assertion from his teachings.”

Jawaharlal Nehru says, “Directly or indirectly he has powerfully influenced the India of today. And I think that our younger generation will take advantage of this fountain of wisdom, of sprit and fire that flows through Swami Vivekananda.”

Gandhiji says, ‘I have gone through his works very thoroughly, and after having gone through them, the love that I had for my country became a thousand-fold.’

What we need today is to know what my idea of success is and compare it to the ‘best’ ideas of success of both India and Bharat, as envisioned by Gandhiji and Vivekananda. We can then try to figure out what success should really mean to us. Unfortunately, our 3R’s schooling and life style have so far focused only ‘objective or material’ success. They have miserably failed to give us character building and nation building education.

Worldly success today is judged thru’ external, objective, visible, material criterion, e.g. more money, status, power, life style, IQ, etc. Thus, the Western and the copy-cat Indian Ladder-of-Success is growth from ‘Worker’ to ‘Manager’ to ‘Administrator’ to ‘CEO’. This ladder is based on the mistaken idea of short-term pleasures eschewing the long-term good, which misleads us into thinking that ‘all that glitters is gold’ and ignorantly we follow the ‘golden deer’.

Short-term gains have become our Goal : As a society we have created systems, which continuously arouse desires in people; we no longer project the ideal of ‘simple living and high thinking’. Political parties are in power for a few years; bureaucrats are frequently transferred; every one keeps looking for promotions and perks.

Practical Vedanta of Vivekananda: The entire scheme of Bharat, of Indian heritage, is based on the central idea – the oneness and divinity of man, life, and of all creation. If each soul is potentially divine, if the ‘kingdom of God is within man, then the goal is to manifest our divinity by serving man as God. Vivekananda says, I consider that the great national sin is the neglect of the masses, and that is one of the causes of our downfall. No amount of politics would be of any avail until the masses in India are once more well educated, well fed, and well cared for. The whole world is full of Lord, open your eyes and see Him. Success is measured by inner fulfillment, ‘sukh-shanti-ananda’ that we experience when our work becomes worship and leads to chitta-suddhi, purity of mind.

This Vedantic worldview of ‘oneness’ is of necessity today to unite us towards higher dimensions of work and life and bring harmony amongt religions to manage, what is being called the clash of civilizations.
