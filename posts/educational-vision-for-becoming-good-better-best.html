India to Bharat · Essay #229

Educational vision for becoming good, better, best

‘Common Minimum Program’ for schools and colleges: The Supreme Court’s Judgment of 12th Sept. 2002, gives the direction, the ‘North’, for HRD, Academia, Faith groups, Policy makers, elders to follow:

Curriculum in schools have to develop key qualities like regularity, punctuality, cleanliness, self-control, industriousness, sense of duty, desire to serve, responsibility, enterprise, creativity, etc.

Students should be given awareness that the essence of every ‘religion’ is common, only practices differ and to ensure that no prejudices or narrow-minded perceptions are allowed, and dogmas should not be propagated in name of education about ‘religion’.

What is sought to be imparted is from Fundamental Duties, Article 51 A. e. ie “to promote harmony and spirit of common brotherhood transcending religious and other diversities”; “And to see that universal values, truth, righteous conduct, peace, love, non-violence be the foundation of education”.

Well known universal maxim:

Know the Truth and the Truth shall make you free

Through ‘righteous conduct’, ethics, dharma, ‘earn’ and ‘enjoy’ to remain on path to ‘highest freedom’.

That is vidya, which enables us to walk the path of virtues and human values towards freedom, ‘mukti’. Vidya gives humility

Mukti’ symbolizes mankind’s universal ‘North’, the ‘highest and best’ human goal, referred variously as Perfection, Bliss, Ananda, Moksha, Salvation, Heaven, Nirvana, etc.

Excellence in vocation is Yoga; calm, peaceful, balanced mind is yoga.

Output that schools & colleges must ensure: The academia have to evolve an action plan to ensure that the students who graduate, have acquired:

The inspiration and intense desire to become better, towards the ‘best’

A healthy worldview of ‘oneness’, and commitment to work and vocation as worship of God in man.

The ability to discern, discriminate; to choose rightly for long-term good for more and more,

The habit of daily practice of self-development processes to develop the ability and powers of mind, e.g. will power, self-control, concentration, focus on higher and gradual withdrawal from lower, etc.

Inspiration to Practice & Focus: In consultation with faith groups, Schooling should provide one-hour daily for self-development practices, and hold weekly workshops where students, mentors, parents, teachers can study, discuss, question, reflect, analyze, on the above issues so as to get an intellectual conviction in the power of goodness and commitment to daily life long practices, to build the ability to walk-the-path.

Let all enlightened citizens come forward to evolve an educational system which can produce citizens committed to excellence in work.
