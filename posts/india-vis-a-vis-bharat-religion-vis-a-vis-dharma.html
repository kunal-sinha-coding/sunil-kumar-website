India to Bharat · Essay #171

India vis-a-vis Bharat & Religion vis-a-vis Dharma

Should value-based-education be allowed to be a part of mainstream education system?

Considering that religions are an important source of value inculcation, will teaching of values not be a violation of the constitutional idea of secularism? This was a question that our legal luminaries were faced with. They examined the question in great detail and have directed that value education must be a part of schooling .

Will our religious and political leaders stop quarreling with each other and work for good of the nation, to establish once again, commitment to dharma and swadharma in society, based on the spiritual essence and wisdom of all religions that have flourished here, from the ancient times.

This is how the legal pundits have drawn a distinction between India’s idea of religion versus Bharat’s idea of Dharma: –

The idea of Religion in India: The English word ‘religion’ does not fully convey the Indian concept of religion. In the Western world religious education implies religious instructions. In India which is wedded to secularism, religious education can mean approaching religions with an attitude of understanding. By religion, we mean organized belief with its dogmas, rituals, mysteries, scripture, mediator, priest, and its way of threatening and holding people, but all this creates antagonism; all assert they worship one God, love all, but instill fear through the doctrine of reward/punishment.

True religion is not a set of beliefs, rituals, hopes, fears. It is to help us be intelligently aware and point a way to wisdom, truth. Indians have been enriched by the integration of various religions. Whoever came as aggressor India has tried to accept the best part of their religion. This is the capacity of Indians to assimilate thoughts of different religions.

The idea of Dharma of Bharat: The word ‘dharma’ has a very wide meaning. One meaning is ‘moral value or ethics’ on which life is naturally regulated. Dharma, righteousness, truth, love, compassion, human virtues are elemental and fundamental. This is what Hindu call Sanatana Dharma meaning religion which is immutable, constant, permanent, and eternal. Religion in a wide sense is those fundamental principles that sustain and without which life will not survive. In this concept of Religion or Dharma, different faiths, sects, schools of thoughts are merely different ways of knowing the truth, which is one. The various religious groups are ‘ panths ’ or ‘ sampradayas ’.

What Schooling should do? During schooling, children should understand the pluralist view without curbing free-thinking, and right to choose. Intellect is not enough. Inner qualities of head and heart and capacity to regulate own life and relation with society should be imparted to them for their own and general good of society and also for achieving the highest goal of life.

Attainment of constitutional ideals is possible only if side by side with intellect, moral character of children is also developed to make them good citizens. How best religious pluralism can be made to accord with “secular thought” can be achieved by properly selecting material for inclusion in textbooks of children appropriate for different ages and stages. Scrutiny of textbooks to see whether they conform to Secularism is to be undertaken

Prohibiting study of religions would amount to denying children right to understand their own religion and religions of others with whom they are living and interact. Study of religions is not prohibited. Otherwise chances of spiritual growth of humans to be the highest goal of human existence would be totally frustrated. A dogma is quite different from study.

We have misunderstood the idea of Secularism: We do not allow students even touch of our religious books. Gandhiji in his lifetime had been trying to create religious and communal harmony. Vinoba Bhave studied all religions and traveled across the country on foot to unite the hearts of people through the message of love and ahimsa. He published the essence of all religions in books like Quran Saar, Khista dharma saar, Bhagvat dharma saar which can be used as textbooks because these have been tested over thousands of years and proved useful for the development of man and society. The study of religions will strengthen Secularism.

Democracy can’t survive unless citizens are not only learned and intelligent but are also of moral character imbued with values of truth, compassion, love and so the learned have strongly recommend introduction of religious education. India is mostly composed of followers of one or other religion; a very small section is of non-believers.

Bertrand Russell on teaching history to students says: I should not hold military conquerors to admiration. In my teaching of history true conquerors are those who did something to dispel the darkness within and without – Buddha, Socrates, Archimedes, Galileo, Newton, and all those who have helped give us mastery over ourselves or over nature. Nehru and Radhakrishnan hold that Science and Spirituality will be two great forces for human excellence. The end of education is Self-knowledge. It is the spirit which is the source of all achievement, creativity, freedom, discipline. The real basis of society must be laid in schools .

The experts have given the go-ahead – What is our Duty now?

The student life is a preparation for being an ethical householder, established in dharma and swadharma. The all-round selfishness, corruption, poor work culture is growing only because academia and religions have failed to work together to create enlightened citizens committed to their swadharma. At one time National Resource Centers for Value Education had been created which included various religions and NGO’s working in the field. Academia, religions and governance now have the great responsibility to work together to create the action plan to create enlightened citizens who are committed to universal dharma of ethics and virtuous living, and to their respective duties, swadharma.
