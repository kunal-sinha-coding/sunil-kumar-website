India to Bharat · Essay #086

USP of India that is Bharat – Through ‘Service of God in man’ to attain ‘Goal of Life’

1. Goal of Life: “An Aryan can be distinguished from a non-Aryans by their respective goal of life! God realization is the goal of life of an Aryan. If anything, short of God realization is the goal of life of a person, even if he is born a Hindu, in this blessed land, he is a non-Aryan”.

“The term Aryan has nothing to do with race, geography, language, religion, etc. It denotes a spiritual outlook to life – thru Righteousness Earn and Enjoy, to attain the Goal of Life. Anyone, no matter what his background, who believes that the ultimate Goal of Life is to realize the potential divinity innate within him, is an Aryan. Anyone who believes differently, no matter what his background, is a non-Aryan”; literally Bharati means ‘Striving for highest Illumination’!

Our civilizational theme of life is spirituality, commitment to imbibe the spirit of tyaga and seva and so, everything else, whether science or knowledge, enjoyment or powers, wealth, name, or fame, must be subordinated to that one theme.

“Millions of our ancestors are watching, as it were, every action of ours, so be alert. And what is the mission with which every Hindu child is born? For the protection of the treasury of religions. and every other problem in life must be subordinated to that one principal theme”.

Our challenge is to understand and orient education and religions to inspire citizens to start practicing commitment to our respective duties and spirit of selfless service as worship in general, then move to service of God in man, and then realize the vision of ‘God within me, my own Self’.

Let the Vedas, and all Religions and Scriptures sleep for a time and let us all agree to first of all learn, practice, teach and inspire all to learn to worship the visible God, all round us.

2. Why should we serve others? There is one “Self/ Spirit/ Rooha/ Atman/ Kingdom of God within”, not many. That one “Self” shines in various bodies and forms. Man is man’s brother because all men are one. A man is not only my brother, but he is also my very own “Self”. Hurting any being of the universe, I only hurt my “Self”. I am one with the universe.

In the Upanishads is presented the great doctrine of the Atman and the Brahman. The Atman, Self, is the same as Brahman, the Lord. This Self is all that is. It is the only reality. Somehow, we get attached to our body-mind but gradually thru’ righteousness, selfless work for good of others, etc. we purify our mind and then we realize our oneness with the whole Creation.

Vedanta formulates, not just universal brotherhood, but universal Oneness. I am the same as any other man, as any animal – good, bad, anything. It is one body, one mind, one soul throughout. Spirit never dies.”

3. “How do we reach a state where we can spontaneously serve the Lord in all beings? If we learn, recognize, and feel that we are spirit, divine, invariably we will look upon others as divine too. If we look upon ourselves as hypocrites, invariably we look upon others as hypocrites too.

So, service of God in man has two distinct, but related phases. In the 1st phase, it is a very powerful spiritual practice, giving very quick results. In the 2nd phase, it is the spontaneous action of a perfected soul. A realized soul clearly ‘sees’ that it is he himself that now stands before him as a separate person.”

“The mind is to be made pure, Chitta-shuddhi, purification of the heart, thru’ selfless service, and the pure heart sees only good, never evil. Let us purify ourselves so that we may be of service to others.

And how do we attain Chitta-shuddhi? “The first is the worship of the Virat – of the Creation, of those all around us. Worship it. Worship is the exact equivalent of the Sanskrit word, and no other English word will do. These are all our gods – men and animals; and the first gods we have to worship are our countrymen. These we must worship, instead of being jealous of each other and fighting each other” – Vivekananda. Our daily work itself is the great Sadhana, self-development process, wherein we build our character and destiny. Character is self-restraint; is the spirit of tyaga and seva. Let us then be perfect in work!

The goal of life is the same for everyone, whatever he may be. Every soul is potentially divine, and the goal is to manifest it. Ramakrishna was known to ask anyone who visited him a peculiar question: ‘According to you, what is the goal of life?’ The answers that people gave him were varied. Ramakrishna would correct them patiently, saying: “God realization is the goal of human life and that all human beings can achieve this goal, even married people”; “Selfless service can purify the heart; but do not mistake the means for the end; with the purified heart, call on God, and realize him in this very life.

Strength is required to be selfless, to be loving, caring and for manifesting potential divinity – “This Atman is not for the weak”. Strength comes when we know our own real nature; that we’re Jiva+atman, body-mind complex enlivened and made conscious by the Atman, Spirit, Kingdom of God within. This knowledge is the essence of all religions and the global education that the world needs.
