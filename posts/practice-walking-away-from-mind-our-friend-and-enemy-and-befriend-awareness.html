India to Bharat · Essay #102

Practice walking away from mind – our friend and enemy – and befriend awareness

People are equal only in the sense that we all are ‘Awareness’ per se – are alive, ‘Aware’ and ‘Conscious’ because of Life aka ‘Infinite divine potential as Existence-Consciousness-Bliss; Sad-chid-ananda’, which makes us living-beings with our respective body-mind. However, forgetting ourselves as Awareness, we get attached to our body-mind and ‘enjoy’ our respective ‘freedom’ to do whatever! And ‘as we sow, so we reap’ and gradually become more and more different, because of the different ‘choices-thoughts-words-deeds’ we’ve been making! The game of life is to practice and learn to sow ‘Good’, ‘Righteousness’ to ‘purify our mind’, chitta-suddhi’, to reap ‘once again’ (religion) and our union (Karma-yoga) with ‘Awareness’ per se and our oneness with all.

What is “The Greatest Secret”? ‘Ancient traditions knew that to hide a secret it should be put in plain sight, where no one will think to look for it. And that’s where The Greatest Secret lies’. ‘So close you can’t see it. So subtle, your mind can’t understand it. So simple, you can’t believe it. So good, you can’t accept it’. ‘What you actually are never dies’. ‘You are not your mind; mind is only thoughts. ‘We’ve missed discovering it because of one small obstacle, a belief that we’re our body-mind’.

‘We came into this world to be a body in order to learn that we’re not a body’! Just as we use a car to go from here to there, our body is a vehicle to experience the world. If we’re journeying in a car, we don’t say we’re the car! Our mistake is that while journeying this world in a body, we have started believing that we’re the body! Being material the body doesn’t know it is a body; but you and I ‘know’ and you and I are ‘aware’ of it. ‘You fear that if the body isn’t, you’re not’; if we want to know the big secret, practice thru’ righteousness to manage your mind and learn to stop holding on to the body-mind identity.

‘We cement our belief that we’re a person with our every thought’. If we check we find that behind every thoughts-feelings flowing in our mind, is a conscious, aware, “Me” and each thought limits me and cements me to our respective bundle of thoughts.

‘In the biblical parable, the man who is identified with the body-mind is the man who built his house on sand. To realize one’s true nature is to build one’s house on rock.’ ‘The majority of us have been constantly looking away from our true selves! Rather than looking at who we are’; we’ve been looking at ourselves from the perspective that we’re a body-mind, a person.

‘You are experiencing a body, a mind and, having the experience of being a person, but they are not you. The secret is that ‘Awareness’ is my real nature. Awareness is always with us; the only constant in my life. Body, mind, thoughts, feelings, likes, dislikes, everything is changing, but the one thing that has never changed is my Awareness of it all. And that ‘Awareness’ is what I really am.

Are you aware? The answer will be yes; else you would not be aware of the question. I am aware. I was aware as a baby and have been aware thru life. When I or you say I, we’re really referring to Awareness, but I and you have been established in the habit of believing that we’re referring to this body and the bundle of thoughts-memories-emotions, samskaras, associated with this limited personality!

This Awareness is that Consciousness which is infinite and the only unchanging ever existing all pervasive Perpetual blissfulness that this “I am this body-mind” is constantly seeking externally.

We have to practice “purifying our mind”, Chitta suddhi, thru Righteous conduct etc. till we can free ourselves of our samskara and can recognize ourselves as Awareness per se! ‘Instead of looking outward to the world for happiness, we need to turn and look inward; it’s only in that direction that we will find everything we’re looking for’.

2. Practices to Befriend and Walk-away from our mind, to make way for “Awareness” to become our true friend. Welcome and open your heart to negative thoughts, feelings, reactions, problems immediately as they appear; and, simultaneously, become aware that you really are “Awareness” per se, and try not to get involved in judging and reacting to the incoming sensations in your mind.

Because Awareness is naturally welcoming, with continuous practice, we can learn to instantly merge negativity into infinite awareness; and this gives us time and opportunity to manage negative reactions and transform them into positive win-win responses and righteousness; these purify the past negative tendencies and calm the mind to enable respond not react to situations and enable us be a karma-yogi who strives selflessly to fulfill all duties and works for long-term good of all.

Practice asking yourself from time to time, Am I Awareness; then practice noticing Awareness; and, staying as Awareness. Question every negative feelings with, ‘am I that, or am I the one that is aware of it. ‘Am I the one suffering or am I the one that is aware of the suffering, rather than one suffering. Awareness says ‘yes’ to everything; allows freedom of everything to be the way it is, because the world and everything in it is Awareness –its own self.

Learn and practice being present right now, because Awareness can only be recognized in this ‘present moment’, which is the eternal ever-blissful Awareness; beyond the idea of ‘time’ and ‘duality’, which are functions of mind – traditionally referred to as, “there is no second – advait”!
