India to Bharat · Essay #180

Vision of bharat presented at UN on peace & harmony

1. Presentation on peace and harmony in the year 1893: A ‘world science trade fair’ was held in Chicago, to showcase the scientific achievements of the West, where electricity was used for the first time on a large scale. It was also decided to hold a World Parliament of Religions to show to the world, once for all the superiority of their religion, science and technology. An unknown, young, English educated, Hindu monk from Calcutta, representing no one, suddenly appeared on the world-stage on 11th Sept. 1893, and created a spiritual-tsunami in the minds of the intellectuals of the Western world who believed, and still do, that their religion alone was true and that science can solve all our problems and bring peace and harmony!

This ‘unknown monk’ had the audacity and boldness to share the stage with the leaders representing all major Religions of the world, and dared to speak on behalf of “the most ancient order of monks in the world”; “of the mother of religions”; and on behalf of “millions and millions of Hindu people of all classes and sects”. And he proclaimed, that we of Bharat are: proud to belong to a religion which has taught the world both tolerance and universal acceptance. We believe not only in universal toleration, but we accept all religions as true…As different streams having their sources in different places all mingle their water in the sea, so, the different religious paths which men take through different tendencies, various though they appear, all lead to One God. Whosoever strives to be good, better, best, attains the same Godhead, through whatsoever form of worship. All men who sincerely struggle and strive to be good, whatever be their religious path, in the end lead to One Omnipresent God.

He added: Sectarianism, bigotry, and its horrible descendants, fanaticism, have long possessed this beautiful earth. They have filled the earth with violence, drenched it often with human blood, destroyed civilization and sent whole nations to despair.

This idea of Bharat, that all religions are equally meant to attain the highest human perfection, peace, bliss, salvation, etc. was totally unknown to the Western world. Unfortunately, the clash of religions and civilizations still continues.

2. Presentation at UN in the year 2000: A similar World Peace Summit was sponsored by the UN and held on 29th August, 2000, for the Religious and Spiritual Leaders of the World at the General Assembly Hall of United Nations, New York. A monk, follower of the same ‘unknown monk’, once again elaborated on the universal and perennial message and foundational vision of Bharat, that needs now to be an integral part of global schooling:

3. Our ancient vision: God is the very stuff of this universe and embodies three basic properties, ‘Existence’, ‘Consciousness’, ‘Bliss or Ananda’. Anything that exists asserts the existence of God. In addition, living beings express two other features.

Therefore, as we exist, we’re ‘born’ Gods, but we appear as good and bad because of our characteristics. God is unmanifest but by our virtuous, selfless deeds, we can awaken the asleep potential God. Just as the seed has the unmanifest, potential plant or tree within it! The infinite God is hidden in our own finite bodies. We’re living Christs; Buddhas. Bharat realized that God is an infinite mystery, and proclaimed: Truth is One. God is One. Sages call it by various names. We accept all religions, many rituals, but we recognize Truth is One. Truth is God, and God is hidden inside us.

But some thoughts are painful. When we hear my God is the only true God, we feel hurt. Christ is definitely the son of God, but what about Buddha, Mahavira, Prophet Mohammed. Are they not sons of God? When we say Christ is the son of God, we’re correct. But if we say Christ is the only son of God; we sow the seeds of dissension and fundamentalism.

Peace is not unilateral. Peace must come from all sides. We do not just tolerate, because toleration means we accept someone who is intolerable. We not only accept, but celebrate other religions. Shouldn’t the world respond today. Shouldn’t academia take responsibility to make it an integral part of global schooling?

4. Accept differences: We need not accept the tenets, doctrines, dogmas of others. But all must accept human values and universal truths like Newton’s laws of gravity or motion, or Einstein’s relativity, because these are universal truths. If I climb up to the top of this 20 storey UN building and say I do not believe in Christian’s laws of gravity and walk out of the window, what happens? I fall down, not up. Universal truths do not care for any society or any Religion. Religions and societies will have to accept truth, or they die. Truths are universal. If it is a universal truth, it is true for you, me, and it is so for everyone. Shouldn’t Religions work together to make good men?

5. Scientific truths: Scientists dealing with quantum mechanics, particle physics, neurophysiology, astrophysics, have discovered two profound universal truths. First truth is that the finite is always connected with the infinite; thus, the finite man is always connected with the infinite God. That is the idea. Thus, Jesus says, “the Kingdom of God is within you”; “I and my Father are One”. Second truth has come out of experimental verification of Bell’s theorem, that “we’re all interconnected”; physically and spiritually.

“Macbeth hath murdered sleep. Therefore, Macbeth shall sleep no more”. If I murder you, I murder my sleep. These two truths now being affirmed by science are the basis for harmony among religions; a call to all world religions to unite and work together with the academia to inspire man to strive to be good, better, best, by working in a spirit of worship for good of humanity.

6. Essence of being religious: If religions quarrel among themselves, how can they inspire the followers to be good! Poverty is not only of money and resources. In developing countries, we see economic poverty. In developed countries we see spiritual poverty. Tomorrow’s need is elimination of spiritual poverty.

Religion comes from intense self-sacrifice. Desire nothing for yourself. Do all for others. No religion can have any place for persecution or intolerance in its polity. The difference between God and the devil is in nothing except in unselfishness and selfishness. It is a privilege to serve mankind, for this is the worship of God; God is here, in all these human souls.
