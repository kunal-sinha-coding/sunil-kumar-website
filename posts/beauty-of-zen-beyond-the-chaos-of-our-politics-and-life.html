India to Bharat · Essay #215

Beauty of Zen Beyond the Chaos of our Politics and Life

Beauty of Zen, of Wisdom: Zen pushes us out of our consistent comfort zone into the chaotic but perfect whole! As 3R’s-educated logical persons we feel that our political scene is absurd, meaningless; our very effort to impose a certain logic on politics makes it look absurd. Instead of all good men working together for good of India as envisioned by the seers of Bharat, all our good men fight to serve their own self-centred interests! Our TV panellists with all their reporters and statisticians, daily shout at each other wishing to impose a certain logical meaning on what is happening out there but somehow the reality slips away and we the intellectuals complain that the whole thing is illogical and meaningless.

Healthy Worldview of Zen – Life is as it is: The animals, birds, trees, rivers don’t feel life or politics is meaningless! Why? Because they have no logic. All is as it is, they are in a relaxed relationship with life, they’re in Rtm, rhythm, Sur, harmony with creation. The river doesn’t think life is very strange, sometimes twisting to right, then to left, how will I reach my goal. If the river had a mind, a logical mind, it would simply go berserk. All nature is in Rtm. So are the masses. For them too, all is as it is.

Limitations of Mind: A highly intellectual, opinionated or fanatical mind means having a certain idea how things should be. Man has become too much attached to his own ideas, good or bad. And, a so-called scientific mind, is obsessed with logic. Logic always chooses, judges, labels; whereas they say: Judge not; as you judge, so shall you be judged. We try to be consistent. We hold on to this or that view, or get attached to this or that name and form; but we miss the illogical, paradoxical, contradictory logic of the infinite-whole that life is, which encompasses and contains all polar opposites!

The Paradox is that Life is all Love: When love arises only then we slip out of logic. Logic is security, safety, protective, and finally, it separates us from life itself! Life is dangerous because it is unpredictable and contains dangers.

To be in Zen is to be in love with Life, with the Whole: Logic is argumentative, articulate. Love is silent. Logic is a necessary evil. Use logic but use it to drop out of it, into life, love, into the realization of oneness with the whole existence, whole awareness, whole happiness. Vedanta says that the whole alone is blissful. There is no lasting happiness in the finite.

The BIG Questions to Know the Whole: The greatest scientists, the most logical among us, are striving very hard to find a unity, a theory of everything that would enable us understand this whole highly complex cosmos of ours. It is worthwhile to note the questions they’re grappling with. These are: Is there a God? How and Why did it all begin? Isn’t it an intelligent universe? Can this infinitely complex cosmos and living beings come into being on their own thru’ matter, thru’ Big Bang? Can Life or Consciousness, come from matter? Who manages, controls, ensures operation of universal Laws? Knowingly we put food into our mouth; who digests and transforms it into energy and waste, without our knowledge? Scientists use logic, imagination, laws of physics and mathematics, to understand how things work; but the nearer they reach a solution, the more difficult they find to go beyond matter to know life, consciousness, the whole. Anyway, the very fact that in spite of all attempts to deny the existence of God, God remains firmly in saddle! Thus, according to the Theory of Falsifiability, God must exist!

Zen proposes a profound way to know the Whole – After all logical thinking, just stop thinking with your mind for a moment and in a ‘Eureka’ moment infinite intelligence of the whole may then reveal itself! Whole cannot be known by a part!

Love cannot be expressed in words: Words are very tiny, trivial, mundane. They cannot express anything that is really profound, authentic, true, infinite. That’s why we invent various way to avoid words and use symbols, e.g. holding hands, hug, be silent together, bow down and, tears arise in gratitude; gratitude is even more difficult to express. Saying thank-you and thank-you notes are mundane. The tragedy with gratitude is that when we have a problem, inability to sleep well, then as soon it is solved and we sleep well, we forget to express gratitude.

Daily 5-Yagnas to Grow in Zen: Wisdom of Zen helps us grow in feelings of oneness with the whole by practising gratitude. When we have good sleep, good food, etc. we forget to note that there were countless of beings because of whom we are what we are! That’s why our ancient Vedic culture had taught us to feel interconnected, interdependent, interrelated, one, with all aspects of creation and express our gratitude on a daily basis, to: all saints and sages thru’ whom we have got wisdom; to God and all gods and goddesses who preside over forces of nature; to all mankind and all our forefathers who have helped us reach where we’re; to the kingdom of animals; and, to plants, nature. Love, care, concern for all is the better way.
