India to Bharat · Essay #029

We’re all one as ‘awareness’ per se; Practice staying in ‘awareness’!

As a culture most people of India that is Bharat and worldwide too, are generally good, simple, wanting to earn and enjoy through Righteousness and this culture has now spread globally and days of continuous fighting and wars in the name of Religions, territories, commerce, as in history has reduced if not gone.

The highest purpose and fruit of all religions, goodness and virtuous working and living is not status, God, heaven, etc., but to be able to not get attached to our self-centered beliefs and interests, but to practice just being ‘aware’, by learning to remain a witness to even our own ‘body-mind’ and thereby taking more and more balanced, righteous, ethical, selfless choices-actions for long-term good of all.

Slowly and gradually, globally, people are moving towards the idea that irrespective of who and what we are, or our religion is, we can all, ourselves learn to practice various “Self-Awareness” exercises as Life-skills and Self-development practices to develop powers of mind to “Know” and “Walk-the-talk”; and Teachers and Academia must be responsible to learn and then establish the young in such practices.

Anyone and everyone can practice being ‘aware of our awareness’, for a few minutes, anywhere, anytime. The practice is simple, profound and practical. Just be aware; don’t focus, don’t change or judge … simply be:

# Become aware that the body is breathing … just witness the Breath coming in and going out … a few minutes of practice regulates and makes breathing rhythmic which in turn helps relax and calm the mind.

# Note that breath is intimately connected with and is the gross manifestation of the Mind.

# Gradually be aware of a miniscule ‘gap’ between each inwards and outwards breath… as we continue witnessing, the ‘gaps’ become longer.

# In the ‘gap’ is the present moment, awareness! Similarly, the ‘gap’ between two thoughts, desires, emotions, is the ‘now’, the present moment. Learn to be in in the present moment when your decisions are likely to be the best, because it is not tainted by self-centeredness and beliefs of the mind. Vedanta suggests that as Awareness we’re all One as Unchanging-existence-Consciousness-Bliss, Sad-chid-ananda.

# We can Practice being aware of everything, to be just a witness of sound, scenery, every action without any judgments. But of course, we will frequently forget about awareness while working….it does need tremendous practice.

# So, we’ve to start gradually, say being aware while Walking; Talking; Eating; Sitting … to get to the stage of being aware even while focused on work…

# Normally when we walk, eat, etc. isn’t our mind active too; we’re not in the ‘present awareness’, but identified with our mind; and the mind is not in the present moment of being ‘aware of walking’ but is in its own cell thinking of past or future!

# So, the Key, is to practice replacing being with ‘thoughts’, with being ‘in the present moment’, in ‘awareness’, spontaneously. Through practice alone can we gradually learn.

# When we realize we weren’t aware, let’s feel happy that, we’re now aware! Even for a few moments if we can be aware of the present moment, rejoice! Change our focus to stay in awareness, instead of identifying with thoughts flowing in our mind – thinking!

As awareness we’re free to choose our actions, but as Body-mind we get enslaved by our mindset, limited to our habits. As awareness we’re one with all and free. I can learn and practice staying as ‘awareness’ as a meditational practice and then learn and practice staying in awareness, in and thru’ all our daily work!

Slowly and gradually, I can notice, understand, appreciate, realize the profound truth of truths, that I am awareness; I am different from the body and breathing; I am the observer, subject, everything else around me are just objects; I am not any object; I am Consciousness, Awareness per se… As awareness, Consciousness we’re one with all. This is said to be our real, eternal, unchanging Self as Existence-Consciousness-Bliss. This Truth is known and discussed from beginning of time, but is not easy to accept, understand, unless our mindset has been purified and expanded thru commitment to Kartavya-palan, selflessness, righteousness.

The moment I stray from awareness, I again get identified with our individual body-mind with all its selfishness, joys, pains, and worries. As we practice being Aware, we gradually realize that the swabhava, nature, of ‘Self’ within us all, is infinite, all-inclusive ‘awareness’; we’re all one as Awareness and consciousness.

Religion in Latin means to Re-gain, i.e., our Identity as one with all as consciousness, awareness; Yoga similarly means union with Self. It is Vedanta alone, which discusses, explores and can intellectually convince us of what we experience when we practice awareness exercises. We can then easily appreciate and relate to the profound Vedantic wisdom of our Oneness as Awareness; based on which all goodness, virtues, religions, ask us to ‘love thy neighbour as your own self. This is the essence, core, and scientific principles of all religions, but Preachers and Pandits ask us to focus on My vs Your God and religious rituals and beliefs.
