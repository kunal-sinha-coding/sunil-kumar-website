India to Bharat · Essay #197

Why schooling? Not for cramming ‘information’ but to learn mind management

1. Wisdom of Bharat: “It is good and very grand to conquer external nature, but grander still to conquer our internal nature (our mind). It is grand and good to know the laws that govern the stars and planets; it is infinitely grander and better to know the laws that govern the passions, the feelings, the will of mankind.” This conquering of the inner man, understanding the secrets of the subtle workings that are within the human mind, and knowing it’s wonderful secrets is necessary to achieve greatness.” “The present system of education is all, wrong. The mind is crammed with facts before it knows how to think. Control of mind must be taught first. If I had my education to get over again and had any voice in the matter, I would learn to master my mind first, and then gather facts if I wanted them. It takes people a long time to learn things because they can’t concentrate their minds at will.” – Vivekananda.

2. I and I alone am responsible for myself – We’re our own creation: At any moment, how we think, talk, act, behave, our likes, dislikes, etc. are all dependent on the constitution of our mind. And so, schooling must help us understand how we’re slaves of the ‘mindset’ that we’re creating by our own thoughts-actions. Simply put, the process is: Sow a thought, reap an action. Sow an action, reap a habit. Sow a habit, reap a character. Sow a character, reap a destiny. Thus, ‘we are what our thoughts are’, and ‘on the quality of our thoughts, depends the quality of our life’. The above process of ‘sowing’ is going on continuously and we’re slaves of the ‘mindset’ or ‘samskaras’ i.e. the sum total or the resultant of all that I have sown and which are stored in our mind and drives us into repeat thought-actions. Thus, I am creating my samskaras, moment-to-moment, by my own thoughts-actions, as I respond to external situations.

3. Education must also help us Manage our Mind: Instead of focusing just on ‘knowledge’, it is imperative that Schooling must introduce us to our own mind, how it works, and show us how in and thru’ our daily living, we are all continuously “refining” or “polluting” our mind and creating “good” or “bad” tendencies and habits, based on our ‘responses’ to desires, people, events, etc.

Daily, regular Self-development practices, e.g. prayers, worship, quality in work, working for good of others, yoga, etc. are planned processes to continuously sow ‘good’ thoughts-actions and refine our samskaras. These practices have to be undertaken regularly, daily, continuously, at all times, in every moment, in every choice that we make, and for our whole life. We have to continuously practice good values and ideals in our daily thinking and actions.

4. Knowing our Mind is the 1st step to Managing it: Mind is our only friend and the only villain. I alone am responsible for using it as a friend and then it can guide me towards the higher. And, the same mind can become a villain if I succumb to our own past wrong tendencies, which take me towards the lower, not the higher. Today we’re slaves and totally dependent upon our mind, because the impressions of our past thoughts-actions are totally embedded and have created our tendencies and habits. Since I am totally identified with my mind and am what my mind is, it is my responsibility to understand all aspect relating to the idea of “mind” and befriend it. A “refined” mind can inspire and introduce me to my real “Swaroopa”, my unchanging, blissful, divine ‘Self’.

4.1 Man, Jivatman , is Body and Mind, enlivened by Consciousness, Life, Spirit, Rooha, Atman, ‘Self’, within. The mind is the “bridge” between ‘body’, which is matter, and ‘Life’ within. The mind is the subtlest form of matter; formed out of the subtlest part of food. It is all pervasive; it draws its consciousness from the Spirit. The mind is our only instrument of perception, of knowing things externally; it also acts as a veil, which does not allow us to experience the full potential of ‘Kingdom of God within us’; that is why we remain unfulfilled, and keep on looking for fulfillment outside.

The whole purpose of education, religion, work, living is to somehow “purify or refine-the-mind”, “chitta-suddhi”, so that it allows us to befriend the “Light of life within”. This “purification” comes thru’ righteous conduct, kartavya-palan, work as worship for good of others, prayers, worship, meditations, etc. So, it is aptly said “Seek you first the Kingdom of God and his righteousness and everything else will be added unto you”. It is up to each one of us to rewrite the constitution of our mind so that it can manifest more and more of our infinite potential and give us the ‘poorna-ananda’ that we hunger for.

Let’s look at the nature of mind: While being one integrated, subtle, whole, it seemingly performs four-functions: As Manas it is the input and coordinator of all the data coming thru’ 5-senses. The data is referred to the Chitta, which is the storehouse of all previous data and the field for flow of thoughts. The Buddhi then analyses, decides, chooses the ‘action’ and activates the body. Finally, the ‘I-sense and me-mine’, the Ahamkara

The mind is constructed to look “outside” through the five sense organs. We’ve somehow to turn its attention inwards, to look at itself, thru’ itself! Once I become aware of my thoughts, I realize that I am only their witness; I am not the thoughts so I can control them. By its very nature mind is restless like a monkey; drunk with ‘Desires’; stung by scorpion of ‘Jealousy’; and also possessed by devil of ‘Pride’, therefore very difficult to control! The more the control we’ve, the greater as a person we’re; we’re the Shrestha, the elite and role model, whatever be my vocation.

Mind is a continuous Flow of thoughts – wanted/unwanted. By its very nature mind has a tendency to ‘flow down’ – be tempted enjoying the experiences of the sense organs, as available in world outside. It needs continuous ‘Sadhna’, i.e. daily-planned-prolonged-difficult-struggle, to turn the mind inwards – like reversing the flow of river. It gets attached to what it likes very easily, but detaches with difficulty. Mind is material in nature, but being subtlest matter, is able to absorbs consciousness, like a pot absorbs the heat of fire below. It Carries the impression of every thought, word and action. Awareness that “I am not the mind” helps in control. Continuous, regular, repetitive practice is needed to purify and develop powers of mind.

There are two fundamental methods for self-development and inculcation of values: “Repetitive thoughts, words and actions” and “learning to focus on the essentials and with drawl from the non-essentials, i.e. “Constant practice” and “Focus on Good, Better, Best”. The first step that schooling should take is to: Teach us the nature of mind and how it works. Then as students it is our responsibility to reflect, introspect and be intellectually convince. Finally, as students we have to walk-the-talk, i.e. use our viveka to know and the willpower, concentration, etc. to choose those thoughts-actions that lead us towards the highest and best.
