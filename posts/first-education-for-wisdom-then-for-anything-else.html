India to Bharat · Essay #227

First, education for wisdom, then for anything else!

1. Need for education to get Wisdom: A Supreme Court Judgment has observed “The past 5-decades after independence have witnessed constant erosion of essential social, moral and spiritual values and an increase in cynicism at all levels. Schools can and must strive to restore and sustain the universal and eternal values oriented towards unity and integration of people and their moral and spiritual growth enabling them to realize the treasure within”.

2. Education must be a Process of Self-development: Success in life doesn’t come from IQ alone. More important are our ability to manage our negative emotions, EQ, awaken our viveka, SQ, and direct our progress towards the higher dimensions of work and life. While we can continuously strive to develop both these abilities throughout life, build character and attain ‘perfection’, we cannot improve IQ very much, in spite of struggles. Thus, not ‘marks’ but it is ‘character’ that guarantees happiness and prosperity.

3. Education must awaken Viveka: Just think, every moment we’re making choices. We have two basic choices. That which gives immediate pleasure or that which may not be pleasurable now but we know, it is for our own good in the long run. Reflection will help us recognize that based on our ‘choices’ and ‘decisions’, from moment to moment, at work or in family, we are actually creating our habits, character and destiny. We have various codes of behavior in family, society, work, as well as rules, human values, ethics, etc. to guide us. Vivek is the ability to ‘know’ and ‘choose’ the long term good over immediate pleasures.

4. Instilling the Habit of daily Reflection: If, throughout schooling, through reflection, exploration, discussions, self-study, we are inspired to recognize that ultimately, in the long run, we all wish to be happy, healthy, successful, peaceful, then we will strive to harmonize our desires and goals towards ‘happiness’. We will then reflect on the question: How do I build my inner strength to ‘know’ and do the ‘right’?

5. We have Freedom of Choice: Ultimately of course, we’re free to choose; and as we choose so shall be our work and life. Thus, we and we alone am responsible for our lives, none else!

Can Schooling provide Time for Reflection & Character-building!
