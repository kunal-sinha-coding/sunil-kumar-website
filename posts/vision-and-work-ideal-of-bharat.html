India to Bharat · Essay #207

Vision and work ideal of Bharat

Why do we Work? According to modern Indian thinking, based on Western ideas, our daily work and profession is meant for earning a living, money, promotions, status, etc. and it is believed that an important purpose of work and career is to get promoted to more money, higher status; the idea being that a worker is low and thus, the goal is to rise from being a worker to the highest rung. This most certainly is not the ideal of Bharat!

Bharat holds that our status, preacher or worker, high or low, is irrelevant . What matters, is: In and through our wok and life are we on the path of ‘ dharma ’, righteous and ethical conduct, to the ultimate goal and purpose of human life, Perfection? If we are, worker or preacher, then alone are we in ‘Sur’, we are the Srestha, elite and a role model, because we are on the path and in close proximity to Perfection. On the other hand, a selfish person of the highest status, indulging in corruption, is ‘Asur’, is not fit for leadership, because he is not in harmony with the goal of life.

Bharat’s work-ideal asserts that our daily work and living is our only opportunity to walk either towards or away, from the goal of life. The issue therefore is: how to engage people in that work and vocation which is likely to be most helpful in their path to highest Perfection, while allowing freedom to earn and fulfill their desires, considering that of course, most people are not even aware of or interested in the higher dimensions of work and life!

Modern worldview is based on the false idea that people are equal: Modern society is still grappling with the problem of how to identify, educate, select and place right people in the right positions of responsibility. Historically all ancient societies, including Indian caste idea, had a thumb rule to solve this perplexing issue, go by status and seniority of birth. Was it such a bad rule, considering that it is universal, is time tested, is still followed and remains the most common yardstick throughout the world? And, even in politics today!

The law of falsifiability states that anything which is false will be exposed and cannot stand the test of time. The very fact that in spite of all attempts to destroy caste, it still remains, should make us think and reflect and try to appreciate and understand the purpose, if any, in the idea of caste. A question arises: If people are born equal why do they get unequal circumstances? If people are equal who is responsible to develop the weaker and why? The question is: Does the principle of ‘as we sow, so shall we reap’ stop functioning or does it continue?

Bharat had created a social structure where people could engage in work and duties, i.e. vocation or occupation, where they could excel, based on their inherited tendencies and aptitudes, and learn the necessary knowledge and skills while growing, without undue effort and time.

Without awareness of this connection between ‘vocation’ and ‘goal of life’, the tendency today is to go after work, which can give more money, power and status. We forget to check for ourselves and ask: In and through my daily work and living am I continuously growing towards maximizing my unlimited potential for perfection, peace and happiness? If my work is beyond my character and competence, then I may not remain on the path towards Perfection.

Today, we ignore the fact that people are not equal! If we allow people full of desires, and prone to wickedness, as we do in democracy, to emerge as leaders and professionals, will they ever work for the good of people? Bharat recognizes that people are not equal; they have different characteristics, tendencies, abilities, etc.

Srestha: All people are to be nurtured, educated, groomed to be a ‘srestha’, to be committed to ‘ dharma’ , i.e. to kartavya-palan , to striving for excellence in work, care and concern for each other and working for good of society. And, in a vocation suited to their character and tendencies, swadharma, where they can excel.

Governance: The wise elders of society are responsible to guide people to fulfill their duties, punish those who do not do so, and socially boycott, and give on the spot judgments, without long lasting legal battles of wit, of today. Only a very, very few are men of wisdom. They alone are fit to be teachers and role models of society. Why? They are by and large, no longer hankering after artha and kaama and therefore, they have the moral authority and duty to guide and mentor the society, without any hankering, expectations.

Administration & Workforce: The men of wisdom have to first train the administrators, who must be persons who have greatly reduced their desires and are now striving to understand the nuances of ‘ dharma ’ and are therefore ready to govern and administer fairly and justly. The Administrators have the responsibility to create and monitor conducive systems within which individuals and groups can engage in work suitable to their tendencies. Among them will be entrepreneurs, with tendencies for creating wealth, and skilled/unskilled workforce, whose tendencies will be towards fulfillment of kaama while giving their best at work.

Conclusion: Bharat’s system of division into four varnas was the stepping stone to civilization, making one rise higher and higher in proportion to one’s qualities, learning and culture. The ideal of caste is meant to raise all humanity slowly and gently towards the realization of that great ideal of the noble and good men, a karma-yogi, for whom work is worship, who through his vocation serves God in man so as to attain the purpose of life, in and thru’ daily work-life.
