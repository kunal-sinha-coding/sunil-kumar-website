India to Bharat · Essay #103

Understanding our only Friend & Enemy – our Mind

Shouldn’t we wonder, how and who is processing and making sense of the vast knowledge of the external world that is coming to us thru our 5-sense organs, instantly and continuously throughout our lives? Where is this infinite data being stored? Why and from where do thoughts come; desires and feelings arise? Who is there that processes, coordinates, understands and makes sense of all the incoming data and then decides to act, react or ignore it all!

Isn’t it really surprising that no one has bothered to introduce us to our mind; our only instrument of Perception for ‘receiving, processing, storing and choosing’?

Unfortunately, modern scientists, academia, religions and we all are busy seeking to know and deal with the external universe of matter but are blissfully ignorant and unaware of how our ‘Subjective’, ‘inner-world’, governed by our Mind, has enslaved and entrapped us! Unknowingly we have allowed our ‘Mind’, the ‘knower’, our ‘instrument’ to control our ‘present’; somehow we are enslaved by our respective experiences, worldview, likes/dislikes, and beliefs.

2. Our Wisdom: Fortunately, our ancients were Rishis and not monkeys, and intuitively realized very early that there was no possibility for ever knowing the infinite variety of matter and living-beings in the universe; so they made it their mission to reflect, to learn to go inwards, strive to know the ‘knower’ and discovered the mind, our only instrument for all ‘knowing’ and the field where flow all our thoughts, feelings and emotions. Let’s get introduced to our Mind and learn the ways how we may become Masters not slaves of our instrument, our Mind.

3. The mind is the bridge between the “Body-brain complex that is but inert-matter” and “Life, Consciousness” that enlivens the body and makes it as a Living-being, Jivaatman. Mind is one integral subtlest possible matter; so subtle that it is not only all-pervasive but is also capable of continuously, seamlessly and instantly performing 4-functions.

4. Being subtlest matter, mind imbibes Consciousness and becomes our instrument as well as the “Veil”, “Curtain”, that separates us and prevents us from realizing that we are Consciousness per se, not the limited consciousness of “I-sense”, that says, “I am the doer”; “I am the enjoyer”; “I experience”, etc. The Mind is the Veil that hides us from our real divine-Self, Consciousness, and makes us get identified with our limited, narrow, apparent-self, based on our “intentions-thoughts-words-deeds”, which says, ‘I am this body’!

5. The body without Mind and Life to enliven it is just dead-matter. It is our real Self, the ‘life’ within, which enlivens the body, and is the source of all potential, perfection, power, glory, peace and happiness that we seek. As ‘Self’, we are all “one”; and therefore, all goodness, ethics, morals ask us to imbibe commitment to human values, ‘helping-others attitude’, unselfishness, etc.

6. The Mind is our ‘enemy’ because it veils our Oneness with Consciousness. However, Mind is our only instrument and can become our ‘friend’ if we learn, practice and strive to pour newer, better thoughts-actions, through “righteous, ethical conduct, commitment to our duties, selflessness, prayers” etc. to purify the mind of the impressions of our ‘unrighteous, unethical, self-centered conduct’ impressions’.

7. The 4-Functions of Mind: The data continuously coming thru’ the 5-sense organs is received and coordinated by the in-put function of the mind, manas. All the new experiences as ‘intentions-thoughts-words-action’ are retained as impressions in the chitta, storehouse of impressions, memories. They are instantly referred to the storehouse of all previously acquired knowledge and experiences, chitta; based on which buddhi takes decisions, right or wrong and these choices-actions become mine and this “I-sense”, “Me and Mine”, ahamkara, is the 4th function.

As long as the mind limits us to Me and Mine it is our enemy; when we learn to awaken our buddhi to Viveka-buddhi, then the same mind can become our friend and help us realize our divinity. Vivek-buddhi, is that which enables us ‘choose’ the right, the selfless long-term good of all; and will-power is its dynamic aspect that enables us to walk-the-talk towards ‘Best’, our universal goal, an idea and ideal of ‘Perfection’, our common ‘North’.

8. Imbibing Vivek-buddhi: Purpose of schooling and growing up according to our traditions was to awaken our Buddhi, and develop strength and powers of Mind to enable us choose and act on “Right” decisions. So, the traditional focus during growing-up was to discuss, explore, discover, develop Viveka-buddhi to determine Right, Good, Better, Best decisions on which we should strive to act.

9. Some feature of mind: Mind is the field for flow of thoughts, feelings, emotions, and repository of impressions of past thoughts-actions, Samskaras. Both wanted and unwanted thoughts flow through the mind, based on our samskaras. We can observe the thoughts; we are thus the subject, the observer of the mind. We are not the thoughts; we are the knower of thoughts. We forget this and get identified with our thoughts, flow with them and ‘react’. With practice we need to learn to respond with viveka-buddhi, instead of unthinking reaction.

10. We cannot see the bottom of a lake, because its surface is covered with ripples. If the water is muddy or is agitated all the time, the bottom will not be seen. The bottom of the lake is our own true Self, the Perfection within; the lake is the Mind and the dirt and waves are the impressions, Samskaras, that we leave in our mind based on our thoughts-words-actions.
