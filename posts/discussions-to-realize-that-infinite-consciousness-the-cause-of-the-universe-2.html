India to Bharat · Essay #134

Discussions to realize that infinite consciousness, the cause of the universe (2)

We’ve seen that, That-infinite is the cause of this finite; and, with our finite mind, it isn’t possible to know either ‘That infinite Consciousness’ nor this ‘vast, infinite universe of matter’. Fortunately, a way has been discovered – we can try to know That-Infinite thru’ the finite world, ‘jagat’ and finite ‘myself’ – Know thy Self, and you know the Infinite!

This grand and profound truth of Truths was discovered by the most ancient psychologists and scientists of mind-brain-consciousness and their discoveries have been stated in Vedanta, and are being continuously discussed, debated and accepted, based on direct observation – not mere belief. Unfortunately, we the brilliant intellectuals and scientists of today have failed to discover this most ancient wisdom of Consciousness but are busy seeking for the infinite in the finite world of matter!

Of course, the ancient scientists had warned that even knowledge of my-self, the self within me, needs and presupposes ceaseless striving, not only ceaseless but pure; and pure striving presupposes a pure heart, which in its turn depends on the practice of the cardinal and causal virtues, ethical and righteous conduct, fulfilling our respective duties, Swadharma, etc. These practices are necessary to purify the mind, to expand its consciousness so that the infinite consciousness within can shine forth and illumine all our thoughts, words and actions. Vedanta isn’t based on belief in this or that God or Prophet; it is an intellectual exercise fit for the best minds, based on reasoning and logic. By studying it, the mind expands towards infinite and we gradually attain the knowledge of the whole; of our self, of purpose of our being, of creation, of That infinite divinity, the one God of all religions; which Vedanta calls Brahman, signifying infinitude.

We can know something by comparing, distinguishing, differentiating its qualities and features from other things, firstly from all others of the s ame family, Visheshan, and secondly, from everything else, Lakshan. However, it is very important to note that though God is one infinite consciousness, Vedanta calls it Ishwar, when we discuss its qualities and features as if it is finite and with qualities and feature! Let’s identify God, based on this principle of differentiation: –

i) Visheshan: We and God both have Consciousness/intelligence which prompts us into actions; so, we both belong to same family! However, there are vast differences between God and us! We can create small things, have some power, knowledge, whereas Ishwar creates the whole cosmos; is all-knowing and is imbued with infinite power. Even our consciousness comes only from Him. Though free from birth and death, for the good of people and to guide them, He incarnates Himself as Prophet, Son of God, from time to time, and Religions get created to guide mankind.

ii) Lakshan: The USP of God which differentiates it from everything else, are:

Satyam, Truth, Existence, that remains eternally and is changeless. God alone never changes. In contrast the world, jagat, is ever-changing and is truth appearing in various Names-forms, as effect. God has to be the ultimate Cause, not an effect. Had God been the effect, we would have had to look for another cause for it. And, then again, a cause for that, and so on, endlessly; and this cause cannot be ‘nothing’ or ‘zero’. And so, God is accepted as the ultimate, root, Cause.

Gyanam , pure Consciousness/intelligence; knowledge per se, not Knowing this or that like us! Intelligence is necessary for initiating/governing any action, though we may call it automatic or natural reaction. Consciousness per se must be changeless for only then it can admit or let go knowledge of this or that. In deep sleep there are no objects to be known and we know nothing; yet on waking up we can say ‘we knew nothing’ only because Consciousness alone was; there was nothing separate to be known! God is all knowing because Its Consciousness illumines everything.

Anantam , infinite; God is unchanging and so, it is one infinite consciousness, totally, perpetually blissful, fulfilled, Ananda, in its inherent nature.

It should be noted that ‘change’ can be perceived only in the background of something, which is changeless! And, unless there is a ‘change’ it is not possible to sense the changeless with our sense organs! Thus, God is the Cause but is not perceivable. God exists as the material cause in the world which is available for all our vyavahaar, transactions. The purpose of universe is to enable us understand and regain our identity with the singular, infinite consciousness, the material stuff, the Cause. God is eternally present as Consciousness; it alone illumines all things and is unlimited and this infinitude, is the third lakshana of God. It is through the effect, the creation, that we are trying to understand the cause, God.

The Mystery and Paradox: Even though God is the Cause, the material stuff, and so the world is non-different from It, i.e., God. We all see the world as independent of God! And, this is natural, because the Cause need not be visible for direct perception; just as sugar in Sherbet or our forefathers not being visible, though they’re the very cause of my being here! Thus, when we see jagat bereft of God it is our error. Note: Jagat, independent of God, doesn’t exist!
