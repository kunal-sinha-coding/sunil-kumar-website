India to Bharat · Essay #161

Who are our role models? What are our national ideals & values?

Who are our Role Models? A well-known principle is, whatever are the ideals of the elite of society, the Shrestha, and whatever they value and do, that becomes the norm followed by people! Who are the elite of society today? What are their ideals and values? Are they aware of the indigenous ideals and values of India that is Bharat? Are our indigenous ideals part of mainstream and minority schooling and civil services exams? Are our Political leaders agreed on our national ideals and values? Are our educated the role models of our ideals and values?

Our Problem – ignorance of our own Ideals! We are not taught, and so are obviously ignorant of the thousands of years of history of our composite culture as depicted in our epics Ramayana, Mahabharat, Upanishads; Life and teachings of our spiritual giants, Mahavira, Buddha, Shankara, Ramanuja; the Sikh gurus, countless Saints in the north south, east and west of ours and other Religions who have taken roots here; and, the recent earth-movers like Vivekananda, Aurobindo, Raman Maharishi, Gandhiji, etc., etc. These spiritual giants depict our history; and these leaders of men, are our historical guides, mentors and role models from whom alone can we get the inspiration to strive to be good, better, best.

These historical saints and sages are the sources from where our political and education systems should have drawn inspiration and determined our ideals along with the spirit of the Fundamental Duties of our Constitution and Vivekananda who has been nominated by the government, as our National ideal and role model to be followed. Alas we all have miserably failed to determine let alone imbibe the national ideals! The USP of India is spirituality; not politics, not history, not antics of money, wars, Kings!

We can’t blame the educated because we’ve denied them the opportunity to know and love our own indigenous culture. All our role models, who control and manage the affairs of the nation, come thru’ schools and colleges. Throughout their growing up, their learning is based on teachers, who may not be good role models; and, ideologies of prescribed books, which are all highly critical of our own indigenous systems, worldview, social issues, etc. based on how the West with its purely commercial and power-grabbing interests, looks down on us. We have thus, pushed the young towards the ideal of money-making; and have failed to inspire them to aspire for higher ideals.

Subjects taught don’t inspire: History ignores our profound ancient spiritual wisdom and civilization of our Kings and their ideals. Philosophy, Psychology, totally ignore our wisdom of ‘Oneness’, of mind, spirit, consciousness. Political theories talk of all Western ideas, totally ignoring our ideals of Raja-dharma. Social Sciences, ignore the scientific wisdom of our social organizations, of Four-stages of life, Four-groups of people, Four- purusartha, i.e. desired-goals, and respective responsibilities, values, duties of each; and the matrix for matching character with duties!

The focus of education today is only on knowing, memorizing, repeating in exams! There is no focus on building character and imbibing virtues and commitment to fulfilling our responsibilities and duties. There is no focus on being a good citizen, who is in love with the nation and is committed to its values and ideals

Schooling doesn’t inspire us to strive to imbibe the spirit of Fundamental Duties of the Constitution – love and respect for our ancient composite culture and the spirit of tyaga and seva displayed by our freedom fighters; our ideal of unity, of oneness, of work as worship, harmony of religions, brotherhood, striving for excellence, etc. No one in education or religions is responsible to help us imbibe these ideals and character!

Shouldn’t religions work together with academia to disseminate our common, universal values and ideal? Shouldn’t they together inspire us to achieve the highest goal of human life in and thru’ our daily working, enjoying and living? Shouldn’t they together inspire us to get established in self-development practices thru the habit of prayers, meditation, worship, etc. according to our respective religions? What is the fruit of our religion? Isn’t it spiritual growth and feelings of love, care, concern for all, thru’ our respective religions?

Unfortunately, modern schooling and religions inculcate no awareness of our ideals, no love for the nation, no commitment to work unselfishly for the good of the nation and its people and, absolutely no common vision, clarity, agreement on what exactly do we stand for!

What are our highest ideals and values? Ideals are to be imbibed by us individually. All schools, colleges, institutes, as well as all Religious leaders, minority schools, NGOs, Faith groups, etc. must be held responsible and accountable for promoting, instilling, imbibing these national ideals and values and the spirit of Fundamental Duties.

The best of East and West: Schooling, particularly Teacher education and the Examinations for entry into the All India Civil Services must have subjects, self-development workshops, exposure to armed forces values and discipline, awareness of societal problems in cities and rural areas, etc. so that they understand, appreciate, imbibe the spirit of our composite culture and Fundamental Duties.

Every teacher, public official must know what our forefathers as well as other nations have thought on the most momentous questions of life. Let them see first what our own values, ideals, goals, are. Let them then see what others are thinking and doing now. Let us find a way which combines the best of both, let us be pioneers of the harmonious blending of our own ancient wisdom and modern trends. Only those must be selected as teachers and leadership positions who have love for India, its ideals, and its composite culture.
