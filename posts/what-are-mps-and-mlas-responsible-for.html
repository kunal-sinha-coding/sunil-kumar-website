India to Bharat · Essay #203

What are MP’s and MLA’s responsible for?

1. Have we ever wondered what are MP’s and MLA’s responsible for? Nothing whatsoever!

Duties and responsibilities of MPs and MLAs are not defined in the Constitution. Even in the Rules of Procedure and Conduct of Business there is no such provision that defines duties and responsibilities of members of Parliament or through which the accountability can be fixed on non-performing MPs. Even the state Assemblies admit that there is no rule through which the non-performing MLAs can be pulled up.

Only Sikkim has a provision which says, “It will be their prime duty to maintain communal harmony and peace among the people of their respective constituencies! No wonder political animals take us for a ride, election after election, glibly making promises, which they know very well they’ve no ability to deliver. Why have our most intelligent ‘indian’ minds accepted this funny idea of governance where neither bureaucrats nor politicians are responsible and accountable for anything?

2. Bharat’s idea – We need ‘rulers’ established in Dharma: Dandniti is the science of governance, punishment, and the mechanism to control the behaviour of all and to guide everyone on the path of dharma. It is a mechanism to destroy evil as the sun destroys the darkness. When all are asleep, it is the dand and the fear of dand (chastisement) which is awake and ultimately it is the concept of dand which regulates the behaviour of all in the society. According to Bhishma, if dandniti is destroyed, the essence of justice will disappear and the social system based on four varnas will be disturbed. On the destruction of dandniti and instability in Rajdharma, all people will suffer from many evils.

The dand is the synonym of dharma: On account of fear of dand, one never involves himself in sinful activities. If dand is not observed everything will be eclipsed by darkness. People can live happily only if they live under the law and abide by the rules and regulations of the state.

3. Today no one seems to have responsibility!

To deliver justice to all without any discrimination, on the basis of dharma is the basic aim of government. The ‘ruler’, king, is the apex of administration and controls all the organs of government. The king appoints those as ministers who are intelligent, dutiful, honest, of strong character, obedient in nature, with a general disposition, knowledge of the scriptures, conduct, birth, self-control, mercy, strength, energy, dignity and forgiveness. “The well-born brahmana, wise and humble, fearless and followers of Dharma, guide the king in every matter.

It is the moral and ethical duty of government to guide everyone on the path of dharma, and take due care and look after the development of all living in the society.

4. Is anyone or everyone fit to rule, irrespective of character? Yudhishthira asks: “How came the need to have a king? “How did the word ‘Rajan’ come into use when a king is addressed? He observes, “a king is like any other human being on the earth. His body and limbs are like those of anyone else. His understanding and his senses are similar to those of many others. He has the same joys and griefs, the same number of years to live on the earth, like anybody else. How then did it happen that he is considered different? This world is made up of men who are far superior to him in intelligence, bravery and all accomplishments. And yet, this one man rules the others: though they are superior to him. Why should it be the rule that one man is worshipped by all the others?”

5. Bhishma replies: “In the beginning there was no king. There was no punishment. These two were not needed then. Men were all righteous and were in ‘Sur’, earning and enjoying and walking in dharma, in harmony, towards the highest human goal; each man protected the other; Vedantic worldview of ‘oneness’ prevailed. As time passed on, however, the hearts of men began to be invaded by Asuric qualities of “Covetousness, materialism, greed, acquisitiveness, self-centeredness; the worldview of ‘oneness’ was lost and was overtaken by the evil of adharma, the mind got clouded and the sense of right and wrong began to wane. This is a natural fall and was even so with the men of distant times.

6. When ‘greed’ comes into life , men begin to want things which did not belong to them. The next passion to be born was lust. Lust can never exist alone. It has to have a companion and so wrath came into existence. As soon as these terrible passions found places in the hearts of men, righteousness had to beat a hasty retreat. Along with this confusion happened another great calamity. The Vedantic wisdom that kept man on the righteous path disappeared. Righteousness was completely lost to the world.

7. The good then prayed and reflected and wisdom issued forth from within them on the subjects of Dharma (righteousness), Artha, Kama, and Moksha (i.e. fulfilment, freedom, ananda, oneness with all, perfection, the ultimate goal of all human life). Since, men are mostly led by chastisement their treatise they called Dandaniti. They chose one of the fittest persons Prithu and he, as well as all those who are born in his line and imbibe dharma, were to be Kings and to make all men regard Dharma as the foremost of all things.

Why Rajan? “Because he pleased all the people he was called ‘Rajan’. Because he healed the wounds of afflicted people he was called ‘Kshat-riya’. And again, because the ‘earth’ became celebrated for the prevalence ‘of dharma’, during his reign, she was called ‘Prithvi’.

8. Who is a King? Kings were recognized and deified across the world and even today Japan, U.K, and many countries, still have a ‘king’. In North and South America, or Egypt and Japan and many others, it was the sun that was worshiped and was considered the ultimate ruler. The king was considered the earthly representative of the sun, and his dynasty was the solar dynasty. Plato recognized that the ideal ruler or administrator is the philosopher king. The most widely idea around the globe was that of the divine monarchy. These were found in India, China, Japan, Mexico, Peru, Babylonia, Mesopotamia, Sumer, Ur, Egypt, Ethiopia, the Sudan, in ancient Greece, as well as in Scandinavia and Celtic Europe. In all these civilizations, through the ages, the king was spoken of as a divine representative to ensure compliance and strict obedience to rule of one authority, educated, trained and imbued with Dharma.

9. Responsibility must be clearly fixed: In an ideal system, just as in a ‘battle’, responsibility and accountability is clearly fixed in the leader on the spot, who has been duly trained to make right choices for the overall good. Similarly, in governance the authority of the ‘king’ has to be fixed, without any doubts and discussions.

‘As we sow, so do we reap’ is a fundamental principle of life and birth and the Mahabharat, the fifth Veda, says that a pure man, when his punya, becomes exhausted, descends from heaven to earth and is born as a king. Such a person is indeed great and is a portion of God on earth. He has a heritage of divine intelligence and he is superior to all the others. He is established by the gods and he is not to be slighted. This is the reason why the world cannot command him but he can command the world because of his spiritual and moral authority; one needs ‘tapas’ even to apply dand! That is why the multitude obey ‘his’ words of command but we’re afraid of punishing the rich and powerful.

Unfortunately, as ‘indians’ we treat justice as ‘blind’ and so we’ve plethora of false witnesses and IQ-chalak-highly paid lawyers, who can easily argue both ‘for’ or ‘against’, depending upon their client; and we keep cases pending for ages! Fees of Lawyers must be fixed and same for both parties. Lawyers should be responsible to separate the trivia from truth; be peace-makers and put One page-brief for judgments based on ‘dharma’!

We should make the MP/MLA’s responsible and accountable for all matters within their Constituency along with the District officers by setting up various ‘panchayats’ of enlightened permanent residents, NGO’s, Academia, businesses, Youth, etc. all working together, transparently, so that the development of Constituency can be planned on long-term basis by those who live and work there.
