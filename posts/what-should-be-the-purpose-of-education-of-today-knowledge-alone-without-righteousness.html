India to Bharat · Essay #092

What should be the purpose of education of today? Knowledge alone without righteousness?

Is it just to give knowledge? Then, what is knowledge? Knowing implies learning, expertise, information, intelligence, knowhow. Knowledge is facts, information, skills acquired by a person. Knowledge is awareness of objects, phenomena, situations, etc. Shouldn’t we at least start questioning, discussing, exploring, what knowledge do we really need, before giving?

Beliefs and assumptions are of course natural, and we take them for granted. Philosophy divides certain assumptions as knowledge through Observation (Empiricism) and knowledge thru’ Reason (Rationalism); And this is where cynicisms come in. Religion and God as our highest goal, are a good example of both Observation and Reason based assumptions!

Have any of us, scientists, academics, teachers, ever wondered what knowledge and life skills do we really need ‘to know’ and ‘walk-the-talk’ towards the ‘higher and highest ‘goal’ of life?

To be educated, be scientific, be enlightened citizens, be a Shrestha/Leader, be religious, the most practical ‘schooling’ would be, to imbibe the spirit of selflessness, of righteousness, of love for our fellow beings so that we may feel love, care, concern for the whole creation.

The highest Goal and the most “practical way to love God, is to love our fellow beings. If we feel for others in the same way as we feel for our own dear ones, we love God and are on the path to the highest goal. If instead of seeing faults in others, we look within ourselves, we’re loving God.

If instead of robbing others to help ourselves, we rob ourselves to help others, we’re loving God. If we suffer in the suffering of others and feel happy in the happiness of others, we’re loving God. If instead of worrying over our own misfortunes, we think of ourselves as more fortunate than many, many others, we’re loving God. If we endure our lot with patience and contentment, accepting it as his Will, we’re loving God. If we understand and feel that the greatest act of devotion and worship to God is not to hurt or harm any of his beings, we’re loving God.

To love God as he ought to be loved, we must live for God and die for God, knowing that the goal of life is to love God, and find him as our own self.”

Why love each other? A priori, is Latin for, what comes first? Assumptions! Life is and must be based on some beliefs and assumptions. The first thing first is to get established in Righteousness, Dharma, because commitment to ethical conduct, commitment to our respective duties, Swadharma, and to universal values of love, peace, truthfulness, non-violence, righteousness, etc. alone purifies our mindset, our tendencies, samskaras, and prepares us to walk-the-talk towards good, better, best.

No one sees God, but many people, educated and religious, believe in this or that God; but instead of loving more and more in the name of God they keep dividing and spreading hatred and ‘killing’ others, who don’t believe in their God/Religion! So, unless established in Righteousness and selflessness, mere Religion or Academics are meaningless! God doesn’t come to say: Hi, I’m God; it’s nice to meet you; glad you could join our religion but not theirs! Awareness of God needs a spirit of selflessness not selfishness.

Idea of Karma Yoga: The first things first, keeping the end in mind, must be an assumption, based on the essence of our universal wisdom that education and growing up must be a process to give us conviction and strength of mind, will-power, and self-control to be able to strictly adhere to ethical conduct, while ‘earning and enjoying’ so as to remain on the path to universal idea of “Perfection”.

Since beginning of time, the Shrestha across the world have discussed these questions and the essence of their universal findings could and must be compiled and discussed as a part of our schooling and growing up so that we may continuously learn and strive to grow towards the highest and best in and thru’ our daily work.

Belief and Sraddha and fixing our universal Goal: To make an argument for God’s existence, if we’re not God or Godman, we’ve to rely on mere faith or believe and probably rely on a rational argument.

We then need to take a rational, scientific, and mathematical approach to discuss and determine the very goal and purpose of working and living; relationship between ‘man-man’ and ‘man-creation-God’, all based on the rational essence of what our ancient forefathers have ‘discovered’ to fix our idea of Perfection, e.g., Know thy self’; ‘love thy neighbour as thy self’; ‘The Kingdom of God is within you. Seek you first the Kingdom of God and his Righteousness and everything else will be added unto you’. ‘Each soul is potentially divine’; ‘Atithi devo bhava …Worship strangers, mother, father, teacher, spouse and river, trees, sun, wind, forces of nature, Saints, etc.

Besides acquiring knowledge of this and that, growing up must be the opportunity to learn and practice managing our mind and universally we’re asked to be ethical, moral, righteous, selfless, serve/help others, to be and to do good, pray, worship, etc. Only such a purified mind can help us in seeking freedom from sufferings, fulfilment, peace, happiness aka Self/God realization, Heaven, Nirvana, Salvation, Mukti, Perfection, etc.
