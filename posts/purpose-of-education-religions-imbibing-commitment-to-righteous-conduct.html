India to Bharat · Essay #091

Purpose of education & religions – Imbibing commitment to righteous conduct

Reflections: Is it good to be good or bad? Shouldn’t we first discuss, explore, argue, have a better understanding of good vs bad? May we not assume it is good to be more and more unselfish, selfless; committed to our respective duties, to care and concern for more and more? Of course, we can easily accept goodness as an ideal to be followed by others or even for our own long-term good. The problem really is that even though we may wish to be and to do good, our mind and our thoughts are usually looking only for immediate pleasures and impel us into wrong, based on our tendencies! So, perhaps we may agree that to be good in the present we need to struggle to practice managing our mind and our responses, moment to moment. And this isn’t part of education!

A. Dissatisfaction with Present: With modern education and divisive religions and politics, we get self-serving political animals and systems where responsibility and accountability are not fixed; promises are made but never fulfilled and people have the freedom to rise to their level of incompetence – there is no balance between aspiration, character, and competence! The undue focus on 3R’s, degrees, individual freedom, more industrialization and production for more employment as an economic model, etc. has given rise to consumerism, greed, deteriorating human values, pollution, and wanton destruction of natural resources.

This mad rush for artha and kaama without character can certainly not be considered either good or the goal and purpose of human life.

B. Shared Vision of Future: We need clarity on the higher and better. We then need to get established in good habits and Self-development practices to develop ethical values, discipline, strength, and a burning desire to walk-the-talk. And we need to learn ‘how’ to build strength of mind, will-power, self-control to grow towards good, better and overcome habits and tendencies which curb our growth.

Can divisive and destructive politics of democracy, inefficient, non-caring and self-serving governance, and mere 3R’s education, ever produce men of wisdom? The modern passion for democracy leads to respect for money and power but not character, and so no one today has the time and conviction to ‘define’ and create good people!

Our duty is to reflect how can we as enlightened citizens, unite and strive to work together with schools, colleges, and parent-teacher-associations, etc. to supplement and complement the modern education and HRD efforts to inspire the youth to ‘know’ and ‘learn to manage and direct our mind’ towards the higher dimensions of work and life, towards the common, universal ‘North’ of being happy, prosperous, fulfilled, healthy, swastha .

How can we as enlightened citizens reflect and strategize how to bring about a paradigm shift in the way we educate, train, select, and guide the young to careers from where they can grow towards good, better, best.

C. Steps towards Highest and Best – our universal “North”: How do we get inspired to practice Life Skills, Self-development processes, to manage our mind to choose and walk towards the higher and better, the long-term good?

Generally, we may agree that a person who has and is striving to have love, care, concern for others, and is also committed to universal values of “truth, non-violence, peace, righteous/ethical conduct aka Dharma” and to “kartavya-palan and fulfils all his duties for the good of samaaja, aka Swadharma”, is certainly good and striving to be better!

Viveka-buddhi: Every moment we have choices. Willy-nilly, whether we like it or not, we are continually in the process of “becoming” based on our choices and deeds. Our freedom of choice is to move either towards good or bad, using our will power to overcome the resistance of our past habits. Habit of fulfilling our duties and righteous conduct if instilled and imbibed during growing up, purifies the mind, which in turn strengthens the will power to be good.

Of course, we all do wish to become better, not worse, in and through all our goals, choices, motives, actions, relationships. And so, it is important to know, discuss, explore, and be convinced of the ‘direction’ towards which we should become better.

As a global society, divided by selfish religious preachers, politics of democracy, scientists of matter, we have yet to realize that ‘bad, average, good, and better’ make sense only if we first discuss, reflect, and get an idea of the ‘Best’, our universal ‘North’, and then strive to walk towards the good, better.

The essential purpose of parenting , growing up, education and religions, is and must be to awaken our ‘viveka-buddhi’ to focus and choose the ‘better’, the long-term good for ourselves and samaaja. And to help us get established in kartavya-palan, so that by fulfilling our duties we could remain on the critical path to attain the highest goal of life. Growing up must give us intellectual conviction and inspiration and ability to ‘know’ and ‘ walk-the-talk’ towards the ‘highest’.

It must prepare us i) to work in a spirit of seva-bhava and worship for good of others, aka Karma Yogi ii) to strive for quality and excellence in whatever we do iii) to consider our daily work as an opportunity for chitta-suddhi , i.e., purity of mind which helps us to be righteous.

Our challenge is not just to be educated but to become Mentors of society. Whether we’re leaders, professionals, entrepreneurs, workforce, we must be role models and mentors, the Shrestha, who could guide and provide long-term vision and systems for our growth towards good, better, best.
