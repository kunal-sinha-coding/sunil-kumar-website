India to Bharat · Essay #116

Bharat treats “Religion” as Righteous Conduct, not ‘Belief’ and “Philosophy as Darsan”, not philosophizing!

1. ‘Bharat’ somehow implies rural, undeveloped ‘poor’, traditional aspects of what ‘was’ rather than to what the modern present day ‘progressive’, industrialised ‘rich’ India, ‘is’! We think rural people as ‘ignorant’ because they’ve had no schooling whereas we’re men of ‘knowledge’ got from books and school!

# The Founders of religions were not intellectual scholars and philosophers; they’re inspirations to us because their words have spiritual and moral authority of Truth; because they’ve themselves directly experienced and ‘see’ the highest Reality, Reality of God and his Creation. Their simple words and practical illustrations are easily understood by the sincere seekers.

# Gandhi and Nehru could perhaps be seen as the representatives of Bharat and India.

# Gandhi stood for ‘God’, for revitalisation of our villages; strengthening our rural agrarian economy; handloom or the spinning wheel as “the panacea for the growing pauperism of India”; Khadi as the alternative rather than socialism, industrialism.

# Nehru was an atheist and advocated a modern industrialized India; “India must break with much of her past and not allow it to dominate the present”. Yet he was fully aware and with his political acumen was canny to accept that both Bharat and India existed simultaneously, and the problem was to produce a synthesis between the majority old and the angrezi-few! Harmony of the best of Bharat and the best of the West, as Vivekananda wished.

# The British traders and Missionaries were bent on portraying Bharat in a negative light; and, to counter them grew the need to portray the idea of a pure, spiritual Bharat that got corrupted by the mlechhas, the foreigners who corrupted and defiled her.

# We need today a paradigm shift towards the best of Bharat, of its wisdom and focus on exploring the inner, spiritual universe, to rediscover the unity within, while striving to work for good of society.

# India is still under the material influence of Western culture. The angrezi-elite who were in power for the last seven decades need to learn, along with the young generations now growing up and undergoing schooling, that our spiritual as well as material past was glorious; we had worthy heroes in the past; we were the richest nation with sound character and good social systems; our cities are corrupt places, but our masses are good, simple, spiritual.

# The good and the bad, the pure and the impure, exist simultaneously in every man; but to bring out the good, we need educational systems and spiritually oriented teachers and rulers who are men of wisdom and learning, so that they can inspire the citizens to be committed to the path of righteousness.

2. It is the purpose of Philosophy, Religions, Schooling to Create the Good!

# Philosophy is aptly referred as Darsana, because the ultimate purpose of any education, studies, Religions, or study of Philosophy is not just to ‘believe’ and intellectually know, but is to inspire us to strive for direct darsan, i.e., ‘seeing’, ‘experiencing’, ‘realizing’ the highest goal of man.

# As Bharat, we treat Religions as different ways to instil in people, commitment to “Sanatana-Dharma” i.e., “Eternal/Universal-human Values and Duties”.

# In contrast, modern religions create divisions in societies, because instead of focusing on commitment to duties, they divide people based on “belief”, into “my and not my-religion” and believe in “My Scripture and My God” and “Belonging to My or Your religion”.

# The Bharatiya spirit of “the whole world as our family” or being religious means being committed to universal, ethical, righteous conduct, irrespective of what our status or religion is!

# Because it is righteous conduct alone – not belief in this or that God or Religion – that is the ‘fruit’ of any religion, and guarantees that we’re on the Critical Path to our highest Goal.

# So, belief or belonging to this or that God or religion, is secondary; commitment to universal values and duties alone, is dharma, Religion.

# Our idea of History also is not just about kings and their doings nor just a subject to read to pass examinations, but it is aptly referred as “Itihaas”, i.e. “This is how it happened”. Our historical epics like Ramayan, Mahabharat, etc. embody righteous conduct, derived from the spiritual wisdom of the Upanishads, to inspire the masses to mould our life towards the highest goal of man and to have role models to emulate.

# Without Inspiration of our Darsana and Itihaas, the result is that modern religions, schooling, politics, have together produced corrupt, self-centred leaders and as a so-called educated society we’re unaware of the very purpose of human life; as discussed by the founders of each religion, and as discussed also in the philosophical Upanishads.

# How can we ever discuss and determine the very purpose of schooling, religions, philosophy without any idea of the very purpose of human life?

3. Our ‘biggest’ modern problem: We the modern educated Indians are taught and are well versed in the best of Western Philosophy, English literature, Science, History, Economics; but are totally ignorant of the idea and purpose of religions and schooling in accordance with our own time-tested wisdom!

Universal human values and righteous conduct as espoused by Religions and Philosophy, must be made an integral part of schooling, because they alone are in a position and, in fact, are meant to give us intellectual conviction in higher goals of work and life and inspire us to walk-the-talk to our common highest goal.
