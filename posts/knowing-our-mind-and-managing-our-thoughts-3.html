Knowing our mind and managing our thoughts (3)

First, a review of the truth of Truths: It is only because of Life, i.e., pure Consciousness, which illumines our Mind, that I am alive and a conscious human-being. And, it is only because the Mind keeps a record of our every single thought-word-action; coordinates the input from sense-organs, and has a power of intellect; that we’re able to interact with the infinite variety of objects. While it is true that it is only because of our mind that we’re what we’re, the truth of Truths is that in reality, it is pure Consciousness that really is the very source of ourselves. Our mistake is that we get attached to the ‘impressions’ of our Mind and thereby start feeling ‘separated’ from everything else and become self-centered.

We forget our real identity of Oneness with the whole creation as pure Consciousness, aka Atman, Kingdom of God, Spirit, Self, Ananda- Swaroopa . This knowledge of our Ananda- Swaroopa, ‘oneness’ with the whole, if imparted during parenting and schooling, can inspire us to strive to be unselfish and get established in practices to improve quality of our thoughts-words-actions which are continuously creating good or bad impressions in our mind.

Modern Problem – Focus on Objective but Ignorance of Subjective: Led by Western worldview, we’re busy studying everything outside-ourselves but have totally ignored the inner study of our Mind and what makes us Conscious. We’re therefore ignorant of the need for daily self-development/self-introspection practices to realize our ‘oneness-with-the-whole’, Ananda- Swaroopa!

Managing our Responses: If from a young age I learn and practice becoming aware of thoughts flowing in our mind, I can learn to pause for a moment, reflect, use viveka , discrimination, and build will power to choose to Respond in a better, positive way for long-term good of all. In this way we can deliberately plan to continuously create good impressions in our mind by better quality of thoughts-actions; and avoid being enslaved by our past impressions.

Processes to improve Quality of our Mind: Not ‘having’ to do what one wants to do, is real growth, freedom. To grow towards becoming better needs ‘struggle’, ‘effort’, it requires going against our nature. This is “Tapas”. The more the action is unselfish and Selfless the better it is, as it purifies the mind; conversely selfish actions, pollute our mind.

Parenting and schooling ought to establish us in self-development processes to learn how to deliberately rewrite and purify the quality of impressions in the mind so as to build the ability to control and direct actions Godward. We must learn and practice how to attain our long-term goals and achieve success, peace, prosperity, while attaining and enjoying short-term goals and pleasures.

Strategies to purify our mind: Whatever be our goals, material or spiritual, there’re time-tested strategies to purify our mind, develop powers of concentration, discrimination, self-control, and yet remain focused on success; without losing sight of our own God-ideal, highest purpose of life, Perfection, etc.: –

i) Constant practice; repetitive thoughts-words-actions ii) Learning to focus on the essentials and with drawl from the non-essentials iii) Knowing, Hearing, Reading, about what is the “most-Essential” , i.e. regaining our Ananda- Swaroopa ! iv) Reflecting, deep thinking, upon the immediate essentials and the most Essential iv) Meditating and practicing acting out of our divine spirit, Ananda- Swaroopa , for good of the whole creation, and ultimately making it our character.

Self-development practices to improve quality of thoughts that are common to all religions, may be covered under 4 broad themes: –

Our daily Work/Vocation is not just for ‘earning’; it is an opportunity to practice Meditation with eyes open and becomes a path to Perfection through: i) Fulfillment of all our duties ii) Quality and Excellence in whatever we do iii) Continuous improvement in our relationships and work iv) Working for the good and wellbeing of all v) Accepting fruits of our work as ‘prasad’ vi) Continuous awareness of Divinity within every being so that all duties and work is done in a spirit of service and worship. Work done with this attitude is Karma yoga and leads to Chitta-suddhi – purification of mind, so that the power and bliss of divinity within can be experienced.

Prayers is talking to God, who is our nearest and dearest, in our own language and making him our partner, with whom we can share all our problems and ask whatever we want, just as we pray to our parents for what we want. Of course, the parents and God give what they think will be good for us! So, accept as prasad whatever you get. Gradually we can realize that the more unselfish our prayer, the better. Since we are all ONE in Consciousness, Spirit, interconnected, interdependent, interrelated, therefore, the best is to Pray: May all beings be happy, May all beings be peaceful, May all beings be blissful.

Meditation is listening to God. Constant Remembrance and Repetition of Holy Name of God, while dwelling on his form, qualities, life and teachings, is one of the best methods of purifying the mind and developing powers of will, concentration, discrimination, strength, fearlessness. The mind acquires the quality of that on which it dwells. If we keep the mind on the highest and best, we attain the Perfection which is our Real nature.

Self-Knowledge: Self-study, introspection, understanding the purpose of work, of life, of who we’re, of how our mind functions, lives and teachings of great men, prophets, saints and sages and godmen of all religions, etc. can inspire us to ‘Seek the Kingdom of God within’ and have the conviction that ‘everything else will be added unto us’.
