India to Bharat · Essay #216

Democracy? We versus they? Or OUR better way?

1. We Vs They: Society is being divided into two opposite sides. On one side are ‘WE’ Indians. On the other side are ‘THEY’, the Bharati!

2. WE are the very few English speaking and educated elite who have been in power since the last many decades. ‘We’ have grown up admiring the glitter of the Western cultures, and we look down upon the religion of our forefathers. We have little understanding or respect for the wisdom of Bharat because we have only heard of its criticism and nothing about its profound wisdom. We are the Nehruiites of India, with a Western mindset. We are not Gandhiites of Bharat with a Vedantic mindset of “the whole world as our family”!

We believe in Western science and technology, their consumerism, and their lifestyle, much more than our own indigenous religious culture which enjoins on us to acquire ‘dharma’ during schooling so as to remain on the path towards ‘Mukti’. Most of us are either atheist or have little respect for rituals, puja, Gods. We and our children have been in multi-nationals drawing fat salaries, in IAS and other Government services, in politics and in armed forces, etc. with all kinds of privileges. We are the leaders in media, advertising, fashion, professional fields and corporations. In fact, after the Britishers left, we are calling the shots, we’re the brown sahibs. Life has been good for us.

3. The CONFLICT: Suddenly, ‘we’ the educated elite feel threatened and find that ‘they’, the 80% of masses, the idol worshippers, seem to be rising! They’re the Craftsmen, workers, labourers, paanwallah, chhotu working in small repair garages, Newspaper delivery boys, street-vendors, etc. They are the ‘exploited’, the illiterate or semi-educated masses, the common man who, according to we, ignorantly believe in idol worship, rituals, superstitions, Kumbh Melas, Teertha Yatras, worshippers of cows, trees, rivers, hills etc.!

4. Understanding the Bharati! They may believe only in some puja rituals and may not know of the wisdom of Bharat but that is not their fault. In any society it is the most intelligent, the educated (which ‘we’ supposedly are) whose obligatory duty it is, to create a machinery to scatter broadcast the highest wisdom to the masses. Unfortunately, we the English educated have been so busy in teaching just the 3R’s and enjoying ourselves and partaking of the consumerism and comforts of West, that we have failed to fulfill our obligatory duty to teach and spread wisdom in society.

And, how could we; because we knew nothing about the foundation of our culture, except all the criticisms heaped upon it by our conquerors – Moghuls conquered only physically but the West conquered us mentally, intellectually and spiritually too, and we still, till today, look down upon our traditional wisdom. We make no efforts even to learn, reflect upon and appreciate why is it that till today, in spite of all efforts to destroy it, Bharatiya culture with all its so-called ills, still survives, whereas all other indigenous cultures have been decimated by the onslaught of modern religions.

5. Our Better Way: Gandhiji presents the broad contours of an ideal Bharat in his Hind Swaraj. He says that under the impact of the British rule India is turning into an ‘irreligious’ country; not any particular religion, but rather of that Religion which underlies all religions; we are turning away from God. Modern India is moving away from our old values.

Unfortunately, in our ignorance and in the garb of secularism, ‘we’ are still not teaching the highest wisdom of Bharat which gives a universal message to all mankind: ‘earn’ and ‘enjoy’ through ‘righteous conduct, morals, ethics, human values, kartavya-palan, i.e. Dharma’ so as to remain on the path to realize and attain the highest goal, aim and purpose of human life. Even though, the Supreme Court, as well as our Fundamental Duties, enjoin on us to include in our schooling the teaching of the composite culture of Bharat, the human values which form the basis of all religions.

6. The Way Forward to “know, make and be good”: It was in 1984 that PM Indiraji decided to present Vivekananda as the role model for us, and Rajiv Gandhi announced it on 12th January 1985. Gandhiji, Nehru, Rajagopalachari, Subhash Bose, Aurobindo, Radhakrishnan, and other freedom fighters all have confirmed that they drew their inspiration, understanding of our ancient culture of ‘oneness’ and, love for the nation through Vivekananda. The way forward is to teach the ancient wisdom of Bharat through the life and teachings of Gandhiji, Vivekananda and others who have love for India that is Bharat.
