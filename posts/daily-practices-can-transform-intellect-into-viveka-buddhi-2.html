India to Bharat · Essay #169

Daily practices can transform Intellect into viveka buddhi -2

When individuals ignore swadharma they alone suffer; when teachers & officials err, society goes astray

1. Buddhi , intellect, lies unperceived, subtle, deep within us and so, in the modern IQ-era of science of matter, we’ve never even thought of Buddhi as a thing; what to talk of studying it! We know Buddhi, as common-sense, brightness, brilliance, smartness, cleverness, slyness, scholarship, IQ, marks, etc. and we know that Buddhi has to be cultivated for success in any worldly pursuits, good or bad.

Training the buddhi is always very difficult and needs focus and practice; it is much more difficult to direct it towards being good and better while focused on the best. However, it is the very purpose of schooling and religions to take steps necessary to culture, refine, purify, train Buddhi ; otherwise we are failing to produce right people for right duty and there is cut-throat competition for being in power for selfish ends.

2 . Aspects of Viveka Buddhi : Academia and religions have to come together and teach how during schooling, the Intellect can be trained to be

  • Alert, careful, discriminative to choose the long-term good for all, determinative to act righteously
  • Subtle to be able to discern the idea of service to god in man and living beings
  • Steady and firm in the conviction that fulfilment of duties alone guarantees happiness
  • Mature and vast to appreciate that the whole world is our family
  • Simple and child-like, trustworthy inspiring trust in others because of harmony in thoughts-words-deeds
  • Sharp, incisive, ability to find solutions to problems – a dull buddhi imitates blindly, justifies wrongs
  • Focused on the ideal and ability to overcome negative tendencies thru’ positive attitudes

3 . Daily Self-introspection, Swadhyaya: Given the choice, mind will willy-nilly go for ‘pleasures’, ease, comfort. To change habits and choose the different, the higher and better is not easy; much more difficult it is to struggle towards excellence in any field, good or bad. And to remain directed towards the highest ‘Perfection’, obviously needs tremendous consistent continuous struggle. It is like stopping the natural flow of river towards the sea, and using the pump to reverse the flow of gravity! Daily Swadhyaya,

Thru’ daily self-reflection and self-study, swadhyaya , the discriminative faculty of intellect, Viveka buddhi, has to be gradually awakened. We have to individually explore and realize that only daily prayers, meditation, worship, according to our respective Religions, and fulfilling our duties, etc. can purify the mind. And, only a purified mind is amenable to control and discipline and, will be willing to lead a virtuous life, focused on the long-term good. We have to gain conviction that mere short-term pleasures are not good for us. Individually, we must gradually gain appreciation and an intellectual conviction not only in what is our long term good, but also clarity on the very aim and purpose of daily work, life.

4. Masters and Prophets of all Religions are essentially teaching and inspiring us only to engage in daily swadhyaya. This daily reflection on the higher powers, gods, prophets, leads to refinement of our samskaras, mindset. Purifying the mind thru’ holy spiritual thoughts, reflecting on lives and teachings of Seers, and serving living beings, etc. is universal spiritual purpose of all Religions.

It matters little which God, deity, or religion we follow. There is no jealousy and fight between Gods. Today Religions and democracies have lost touch with their spiritual essence and have become self-centered and divisive forces. It is now the responsibility of the spiritually mature of all religions, academia and governance to once again engage the youth in swadhyaya , during schools and colleges, to get the inspiration to strive to purify the mind towards the highest and best and strive to discover the higher purpose of life.

We human beings alone have the faculty of intellect to discriminate between various options and choices and freedom to determine all our passions, pursuits and goals; good or bad. In an epic, father of Dhruv chooses Suruchi over Suniti ; pleasure over righteousness , illustrating that it is the very nature of our mind to choose pleasures led by an untrained intellect.

5. Daily Self-development practices, Sadhana : In addition to daily swadhyaya to acquire knowledge and inspiration to struggle for the higher we also need to strengthen the determinative faculty, the will power to struggle to give up lower pleasures for the sake of long term goals, which may not appear to be be relevant now. Through daily swadhyaya and sadhana we develop powers of mind to know and do the ‘right’.

6. Character or Spiritual strength, Atma bal: Modern society has progressed from mere physical strength of ancient era, to the age of Intellectual strength. However, we’ve failed to learn from our ancient forefathers a universal principle: “when we strive for the higher the lower is automatically fulfilled”. And so, Bharat had focused on developing Pure-intellect, Vivek-buddhi. It not only fulfilled our needs and desires, but kept us engaged in our respective swadharma . It helped us rise beyond intellect to spiritual strength and enabled us make better, long-term good choices vis-à-vis mankind’s highest human goal; not mere science and technology of which we’re so proud today.
