India to Bharat · Essay #219

Wisdom of bharat through words of daily use!

The wisdom and culture of Bharat arises out of the scientifically experimented and verified Vedantic reality that though we appear separate, we are all one, interconnected, interrelated, interdependent, integral whole, and it is only when we realize this oneness with creation that we will be really fulfilled. This realization is the ultimate purpose of our daily work and life and is expressed through various terms like salvation, heaven, perfection, mukti, nirvana, yoga, and through pithy principles, e.g. ‘Let noble thoughts come to us from all directions’; ‘Atithi devo bhava’; ‘Vasudeva kutumbukam’; that is vidya, which leads Perfection, mukti’, etc. It is to fulfill this purpose that society was structured based on the 4-ashramas (stages of life), ‘the 4-purusatha (strivings), and the 4-characteristics, etc., i.e.: –

  • Acquire commitment to values, ethics, righteous conduct, i.e. Dharma, as a student. It was the responsibility of mentors to establish students in a daily and regular practice of ‘sadhna’ and ‘swadhyaya’ for only then can they remain on the critical path of Dharma to attain perfection. Education was not mere 3R’s, literacy, etc.; nor did it generally allow people to enter a vocation beyond their character and competence, of course barring exceptional cases!
  • Being established in Dharma, the student became an enlightened citizen and a responsible Householder committed to ethical conduct while ‘earning and enjoying’.
  • After retirement, householders worked for good of society, contemplating and realizing the highest goal of life; and sharing wisdom.
  • Such a plan of life was based on the holistic wisdom and the idea that the ‘result producers’ and ‘entrepreneurs’ focus of creating wealth for society and fulfilling their desires, through Dharma. Similarly, only those who had satiated their desires for artha and kaama, could be fair and just as mentors and administrators, and take responsibility for establishing Dharma in society and mentoring students.

It is a pity that this holistic wisdom is not a part of society and school curriculum even though the Fundamental Duties, Art 51A enjoin on us to value and preserve the rich heritage of our composite culture. This wisdom and healthy worldview of ‘oneness’ if included in schooling, will inspire people to strive for the highest in and through their daily work and life. It will also bring harmony between science and religions; amongst religions; amongst man and man; amongst man and nature. This separation of science and religion has its root in the materialistic western culture where Scientific enquiry and reasoning was suppressed by the church. In fact, the Royal Society took birth in Oxford with the motto of ‘ Nullius in Verba’ , i.e. not to accept words of Authority (of the Church).

Philosophy expressed thru’ Sanskrit: Sanskrit implies good-creation and is now found to be the most logically structured computer-fit language, fit for language translations. In fact, a few words of Sanskrit can place before us the essential wisdom of mankind. Here’s an example: –

  • VIDYA = That is Knowledge which leads to vinayam, humility & mukti , freedom
  • VIDYARTHI = He who has a burning desire for vidya
  • JIVATMA = Living-beings. Body that is matter plus Atman – ‘Life, Consciousness’
  • BHARATI = He who is striving for highest illumination (‘Perfection’; ‘Wisdom’)
  • SRESTHA = He who is striving for highest and best, Perfection. He is in ‘Sur’, harmony. Those who don’t strive for the highest are the “Asur”.
  • SWAROOPA = That form of mine which never changes, Atman. It cannot be body-mind!
  • SWASTHA = To be stable/established in my own Swaroopa, unchanging form. Why? Because my unchanging form, Swaroopa, is of nature of “Sat – Infinite Existence; Chid – Consciousness; Ananda – Bliss”. Being of nature of infinite Bliss, I will only be fulfilled when I expand my consciousness to feel one with the whole creation!
  • KARMA = Thought, words, actions and their effect on our Mind in the form of Memories
  • SAMSKARA = Memories permanently impressed in our minds by our Karma, which then enslave us and we become creatures of our own self-acquired tendencies, worldview, values, beliefs, conduct, habits, likes, dislikes, characteristics. We are enslaved by our own samskaras. Therefore, we are unequal; and, I and I alone am responsible for what I am now and also for what I wish to be!
  • YOGA = To join (i.e. with our Swaroopa, which is of nature of ‘Ananda’, by purifying our mindset, which seeks happiness outside, ignoring the reality that our Swaroopa is itself “ananda”!)
  • RELIGION = To regain (our lost happiness)
  • JAGAT = That which is changing/in motion
  • SAMSAR = That which comes and goes
  • ACHARYA = He who follows righteous conduct

Sanatana Dharma’ expresses the universality of Wisdom of Bharat and tells us that following sanatana dharma, i.e. universal and eternal human values, any and all of us can attain Perfection, the goal of life. It holds that anyone who sincerely practices ‘ Sadhna’ , Self-development practices and acquires the competencies and disciplines, will also attain the same infinite Truth, of oneness of the whole creation. And, it is the Dharma of all beings to strive to realize this infinite divine Truth, following their tendencies, religions and paths!
