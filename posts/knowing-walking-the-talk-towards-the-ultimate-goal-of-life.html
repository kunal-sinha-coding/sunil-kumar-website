India to Bharat · Essay #035

Knowing & walking-the-talk towards the ultimate goal of life

1. It is Vedanta that defines India that is Bharat and its unifying worldview of “oneness” which differentiates it from the worldview of “separateness”, followed by the rest of the world and propagated by religions, science, democracy, etc. which divide people into conflicting groups. It is India’s duty to globally scatter broadcast this worldview of “oneness”, to tackle fanaticism, terrorism, and bring harmony among religions so that together we can guide people to get established in daily self-development processes, to gradually grow towards the highest and best in and through their daily work and life.

2. Work itself is Sadhna – Purpose of Work is to experience the goal of human life, aka Karma Yoga: The foremost ability and attribute to realize our atma-swaroopa , the goal of Vedanta and of human life, is ‘ chitta-suddhi’ , purity of mind, which accrues as a result of good karma, and that is why the purpose of all our daily work and 24x7actions is said to be ‘chitta-suddhi’, and not just roti-kapada-makan.

It is therefore important to engage in that work that is in keeping with our inherent qualities and tendencies, where we can excel, fulfil our duties, and do our work as worship and as our contribution for the good of society. Such daily work, high or low, performed as kartavya-palan leads to chitta-suddhi; ‘wages’ and ‘profits’, etc. are our compensation for service to society, while the purpose of organizations is to satisfy the needs of society.

2.1 Catching the spirit of Vedanta is not dependent on education/status, but on ‘purity of heart’: We see many great scholars, pundits, intellectuals well versed in sciences, who may know all Vedanta theories, but are unable to grasp the spirit of Vedanta of Selfless service of God in man and all Creation.

On the other hand, there are many others who may be illiterate, may know nothing of the intricacies of Vedanta, and engage in a menial vocation, but somehow they seem to have imbibed the essence and spirit of Vedanta; and are therefore kind, loving, helpful, straightforward, etc.

And so, we can infer that realization of the goal of life is beyond the intellectual understanding and beyond our education and status in society; and therefore, ‘who is where now, and why’ cannot be explained just thru’ what happens in one lifetime!

So, the journey towards realization starts from good karma, kartavya-palan, righteous conduct, virtuous life, etc. to attain purity of heart.

2.2 Here, a doubt needs to be answered: The ultimate realization is that there is no ‘second’, because in ‘Oneness’, akela , there is no room for doosara , second – no Time, so no hours-minute- second . How then can ‘work-actions-sadhana’ which are based on duality take us to Oneness! An example can explain:

When a poisonous thorn pierces us, we have no option but to use another thorn to take it out. But that thorn must itself, be without poison. Similarly, we have seen that the cause of dukkha, sorrows, is ‘desire’ and ‘unrighteous or prohibited actions. They are the poisonous thorn. Fulfilment of duties and kartavya-palan, done without any desires, nishkaama-karma, action done as obligatory duty, kartavya , aka Karma Yoga, is the other thorn without poison, which leads to purification of mind, Chitta-suddhi, and therefore is the critical path to the highest.

Through nishkama-karma the mind is purified and reaches the state of equanimity. Then, the spirit of love, care and concern for all, vairagya, detachment, grows and the mumukshu , the seeker after freedom, approaches the highest goal. At this level, the mind becomes pure and calm through devotion.

Only a pure mind, purified thru’ Righteous conduct and spirit of Karma Yoga, can grasp and absorb the spirit of Vedanta, of Oneness. So, kartavya-palan, with spirit of nishkaama-karma is a necessary prerequisite sadhana, for realizing the highest goal of life, i.e. moksha.

3. Understanding the Swaroopa, ‘Unchanging form’, of Perfection, Moksha, Freedom, the ultimate goal of all life?

All beings seek a state of perpetual happiness, bliss, salvation, nirvana, ‘perfection’, Ananda. This state of Brahman-gyana, is totally free of all traces of sorrows, dukha , is commonly understood as moksha. But its real Swaroopa is a matter of many differing views.

# Those who do not believe in God and an afterlife, hold that death itself is heaven, moksha, because they do not believe in rebirth.

# The Buddhists do believe in rebirth but for them shoonya or nothingness is moksha, nirvaana.

# The worldly people hold that to live happily, peacefully, itself is moksha.

# Some believe that heaven, Vaikuntha, is moksha. But we know that some had to get out of heaven for some reasons. So, the sukha of heaven can only be temporary, not changeless!

# Even being in the company of God cannot be moksha, because they seem to be a temporary case – so none of these are the true form or swaroopa of moksha.

# Another name for moksha is ‘ bhooma ’, infinite. Whether it is fear or dukha , the living-being, jivaatman, will experience it when he sees anything ‘second’; as “separate from himself”! One fears only something other than himself. When there is absolutely nothing separate from myself, why should I become afraid?

# Thus, shruti makes it very clear that the realization that there is anything other than myself, is moksha. It means getting rid of dwaita-bhaava , feeling of duality, seeing the ‘second’ as in Creation where our impure mind gets enslaved by Time and law of cause-effect!

‘Sravan-manana-nidhidhyasan’ is the Sadhna: This Brahman-gyana is acquired through ‘ listening-reflection-and acting out of the Vedic conviction that I am Brahman’ .
