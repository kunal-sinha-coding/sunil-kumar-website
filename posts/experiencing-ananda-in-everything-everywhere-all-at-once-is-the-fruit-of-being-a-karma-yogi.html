India to Bharat · Essay #094

Experiencing Ananda in ‘Everything Everywhere All at once’ is the fruit of being a Karma Yogi

1. We need the Shrestha more than the educated, because only the Shrestha can inspire us to strive to be good, better, best. If we’re in a leadership position, wherever and for whatever reasons, we’re in a way accountable to deliver results. Only the Shrestha can help us ensure that we enjoy and not suffer the consequences, the karma-phala , fruits of our good-bad ‘choices and action’ – thanks to the Cosmic-Intelligence that maintains and keeps in Order, Rtm, the principle of cause-effect in the Universe.

Of course, we all work 24×7 and for karma-phala, for fruits of our actions, and so naturally our mind gets disturbed, unstable, restless, both in excitement on success and in disappointment with failure. Shrestha must help us learn that the highest ‘skill in work’, kaushalam, ‘skill’, is to practice giving our best and focusing on fulfilling our obligatory duties with a calm, composed mind, (as if) unconcerned with the fruits; with the conviction that it is our duty to fulfil our duties and only with a calm mind can we really do our best work; and that only ethical and righteous conduct purifies our mind and only a purified mind is amenable to control and can be trained to remain calm.

2. People are different from birth. On what basis is our birth decided? It cannot be a random accidental decision when we see that things in this universe are in Rtm, harmony, order! So, a realistic understanding is that birth is a continuation of the law of karma and “as we sow, so will we reap”. We get the ‘fruits’ not just after death on the Day of Judgment, but also at every moment in the form of happiness/misery and then also thru’ birth in different circumstances appropriate to our ‘fruits’!

3. Modern idea is that we’re born equal and must be free to choose our work and vocation. The focus is on individual freedom and on fulfilment of material desires; on getting more goodies, money, power, status, etc. for comfort and pleasures – not on learning and practicing selflessness, the ‘skill’ in action to grow towards the Best!

4. In contrast o ur traditional societies were led by the Shrestha, the Mentors, teachers, men of wisdom, role models imbued with, and striving to imbibe the spirit of a Karma Yogi; they inspired and mentored the Administrators who then managed and guided the Entrepreneurs/Farmers, etc. who could then train the Workforce.

Similarly, a global idea was and still is, that our birth is not accidental but planned to give us the best and most optimum circumstances to continue our journey Godward, using the ‘skill in work’; even while we enjoy/suffer the fruits of our previous actions as stored in our mind, samskaras.

Inspired by the Shrestha, generally people do follow the family values, engaged in occupations in accordance with their mindset, characteristics, samskaras and do strive to work for good of society in the spirit of ‘simple living-high thinking’, in majority of cases; exceptions prove the principle of 80-20!

5. Today we have no Shrestha in Politics and Administration, and neither Religions nor Teachers have inspired us, the highly educated, to imbibe the ‘Skill in work’ so that we may engage in an occupation and work in a way that fulfilling our respective duties we may continuously grow towards the good, better, best. i.e., ‘re-gain’ (religion in Latin) our Oneness, Rtm, Harmony, Order with Creation and consequently with the Creator, aka God – an idea of Best, a Godly/Heavenly-state, where we’re in Ananda in everything, everywhere, all at once!

6. Today we’re not learning that the very purpose of all actions, work, vocation is to imbibe that ‘kaushalam’, ‘skill’, in actions that leads to Chitta-suddhi, purification of the mind.

We need time, opportunity and environment to reflect and get convinced that as human-beings we’re Conscious-Intelligent beings. It is only because of this Consciousness that we can act; but mistakenly we assume my-ego-mind-body is acting and therefore to suffer the ‘fruits, i.e., misery-pleasure’ of our ego-led actions we need births! Once we purify the mind thru’ ‘skill’ in work, we realize our reality as Conscious-Intelligence and attain a state of perpetual Ananda, not just happiness that comes and goes! The ‘skill’ to be free from bondage of birth is to strive to fulfil our duties without attachment to results not considering ourselves to be the doer nor the enjoyer of the body-mind-ego-actions.

7. Meditation with eyes open: We can practice focusing on this Conscious-intelligence in everything, everywhere which also purifies the mind; we will then see all beings as living-gods, and we will learn to work as ‘worship of god in man’; and ‘in all beings and in all Creation’.

Our minds are restless due to attachment to fruits of actions and this healthy worldview of work and life calms the mind and we realize that the Self, soul, is immortal, only bodies die. Our duty is to be on the side of ethics, righteousness. Since karma binds one to the fruits of actions, we should learn to fulfil our duties and mentally dedicate the fruits of the works to the Conscious-Intelligence, and actions in this spirit become karma-yoga , or “united with God through works/duties.

When work is done with devotion in a spirit of worship, it purifies the mind. The mind then becomes calm, peaceful, tranquil, and vocation and daily actions become meditation with eyes open seeing Conscious-Intelligence everywhere!
