India to Bharat · Essay #030

Academia, religions, science, must work together as Karma Yogis to be & make mentors of Society

Newton held that there is only one invisible God, which corresponds to the essential worldview of religions also. Newton therefore agreed with the school that we might venerate the ‘one mediator between God and man the man Christ Jesus, who had been filled with the divine spirit, but he could not be one with God’. During the time of Newton there was a controversy between Arius and Athanasius schools of Biblical thought and the latter, church-accepted view was that Christ was equal or ‘consubstantial’ with God.

The Arius view was considered heresy in those times and had Newton said it publicly at the least he would be banished from all public offices and support and would be thrown out of the professorship (he got it at the youngest age of 26, only 8 years after entering as a freshman) at the Cambridge College, where, as a Fellow he was obliged to swear ‘ that I will embrace the true religion of Christ with all my soul ’. Newton would often consult his conscience and books about what that ‘ true religion’ actually was.

It was because of the rigid control of church over even scientific enquiry that idea of Secular emerged, and Academia and science still reject religion as mere Belief, not open to enquiry to seek and experience truth directly! Therefore, Royal Society established in Oxford in the 1660’s excluded all questions of religion and politics, and their motto became Nullius in verba: ‘Nothing in words’, or nothing on authority (of the church or any other).

Newton of course was a firm believer in ‘prisca sapientia’, or ancient wisdom and believed that they were the untapped source of great power that could be released into the modern world and said that his mathematical research was only rediscovering the lost principles of Pythagoras. Newton had been described in his times as the last of the magicians, mystics who could look upon the words visible and invisible, with serene eyes.

Aristotle, born in 384 BC, raised the question: Suppose there was matter, totally deprived of form – utterly formless matter. It would not actually be any kind of matter, but potentially it could have the capacity to take on any form – it therefore is potentially everything…. Greek philosophers give a very good example, ‘A rabbit dies, it decays and eventually disappears. The rabbit form is gone. Or it is eaten by a wolf and the rabbit form transforms into wolf form. In both cases, matter remains but forms change.

Of course, we can say that without any form, it would be nothing! True, but ‘no’-‘thing’ ‘exists’, and changeless-existence is therefore potentially everything. Aristotle tries to explain this concept within the limit of matter, through explaining transformation of matter during birth and death.

Vedanta does not limit itself to matter and goes beyond matter to the concept of Atman, Consciousness, akin to matter ‘ totally deprived of form – utterly formless matter’ and it does so not based on mere philosophizing, but based on the Truth actually experienced by Seers, Rishis thru’ righteousness, meditations, etc. It is a scientific reality that a living-being is ‘body’ as matter enlivened by ‘Consciousness’, as Life, Self, Atman, Rooha, Kingdom of God within; it signifies all-pervasiveness, divinity, infinitude. As Self we’re all One. Purpose of education and religions is to inspire us to learn to work and live in a selfless, loving way, so that in and thru our work as worship we realize our Oneness with all, as Self!

Vedanta is not belief based but is open to enquiry, question, discussions and goes on to explain that as ‘Existence’, we’re all one and interconnected and each of us have to ultimately experience our divine oneness as existence, by becoming ‘one with Existence, aka God’.

Science accepts that, ‘that which Exists, can never go out of existence; and that which doesn’t exist, can never go out of existence’! If therefore we exist or anything exists, then we exist eternally as Consciousness, One with all, though our body-mind, may keep changing! This wisdom of our Identity as “Existence-Consciousness- Bliss” not changing Body-mind, must be an integral part of education.

Action Plan to Be & Make Mentors : Let students, teachers and all working men focus on ‘Artha-Kaama’, i.e., ‘Production-Enjoyment’.

As senior citizens and devotees of different religions residing in various colonies, cities, districts, it must be our duty as Mentors, to study, understand and evolve strategies to focus on spreading awareness of the idea and ideal of ‘Dharma-Moksha’, i.e., being selflessly committed to our duties, kartavya-palan, Ethics, Righteousness, to attain/experience Oneness with all aka Freedom, Perfection, Heaven, Nirvana, etc.’

To counter narrow-Preachers who spread belief and faith only in My vs Your religion and God, we now need Scientists and Teachers as Mentors who understand and can teach harmony of religions and focus on giving us an intellectual conviction in the power of goodness and Self-development processes aka Life skills, to ‘Know’ and ‘Walk-the-talk’ towards the highest and best human goal.

“If there is any land on this earth that can lay claim to be the blessed Punya Bhumi, to be the land to which all souls on this earth must come to account for Karma, the land to which every soul that is wending its way Godward must come to attain its last home, the land where humanity has attained its highest towards generosity, towards purity, towards calmness, above all, the land of introspection and of spirituality – it is India – Vivekananda.
