India to Bharat · Essay #225

Wisdom of Bharat – ‘Whole World as our Family’

The wisdom of Bharat is to express the highest profound truths in simple, straightforward pithy statements. For example, our Rishis, seers, divide the universe into just three entities i.e. Jagat, Jivatma and God and categorically state, without any fanfare, that though they appear as totally separate entities, are in fact but one and one alone; they are but ‘one’ (uni+verse!), in their ‘swaroopa’, literally ‘own-form’.

It does seem unbelievable to us but this statement is the foundation on which the entire humanity and the civilization of all mankind stands. And, in fact all virtues and all religions and even the very idea of democracy is based on this worldview of ‘oneness’ and equality. That is why goodness, virtues, ethics, dharma, all teach us to treat all beings fairly and justly, with love. In fact, the mother’s spontaneous love for her creation, i.e. baby, is a great example.

What is our Swaroopa? It is a profound word! It refers to that unchanging, basic substance or stuff, without which that objects cannot ‘exist’, and which remains with it at all times. For example, ‘gold’ is the ‘swaroopa’ of all gold-ornaments, irrespective of whether it is a ring, glass, necklace, etc. The individual names and forms, may be whatever, but in all names and forms, the real unchanging stuff that ‘exists’ is but one! Similarly, swaroopa of jivas, living beings, is God, called Brahman, which literally implies ‘greater’! Similarly, inert nature, universe, ‘exists’ and ‘existence’ itself is a property, a quality, an attribute of God, termed ‘satt’, i.e. ‘unchanging existence’, from which comes ‘satya’, truthfully saying what ‘is’.

What is the purpose of knowing this ‘oneness’? It is a state where we remain perpetually happy, blissful, fulfilled, peaceful, no matter what the situation! It is a realization that everything is obtained and there is nothing more to be known or gained. This is our inherent, deep inner desire for being fully free, independent and blissful, which is referred to variously as ‘Perfection, Heaven, Salvation, Nirvana, ‘moksha’, the ultimate purpose of our work and life. This freedom from all pain and grief, of poorna-ananda, is not dependent on any external factors. In fact, this is really our own inherent nature, our Swaroopa; what we are all really seeking in life, in and through all our activities. Thus, ‘Swastha’, health, literally implies ‘being established in Self, i.e. in our Swaroopa’.

Goodness: Our basic inherent nature, Swaroopa, being ananda-swaroopa, divine, we can never be really happy till we ourselves experience this divine ananda and, oneness with all. And, it is this realization that expresses itself proportionately, as empathy, compassion, spontaneous feelings of love, care and concern for others, etc.

Trying to understand this Wisdom is itself a process of becoming better!
