India to Bharat · Essay #173

Ideas and Ideals – ‘Bharat’ vis-a-vis ‘India’!

1. Bharat is being in Sur, in Harmony with Creation: Everything in creation, forces of nature, matter, ‘living-beings’, follow their respective nature, swabhava, swadharma, behavioral-characteristics and duties. Sun, wind, earth, rain, rivers, seasons, plants, etc. follow their nature under the command of some Superpower or Super-intelligence, aka God. Similarly, the infinite varieties of living-beings of air, water, land, whether big or infinitely micro, follow their respective nature, swabhava, swadharma, – they come into existence, sustain themselves, and disappear as living beings, leaving behind their carcass. So far, so good!

1.1 Man – the Problem child of Creation! The entire creation is existing harmoniously according to its plan; excepting us, human beings. Everything and every being in creation are spontaneously following this plan of creation; excepting we; particularly the modern 3R’s educated people.

You and I have been given the option, the freedom to choose; to be or not to be, in harmony with creation. Using our intellect, ‘buddhi’, we can do many things and we’ve learnt to control and manage many things and beings. And, in so doing, we can choose to move ‘away ’ or ‘towards’ being in harmony and thereby realizing our eternal union with the whole creation, from whence started our journey.

And guess what man has chosen? Just as water flows down, given freedom of choice, our mind also has chosen the path of temptations, selfishness, comforts, entertainment, pleasures, trivia and irrelevant. Modern education and modern My-versus-Your Religions have failed to inspire us to live in harmony with creation.

1.2 Bharatiya wisdom and ideals of ‘sanatana-dharma’, on the other hand, are based on its vedantic foundation and clarity on the ultimate purpose of human life. Our rishis and sages had ensured and provided the educational, social and governance systems, codes of conduct, following which, in and thru’ daily ‘earning and enjoying’, we were not only inspired but shown the self-development practices thru’ which we could keep ‘ moving towards’, fulfilment; harmony with the whole creation.

2. Best of Bharat + Best of India: So far, aping the western cultures, India has adopted science, technology, 3R’s education, democracy, as the new Religion; but without a spiritual foundation they’ve made us self-centered, greedy and materialistic. Unfortunately, religious preachers have remained narrow, petty-minded and so spiritual practices to awaken Viveka and build the strength of mind to struggle against lower temptations and rise towards ‘perfection’, are no longer an essential part of schooling as they ought to be. Religions and academia have failed to unite and rise up to the level of maturity, where they can spread the need for daily universal spiritual practices within the mainstream schooling systems, to build character, to be good and to do good.

2.1 Am I in harmony, in Sur ? If so, I am a Bharati. I am in harmony with nature and will soon, once again, ‘regain’ (literal Latin meaning of religion) fulfilment; peace, happiness, oneness or Union (literal meaning of Yoga) with the whole creation. How? By simply following my respective swabhava, swadharma. If not, we may be anyone, Indian or any other, we’re Asur ; we’re going towards misery and unhappiness. Bharati literally means, those striving for the highest illumination . The highest illumination, the ideal of human life, must be total fulfilment; aka heaven, nirvana, self-realization, ‘perfection’, etc.

A refined buddhi, intellect, alone can give us the wisdom and the ability to struggle against our selfish tendencies and learn and practice choosing the path of unselfishness, of being virtuous, of following our swadharma, of being good and doing good; this is the universal virtuous path to the highest illumination. That is why modern academia and mature spiritual devotees of different faiths have to sit together and understand that the essence of the teachings of saints, rishis, prophets and masters of all religions is that during childhood, i.e. schooling, we must provide time and inspiration to refine the buddhi of the young thru’ daily prayers, meditation, charity, good deeds, reflection and practices etc. Schooling must ensure that the educated are in a vocation where they shall excel for the good of society.

3. The Educated must treat Vocation as an opportunity to serve: Only when schooling has instilled in us the wisdom that work is worship will we realize that Profits do matter, but service to society is a far greater purpose of enterprises. Also, when we sincerely go forward to serve, to unselfishly strive to develop the nation, then somehow help comes from many quarters, and it leads to our spiritual growth towards harmony, peace, bliss.
