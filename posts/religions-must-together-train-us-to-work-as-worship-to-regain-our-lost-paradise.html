India to Bharat · Essay #107

Religions must together train us to ‘Work as Worship’ to ‘Regain our lost Paradise’

We all Work! Whosoever and whatsoever we’re and irrespective of our religions etc., we’re all doing Karma, actions, every moment, all the time, wherever we’re, at work or otherwise. Yet isn’t it surprising that no one, and no Religions teach us “what exactly is karma or action” and “h ow our daily “Karma/Work” itself can gradually lead us towards either Hell or Paradise, depending on whether it pollutes or purifies our mind”!

All religions have an “Essential” universal aspect that is common to all humanity and unites us in love with the whole Creation. And a “non-essential” part that divides us into “Mine vs Yours”. Growing up must be our opportunity to explore, discuss, debate and strive to ‘know’ and imbibe the “Essential” aspects.

The Essentials that Religions must teach: What exactly is karma/action? Let’s understand that every “Intention-Thought-word- action” is Karma and it creates a result outside and simultaneously it leaves a permanent impression in our minds which then enslave us and with repetition become our characteristics and habit. We must therefore learn to manage quality of our thoughts and direct them towards the long-term good and better for more and more, which purify our mind! Actions for short-term pleasures for me and mine will tend to pollute our mind.

What are good, better Thoughts? Religions and education must teach us that selflessness is better than selfishness. That the fruit of religion is unselfishness; not just ‘belief and belonging’ that limits us to “me and mine”! That we must strive to love and care for more and more till we can love the whole creation, selflessly. That development, growth, becoming good and better only means, the ability to work selflessly for good of all! That any selfishness that makes us narrow, cannot be the path to paradise!

Religions must therefore teach us from our childhood “how can Karma take me towards good, better, best?” How can my Thoughts-words-actions “Unite (Yoga) me with my “higher Self” and “help me Regain (Religion) my lost Paradise” by adhering to universal values of Truth, Peace, Love, non-killing, Righteous conduct and commitment to Kartavya-palan; and to continuously grow in selflessness and in compassion towards more and more till we can feel love for the whole creation, equally.

A Mother is the best example of selfless love; as are all common Indians who focus on their work! Aren’t we all seeking Heaven, a state of Bliss, Ananda, Fulfilment – “Perfection”, Poorna-ananda, because that alone can give us perpetual fulfilment?

It must be the obligatory duty, responsibility and accountability of our respective religions and education to help and guide us to ‘know’ and ‘imbibe’ the spirit of the universal humane values. If we allow preachers to talk only of the non-essentials it can lead to hatred for the ‘other’; and we fail to imbibe the Essential universal values to be followed by all, irrespective of our religions differences. We should make Minority and Mainstream schooling responsible and accountable to teach and ensure that every citizen learns to love and treat the ‘whole creation as our family’; imbibes the worldview of “love” and “Oneness” not of “Hatred and Separateness?”

Our Vision for 2047: A glorious India will rise out of this chaos and strife by harmonizing the intellectual worldview of Oneness as ‘Spirit’ with the modern worldview of Separateness as practiced by modern religions and political parties who ‘divide’ and ‘polarize’ people into ‘My vs Your’ for self-centered reasons.

If we preach hatred for ‘others’, we reject God’s message of love, compassion, mercy, justice, etc., for all. No religion ever persecuted men, no religion ever burnt witches. What then incited people to do these things? Politics, but never religion! And if such politics takes the name of religion, whose fault is that?

Religion is neither talk, nor theory, nor intellectual consent. It is realization of love for all in the heart of our hearts; it is touching God; it is feeling love for all creation. Look at all the great, spiritual men and women in every age and country, and we will see that they are not really at variance with one another; they all insist first on universal ideal of being virtuous and good and doing good to all!

Mission Karma Yogi: The problem which we have to tackle is: How to attain material prosperity without harming inner peace. And GOI has rightly suggested that the idea of karma yoga and the ideal of being karma yogis can harmonize the best of Western material and external work efficiency with our inherent need of Inner peace and happiness!

To Remind Civil Servants of their Motto, “ Yogaha karmasu kaushalam ”, GOI has very rightly decided, to launch programs to train them to be “Karma Yogis”; and Religions and Schooling must also focus on inspiring us to be Karma Yogis so that we may all learn to work together to guarantee both “outer prosperity and success” and “Inner peace and bliss!

Religions and education must inspire us to be a Karma Yogi who personifies more than just Excellence in Action; for him daily Work and Relationships itself become an opportunity to attain both external prosperity and inner peace and happiness.
