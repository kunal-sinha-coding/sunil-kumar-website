India to Bharat · Essay #204

Five-Profound reasons why all of us may trust the Vedas:

Elections are now over but it has clearly divided the nation into two distinct warring ideologies, the ‘English-medium-minorities’ vs ‘Hindi-medium-masses’. On one side are those sahebs who have been in power and in the forefront for the last hundred years. They consist of the many Convent-educated or English-speaking educated elite who have been in power not just in politics but in all areas, professional, media, etc. They’re for Hollywood, and for Western life style and comforts with little love or knowledge of what Hinduism is all about. They’re ‘secular’, because religion means nothing to them, other than the faults and criticism heaped upon Hinduism for ages as highlighted by the missionaries. They’ve a disdain for Hinduism and look down upon the common Hindus, while allowing minority religions to teach their idea that my-religion-my-prophet-my-scripture alone is right in their schools but do not teach and ensure that all citizens imbibe the universal spirit of Hinduism that all religions sincerely practiced lead to one God, and only thru’ fulfillment of our duties thru’ dharma, righteous conduct, we remain on the path to our highest goal, ‘Perfection’.

While they hold Gandhiji as their ideal, they’ve treated as garbage the simple living and high thinking, and the spirit of tyaga and seva that he stood for. The sahebs have thrown in the dustbin all his ideals and suggestions expressed in his ‘Hind Swaraj’ but they shout down those who talk of his assassin. In fact, there have always been debates for and against Bapu who was full of love for Hinduism and because of its Vedantic spirit had equal love for other religions, even though the other religion insisted on dividing the nation on the basis of religion. The common Hindus are suddenly in power, and now is the time for us all to appreciate the idea of Vedas, the foundation of Bharat that has become degraded into India!

Why should we trust the Vedas? Let us look at 5-ideas that support: –

1. No author: No one has ever claimed the authorship of the Vedas and every great name in the history of Indian culture has accepted the validity of the Vedas! Had there been an author there was bound to be some mention somewhere. We know from our experience today, how the scientific people claim authorship, patents, etc. for everything. But how is it that none has ever claimed the authorship of the Vedas. The various names mentioned are the mantra-drsta, Seers-of-the-mantra, to whom they were revealed in their meditation.

2. Vedas have remained unchanged: Not many of us may know that every letter, every syllable, and the duration and rhyme for its chanting, is permanently fixed! We know from our experience that people love to change tunes, words, pattern of chanting, etc. but surprisingly every swara, tone, of the Vedas has remained unchanged and fixed for eternity! If the ancient Rishis visit us today, they will find no change in the way the Vedas are being chanted today! It is really mind-boggling to imagine that any human being could have developed and written it down and that these have remained unchanged for tens of thousands of years and are chanted today as they were chanted in the beginning of creation shows that they are eternal.

3. Vedas are huge: The Vedas are immensely large, beyond all imagination! If it is written into pages, it will run into millions of pages! In fact, most of the Vedas are now lost to us yet whatever fraction of it remains, takes 245 hours for chanting from beginning to end. This massive Vedas cannot be the creation of any one single human being! If we say many different people have written it at different times, then there are bound to be contradictions. Moreover, even if one person had written it, we know that people are not consistent in their views, and keep changing and revising their views.

4. Vedas reveal unknown facts: There are certain statements made in the Vedas, which are being discovered by scientific minds with all the instruments at their disposal only, now. The simplest example is that there is a mention in the Atharva-Vedas of seven continents, whereas, the 7th, i.e., Antarctica has been found only in 1911, and it is called Shaak-dweepa.

5. No contradictions: Surprisingly there are no contradictions in this huge ocean that the Vedas are! The very fact that there are no contradictions whatsoever in the Vedas, proves their divinity; they cannot be of human creation. Of course, at a superficial level when we read through translations, people can apparently point to some inconsistencies, but when we study them after proper preparation and training, along with the rules of grammar, logic, bhasyas or commentaries written by Shankaracharya, on the Prasthan-trayas, i.e. the 3-brief-scriptures to understand the essence of Vedanta viz: Brahma-sutras, Bhagavad-Gita, and the Upanishads, we will have the intellectual conviction in Vedas. Thus the Vedas were never written down and were only transmitted by a Guru (Vedi, Dwi-vedi, Tri-vedi, Chatur-vedi, knower of One, two, three, all four Vedas).

Max Muller translated the Vedas into English: It is interesting to note why the English had paid a very huge sum of money for it. Max Muller writes to his wife that he has been asked to bring out defects in the Vedas so that people will no longer have faith in it, so that the Christian Missionaries could counter and convert the indian (i.e. uncivilized) heathens! It took him 30 years on this work and when Vivekananda visited him he had become a Rishi; he refused to come to India, saying that he has now developed so much love for Vedas and India that he’ll never be able to return!

Vedas couldn’t be man-made! If we really reflect on the above points, we will come to the conclusion that the Vedas must be divine in origin. Today people without any purity or tapas or training in systems, logic and methods of studying Vedas pick up some points from the translations and try to show contradictions etc. We should not worry about those whose only agenda is to show contradictions in the Vedas for their own ulterior motives.

All Religions lead to ‘Perfection’: The Vedas or any scripture of any religion, are trying to place before us that knowledge which is beyond sense-perception and our limited intellect and experience. The only option for the time being is to proceed, accepting the Vedanta or our respective scripture, as a valid authority by themselves, and able to reveal that wisdom, that cannot be revealed by any other proof, other than by ourselves experiencing it following righteous conduct, prayers, meditation, etc. as per our respective religious faith. This is the advice of saints and sages of all religions from time immemoria
