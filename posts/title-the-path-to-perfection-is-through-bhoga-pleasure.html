India to Bharat · Essay #179

“Title: The Path to Perfection is through ‘Bhoga’, Pleasure!”

Virtues over Pleasures: In the last some centuries we’ve adopted Bhoga, gratification, insatiability, pleasure as the aim and purpose of work and life. We seem to have forgotten our time-tested wisdom that wealth and pleasures are just milestones in our journey and are not the goal of life, Perfection, Mukti, a state of perpetual Fulfilment and Ananda. Our challenge is to thoroughly enjoy the materialistic glory of Ravan’s sone-ki-lanka on one hand, and understand and appreciate the need to combine Bhoga with Mukti to attain the fair, just and spiritually awakened Ram’s Ram-rajya. And, it can best be done by a marriage of modern ‘ science of matter ’ with our ancient ‘science of the spirit’; not by rejecting and divorcing from the ancient wisdom of our spiritual nature.

This world is certainly for bhoga, for enjoyment, but after all our struggles and strivings, don’t we seek peace and rest in relaxation and sleep. The question is why should sleep give us Fulfilment which we didn’t find during all our strivings for happiness and pleasures? What exactly happens in sleep? We don’t know; but we have to acknowledge that it refreshes us. In fact, we cannot survive without our daily dose of sleep! For some inexplicable reasons this sleep is a state where we are totally fulfilled and in our own spiritual Self, free from all that is matter and material. This Spirit, that we become one with, during deep sleep, somehow seems to be the ‘food and medicine’ for peace, fulfilment, happiness; not matter! So our quest is to befriend the source of our very being, even in the waking state!

And so, Eureka! The lesson is that we need both ‘pleasures of matter’ and ‘peace of spirit, already within us’. We must be thankful that our time-tested spiritual wisdom is not only easily available, but is now presented in the manner and language that scientific minds of the modern era are also easily comprehending.

Am I only a visible body? Modern science and religions have made us believe that man is only a Body-Mind complex, which is matter, and so we are identified by our visible body and external visible behavior. Of course, at the same time, for some inexplicable and vague reasons, we intuitively also recognize that we are not just a body-mind complex! And, there is something, somewhere within us, which enlivens us, makes us conscious, aware, and as long as that ‘X’ is with or within us, we are characterized as a living-being.

Isn’t it surprising and a great wonder that with all our education, science, technology, religions, we’ve never ever even been curious to explore this profound secret, which is really our very essence, and is so very near us; in fact, within the heart of us all! As long as man thinks he is just the body he will keep on pampering the body and his whole life will be aimed towards looking for bodily pleasures and comforts. And, this is what is happening today where all advertising, manufacturing, supermarkets, industry, businesses, are trying to sell us fashion, cosmetics, clothes, and a vast array of consumables to pleasure and titillate our body-mind.

If only schooling and academia had realized and imbibed in us the habit of a daily dose of reflection, questioning, self-study, self-introspection, prayers, meditation, etc. we would have found time to think and reflect and wouldn’t have been busy running hither and thither pampering ourselves. Only when we start thinking, will we start wondering and questioning about why we’re here, for what purpose, and so on.

Essential Purpose of all Religions: It is only Bharat that has scattered broadcast the reality that all spiritual practices that various Religious teach are meant only to refine and purify our minds. There may be many different Religions, different Masters and teachings, but the purpose of all religions is ultimately to inspire us to get established in a routine of daily spiritual practices of reflection, prayers, meditations, worship, virtuous and righteous conduct, commitment to our respective duties, and to love, care, compassion and concern for all, etc.

Why Virtues? Compassion? Empathy? Care for all? Why do religions insist on virtues and spiritual practices? Because only selfless and virtuous conduct purifies the mind; and, only a pure mind is amenable to values, virtues, disciplines and control and so, can be inspired to strive to feel one with the infinite-blissful spirit within us all; be Free; be Perfect.

Once man learns of his divine blissful Self, he can gradually struggle to purify the mind and befriend his divine self through daily spiritual practices, and through the practice of Dharma, obligatory duties and human values, in his daily life. Unless man has this clarity on his ‘swaroopa’ , i.e. his own unchanging form, and his relationship with other living beings and nature, he will not be able to fix the aim and purpose of life and will remain in a state of perpetual purposelessness.

This is how West expresses this profound truth that each soul has the same divine Spirit within: “If I truly love one person, I love all persons. I love the world; I love life. If I can say to somebody else, “I love you”, I must also be able to say, “I love, in you, everybody; I love, through you, the world; I love, in you, also myself.”

This is a wonderful expression based on the hard reality and the ancient Vedantic-wisdom that as the ‘Atman or Self’ in Jivatmas, living-beings, we’re all united and integrated as one whole interconnected entity. This is how this idea has been expressed in pithy statements: ‘That Thou Art’; ‘Each soul is potentially divine’; ‘The Kingdom of God is within you’; etc. This really is the very essence of all religions, though modern preachers of religions have failed to live by their ideals and are now become divisive and warring entities, instead of growing spiritually to have and to teach love, care, respect for each other and their respective ‘ Ishtam’ , i.e. favored-ideal-of-God.

Conclusion: Of course, changing our mind-set, to imbibe this profound ideal, is extremely difficult! Therefore, schooling and society should provide time and environment to daily and constantly hear, reflect, practice to remain Soul-conscious in all our actions to remain on the path to Perfection!
