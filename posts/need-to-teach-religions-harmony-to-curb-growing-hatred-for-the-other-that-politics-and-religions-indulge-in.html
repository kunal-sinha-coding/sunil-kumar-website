India to Bharat · Essay #124

Need to teach religions harmony to curb growing hatred for the ‘Other’ that politics and religions indulge in!

On September 11 2021 USA paid homage to those who lost their lives when the twin towers went down, due to the believers of My vs Your ideas of “Separatist” Religions, based on “belief” but not on “direct realization” which the founders of Religions had, based on which they had love for all equally. History tells us what religious wars, mass-killings, fanaticism, and now terrorism, etc. have been caused and are happening now by this “Separatist” idea of religions. So, how do we work for religious harmony?

It was on this very day of 11th September in 1893 when the idea of “Harmony of Religions” was brought to the scientific, modern West at Parliament of Religions, Chicago. Students of philosophy and religions need not be busy in their scholarly disputations; they need to become once again relevant to societies, as they once were!

Our duty? Isn’t it the purpose of education, academia, philosophy, religions, leaders of governments, to create awareness of the idea of being a good person and a good citizen and create the environment and systems that may inspire their citizens and enable them to strive to become good, better, best, working selflessly for good of society as a whole?

At one time philosophy and religions were the most respected; they were mentors, role models, leaders and guides of societies. Today, philosophy remains just an academic subject for study like any other subject and has no relevance to needs of society; they’re happy debating among themselves, in text torturing, using jargon which common man doesn’t understand. Religions have become divisive forces instead of uniting together and inspiring all people to strive for the highest.

Both of them were supposed to be seekers of the highest truth, but have been thrown out of mainstream systems, and seem to have no relevance to problems of today! Religions have become “belief” based and are no longer striving to the highest realization that their founders had experienced. Similarly, science is fixated in a fanatical way on matter; and refuses to acknowledge the idea of one-infinite-all-pervasive-Spirit, Consciousness, Self, the Subject.

How can we help evolve an action plan for mainstream schooling, where the idea of being good can be openly discussed and time and environment provided for all for reflection and to undertake some self-development practices?

It must be noted that we can imbibe ethics and human values in our character, not thru’ academic teaching, but only by striving thru’ daily Inner governance/self-development/self-transformation practices to imbibe the spirit of unselfishness, ethical, righteous conduct, commitment to fulfilling our respective duties, love, care, concern for all equally, and working for good of society.

These righteous actions and daily spiritual practices alone help purify the mind to realize highest truth ourselves. Belief in a religion must help becoming inspired to righteous conduct, higher realizations; not lead to violence, deceit, selfishness, hatred for ‘other’. Belief based religions somehow do not believe on ‘direct realization’ of that highest truth that their respective founders had experienced.

Situation in India : Some years back, our ministry of education, had created National Resource Centres for Value Education. This included various NGO’s of all Religions, Christian, Islam, the RKM, Aurobindo, IIT, IIM, etc. They had also created books on self-development. Life skills, etc. UNESCO has also brought out teachers manual for Peace Education. But the idea remained on paper when government changed! We have also declared Vivekananda’s Birthday as National Youth Day but his wisdom is not part of mainstream. Supreme Court has also directed that basic of all religions must be introduced to students to make them appreciate that essentials of religions are common and overcome hatred for other religions. However, these remain on paper!

Of course, independently a lot of NGOs are working actively but not as mainstream schooling. And we allow minority religions to teach their “Belief” religions in their schools, but idea of harmony of religions; of whole world as our family, though written on gates of Parliament, is not a part of mainstream schooling!

Now government of India has introduced the idea of Karma Yoga for government servants and International Yoga Day, etc. However, somehow we the educated still look up to West, and ignore our own wisdom! How to create practical action plan to inspire citizens to strive to be ‘good’ citizens is our challenge!
