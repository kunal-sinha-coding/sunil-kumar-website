India to Bharat · Essay #231

Completing the jigsaw puzzle of life

Can we complete a jigsaw puzzle without knowing the whole picture? Throughout the ages, people have been seeking happiness and the resources to remain happy but so far this happiness has remained elusive. Modern man had thought that 3 R’s education, science and technology will finally give us that happiness, but that does not seem to be happening!

Spirituality is happiness. Happiness is our universal need and goal. Happiness is only an ‘effect’; its ‘cause’ is goodness. And to be good and to do good we need a healthy worldview of reality so that we can walk-the-talk to happiness.

Religion and Yoga literally imply “Process to regain happiness” : Without learning, during schooling, about the correct process, map and skills needed to traverse the journey of life happily, we have been incorrectly believing in a worldview of ‘separateness’ and an ‘objective’ focus.

Selfishly, we treat ourselves, our family, our religion, our organisation, our party, our nation, all as ‘separate’ from each other. We seek answers for our happiness and miseries, in the world of ‘objects’ outside ourselves. We were hoping that modern political systems, education, and technology would finally give us back his lost happiness; but there are no signs of it, yet.

The ‘subjective’ focus of wisdom forces us to ask fundamental questions like: Who am I? Why am I here? Where am I going? How to get there? And then seeks for answers within. So, when we want happiness and want to become better, we need to look for answers both in the objective world outside and the subjective world within ourselves. In the objective world, we now judge ourselves by our modern standards of knowledge, living and enjoyment.

However, with a subjective focus we will ask the questions which Indian seers asked: What is that by knowing or getting which, everything is obtained and there is nothing more to be known or gained and by acquiring which we will be perpetually happy, blissful, peaceful and fulfilled. In seeking answers, we become reflective and selfless!

Let us discuss and reflect on the following issues:

  • What do you understand by happiness?
  • Is happiness in ‘objects’ or is it within us?
  • Do you think modern education, science and technology can give us happiness?
  • Do you think schooling should conduct course on Yoga and happiness?
  • Do you agree that happiness is a universal goal of mankind?
  • What in your view is the relationship between man and man, man and nature?
  • Do you believe in a worldview of oneness or separateness?
  • Are we moving towards or away from happiness?
