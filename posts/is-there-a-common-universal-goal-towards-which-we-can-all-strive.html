India to Bharat · Essay #226

Is there a common universal goal, towards which we can ‘all’ strive?

Individuals and families become a civilized community, society, nation when they have a common goal, a higher direction of work and life, towards which they all try to go forward.

Do we as India, that is Bharat, have a common goal?

Yes, we as Bharat have a goal. However, as Indians, that universal goal has got lost somewhere in our desire to copy and follow the Western models of education and governance which have led to consumerism, materialism, greed, as a goal, without awareness of the higher purposes of work and life!

So, what is the USP of Bharat?

The very term ‘Universe’ indicates the idea of unity, that the universe (uni+verse) is one integral whole. All its apparently separate parts working harmoniously towards a common purpose! In a similar manner, the multifarious waves, foams, droplets are one integral whole with the sea-water. While each wave may appear as a separate entity, it is actually nothing but sea-water appearing as a wave for a moment, before merging back as sea-water!

Just as the sea-water is the reality and the waves may ‘think’ of themselves as different individuals for the moment, in the same way, Bharat suggests that there is One Omnipotent, Omnipresent, Omniscient Blissful Consciousness, call it God or by any other Religious name or idea. And, the entire universe and living beings are in the ‘process’ of becoming or merging back into the ‘Blissful Consciousness’ or ‘ananda’, from where they emerge!

Scientists are now calling it an Intelligent-universe! This indicates the direction of growth and development of individuals and humanity. It is not our IQ, status or wealth that matters. What matters is our growth towards feelings of oneness with more and more, till we feel one with the whole creation. Growth is from selfishness towards unselfishness.

The process of becoming: Therefore, the clear and simple wisdom of Bharat says: “Strive to ‘Earn’ and ‘Enjoy’ through ‘Righteous conduct (dharma, ethics, human values, karatvya-plan)’ so as to remain on the critical path to ‘Perfection’, bliss, Ananda”.

The wisdom of Bharat suggests that we can never be really happy, peaceful or fulfilled till we experience Ananda, which is our inherent blissful nature, our natural swaroopa, as distinct from happiness which is dependent on sense-perceptions and on things outside us.

This clarity on our common goal can then inspire us to continuously cooperate and together struggle to harmonize and direct all our work, thoughts, choices, motives and activities towards experiencing our inherent blissful nature, ananda-swaroopa!

This, in essence, is the universal message of Bharat to all mankind. Different religions (literally meaning ‘regain’) suggest various ‘processes’ of becoming pure blissful consciousness.
