India to Bharat · Essay #095

Making the educated as Karma Yogi fit to be Shrestha of Samaaja

Whatever the Leaders do that people follow! As the educated, we’re the leaders of society and so we must reflect, are we as politicians, teachers, administrators, result-producers, workforce, good role models of society? And the global answer will be that we’re not! Why? because our teachers and politicians are not only not men of character, but we have no long-term vision and are in a state of “Perpetual Purposelessness”!

Our civilizational stupidity! Growing up must give us an opportunity to discuss and determine our short-term and long-term goals and ensure that they are in harmony, Rtm, with attaining the universal human aspirations for peace, happiness, prosperity for all. And Krishna explains how, i.e., learn to become ‘skillful in action’ and explains what a Karma Yogi is and how to be one. Incidentally, ‘be skillful in action’ is the motto of the Institute for training of our IAS officers; but alas our educated or the officers and politicians are never trained to be Karma Yogi. We govern and teach religions not through harmony and cooperation towards a vision of ‘oneness’ but thru polarization and divisions, led by money and power not Character and goodness! This is our civilizational stupidity, specially of India that was Bharat!

2. Will we not strive to perform our respective role/duties/swadharma to our very best if and when we happen to be on stage in front of a large audience, whatever be the size of our role? Isn’t this universe a stage where we come to perform our ‘duties’? The drama, orchestra, dance, or whatever is done on stage is pleasing when all individuals perform their duties to perfection and the whole ‘play’ is in Rtm, order, harmony. Do we know our duties and are we really in a vocation in harmony with our inherent samskaras? Are we striving to perform our duties to Perfection?

If we reflect, we will see the reality that this entire Creation, is designed and governed by a Conscious-Intelligence, aka One Supreme God. It is this Conscious-Intelligence, God, that then seemingly governs all different forces of nature, symbolically termed ‘conscious-intelligence’, aka devata/devi or deity.

Thus, God in cooperation with conscious-intelligence of deities creates and governs this universe and various inert-forces of matter and nature, according to the law of cause-effect, i.e., law of karma that ‘as we sow, so we reap’; and this helps to maintain Rtm, order. And the purpose of our having freedom of choices as humans is to learn the secret of how to work and enjoy this Creation and in and thru’ work how to come out of this chaos and disorder, using our ‘conscious-intelligence’ to once again regain (Religion/Yoga) Rtm with this Creation and Creator.

This idea of “conscious-intelligence” of human beings and deities representing all divine forces of nature, for which you and I are not responsible for, is just like the modern idea of Artificial-Intelligence, AI, of inert-robots governing various matter-forces/data!

Since mankind has the freedom of choice we must consciously, not artificially, learn and strive to fulfill our duties to go back from disorder to be once again in Rtm. This performing our prescribed duties, in the spirit of yajna for the good of society, purifies our mind, awakens our viveka-buddhi and we satisfy the deities; thru’ this mutual cooperation peace, prosperity, happiness is the fruit.

In accordance with the principle ‘as we sow, so we reap’, those who “enjoy what is given to them, without making ‘offerings’ in return (i.e., not fulfilling their duties), are verily thieves”; whereas those who selflessly work in the spirit of Yajna, spirit of Karma Yoga, purify their mind, Chitta-suddhi, to attain harmony; while those who don’t and are selfish, pollute their mind and thereby delay realizing Rtm. Am I a ‘thief’ just ‘receiving’ without serving? Am I enjoying money without earning it! From this holistic vision the small-entrepreneurs and skilled workers and laborers who satisfy the customers and then earn, are the great ones while we who earn without serving the society are the ‘thieves’!

Krishna explains this cycle of Creation, ‘All living beings subsist on food, and food is produced by rains. Rains come from the performance of Yajna, sacrifice, and sacrifice is produced by the performance of prescribed duties in the spirit of Karma Yoga.

Those who do not accept their responsibility of fulfilling their duties to maintain Rtm, Order, thru’ this spirit of selflessly fulfilling their duties for good of all, Yajna, are ‘sinful’; they live only for the delight of their senses; indeed, their lives are in vain. But those who rejoice in Rtm, may be considered as Self illumined, fully satisfied in the self; for them, there is no duty, though they seemingly work.

Whatever the Leaders do that people follow! If the wise ones ceased to perform prescribed actions the common people will follow them and there will be chaos that will destroy the peace of societies.

So, all those in leadership position anywhere – the Shrestha, the educated, the seniors and elders, the head of families, the head of departments, institutions, societies and particularly the elite, the leaders and the teachers must act without attachment, for the sake of leading people on the right path. The wise should not create discord in the intellects of people, who are attached to fruitive actions, by inducing them to stop work i.e., engage in some other work which may be beyond their character and competence for sake of more status, money, power. Rather, by performing their duties in an enlightened manner, they should inspire people to do their prescribed duties.
