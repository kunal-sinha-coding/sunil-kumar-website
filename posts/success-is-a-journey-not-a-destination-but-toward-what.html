India to Bharat · Essay #220

‘Success is a journey not a destination – but towards what?’

‘Am I’ not an integral part of Nature? Why then ‘I am’ not a subject of study in schools? Isn’t it very surprising, if we think of it, that while scientists have been very busy in trying to know all about everything, they haven’t bothered to study the human-being, the Jivatman who is not just integral with the universe, the ‘object’ being studied, but he is also the ‘knower’, the observer, the ‘subject’, who does the studying! Similarly, schools teach us all kinds of subjects relating to the jagat, but funnily enough, while teaching everything, they somehow exclude ourselves from it.

How is it that this curious and amazing fact has never dawned on us? One reason is that we are busy dealing with the objective world and worldly successes! We have not been established in the habit of daily self-introspection, during schooling. It is only when we strive to ‘know’ and ‘explore’ our own inner nature, we shall come to know ‘who I am’!

What will we gain from this study of ourselves? Study of ourselves, Self-reflection, going inwards, will make us realize that even though, I identify myself with my continuously changing ‘body-mind complex’; it just cannot be the ‘knower’, which witnesses all change! I will realize that without ‘Life’ our body is just dead matter! So, my unchanging self has to be ‘Life’, Atman, not the inert-matter, that the body is! And, only identified with the Atman within, we will be fulfilled; will feel ‘one’ with each other and have love, care, concern for each other.

Bharat’s Worldview of ‘Oneness’ was understood as: identified with body-mind complex we are different; when we purify our minds through ‘good thoughts and good actions’, fulfilling our duties, we grow in experiencing our oneness with our divine spirit, Atman. This growth manifests as gradual selflessness, from selfishness, as love, care, concern for others; as growth in feelings of apanapan with more and more, till we feel ‘one’ with the whole creation.

This ideal of ‘oneness’ is the foundation of all virtues. It was expressed in the instruction, ‘atithi devo bhava’, ‘worship the stranger, the guest’; ‘the whole world as our family’; etc. Just as a mother has spontaneous love for her child and will willingly face even a lion to protect her child; similarly, it is only this realization of our inherent nature being ananda-swaroopa that ultimately results in empathy, compassion, spontaneous feelings of love, care and concern for all.

Why should we struggle to be good? When we purify our mind thru’ virtuous working and living we are able to experience fulfillment, satisfaction, contentment which is my inherent nature, and attain to a realization that everything is obtained and there is nothing more to be known or gained; and, we remain perpetually happy, blissful, fulfilled, and peaceful, in the midst of all disturbances outside. This feeling of freedom from all grief, of poorna-ananda, of oneness with the whole creation, is really our own inherent nature, our swaroopa, and unknowingly it is this that we are all really seeking in life, in and through all our activities.

The Ideal of Work as worship, Karma-yoga : Excellence in work and fulfillment of ‘duties’ was insisted upon because Bharatiya wisdom asserts that we are at ‘work’, 24×7. Work is not just vocation; it includes all our thoughts, choices, motives, actions, goals, beliefs, value system, our words. We are doing ‘work’ all the time and each ‘work’ has left and continuously leaves a ‘mark’ on our mind and these ‘subjective’ impressions enslave us.

Our ‘work’ makes us what we are. Our work forms our worldview. Whatever I think or do or desire now, is governed by these impressions already stored in our mind by our own past actions. Purpose of work is therefore ’subjective’, i.e. ‘chitta-suddhi’, purity of mind. We should constantly strive to awaken our vivek and ask: will this choice or action purify or pollute my mind? Commitment to kartavya-palan and to quality and excellence in work, and to performance of all work and duties in a spirit of tyaga, seva and worship, for the good of samaaja and not merely for selfish ends, is the ideal which must be instilled during schooling.

This abstract “North”, the universal, common, ultimate goal of “Perfection”, of “Moksha”, may not be our immediate goal and we may not be interested in it at all at this stage. Nevertheless, this is the ultimate goal of all life. Once we know and fix our “North” we can slowly, steadily, at our own pace, grow towards it. And, this is the basic idea of goodness, of struggling to become good, better best and also helping all who are trying to be and do good, that is propagated by all Religions.

What then is worldly Success that we all are seeking? It is wisely said that success is not a destination, it is a continuous journey; but towards what? Towards more and more worldly achievements or towards more and more fulfillment, peace, happiness? This idea of our oneness with all and the ideal of work as worship must therefore, be a compulsory part of schooling.
