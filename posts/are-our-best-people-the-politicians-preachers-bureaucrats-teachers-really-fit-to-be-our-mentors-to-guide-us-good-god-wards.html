India to Bharat · Essay #063

Are our best people, the ‘politicians, preachers, bureaucrats, teachers’, really fit to be our mentors to guide us good/God-wards?

Recruitment: We the educated have to suffer the rule of politicians whose goal is only how to win elections; and vision is limited just to 2-3 years. Therefore, as intelligent educated and common good people, it becomes our obligatory duty to reflect and plan how to educate, train, recruit i) Teachers as Mentors from among those who undergo schooling thru’ B.Ed. courses to become Teachers, and ii) Administrators as Mentors, from those who undergo about a year training at their respective Training Institutes, watched by a Faculty, who can Mentor and select from the selected!

Just because someone does well intellectually, he may not really be fit to be a role-model of society. If both Teachers and Administrators are trained “To Be and Make Mentors”, before and frequently after recruitment, then they can be inspired to imbibe the spirit of commitment to fulfilment of duties as a sign of progress, development, goodness and suggest to their trainees, various ways to awaken viveka-buddhi and build powers of mind, will-power, self-control, concentration to know and walk-the-talk.

Only with such training and inspiration can Teachers and Administrators also learn to be Mentors and Counsellors during their interactions. They can then not only mentor, but also identify those prone to indiscipline, arrogance, etc. and nurture them and even have them disqualified!

What are the current social, political, educational, technological predicaments after the past ideas of fascism, autocracy, dictatorship, communism, unjust rulers with money and power, and so-called evils of Brahminism and caste, etc.? Are we happier with the present ideas of AI, IT, liberal, secular, free market capitalism? Are we really happy with present ‘political animals’ as our parents and leaders in all walks of life, thanks to an Administration and Governance bereft of all focus on goodness and character?

Do we need to go back further and draw hope and wisdom from the wellspring of ancient philosophers and Mentors of global societies?

2. Today we have learnt from the West, to judge people based on their outer, external behaviour and we teach ‘Behavioural sciences’, so that our behaviour and manners appear good, and we judge people based on external visible ‘possessions’ like Money and Position. And entire governance, education and development is planned towards fulfilment of ‘Artha and Kaama’, ‘money and fulfilment of desires. These are now our universal goal, common North! Thus, all schooling and evaluation is based on external ‘possessions’ – intellect as marks and degrees, money, position, status. And character is nobody’s concern!

In contrast India that is Bharat, has always given highest respect to the ‘Inner-quality of Mind’, that alone manifests as behaviour and character, irrespective of money, vocation, position!

Our scientists have focused on studying our Inner, Internal human personality of Mind-Prana-intellect. And we’ve discovered that lower intellect is IQ whereas higher intellect Viveka can go deeper within and realize that as ‘body-mind’ we’re all nothing, but dead-matter and so higher goal is to realize the fact that we become a living-being only because’ our matter-mind is enlivened, made conscious by Consciousness, Self, Atman, Spirit.

And so, we try to go inwards to Know the Kingdom of God within and gradually realize that only thru Righteousness, Goodness, Selflessness etc. can we befriend the Self, within and ‘love thy neighbour as thy OWN, Self! Modern society believes we’re ‘Body-mind-intellect’, BMI only! So naturally, led by our ‘I-ego-sense’ we treat each other as “separate” and so generally are “Self-centered”, if not selfish.

3. Need for Mentors as our Administrators: All things in creation are based on a mixture of three qualities of Tamas, Rajas, Sattva, TRS. Only human minds, have freedom of choice and so we continuously reform or pollute our minds thru’ our thoughts-actions, and become different with different Quality of TRS Mind, Samskaras.

Growing up, training and recruitment, must be a process of educating us to rise from Tamas (inert, lazy, unrighteous) thru’ Rajas (active, ambitious) to attain a predominantly Sattvic quality of mind (calm, peaceful, open to higher wisdom) thru striving to fulfil our duties, selflessly working for good of others, prayers, worship, etc. etc.

Preponderance of TRS quality of mind effects both our general nature; and at different periods, we can have preponderance of any combination of these. The Administrators who are supposedly the “best” of all the educated people of our nation, can easily understand this technical (!) idea and then Learn to be and make mentors!

A Sattvic mind is more amenable to Righteousness, Commitment to Duties, etc. and more ready and willing to launch towards a common, universal idea of good, better, highest and best, and be determined to realize the purpose of life, a “North”, “Perfection”, “Heaven”, Freedom from all bondages, etc., howsoever we may understand it!

Thus, whatever may be our Money or Vocation or Position, it is only the Quality of Mind that really matters, not mere Behaviour! Throughout history India has respected character, goodness, righteousness, and our entire culture is based on highest respect to Dharma and Moksha, i.e. Righteousness and “Perfection” i.e. “Freedom from life-death cycle. Artha and Kaama must be governed and controlled by not political animals but by those Leaders who are fit to be our Mentors and guide us God-wards, not mere money, though money is fun!
