India to Bharat · Essay #205

Were our ancestors Monkeys or Rishis?

1. 3R’s Secular Education has kept us away from our Composite Culture: To his dismay, an Indian young fella has just learnt in school that his forefathers have evolved from ‘monkeys’ not from Rishis as his mother had told, and while doing his homework he questions his scientist father who confirms it.

At bedtime, as per daily routine, his mother, an uneducated Bharatiya rustic, narrates to him stories from our Vedic lore, of how great were our forefathers. The boy suddenly sits up and laughs, no mother you’re wrong, our ancestors were not sages and heroes but were monkeys, father has also confirmed! With her rustic humour and wisdom the mother tells the boy, as you know we come from different families and before our marriage we lived in different places and so, from their arrogance and antics, it is likely their ancestors were monkeys, but I am certain that our ancestors were great Rishis who have intuitively discovered and freely given the Vedantic wisdom for mankind in our Upanishads, Ramayan, Puranas, etc.! Do ask your teachers to explain how could monkeys sit together and create different languages and the mammoth Vedas, the spiritual wisdom of all mankind.

2. Worldview of Oneness & Subjective Focus: Son, the mother continues, the choice is yours, take the best of both. Be an Indian and go after the riches of the external world without greed, acquisitiveness, the “ Asuric ” materialistic qualities. However, be also a Bharati. B e in “ Sur” , in harmony, reflect upon the Vedantic worldview of “Oneness” and its focus on the “Subjective”; and, in and thru’ all your thoughts-actions strive to purify your inner-mind so that your consciousness gradually expands so that you have love, care, concern for the whole creation and grow towards the Vedantic worldview and feeling of “oneness” with all.

3. The Modern problem – Where am I going? ‘If you don’t know where you are going, any road will get you there’, says Lewis Carroll, in Alice in Wonderland; we are all like Alice, in a state of perpetual purposelessness! Vedas clarify who I am, why I am here and where am I going! The Vedas are said to be the creator’s design of creation and the way to meet the creator itself! They’re therefore, concomitant with creation, and the most ancient spiritual wisdom for all mankind!

4. Understanding the Vedas: Shouldn’t there be a creator’s design of creation? Shouldn’t the very first men have spiritual guidance or should they await a Prophet? Can just a Prophet satisfy all unequal people for all times? Unfortunately, in the wake of material and spiritual attacks on Bharatiya culture, in the garb of secularism and for minority-appeasement, we’ve been denied the ideas and wisdom of our Vedas and Vedanta, even though the Fundamental Duties enjoin on us to know our composite culture so as to have brotherhood among us, and not allow religions and democracy to divide us. T he Vedas are that by the study of which we attain that consciousness by attaining which everything is known! We attain the knowledge of the ‘whole’; of our self, of nature, the purpose of our being, of creation, of the one infinite, Om-nipotent God.

4.1 Veda literally means Knowledge & Vedanta implies that essential Knowledge in a nutshell, which is beyond sense perception, and which leads to the wisdom of ‘oneness’. Unlike other modern one-person, one-scripture separatist religions that we know of today, there is no religion as Hinduism. People living in this region of Bharat , were called Bharati , because they were focused on ‘ striving (rati) for the highest illumination (Bha)’.

4.1 What inspired them? The very creation is obviously a highly complex, conscious, and ‘Intelligent-design’ (as Science now acknowledges) and so, from the very beginning of creation, and concomitantly with creation itself all the very first men, need to know millions of things, including a ‘religion’ to guide and inspire them to be good, and the Vedas and Rishis, serve this purpose for all mankind. They address themselves to all humanity and include in their infinite fold the very idea and process of creation itself.

4.2 Vedas, as mother of creation, posits, and present the Vedantic worldview of ‘ oneness ’; of ‘ the whole creation as one family ’. This oneness is also apparent from the term ‘ uni+verse ’, meaning ‘one, united, unit’. Vedanta does not divide people by talking of ‘my religion, my Prophet, my scripture’ versus ‘your ‘religion’, your Prophet, your scripture’, but addresses all mankind and inspires people to strive to know and attain the highest, in and thru’ their daily vocation and living, following their own ‘religion’, at their own pace, by developing their own skills and competencies. ‘Religion’ literally means to ‘regain’ (our identity of love and oneness or unity (Yoga) with the whole creation. Vedanta doesn’t ask us to belong, believe, hero-worship or get attached to this or that religion; it inspires us to struggle, strive unto death as it were, to acquire, the viveka, self-control, etc. to walk-the-path towards oneness with the whole, in and thru’ our daily work-life.

5. Schooling must harmonize the divisive idea of ‘My vs. Your’ Religion: Today, schooling and academia have the responsibility and wherewithal to nurture and transform citizens into enlightened citizens thru’ the idea that the universal purpose of all religion is only to inspire us all to strive to become good and better towards a common ‘best’, in and thru’ our daily vocation and living.

In this globalized, 3R’s and e-linked world we don’t need the divisive idea of ‘my Religion; my God; my Scripture’ Vs. ‘your Religion; your God; your Scripture’. Schooling must teach and help us sincerely practice our respective self-development religious processes, so that we together may develop growing feelings of oneness and love for all creation. This alone can ensure that we do not allow those religious ideas to grow which lead to persecution, communal strife, and intolerance.

And, this will happen when all schooling, mainstream or minority, provides one-hour daily for self-study and self-development practices so that we all recognize divinity in every man and woman, and our whole effort and scope will be to realize our own true, divine nature and in aiding humanity, similarly.
