India to Bharat · Essay #011

Holistic worldview: Is the uni-verse illogical, accidental, divisive or logically loving & uniting!

Need for a universal Holistic Worldview of my ‘Self’ as whole ‘Reality’: In our daily Workings and living, every moment, we deal with ‘things, beings, situations, experiences’ based on our respective Selfish and Self-centered perceptions and ideas as stored in our respective Mind. And therefore, our Perceptions and Responses to same situations are generally self-centered and different.

I am what my Self is, but my Mind thinks otherwise! Without the Mind knowing the ‘Reality’ as it is, naturally the Mind makes us all, self-centered!

I/we must reflect and appreciate that the external world of things, people, situations, etc. that I/we perceive and interact with are nothing but a projection of my mind. And I continuously refine/pollute my Mind based on my choices, actions and their consequences – and thereby form memories, beliefs, values, ideas, which get impressed and stored in our mind and become our Worldview, Samskaras, mindset, characteristics, tendencies, etc. I and I alone am the creator of my own future, selfless & good or selfish & less good`!

2. As Protectors of Samaja, Governance and Academia have the obligatory duty to inspire and prepare citizens to be intellectually convinced in the truth that ‘my future will be ‘good’ only if I strive to be and make the ‘good’, and we all build the ability to know and fulfil our respective duties, according to our inherent nature (Kartavya-palan, Swadharma) and Dharma (righteous, ethical conduct), irrespective of personal biases!

When we do wrong and fail to fulfil our duties, our mind becomes impure, i.e. narrow, petty, self-centered, and so we tend to slip out of logic, security and safety and thus break our connection with the ‘whole’; we become out of tune with the Rtm, Order of the universe and suffer the consequences in the form of pain and misery.

During growing up, we must all get established in self-development practices to get intellectual conviction in the ‘Reality’ that Selfless, Righteous fulfilment of duties alone can guarantee the continuous unchanging peace, bliss, joy, Ananda, that is our real ‘need’, because that is the Inherent Nature of my/our SELF.

3. Study circles: Our Mind being limited and logical, finds it difficult to perceive the paradoxical, contradictory logic of the whole Universe as One, encompassing and containing all polar opposites, good and evil – but why ‘uni’- ‘verse’! During growing up we can discuss, reflect, debate, explore together in academic study circles to acquire a Healthy Worldview of our ‘Inner Blissful Nature’. Without clarity on where to seek for peace, happiness, bliss that we all inherently desire, we ignorantly seek pleasures and happiness externally!

4. The universal reality and essence of all Religions as logically expressed in Vedanta is that identified with Life, Consciousness, we’re all ONE and thru’ selfless fulfilment of our duties and love for all, we Re-gain (meaning of religion) our Oneness with all. Vedanta accepts all religions and does not justify killing, violence, threats and bribery. However, like it or not, the fact is that we’ve all committed many wrongs, sins, selfishness, violence, etc. in our life/lives to reach where we now are, thanks to the principle of cause-effect.

We, i.e. our Mind, needs to be trained, reflect, practice and learn that mere happiness and pleasures just come and go and are always accompanied by pain and misery, if obtained thru’ wrong and selfish deeds. As enlightened citizens schooling and preaching must give us the conviction that excessive desires, passions, hatred-love are likely to lead to fanaticism!

Like all other creatures, man is also meant to live with and in a relaxed relationship with life in Rtm, rhythm, Sur, harmony with creation. Mankind alone has freedom of Choice and therefore has to learn from childhood how to train and educate its respective Mind to strive to realize and follow its Swadharma, i.e. righteous, ethical, moral, conduct based on universal values or truth, love, peace, non-violence, etc. to Re-gain Oneness with all.

5. ‘To be in Zen is to be in love with life. Logic is argumentative, articulate. Love is silent. Logic is a necessary evil. Use logic but use it to drop out of it, into life, love.

Love cannot be expressed in words. Gratitude is even more difficult to express. Words are very tiny, trivial, mundane. They cannot express anything that is really profound, authentic, true. That’s why we invent various way to avoid words and use symbols, e.g. holding hands, hug, be silent together, bow down and tears arise in gratitude. Saying thank-you and thank-you notes are mundane. The tragedy with gratitude is that when we have a problem, inability to sleep well, then as soon it is solved and we sleep well, we forget to express gratitude. When we have good sleep, good food, etc. we forget to express gratitude!’

That’s why we are taught by our elders to daily express our gratitude to i) all saints and sages thru’ whom we have got wisdom ii) to God and all gods and goddesses who preside over forces of nature iii) to all mankind and all our forefathers who have helped us reach where we’re, and iv) to the kingdom of animals, plants, minerals kingdom, nature.

We “fall in love” attached to finite-self & things; but we Rise in Love when we See & Worship God in all, thru’ Work.

The Infinite Divine Self, God, contains all that is finite; Mere Knowledge of the ‘finite’ will always keep us limited, self-centered, selfish! So, exhorts Vedanta, Arise, Awake, realize your Divine Infinitude – This is essence of Practical Vedanta and Karma Yoga.
