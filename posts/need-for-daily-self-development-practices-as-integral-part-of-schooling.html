India to Bharat · Essay #195

Need for daily-self-development practices as integral part of Schooling:

Our Responsibility: Without daily self-development practices we cannot remain on the growth path of values and ethics to attain perfection. Academia must take the leadership to learn of various self-development practices as available with Religions and Yoga to counter the growing materialism, selfishness, terrorism, greed, etc., and inspire us towards Perfection. Religions and politics having become divisive forces, it now becomes the responsibility of academia to engage with religions to evolve and establish the young in daily time-tested self-development practices, as available with various religions, so as to inspire them to ‘know’ and ‘build the strength’ to walk towards the highest goal of mankind.

2. Shared Vision of Future: In various ways, different Religions claim that there is but one God; that God is omnipotent, omnipresent, omniscient. And, God is the creator of both the living-beings and nature. From this rational and universal belief of an all-powerful and all-knowing entity God, arise some fundamental, academic, and logical principles, i.e.

In the beginning of creation, God alone is.

God is the ultimate “Cause” of the living-beings and all nature.

‘Living-beings and nature’ are the “Effects” of which God is the ultimate “Cause”.

As academia a fundamental principle we should understand and remember: Effect is not different from the cause, but the cause is different from the effect.

i.e. Universe is not different from God because it has no independent entity, but God is independent and different from the Universe, the effect.

Similarly, Living-beings are not different from God, but God is different from Living-beings.

All these principles arise from the fact that all religions accept God as the sole cause of the Universe (literally one-song!); if so, there can be nothing different from Him. Even Science is now accepting the idea of an Intelligent Universe and is in search of the Unity behind the variety! Of course, apparently “God, Living-beings and Universe” appear different from each other!

3. So comes the question: what is the purpose of work and life? Is it not to re-connect ourselves, the living beings to God? Shouldn’t academia sort out the apparent differences between religions and draw out from them, a set of universal self-development practices thru’ which we can try to re-connect. Students can be provided one-hour daily throughout schooling for e.g. prayers, meditation, worship, etc. according to their respective religions and to study, question, argue, debate, introspect, to get an intellectual conviction in the idea that being good is really good, and how to be good, better, best!

4. Purpose of Religions & Yoga: Religion literally means to ‘regain’ and implies that it is man’s duty to regain its lost connection with God, the highest and best pursuit towards the highest and best human goal, ‘Perfection’. Thus, the purpose of religions, Yoga, human values, being good, virtues, character, etc. is to resolve the apparent contradiction and show our common goal, the highest and best goal, ‘Perfection’ and how to attain it in and thru’ our daily working and living.

Similarly, Yoga means ‘to unite’ and suggests various self-development processes, practicing which everyone and anyone of whatever race, religion, nationality, gender, etc. etc. can direct themselves towards ‘Perfection’. We have Karma Yoga, Bhakti Yoga, Gyan Yoga, Raja Yoga, i.e. we can strive to attain perfection thru’ our Work and vocation, doing it with all Devotion, with Knowledge of our common goal, thru’ Self-development practices of prayers, meditation, yoga-asanasa, pranayam, etc. according to our respective faiths.

Religion and Yoga are thus a pursuit of a universal idea of ‘Perfection’ to regain our connection with God thru’ our daily work and living. Shouldn’t schooling help us become good, better, best!

5. Need for Academia to bring essence of Yoga, Religion in Schooling: Religion involves ‘knowledge’ of the omnipotence and oneness of God and it needs daily self-development practices to improve quality of our mind and thoughts and direct them towards the highest and best, thru’ daily routine of prayers, worship, meditation so as to develop sraddha and devotion to excellence and perfection in work, done unselfishly for good of society. To imbibe these ideals needs lifelong continuous practice.

It was the responsibility of society to create educational and social systems which would engage people in vocations thru’ which they could put in their best, in an effort to regain and attain the highest and best.

The problem is that modern religions, instead of working together with academia to educate and inspire people to ‘regain’ their connection with God, have become divisive forces by dividing themselves into ‘my-religion’ versus ‘your-religion’.

Religions now ‘believe’ that only their God, only their Scripture, Prophet, Master, rituals, ways of worship etc. are real and the one and only path to ‘regain’. Naturally, this narrow-minded belief has created fanatics, terrorists, ‘clash of civilizations’, forced conversions, disharmony and conflicts between religions. And, religion is no longer a respectful idea

Religions have become ‘belief-based’ and separatist. And, since they no longer allow intellectual and scientific questioning and academic discipline, they’re no longer part of mainstream schooling.

6. Conclusion: Society is becoming more and more selfish, materialistic, greedy. We’re no longer striving for the highest and best. Since Politics, Religions, Administrators have failed, it is now up to the Academia and Schooling, the ‘Brahmins’, to take the responsibility once again for inspiring and educating people to become good, better, towards the ‘highest and best’.
