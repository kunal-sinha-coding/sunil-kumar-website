India to Bharat · Essay #016

To be ‘Karma Yogi Public Servants’ that GOI envisions, education must establish citizens in DHARMA, righteous conduct

1. ‘Dharma protects those who protect Dharma’: We must have an intellectual conviction, not mere belief, that if we don’t follow Dharma, we are bound to suffer the harmful consequences of adharma.

Just as parents protect their children, it is the obligatory duty of those who occupy positions of power in governance and as teachers, to protect the society by ensuring that the young and old get established in Dharma and are continuously inspired to work for the long-term good of society as a whole.

India from the ancient times was aware that this ‘space-time-causality’ Uni-verse is governed by the principle of cause-effect. Knowing this, Indian idea of religion and education was focused on establishing us in commitment to righteousness, Dharma so that we could all then live together peacefully and happily as a family, by knowing and having the powers of will and self-control to be able to follow our respective dharma, Swadharma.

Unfortunately, thanks to modern ideas of education as mere 3R’s we don’t even understand the meaning of Dharma, let alone the deep, profound nuances of the idea and importance of following our respective Dharma!

As educated leaders of society, we’ve miserably failed to focus on creating systems that encourage us to imbibe the spirit of Dharma-shastra! The sad result is that as modern India we have forgotten the truth that I and I alone am responsible and am continually creating my own future every moment through my intentions, actions and attitudes to work.

2. In a way, Dharma Shastra is a science of desire management: Dharma-Shastra may be defined as “That which protects and saves us from grave dangers and that which indicates what is good or not good for us and for our welfare and well-being. Only once we know, can we learn, imbibe and practice following it. Dharma is a very holistic word having many meanings; it has no equivalent word! it certainly isn’t religion as commonly understood.

3. That is Dharma, good conduct which prepares (purifies) our mind so that by practicing dharma in our daily work and life all human beings, whatever be our work, status or religion, can reach the goal of all-round peace, prosperity, happiness, perfection. And we must learn that the mind needs to be disciplined, prepared from a young age thru’ life-skill practices and learning to respect the elders, parents, teachers, atithi and learning to work as service to society and/or as worship of God in jiva-atman.

Dharma consists of mutual love, respect, sadaachaara ie conduct which when followed gradually takes us towards ‘satt’, ie, eternal, changeless, conscious, blissful existence that is the same self, within us all. Dharma is integration of thoughts, words, deeds; dharma means that which integrates, unites (Yoga), re-gains (Religion).

Dharma is that which sustains, upholds, integrates, maintains the whole universe – it is that righteous conduct, kartavya-palan which ensures prosperity of individuals and nations when citizens are guided, protected, disciplined and nourished by Dharma.

Education must ensure that every citizen is trained and inspired to imbibe the spirit of Karma Yoga and Fundamental Duties just as every citizen is obligated to follow the Constitution of India. Only when all citizens are established in Dharma, can they regulate their activities of Artha-Kama thru Dharna to remain happy in this world and beyond….

4. Education must give us an intellectual conviction in the power of Dharma, goodness, righteous conduct and establish us in Mind-management practices; only then can we be inspired to strive to learn to know and choose every moment that ‘intention-choice-action’ that leads to our mutual long term good!

Teaching and Preaching are not factories to produce babus’ and munims’ and blind fanatic followers … we need enlightened citizens who are capable of providing leadership to our self-governing institution. We need citizens imbued with the spirit of commitment to duties, selfless service, creativity, liberal in outlook.

We must overcome the harm caused by the Macaulay’s doctrine that stated, “We must at present do our best to form a class who may be interpreters between us and the millions whom we govern; a class of persons, Indian in blood and colour, but English in taste, in opinions, in morals, and in intellect’.

5. The cause of my ‘happiness and prosperity’ or ‘misery and poverty’ are the direct consequences of my own ‘Virtuous or non-virtuous’ intentions and deeds … my status is not caused by anything external, but it is directly caused by my own selfless or selfish intentions and deeds!

Teachers and Preachers must realize that mere giving of knowledge secular or spiritual isn’t enough… the fruit will be judged by our ability to create citizens and believers who are proud of their nation while not narrow in their outlook and working to promote peace and happiness, filled with lofty ideal of whole world as our family, not hatred for others, but by realizing there’s no other; we’re all one as conscious existence!

Education and preaching must focus on inculcating in our minds the love for truth and the spirit of reverence and passion for selfless work as worship. At the same time, it must also foster in us love for the motherland and the spirit that enables the soldiers to sacrifice everything for the good of others and one’s country.
