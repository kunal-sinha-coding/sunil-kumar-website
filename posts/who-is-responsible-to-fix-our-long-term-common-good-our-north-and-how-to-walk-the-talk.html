India to Bharat · Essay #104

Who is responsible to fix our long term common good, our ‘North’ and how to walk-the-talk?

Don’t we see our best people, religious heads, preachers, leaders, spreading hatred for the ‘other!’ Don’t we see our best people, even the Supreme Court judges, differing on big issues and giving 5-4 judgments? Can even our best fix our North? Is education and training inspiring and guiding us to explore, debate, discuss, discover our ‘long term common good?’ Is it telling us ‘how to walk-the-talk’ towards our ‘long-term good’, our common North?

Modern schooling is teaching all about the objective, ‘observable’, outer, external world and how to solve the problems ‘outside’ but is totally ignorant, unaware, and unconcerned with teaching about our individual ‘inner’ self and discussing and determining the basic question of our ‘North’: why am I here; where am I going and how to get there.

All religions are also focused on the outer whereas religions have two distinct aspects: subjective & objective: personal & collective; spiritual & social; inward-spiritual practices & rituals/ceremonial; internal peace, bliss & external ‘love or hatred’ for others; essential & non-essential’, etc.

Belonging to a religion or conversion to another religion means just that we learn to accept the social norms of my or another religion. It is only the outer, the collective aspect of a religion that converts.

The inner, personal aspect of any religion is the same, pray, meditate, reflect and be inspired to walk-the-talk towards good, better, best, a “North”. Can the North be different, just because we follow different outer norms?

However, the two aspects ‘internal’ and ‘external’ are so closely mixed up together that one leads to the other. With the foreign religious invasions too, our social norms were totally upturned – freedom to believe in various ways of worship was laughed at and the idea of ‘only my god and my way” was tried to be imposed! Modern religions believed that, ‘my way’ is the only way, leading to ‘clash of civilisation’. And of course as per our social norms we happily accepted each of them! They of course, still keep fighting and trying to impose their idea and belief in ‘only my-way’, thru terrorism, bribery, force. But are we growing inwardly and spiritually towards selflessness and love for all?

But, adopting the social norms and religions of the invaders, has India once again become a sone-ki-chidiya and a spiritual leader as we were throughout history? Have we become more spiritual and blissful? Have we become more prosperous, selfless, caring for others? Are modern schooling helping our inner growth towards love for all externally and peace within?

Instead of my vs your religion/god, we must identify and highlight the universal, uniting, ‘loving-all- equally-aspects’ of religions.

What is under my control? Where can I best focus? My ‘self’ or others? Only the former aspects, i.e. subjective; personal; spiritual; inward-spiritual practices are the essential part of schooling and growing up and under my ‘exclusive’ control. Only I am responsible for managing my responses to any situation. However, I need to learn not to react but learn to “respond” to situations and gradually practice win-win responses by learning to manage and direct our thoughts from selfishness of me-and-mine to unselfishness of love for all; of the whole world as our family.

Fruit of schooling and religions must be to give us the ability for win-win responses. Only if I grow towards love for all equally am I fit to be a leader, preacher, and mentor of society! If we as governance and academia allow preachers to spread hatred for the ‘other’, then naturally we’re responsible for the spread of strife, discord, conflicts in society.

The fruit of schooling, religions and growing up must be to be convinced and to realise ourselves, that there is no ‘other’; that all people and creatures are created by God and, all are equally God’s children.

We shouldn’t allow followers of religions to create their own ideas of ‘other’ & then spread hatred for ‘them’. Schooling must inspire us with the idea and ideal of ‘love for all’; and show us how thru’ righteousness, and our respective religious practices, prayers, etc. we can walk-the-talk. Because of the imagined ‘others’ and hatred for ‘others’, modern so-called rational and educated world has thrown away religions from mainstream schooling and systems!

As the educated leaders, academia and devotees of all religions, we have to unite and together explore how we can be inspired to imbibe this ideal in our character.

In this global and inter-connected world as a village, governance and academia must be made responsible and accountable to keep religion within its proper limits of ‘love, care, concern for all equally’ and give freedom to schooling, academia, society to grow to ‘know’ and learn to walk towards the good, better, best – our universal “North”.

Basically, we all are social animals and as a slave of society we willy-nilly follow its norms and beliefs and are nowadays being led by political animals in the garb of self-centred preachers and leaders. Even reformers with very good intentions make the mistake of holding religions accountable for all the killings, terrorism, horrors of priest-craft and degeneration we see in the name of religions!

The time has come to insist that all HRD, education, governance, religions, must be held directly responsible and accountable to focus on the essentials – to produce enlightened citizens who are inspired and committed to striving to be a ‘karma yogi’ for whom work and vocation becomes their highest worship; opportunity to serve man and the creation as God.
