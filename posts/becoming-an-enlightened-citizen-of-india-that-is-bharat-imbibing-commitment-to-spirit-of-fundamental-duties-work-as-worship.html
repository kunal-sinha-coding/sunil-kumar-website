India to Bharat · Essay #061

Becoming an enlightened citizen of India that is Bharat: Imbibing commitment to spirit of fundamental duties & work as worship

1. Our Challenge: i) How do we describe, if not define an enlightened citizen of India that is Bharat? ii) How do we ‘Be & Make Mentors’ !

2. Enlightened Citizen: Besides the present teaching in schools and universities, we must provide one-hour daily throughout the duration of our academic and career forming training, the time, space, inspiration, opportunities to understand and imbibe the spirit of the ideas of ‘Values, Morals, Ethics, Righteousness’; ‘Fundamental Duties’; ‘Work as Worship’; Harmony of Religions’; ‘Worldview of Oneness’; etc. and various Life skills and Self-development practices for managing our Mind and awakening our Discriminative Intellect to plan and walk towards our long-term SMART goals.

And we must discuss and understand the importance of imbibing the spirit of Mentorship as teachers and officers in any field.

3. To Be & Make Mentors!: Besides teaching of various subjects as teachers and as parents and officers, sending our children to get educated, if we can also be made conscious of our role as Mentors, we can all consciously learn to be a good, responsible, accountable, enlightened citizen. We can imbibe the essence of education as described by UNESCO as, Learning the Treasure Within and Learning to be, and to live together; besides the usual Learning to know and do.

We all need to understand the need not just for the educated and literate citizens but Enlightened Citizens! Unless we’re striving to be good how can we inspire others! We must explore the possibilities and opportunities for learning to BE and MAKE MENTORS.

4. Selfless work as worship, aka KYi, and ii) the Fundamental Duties of our Constitution: Mentorship programs can help and inspire us to imbibe the spirit of FDs:

a) “Strive for Excellence”, including Inner Human excellence.

b) Understand the “spirit of common brotherhood”.

c) Understand what the foundation of our ancient civilizational “composite culture” is, which we’re required to “preserve”, now expressed as “whole world as our family”, and as G20 Global vision of One World. One Family. One Future” and discuss how to “Preserve the Composite Culture”

d) Protect the “Environment”

e) “Develop scientific temper” (not mere belief, but spirit of enquiry)

f) “Follow the Ideals of the Freedom Struggle”.

5. As teachers and officers we must imbibe our Vedantic ideal of: “First learn, be intellectually convinced in the power of goodness, and strive to imbibe and get established in Righteousness; then ‘Earn’ & ‘Enjoy’ and be determined to Walk-the-Talk to Perfection, Happiness, all round prosperity”!

6. Whatever be our religion or politics our challenge is how do we be and make mentors who are inspired to bring this wisdom to mainstream Schooling! Maharishi Dayanand, one of the great spiritual sage, intellectual thinkers and well-wisher of our nation formed the Arya Samaj for the rational, emotional, and religious renewal of the Indian Society, with a call, “Back to the Vedas”, combining the best of West and Bharat thru’ the spread of education and our worldview of ‘whole world as our family’. He believed that this was necessary to curb and prevent Westernization of the educated, and ensure that the educated would once again return to know, love and respect our time-tested ancient ideal of work as worship and to treat our mother, father, teacher, even Atithi, guests, as worshipful.

7. Shouldn’t we learn the wisdom of our ancient Indian thought, traditions, and cultural values of Satyam vada, truth, honesty, and universal brotherhood!

8. Are we producing a Truthful nation? Is modern western led literate, educated, liberal, freedom oriented democratic society, really truthful?

Don’t we all accept those as gentlemen and our political masters whose ‘thoughts-deeds’ aren’t in harmony! Most think of satya, being truthful, as not lying! It is much deeper than that! Satya is derived from Satt which means the One, infinite, omni-present, omni-scient, omni-potent, Changeless Existence, aka God, howsoever preachers and devotees, may express that Infinitude.

Are our ‘thoughts, words, intents, actions’ really in harmony , Order, Rtm with the Changeless Existence! Righteousness, ethical conduct, goodness, spirituality, etc. are those thoughts-actions which takes us towards Rtm, Oneness with the whole Creation as our Family – G20 Global vision of One World. One Family. One Future thru’ Work as Worship, aka be a Karma Yogi.

Don’t we all know the advice of Satyam jayate; Ten Commandments; Be good and do good; etc. taught by all parents, preachers, teachers, etc.; but by not following them don’t we become hypocrites and liars! Why don’t we ensure that the educated and particularly those in academia and governance are those who sincerely strive to imbibe commitment to following universal values of ethical and righteous conduct; otherwise, aren’t we all being educated to be liars and political manipulators?

Should we focus only on Teaching & Preaching during childhood, schooling and growing-up? Shouldn’t we also discuss and try to give an intellectual conviction to the young in the power of goodness, and also inspire the young to understand the Need and Importance of Daily INNER-Governance Practices, for Imbibing, Inculcating, Nurturing, Identifying, Evaluating based on the spirit of Righteousness.

It was not by accident that the greatest geniuses of all ages were deeply spiritual beings! Albert Einstein says: ‘I’m not an atheist. The problem involved is too vast for our limited minds’. ‘Everyone who is seriously involved in the pursuit of science becomes convinced that a spirit is manifest in the laws of the universe – a spirit vastly superior to that of man, and one in the face of which we with our modest powers must feel humble.’

David Bohm says, ‘ Deep down, the Consciousness of mankind is one’ – Man is Jivatman, Body enlivened by Consciousness; United (Yoga) with Consciousness, i.e., Re-gaining with which (Religion) we’re all one family!
