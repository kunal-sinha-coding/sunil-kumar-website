India to Bharat · Essay #083

Knowing & managing our Mind towards “Good, Better, Best”

# “From our childhood upward, we have been taught only to pay attention to things external, but never to things internal; hence most of us have nearly lost the faculty of observing the internal mechanism. To turn the mind, as it were inside, stop it from going outside, and then to concentrate all its powers, and throw them upon the mind itself, in order that it may know its own nature, analyze itself, is very hard work. Yet that is the only way…” – Vivekananda

# “Sow a thought, reap an action. Sow an action, reap a habit! Sow a habit, reap a character! Sow a character, reap a destiny”! ‘We are what our thoughts are.’ “On the quality of our thoughts, depends the quality of our life.

”We are identified with desire alone. What we desire, we resolve to do; as is our resolution, so is our action. And, whatever we carry out into action, that we reap – as we sow, so we reap. All this happens in our mind, our instrument, and enlivened by Life, Consciousness.

Desires arise from tendencies we are continuously acquiring, every moment, by our Choices, Intentions/Motives, Thoughts, Words, Actions”, which leave ‘Impressions’, ‘Samskara’, and continuously form – Refine or Pollute – our Beliefs, worldview, tendencies, skills, likes/dislikes, etc. Desire therefore for the good, better, higher.Why not therefore first discuss, explore, discover, ‘know’, our Mind and the highest and best, towards which we may desire to grow?

Nature of Mind is to flow down like a river towards our Likes & Dislikes, canals of samskaras; so, no need to force mind to do things which we like and enjoy! Concentration and effort are needed to hold mind on the higher idea, problem, etc. to develop skills to be a musician, artiste, worker, professional, whereas mind enjoys playing video games, etc.!

I am not the Mind! I am just the ‘knower’ of the mind, which is a field where flow all our thoughts, feelings, emotions, good or bad and a storehouse of memories, which enslave us. Mind is our only instrument, so we must learn to know and manage it. Mind is extremely subtle. Subtlest than even Space.

So subtlest that it is really all pervasive and in closest proximity to Consciousness. We’re a Living-being, Jivatman, Body+Brain+Mind (which are matter; ‘known/objects’), Enlivened by Life/Consciousness (‘Knower/ Subject) which makes us alive.Our Behavior is driven by our Samskara, Mindset, which form our Worldview, Beliefs, Behaviors. We’re continuously refining/polluting our Mindset by our Intentions-Thoughts-Deeds righteous/unrighteous.

Daily ‘Self-study & Reflection’ for a few mts. inspires us to learn to be aware of our reactions to situations and manage our Responses towards Righteousness and “Good, Better, Best”.A simple exercise! Are you Aware, Conscious? Yes; if I am awake and alive! For a moment just be aware of your awareness.

We will realize that our attention gets diverted, and we get attached to the various thoughts flowing in our Mind. Am I the Awareness per se or the thoughts? Realize that we’re just the Subject, Knower, Observer, Consciousness. Like any other objects, our Thoughts are just Objects of our Perception, subtle-objects flowing in a field that we may refer as Mind.

The most profound and important idea is that we’re that infinite Consciousness because of which the Mind has become conscious. However, somehow, we’ve got attached to this limited particular ‘mind’, with all its idiosyncrasies and so are limited self-centered individuals, enslaved by our own mind. We seem to have forgotten that as Life, Consciousness, we’re all One and interconnected; and so, we say “I”, “WE”, “HUM”.

The purpose of working, enjoying, living is to ‘Regain’ and ‘Reunite’ with our reality as one singular, eternal, Consciousness (Meaning of ‘Religion’ in Latin and meaning of ‘Yoga’). Righteous conduct, Commitment to Duties, kartavya-palan, selflessness, etc. gradually purify the Mind and expand it towards feelings of oneness with more and more till we can feel one with the whole Creation!

Our Mind is one entity but is continuously, instantly performing 4 functions at more than lightning speed. These functions are: Manas, inputs external data. Buddhi, determines, decides; Viveka is refined buddhi; ‘Will’ acts. Chitta/Mind-stuff, is storehouse of all memories, samskaras. Ahamkar, is ‘I-sense’!Understanding and Managing our Mind: Doing what ‘I’ want to do implies doing what the mind wants to do.

Freedom is in not having to do what the mind wants to do! The motive power for all actions is our desires. Actions which refine our Mind are Righteous. They calm the mind and only a calm mind can really be managed. Mind imbibes that on which it dwells; it likes to dwell on what it likes! Mind goes to Trivia/Likes/Dislikes. From Trivia, learn to withdraw through Practice. From likes/dislikes Learn to detach, by focusing on the higher.

We’ve to learn to Manage our Thoughts. Reduce Quantity; Improve Quality; Direct thoughts towards best. ‘Unwanted/Trivia Thoughts are our Enemies.

Mind wants enjoyment. ‘I’ want success. Mind will not give up easily. Only repeated better thoughts can overcome past impressions. There is no short cut! GOI Mission Karma Yogi is a profound idea towards ‘Best’. Simply stated, it asks us to first be committed to kartavya-palan, our respective duties! This purifies the mind, chitta-suddhi.

Conclusion: Unfortunately, we’re not being educated and trained to learn to manage our mind. Only then we can learn to Selflessly work for good of society as a whole! As elders we’re mentors and it becomes our responsibility to strategize how we can inspire others within our areas of influence through On-line or through informal discussions, etc. towards learning to manage the mind.
