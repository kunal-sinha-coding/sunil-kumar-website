India to Bharat · Essay #122

What ought to be the ‘Quality of Mind’ of policy-makers, administrators, citizens?

It is common sense that the respected elders of society, the Shrestha, the policy makers, preachers, teachers, must be those who are inspired to ‘know’ and strive to attain the ‘highest’ goal of man. Only such Shrestha can guide and mentor society towards the higher dimensions of work and life. Similarly, the administrators must be those who have the vision, creativity, viveka and strength to create systems where people are inspired to strive for the highest.

A Question: Is modern idea of democracy, administration, schooling, teaching, recruitment, etc. designed to educate, nurture, identify, select, promote the Shrestha to leadership positions as mentors of society?

Our Problem: Modern society is based on western ideas of democracy that divides people into my party vs your party. And religious beliefs that ‘divide’ people into ‘my-god’ and ‘my-religion’ Vs “your-god and your-religion”.

It also has a ‘belief’ that we’ve only ‘one life’ and only if one believes in ‘my-god’ and gets converted and belongs to ‘my-religion’ can the person attain the highest.

Religious Preachers focus too much on “Belief and Belonging”. Shouldn’t our vision be to inspire people to learn and imbibe the ideal that our common, universal commitment must be to build powers of mind to follow good, righteous ethical conduct, commitment to duties and to have love, care, concern for each other, and for all equally, irrespective of any differences based on religions, vocations, etc. This humane aspect of working together for the good of the nation is more important than our individual worship and religious affiliations.

This unity and harmony must be a major focus during 20 years of schooling and criterion of selection for leadership in any vocation or field. Schooling must help us to appreciate and imbibe the ideal that our common ‘North’ is the ability to experience our ‘oneness’ and realize the whole Creation as our family.

The Issue that arises: shouldn’t elders educate people during 20 years of schooling to strive to ‘know’ our common, universal ‘North’ and be inspired to strive to ‘build powers of mind’ to ‘walk-the-talk’ of righteous conduct, to attain love, care, concern for more and more? How can our religions help to refine our mindset, our samskaras thru’ ethical conduct and our respective religions and ways of worship?

We are all born with our ‘mind’, our ‘ worldview ’, likes, dislikes, etc., i.e., samskaras. Our mind keeps a record of all our motives-thoughts-actions, samskaras, and it is this ‘mind’ with its samskaras, that results in our birth with our own inherited tendencies, i.e., the law of ‘as we sow, so do we reap’ continues even after death thru’ our ‘samskaras’.

Thus, every person has to be inspired to expand and refine its mind, thru’ ethical conduct and fulfilling their respective duties and their respective spiritual practices according to their religion. The Mentors in any vocation will be those who are striving to feel love, care, concern for more and more. Thus, the degree of unselfishness acquired by a person, whatever be his vocation, is the best indication for being a mentor, leader, teacher, preacher of society.

Understanding our Mind, our Worldview: Today we differentiate people based on degrees, status, wealth, religion, caste, etc.; all based on “external” factors. However, we are what we are, based on the quality of our ‘thoughts’ that we’ve been entertaining in our mind. Our thoughts, choices, motivations, intentions, desires, continuously form – refine or pollute – our worldview by remaining in our mind as ‘memories’; conscious or deep within. The outer behavior is just the effect; their cause is our stored up ‘memories’, samskaras.

Differentiating people based on Quality of mind, worldview: The entire creation, including what we eat, and ‘ideas and thoughts’ that we intake as ‘food’ into our mind, can be considered as a mixture of three qualities or ‘guna’, Calm-Active-Dull, viz. Sattva, Rajas, Tamas, SRT; and we can differentiate people as being predominantly Sattvic, Rajasic, Tamasic.

Sattva, Rajas, Tamas are qualities of our narrow, selfish- limited-mind, with which we’re identifying ourselves, because we’re in ignorance of the unlimited-potential of mind. We can learn to manifest our unlimited potential by striving to refine our mind thru’ ethics and prayers.

The predominantly Sattvic people have the mind-quality that manifests itself as purity, knowledge, harmony, goodness, joy, nobility, contentment. Rajasic people are likely to be predominantly full of passion, action, energy, ambition, which leads to attachment, longing for fulfilling goals, desires. Tamasic will generally be lazy, dull, prone to impurity, darkness, violence, cruelty, content with routine, work.

Thus, t hose with Sattva will be the best Mentors of society. They can mentor, teach and guide those with predominance of Rajas-Sattva to Administer societies, where Entrepreneurs and the Workforce, the only result-producers and wealth-creators can be as materialistic as they wish.

Summing up: The game and purpose of life is to continuously strive to refine and purify the quality of our mindset, thru’ virtuous thinking, prayers, ethical conduct, etc. till it can find fulfilment, peace, heavenly bliss here. The problem of democracy is, no one is responsible to take responsibility to inspire people towards good, better, best. Who will judge who is fit to be a mentor? So, we decide all ‘are equal’ and it is free for all! And we invent Degrees and wealth and power, as the modern idea of ‘best’; not Sattva, not Character!
