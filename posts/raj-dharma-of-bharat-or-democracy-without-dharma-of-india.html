India to Bharat · Essay #113

“Raj-dharma of Bharat” or “Democracy without dharma of India”?

1. Raja-dharma: The ‘Ruler’, aka King, has to perform all his duties according to Dharma; he is forbidden to do such things, which the people with reason have denied and he should protect the people as well as Dharma.

What is Dharma? Dharma is not religion but Character, aka Righteous conduct, ethics, morals, human-values. Character or Dharma has to be instilled and imbibed during schooling and growing up. In fact, this imbibing of ‘character’ was the purpose of education in Bharat and should be so for India too!

# The ‘Ruler’ should remain immensely involved in the well-being of his subjects and be prepared to do anything possible for their protection. All duties of the ‘Ruler’ should be performed according to the provisions of Dharma, which are to be respected in every situation, not only by the ‘Ruler’ but also by his subjects. The good done by the public through adherence to the principles of Dharma, would be added by one fourth part into the good performed by ‘Ruler’ himself.

# Power is as important as Dharma particularly in Raja-dharma because it is only with power that the Dharma could be established and affirmed.

# Everybody in the society has to perform some specified roles which is termed as his Dharma, and any violation of the rules is against the norms of the society and therefore, requires punishment from the authority designated for that purpose.

# All are secured only when there is an able ‘Ruler’, performing his duties of providing good conditions of living and security, proper administration, welfare policies. And he has to make necessary arrangements so that people may not deviate from the path of Dharma.

# The Ruler should involve all the populace to follow their respective duties and instruct them to perform their assigned functions according to Dharma.

# Overall happiness of the subjects, strict adherence to Dharma and sincere behaviour are the main duties of the Ruler. Anarchy, lawlessness, is the gravest sin on earth.

2. The Problems of Democracy & Modern Schooling bereft of Character: Schooling and education have no concern with building of character or of appraising character and the result is that democracy, that represents the ‘Rulers’ now, is no longer committed to dharma; in practice it is the art of winning popularity!

Democracy divides and has nothing to do with character. Politicians are skilful in talking, deception and seduction and to become ‘rulers’, offer platitudes of milk and honey and focus on manipulating and harnessing public opinion through various modern means. They’re willing to do all they can to marshal public support while camouflaging their self-interests, viz. to get elected to become a ‘ruler’!

3. USA has blessed Character Education; now we too can include it our Schooling! Intelligence is not enough. Intelligence plus character, that is the goal of true education. Martin Luther King Jr.

# US Department of Education, Office of Communications and Outreach has brought out a book “Helping Your Child Become a Responsible Citizen”, with activities, for elementary, middle and high school-aged children, under a 2002 Act which helps development of good character and good citizenship.

# Signing it, Bush stated: Our Founding Fathers understood that our country would survive and flourish if our nation was committed to good character and an unyielding dedication to liberty and justice for all. Throughout our history, our most honorable heroes practiced the values of hard work and honesty, commitment to excellence, courage, self-discipline, perseverance. Today, as we work to preserve peace and freedom throughout the world, we are guided by a national character that respects human dignity and values every life”.

# Some extracts from the book: ‘As parents, we all want our children to grow up to be responsible citizens and good people. We want them to learn to feel, think and act with respect for themselves and for other people. We want them to pursue their own well-being, while also being considerate of the needs and feelings of others. We want them to recognize and honour the democratic principles upon which our country was founded. We want them, in short, to develop strong character’.

# If children do not learn proper values and behaviour when they are very young, problems can develop. These problems can mushroom with serious consequences as children grow older. The most important thing we can do for our children is to help them acquire values and skills that they can rely on throughout their lives. In doing so, they will have the best chance to lead good lives as individuals and as citizens of their communities and of America. ‘Children need to learn that they sometimes have to be willing to work hard and wait for good things to come to them.’

4. Recommended National Ideal & Vision of India + Bharat: Today, society is in disorder because schooling and growing up has nothing to do with character; no one learns of the goal of man, nor are people inspired to build strength of mind to be committed to their swadharma and grow up as men of wisdom, the Shrestha, who can mentor societies.

# And so, Vivekananda has placed before the modern global society a universal spiritual ideal that God is present as Life, Conscious, in man; when we serve God in man then our vocation itself becomes the highest worship; i.e., we can realize the highest goal of man by working for good of society. This profound but simple ideal harmonizes all religions and can be made an integral part of schooling.

The Big Blunder of modern thinking: Creating and allowing self-centred, character-less people, full of desires and prone to wickedness, to emerge as leaders! Can such, ever work for the good of people?
