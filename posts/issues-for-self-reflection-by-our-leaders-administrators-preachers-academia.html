India to Bharat · Essay #089

Issues for self-reflection by our Leaders – Administrators, Preachers, Academia

1. Why have we failed to categorically realize that the purpose of Uniform Civil Code, Governance, Religions, Parenting, Education is to create enlightened citizens and evaluate and guide them for careers based on their Commitment to:

* Universal values of ‘Righteousness’?

* Fundamental Duties of our Constitution?

* Kartavya-palan, respective duties?

* Working for long-term good of society, not just for short-term, self-centered, mine vs yours interests – be they political, religious, or any other?

1.1 Why has Governance failed to realize that religions and politics are dividing societies and spreading hatred for ‘others’ and education focuses only on Marks and Degrees, not character?

1.2 Why have we separated Schooling and Religions instead of asking them to learn to work together and take full responsibility and be fully accountable to make good and enlightened citizens, and then guide them to be Mentors, Administrators and Result-produces of society based on their character and competence?

1.3 Why are we still focusing only on economics, employment, and schooling for masses, but totally ignoring the need for and importance of creating the Shrestha, men of wisdom, who can guide and inspire society to strive towards good, better, ‘best’.

1.4 Why have we failed to explore, debate, discuss and discover ways and means for making the good citizens?

2. Surely, we can agree that unselfishness, goodness, righteousness, love, care, concern for more and more are good and better than selfishness and self-centeredness’. If so, why should political animals be allowed to spread hatred for the ‘others?

Shouldn’t education, religions, teachers, preachers, be made directly responsible to inspire citizens to strive to adhere to the universal Principles of truth, non-violence, peace, selflessness, the ability to feel love, care, concern for more and more.

3. Universal Principles to Be Good and Do Good: It must be the responsibility of the Shrestha from administrators, academia, IIT, IIM, NGOs of various religions to work together to make good citizens, irrespective of our religions or other differences.

Religions are many and sincere religions must obviously work together to inspire people to be good, Shrestha! Instead, they become self-centered, petty, narrow, divisive, when they claim that ‘my religion, my god, my scripture, my way alone as true’!

Religions are founded by pure, selfless men of wisdom, Rishis, Seers, saints, Son of God, Prophets, etc. who all exhibit by their life and teachings the universal human virtues of love, peace, compassion, truth, righteous conduct, etc. and therefore, till today people look up to them to guide us towards the highest goal of life. Their wisdom is available to us thru’ the multitude of religions, gurus, scriptures, Bible, Quran, Vedas, Upanishads, etc.

4. The Problem is the Quality of Preachers, Teachers, Politics! The masses and the followers of different religions will blindly believe and mechanically follow rituals according to what their respective preachers, mentors, gurus, tell them, without any real realizations!

The problem is that the Teachers and Preachers are not educated and trained to inspire people to practice self-development processes to awaken powers of discrimination and build powers of Mind, Self-control, Will power, etc. to have love, care, concern for more and more till we can feel one with the whole creation, by working for long-term good of society!

Most of Teachers are concerned only with teaching of their ‘subject’ whereas modern Preachers are mere ‘believers’ without spiritual maturity to appreciate either harmony of religions or that without basic goodness and righteousness, religions or education are meaningless!

Academia and governance have failed to create an environment that inspires discussions to appreciate the essence and harmony of religions and to get established in daily self-development processes to build the ability to strive for righteousness and commitment to our respective duties.

5. Focus on creating and identifying the Shrestha: In every religion, administration, academia, vocation, there are a few good, enlightened men of wisdom, the Shrestha, who are spiritually mature and who can be the inspiration and mentors of society.

Administration, education, religions must learn to work together to create and identify the Shrestha, who can then inspire society to be committed to righteousness and to the universal principles of ‘love thy neighbour as thyself’, based on the biblical and universal principles of ‘the Kingdom of God is within us. So, seek first the Kingdom of God thru’ ‘Righteousness’ and Be Perfect, treating our Work and Vocation as our highest worship.

Why should we allow preachers, teachers, politics, to spread hatred, when all Religions, Prophets, Saints, inspire us to be righteous and strive to reach the highest goal of life, by getting established in the habit of daily self-development processes?

Instead of spreading hatred, shouldn’t religions work together to inspire all citizens to strive to be good, and then evaluate goodness and ensure that the good, the Shrestha, aka best people, become our teachers, preachers, and leaders?
