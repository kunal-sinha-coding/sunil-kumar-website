India to Bharat · Essay #232

Action plan for imbibing the spirit of fundamental duties

Should ‘Bharat’ accept the present model of ‘education’ and ‘governance’ or should it focus on requesting academia and faith groups to come together to evolve an action plan to inculcate/imbibe the spirit of “fundamental duties” to produce enlightened citizens?

‘India’ may have happily accepted 3R’s and IQ oriented education which produces unemployable degree holders and corrupt, self-centered leaders, incapable of coming together and working for good of society? Shouldn’t Bharat find alternatives?

What is our role model for good Education & Governance?

The vision before the freedom fighters was ‘Ram Rajya’ as expressed by Gandhiji and elaborated in Hind Swaraj. The foundation of good education and governance and Ram Rajya is the rule of dharma, i.e. individual, societal and institutional ethics, morals, human values.

Shouldn’t Bharat focus on man-making, character building, nation building education, to create enlightened citizens as envisioned by Gandhiji and Vivekananda who was officially declared as the role model for the youth, by former PM Indira Gandhi and Rajiv Gandhi.

The ‘leader’, whether a teacher, a policy-maker, administrator, entrepreneur, or result-producer, all have to be role models of ‘dharma, ethics’. Only such enlightened citizens can create educational processes and institutional ethics so that everyone is fully ‘aware’ of their dharma and are inspired to continuously strive to build strength to ‘know and do the right’.

The higher the status of an individual, the greater is the demand from him for righteous conduct, because he has the ability to indulge in unethical practices and harm society, unless he is a man of character and competence! Justice must be swift and proportionately demanding on the higher placed wrong-doer while nurturing the commoner towards the higher.

The good news is that the new education policy is focusing on two very important issues: firstly, Yoga and secondly on ‘reducing the syllabus’, which can provide enough time.

Now it is up to us, elders, parents, faith groups, academia to ensure that schools can provide at least daily one-hour for self-development practices, since imbibing virtues and values, is much more than academic textbooks and IQ-studies. It comes from daily regular inner-governance practices, self-reflection, brainstorming discussions, Q-A sessions, etc.

Yoga is not religion. It is not a mere physical exercise. It is a planned practice to build powers of mind, self-control, will-power, concentration, discipline, etc.

Quality and excellence in our daily work and duties for good of society is Karma-Yoga.

Devotional practices, prayers, worship according to our respective faiths are Bhakti-Yoga.

Study of scriptures, acquiring sacred and secular knowledge for self-improvement and for good of humanity is Gyana-Yoga.

Meditation, etc. according to our respective faiths, is the highest practice, Raja-Yoga.

Various government committees, Unesco, Supreme Court, etc. have recommended the need for value education. Many NGO’s and faith groups are working in this area. NCERT, in their value education book, have identified various interventions for value education, but there is no planned action in the mainstream systems to instil the spirit of fundamental duties.

Shouldn’t school provide at least one hour every day for value education, a practice of inner governance and self-development processes like yoga, prayers, meditation, etc. to refine our mindset, and discuss and find the key to happiness? After all, University of Berkeley has now introduced a course on ‘science of happiness’; so has IIT-D and Delhi government schools, and Madhya Pradesh has created a happiness ministry.

Who will ensure that academia and faith groups evolve a ‘product’ that can then be implemented in schools and colleges? Will the young leaders of all parties take this responsibility?

Sincere citizens, academia, faith groups are invited to share their ideas to evolve a common minimum program to create enlightened citizens!
