India to Bharat · Essay #213

Where are the Peace Makers?

1. We are seeing that our Politicians haven’t been able to control cross-border incursions, training, and radicalisation of youths, particularly Kashmiri youths. The idea of Kashmiriyat, of Sufism, of oneness, of love and care for each other and respect for other religions, are not taught in schooling or anywhere by anyone. On the other hand, those who want to create divisions, e.g. between Hindu-Muslims, are freely allowed to teach their separatist ideologies, which receives support from across. We’ve allowed Kashmiri Pundits to be thrown out; without any positive affirmative actions by people, preachers, politicians in power.

2. It is time that along with local statesmen, academia and spiritually awakened persons, not politicians who thrive on divisions, we review all actions which tend to create a sense of alienation and estrangement and replace them with those actions that will create a sense of ‘oneness’. For India, the Fundamental Duties enjoin on us to spread brotherhood, love for the nation, etc. but imbibing the spirit of the FD’s is not a part of schooling! For Kashmir, we need to examine whether Art 35A and 370 are leading to alienation or do they promote a sense of integration with the rest. Let this group of mentors create an action plan to spread the Kashmiriyat ideal of oneness.

3. Terrorism is spreading across the world only because we’re allowing those who want to create divisions in society, to teach their ideology of hatred for the ‘other’; whereas those who wish to have peace and wish to spread love, care, concern for each other, the ideal of oneness, have failed to teach the way of acquiring Empathy. We see around us that narrow-minded preachers of Religions create divisions and teach hatred and violence towards others; and of course, history is full of examples of massacres done in the name of Religions across the world.

4. During his now-famous speech at Parliament of Religions on 11 th Sept. 1893, in Chicago, Vivekananda brought to the modern religions the idea of harmony among religions; that all religions sincerely practiced, lead to the same goal of perfection. And, this was the ideal of Kashmiriyat, hitherto. And this ideal is being lost across the world. This idea must be a part of Schooling.

5. UNESCO’s Policy on Education asks us to focus on “Learning to Live Together” and UNESCO have brought out a text book “Learning the Way of Peace”. To counter the attempt of separatists to create divisions and enmity, let us invite UNESCO to conduct workshops and brain-storming sessions throughout the state for schools, colleges, citizens to Learn the Way of Peace; to build Empathy, EQ or emotional maturity and SQ or spiritual intelligence, to know the purpose of life and the ethical conduct to attain it, and spread the ideal of living together in peace and amity.

6. Academia and Elders have failed to act as Peace-makers. We’ve promoted divisions by allowing ‘minorities’ to preach that ‘my way is the only right way’ in their ‘minority’ institutions while not teaching in mainstream schooling the ideal that all religions, sincerely taught and practiced, will inspire us to be good, better, best, by taking care of each other and working ethically for good of society. No true preacher or religion can ever teach killing each other in name of religion.

7. Teaching 3R’s, arts, science, various subjects, and examinations, build only IQ. They cannot build Empathy, Emotional maturity, etc. 3R’s and IQ do not create any inspiration to have love, care, concern for each other. They do not create commitment to duties and they do not promote working together in a spirit of service for good of society. Schooling should focus on building Empathy. Schooling should help students get clarity on higher dimensions of work and life so that we get an intellectual conviction in the higher and better in relation to the highest and best; and then strive to awaken powers of discrimination to know the right and build the ability, self-control, will-power, powers of mind to do the right that takes us towards the highest, and refrain from wrong.

8. It is high time that we act to spread awareness of the ideal of Kashmiriyat, of Sufism, to promote oneness. We need plan and strategies to involve parents, preachers, elders, teachers, to act as mentors to teach the ideal of oneness to oppose those who teach hatred and divisions on basis of religions, etc. It is the character of Politicians to promote divisions for Votes so we need to bring out local Statesmen and spiritually awakened citizens to spread the idea that Religions are meant to inspire us to be good and to follow ethical and righteous conduct; that Religions are meant to spread peace and love for each other; not hatred and killing.

9. It was for the best of men to understand and spread spiritual awakening in society. Unfortunately, in the wake of materialistic culture of the West, the best want to join the civil services and become engineers and doctors! Perhaps it is thus, that the best have allowed the not so good to become narrow-minded politicians and preachers!
