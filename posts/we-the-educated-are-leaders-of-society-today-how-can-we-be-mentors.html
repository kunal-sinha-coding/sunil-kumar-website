India to Bharat · Essay #084

We the educated are leaders of society today – How can we be mentors?

Modern Challenge – who and where are Mentors of Society?

Modern society and systems are copies of the materialistic western cultures and so we have Preachers, Teachers, Political animals as leaders, but no Mentors of society, the Shrestha.

It must be obvious that ‘good, better, best’ is not just ‘Marks, Degrees, knowledge of various subjects, and Artha, Kaama’. Our traditional idea of ‘good, better, best’ is: “through Dharma (universal values, Righteousness, Commitment to our respective Duties, etc.) ‘Earn and Enjoy’ to attain Mukti (aka Freedom, Salvation, Nirvana, Heaven, Perfection, highest goal).

Only Mentors can produce Mentors, not political animals: In our culture, the tradition of educating and producing Mentors was maintained by the Gurukuls based on the ancient Vedic and Vedantic wisdom, as continuously reformed by saints and sages; and the reforms continues till date, while maintaining the principles!

It was the Responsibility of Mentors to suggest systems of governance and remain as advisors to ensure that during 20-25 years of education and growing up every citizen was established in the habit of daily Swadhyaya and Sadhana, ‘Reflection and Self-study’ and ‘Self-development practices’ to develop Viveka and powers of mind to remain committed to our civilizational ideals, e.g., commitment to universal values; harmony of religions; spirit of tyaga & seva; kartavya-palan; work and vocation as worship of God in man; etc. And to it we may now add, commitment to love for the nation and all its people and to our Constitutional Fundamental Duties.

We the educated are the Mentors today: Like it or not, today we the educated ‘Devotees’ of various faiths, elders, parents, teachers, youth, are the Shrestha and, in a way, responsible to learn to be and make Mentors of society!

In modern times it is Vivekananda who was declared by the then PM Indira and Rajiv Gandhi as the National Youth Ideal and the GOI recent initiative of government servants as Karma Yogi, is inspired by Vivekananda’s Practical Vedanta and Karma Yoga – ‘Commitment to duties and Righteousness’, as the highest Religion; ‘work/vocation as worship’; work as service of God in man!

Our Responsibility as Mentors: “Be and Make” is the mantra given by Vivekananda. Society needs guidance from Mentors who have the wisdom, worldview of ‘oneness’, character, and ability to inspire and guide people to be good. We need Teachers as Mentors who don’t just teach. We need Preachers as Mentors of society who don’t spread hatred for others. We need leaders as Mentors who can create systems which don’t divide societies, but inspire all people, irrespective of caste, religion, status, to learn to love and care for each other. We need Mentors who can guide people to discover and walk towards good, better, best, our common, universal ‘North’.

If we become conscious of our role and responsibility as Mentors, we can consciously learn to help, nurture, guide others within our respective areas of influence to engage in learning and working in occupations based on our tendencies and within our character and competence, so that in and thru’ our daily work and life, we may remain on the critical path of Righteousness and ethics, towards the ‘highest and best’, ‘Perfection’.

Every person is on this journey to Perfection, in and thru’ daily work and life and it is our quality of ‘samskara’, mindset and tendencies, which we are continuously creating, that indicates how far have we progressed, and engaging in what work and vocation, can I remain on the critical path to Perfection. Our Samskaras are a mixture, in various proportions, of qualities of Tamasic, Rajasic, Sattvic, i.e., dull, active, calm. Thru’ Righteousness, selflessness, Swadharma, we refine our samskara towards Sattvic tendencies and Perfection.

In fact, we’re unfit to be educated teachers and leaders of society, unless we are continuously striving to imbibe in our character, the inspiration and competence to remain committed to Kartavya-palan and Righteousness so that we remain on the critical path to consistent success and fulfilment!

GOI has now directed that all government servants must be trained to be a Karma Yogi, KYi! So, what is Karma Yogi? It is a profound idea towards ‘Perfection’, but simply stated, it asks us to first be committed to kartavya-palan, our respective duties! Why? Because selfless commitment to duties purifies the mind, chitta-suddhi, and prepares us not just for higher responsibilities in work and society but is a necessary condition for growth towards Sattvic tendencies and ‘Perfection’.

However, so far, ‘neither Governance nor politicians’ and ‘neither education nor religions’ have any action plans to focus on producing a KYi, an enlightened citizen, who is inspired to selflessly work for good of society!

Earlier it was the parents, elders of family and Mentors who were providing this inspiration to be committed to kartavya-palan, our respective duties. Who will provide the training today to be a KYi?

Can we devotees volunteer to develop ourselves as Mentors of society? Can we discuss in our Study circles and within our areas of contacts and influence how we can inspire ourselves and others to strive towards ‘good, better, best’?

Some Questions for Reflection: Mentors can initiate reflection, discussions, studying ideas like, say: ‘Good, better, best’, Perfection; Nature of Man, Mind, Consciousness; Purpose of Work and life; Worldview of ‘Oneness and Subjective focus’ Vs ‘Separateness and Objective’; Equal or Unequal and why; How can I/we continuously improve? Steps for walking towards good, better, best, etc.
