Idea of being a karma yogi – All religions must teach how to work: Part 4

We all Work – Academia & Religions must help us learn to treat our daily Work itself as Highest Worship: All Religions keep us divided. They ask us to believe in this or that ideology, Prophet, God, Scripture, ways of prayers, worship, alms, charity, fasting, etc.

No Religion categorically states that our daily work and vocation itself can be a way to God! It goes to the credit of Vivekananda that he put our daily work itself as our highest worship. He announced at the world platform that work done seeing ‘God in man’, is our highest worship and thru’ a series of talks on Karma Yoga recreated a universal way of worship which applies to all Religions. His message was “Seek Salvation, Mukti, Nirvana, God, by transforming your daily work, vocation, for good of society”, “Atmano Moksartham Jagadhitaye cha”. Of course, Hinduism does mention karma yoga and our epics Ramayana and Mahabharat elaborate the importance of doing our duties; Krishna talks of it in detail in the Gita.

Knowing the importance of ‘work’, vocation, our Rishis, had created a varna ashram system. Unfortunately modern era and Religions, never understood the idea of vocation itself as worship. For them ruler was higher and workers were slaves! This is totally incorrect if we study the ideal of Karma Yoga, which asserts that each vocation done for good of society leads to God.

The Rishis categorized people into 4 distinct callings, based on their inherent inborn tendencies, samskaras, created by their own previous karma , e.g. teachers and men of wisdom; administrators; farmers, traders, businessmen; and, the workforce. All these were men who respected and followed their respective duties based on Dharma, Swadharma, i.e. universal and respective codes of ethics, depending on their ashrama. Those who were undisciplined, were not considered responsible members of society.

The Rishis then specified that during studentship the student has to be established in his respective Swadharma and Dharma so that they their vocation is an obligatory duty, where they can excel due their samskaras, and work for good of society. Thus, their vocation became the highest selfless worship. Only thru such schooling we can become Householders, and learn to “earn” thru’ our vocation and “enjoy” thru our Swadharma to remain on the critical path to highest human Goal.

Karma Yoga (Continued ): Work incessantly but remember if we’re working with a motive, we become attached to the results. Due to this attachment, our ‘thoughts-words-actions’, Karma, produce deep impressions on the mind, Samskaras, which then enslave us and govern our present Karma.

Therefore, Karma Yogi strives to know and fulfill his obligatory duties to his best, unattached to fruits. All work is by nature composed of both good and evil. But good and bad are both bondages of the soul. What I am just at this moment is the effect of the sum total of all the impressions of my past ‘thought-words-actions’. So, learn to do your duties like a master, in freedom and love. Not as a slave who has to work; no true love for him! Every act of true love brings happiness; there is no act of love which does not bring peace and blessedness as its reaction.

Infinite Existence, Consciousness, Love and Ananda are eternally connected with one another. They are the three aspects of God, the One without a second; where one of them is, the others also must be. When that Existence becomes finite , we see it as the world. That Consciousness becomes in its turn modified into my-knowledge of the things of the world as we store in our mind.

The ideal is to be able to Love your husband, wife, children, the whole universe, in a manner that there is no reaction of pain, jealousy, selfishness – this is to be unattached. Krishna says, “Look at Me, Arjuna! If I stop from work for one moment, the whole universe will die. I have nothing to gain from work; I am the one Lord, but why do I work? Because I love the world.” God is unattached because He loves; that real love makes us unattached.

Wherever there is attachment, it is all physical attraction. To attain this unattachment is almost a life-work. As soon as we reached it, we have attained the Highest human Goal! Do you ask anything from your children in return for what you have given them? In whatever you do for a particular city, or a state, assume the same attitude, expect nothing in return.

Today it is every man for himself. Karma Yoga means, even at the point of death to help anyone, without asking questions. Be cheated millions of times and never ask a question, and never think of what you are doing. Never vaunt of your gifts to the poor or expect their gratitude, but rather be grateful to them for giving you the occasion of practicing charity to them. To be an ideal householder is a much more difficult task than to be an ideal monk.

What is duty? One idea is that good men follows their conscience. But what is it that makes an act a duty? To give an objective definition of duty is not easy but there is duty from the subjective side – Any action that makes us go Godward is a good action, and is our duty; any action that makes us go downward is evil, and is not our duty. There is, however, a universally accepted maxim, “Do not injure any being; not injuring any being is virtue, injuring any being is sin.”

It is therefore our duty to do that work which will exalt and ennoble us in accordance with the ideals and activities of the family, society in which we are born.
