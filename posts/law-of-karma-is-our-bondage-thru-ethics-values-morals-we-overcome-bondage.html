India to Bharat · Essay #027

Law of Karma is our bondage – Thru’ ethics values morals we overcome bondage

“I and I alone am responsible for what I am – good or bad”: Modern society, led by Western cultures is focused on Money & Desires’! No one today is responsible and accountable during schooling and growing up, to make us know ‘good from bad’ and to inspire and prepare us ‘to be good’ and ‘to do good’!

Understanding the Law of Karma, cause-effect: All actions, deeds, including our intentions, motives, thoughts behind our deeds and words, is “Karma”, ‘action’, and each Karma, has its Karma-phala, fruit of the actions, both external and inner results as ‘impressions’ on our mind, ‘Samskara’, which then make me whatever I am!

While external results of karma are visible and objectifiable, the inner “impressions and memories” are “subjective” and invisible, even to us and are also termed karma; and form and become our ‘Samskara’, i.e., our mindset because of which we are what we’re; and which form our habit, destiny, character, likes/dislikes, tendencies, skills, knowledge, etc.!

Our good/bad, i.e., ‘selfless’ or ‘self-centered’ actions, Karma, continuously either Purify or Pollute our Mind. Our past and previous samskara prompt our present and subsequent actions. Our good and bad actions are continuously refining or polluting our mind.

Therefore, the importance of learning to consciously strive to follow ethics, morals, dharma, kartavya-palan – be a Karma Yogi.

2. Law of Karma, “as we sow, so do we reap”, applies to each single individual! It has nothing to do with my or your idea of God, Religions, education, status, rich or poor! The hard scientific fact is that only thru’ Righteousness, Fulfilling our duties, & Selflessly working for good of society, i.e., treating our work as Worship of God in man and nature, aka Karma Yoga, can we expand our mind to have care and concern for all; and also attain perpetual prosperity, peace, bliss and ‘Freedom’, Mukti, from bondage of Karma, and Rebirth! As long as we work for self-centered purposes, we remain bound and subject to bondage to law of cause-effect, karma.

3. We all know that it is good to be good; to be ethical, moral, value based! However, the exact definition and purpose of ‘Dharma’, being good, righteousness, is generally and mostly, not clear. Our epics, Mahabharat discuss ‘The Difficulty of Being Good – on the Subtle Art of Dharma’!

Modern schooling doesn’t give us any opportunity to be exposed to discussions and reflections on the subtleties and nuances of ethics, righteousness, importance of kartavya-palan, aka ‘dharma’.

No effort is made to give us intellectual, academic conviction in the power of goodness and we don’t realize that Personal Effectiveness means the ability to successfully accomplish Goals set by Self, Family, Organization and/or Society in an Ethical and Moral manner!

The spirit of selfless work and vocation as highest worship must be drilled into every citizen during schooling and growing-up, irrespective of our religious beliefs.

4. In common parlance Ethics are exhibited behaviours of an individual or Institution. They cover legal requirements and even exceed them. Values are the extent by which Ethics exceed the legal requirements. Morals are the expected behaviours which the society expects from an individual.

Isn’t it obvious that good actions give good results and bad, bad; this is the law! While we all understand the commonsense fact that all ‘actions have results’, then why’re we not all trying to be good? Simply, because we haven’t been given the opportunity during 20-25 years of education and growing up to explore, study, discuss, reflect and strive to imbibe and absorb this deep and profound idea that good, ethical and moral actions alone will give good results.

5. The law of ‘Karma’, ‘as we sow, so do we reap’ is known to all, even to babies and animals, but somehow modern leaders, religions, preachers and we the educated of today, all seem to be totally ignorant of this reality! Society is today organized on the apparently wrong idea given by modern Preachers that all men are born equal with a clean slate; obviously to counter the old era of privileges, inequality, slavery, etc.!

We fail to recognize the logical conclusion that law of karma must prevail, till thru’ good karma we transform the bad, and then learn to perform actions in the selfless spirit of Karma Yoga; not as a ‘doer’ of action.

6. It is ‘doer-ship’ that binds me to consequences of actions, which then becomes a Bondage for future Karma, and I then have to suffer its good or bad consequence. We must learn, be convinced, and then continuously practice the ability to act in the spirit of karma yoga, i.e., just as an ‘instrument’; just as a duty; not for ‘fruits’ of actions but like all forces of nature, e.g. sun, earth, wind, fire, etc. who silently fulfil their duties!

Only good ethical actions, including selfless prayers, worship, etc. can overcome the influence of previous bad samskaras and only a generally good samskara will enable us and inspire us to strive to act selflessly. Bad samskaras are not amenable to our control!

Only mankind has freedom of choice to be and do good or bad, and so gets entrapped by law of karma. All other creatures and forces of nature act according to their samskaras. Man alone has the freedom to pollute or purify their samskaras, by their bad or good; or act selflessly, just as duty to be free from bondage!

Therefore, education & parenting must focus on helping us understand ‘karma’ and inspire practice for awakening Viveka-buddhi and building powers of mind to remain committed to kartavya-palan, etc.
