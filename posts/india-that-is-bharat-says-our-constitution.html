India to Bharat · Essay #233

India that is Bharat, says our Constitution

Let us pick up the best, not the worst of India and Bharat, to build the “saare jahaan sey acchha Hindustan hamara”

1. Dissatisfaction with India of the Present: What is wrong with India? Not just selfishness and corruption, but the cause of all our problems are:

  • Poor character of Elected representatives & religious leaders who both divide people instead of uniting them towards the higher; no ‘good’ role models
  • Lack of proper and responsive ‘systems’ of Democracy, Governance
  • Chalata-hai-poor work culture
  • Lack of commitment to “kartavya-palan”; duties to Self, society, work
  • Lack of striving for quality, excellence, creativity to solve societal issues
  • Not striving for Continuous Improvements in Life and Vocation
  • Lack of spirit of service; Failure to treat work/vocation as worship
  • No love, care and concern for ‘others’ or for India and its development
  • Failure to provide meaningful livelihood to the vast majority
  • Education focused on 3R’s& IQ not on Character/Nation building

2. SHARED VISION FOR FUTURE: Bharat is the most ancient civilization of the world, based on the Vedantic worldview of “Oneness” and a “Subjective” focus, which in practical terms categorically means ‘vasudaiva kutumbukam’, as carved on the gates of our Parliament i.e. the whole world is our family.

The literal meaning of Bharat is “Striving for the highest illumination” and so anyone who strives to become “good, better towards the best” is a Bharati, is in “sur”; though he may be of any country, religion, race, now or in the future or past. This ideal is a universal wisdom for all of mankind.

The universal ideal for any ‘good’ person, therefore is: In and through our daily enjoying, earning, working and living, let us together continuously strive to build the mental ability to ‘know’ and ‘walk-the-talk’ for “becoming good, better, towards the best”, engaged in a vocation, in harmony with our inherent tendencies, skills, character and passion.

Let us not divide this universal wisdom which encompasses all mankind, into my-religion, my-nations versus yours!

3. INDIA TO BHARAT – AGREED ACTION PLAN: The modern challenge is to see how Media, Education, Religions, Governance, Academia can work together to evolve a “COMMON MINIMUM PROGRAM” to inspire and unite all citizens, public servants, professionals, etc. to become responsible, peaceful, useful citizens, who are striving to become good, better, towards the best, in and through their daily work and life.
