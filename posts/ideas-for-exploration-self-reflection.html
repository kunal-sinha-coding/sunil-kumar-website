India to Bharat · Essay #119

“Ideas for exploration & self-reflection”

1. What is ‘work’; Why do we work; How should we work? Should our work help us grow towards our “North”?

# Who and what is Samaaja? Who are ‘we’? Are we Separate or Inter-related, Inter-connected, Inter-dependent?

# What could be the Worldview of this Uni-verse? “Separateness” or “Oneness”?

# We all want to grow because Growth gives happiness!

# Am I trying for Continuous growth and improvements in relationships, in work, in areas of interests? How can I continuously improve? How will I rate my effectiveness?

# Within my area of control how can I help improve things; what opportunities can I give people for their growth?

# What steps can I/we take, within my/our area of influence, to improve things; Short-term & Long-term?

# Am I a problem solver or do I create problems for others?

# In what way am I dissatisfied with the Present state of things and within my area area of ‘influence’, where can I make a visible improvement in a ‘short time’

2. Introspection: Who am I? Why am I here? Where am I going? What’re the better ways to get there?

# What are my Immediate, Short-term, Long-term, Goals? My goals for next 10-20 years; 50-100 years?

# Don’t we have a common long-long term goal, our common ‘North’? What could it be? For me, my family, my Institution, whole Society?

# We know we’re “living-being”, “Jiva+atman”; but, though ‘I am’ a living being, it has to be the “Life” that makes me what I am, because the moment that mysterious “Life” goes away, then that “I am” is gone and what remains is a dead-body, plain matter like the rest of nature!

# So, we’re asked to “Know Thy Self!” Then I discuss, explore and I am told: “Kingdom of God is within us”; “That Thou Art”, “Each soul is potentially divine”.

# We reflect and recognize that ‘I am’ ‘Body’ + ‘Mind’ + ‘Life’; I can also note that this body is matter but this Life must be the “Consciousness”, (aka Atman/Spirit/Rooha) within me, that makes me a living-being ‘conscious’ of the world.

# Intellectually I can now recognize that ‘This Atman in me is my unchanging Self’, whereas body and mind are constantly changing! But ‘I am’ aware of the changes! So, perhaps ‘I am’ ‘That Consciousness’, that is aware/conscious of the ‘unchanging Self’. ‘I am’ the Consciousness, not body-mind complex!

# The Atman, ‘our innermost self’ is pure Consciousness. “This Atman, aka Self, Consciousness, Life, Spirit, Rooha, is That. It is the only Subject, Knower, Observer.

# How can I be That! I reflect and negate all “Objects”, as they are ‘things’, observed reality and so are not consciousness. So, I cannot be the body, mind, thoughts, emotions, because they can be perceived, they’re objects! So, ‘I am That’, the one ‘Consciousness’, one Subject, the one Knower of the variety of changing things; just as I observe my own changing body!

# Slowly I realize, as I reflect and remain committed to ethical, righteous conduct, fulfill my Duties, that I am being asked to separate my-Self from all ‘matter’. I negate the 5 coverings of Body; Praana; Mind; Intellect; Happiness! And then I realize that I am pure Consciousness!

# Not mere “belief”: We’re told, “seek and you shall find”! So, I must search for this Atman, Kingdom of God, within me; temples or even scriptures can only be guides to show the way; but, I must Experience myself!

3. Let’s reflect on how do I seek within? When I try to look within, I am bombarded with all kinds of thoughts. Where do the Thoughts live and arise from?

# Our Mind is the field where Thought’s flow and are stored.

# So, I must practice and learn to manage my Mind. How? By improving Quality of Thoughts and directing the Thoughts to remain focused on that which will improve their quality

# So, what will improve the quality of Thoughts? The universal methods are: Virtuous life, Ethical, righteous conduct, fulfilment of our respective duties, love, care, concern for each other, selflessness, etc., as well as our respective sincere spiritual practices, prayers, worship, etc.!

# Similarly, meditating on lives and teachings of Founders of Religions, Prophets, Sages, Saints, God-like-men/Incarnations, etc. are a great help; they purify the thoughts and gradually take them towards the higher, better, best.

4. Education, Parenting, Societal norms must inspire us to reflect, question, discuss, explore.

# Reasoning backed by personal experience is the goal, not mere “belief”!

# The purpose of life, of work and living is to recognize our real, unchanging Self, the infinite, all pervasive Consciousness within and without, from which we emerge and are to return, after experiencing our oneness with the whole creation!

# This is the purpose of this Creation! I have to realize that I am That I am! After understanding that I am That, I have to expand to realize “I am all that exists”; I am the whole Creation!
