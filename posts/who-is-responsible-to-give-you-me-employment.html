India to Bharat · Essay #209

Who is responsible to give You & Me, Employment?

1. The Indian Worldview: It is our foolishness to imagine that an inefficient and elephantine government, as all governments are, managed by self-centred politicians and administrators, with narrow selfish-interests, with no long-term-vision and no-long term-stake in any serious issue, can ever be expected to do the thinking and planning needed to give ‘you’ and ‘me’ an appropriate employment! And, so to get our Votes, we beg and they throw us Freebies, instead of ensuring that we get meaningful education and occupation so that we may be equipped to serve and solve problems of our communities.

Just consider the mismanagement of govt. departments, Railways and Public Sector Units which are allowed to continue inefficiently and in spite of growing losses, merely on the grounds of ‘a source’ of ‘employment’. We’ve bled AI and have nationalized banks and then forced Banks to keep giving loans to loss-making companies Jet, Kingfisher, etc. etc.

1.1 Not Government but Businessmen create Employment & Wealth: Politicians are total untrustworthy, is the public perception. Secretly they beg, borrow or steal from businessmen. Even Gandhiji had respect for the Birlas and the Bajaj’s. Openly they keep criticising them; slogans being ‘suit-boot-ki-sarkar’ for rich-businessmen not for the poor, ‘garibi-hatao’, while the poor grow poorer.

1.2 The Politicians Paradox: Poor and the workforce do not create employment or wealth, but they’re the largest in numbers and in democracy, rightly or wrongly, ‘numbers’ alone matter, not quality! The good people are always in the minority but they’ve to say and do that which numbers wish for; not what is for the long-term good of society as a whole! And, so we’ve a class of political animals in all walks of life who loot us all in the name of democracy.

The reality is that politicians or govt. have no real expertise for creating employment Opportunities! It is the Vaisha-varna, the Entrepreneurs, Businesses, Corporations, Traders, Vendors, Farmers, etc. who alone have the creative ideas; certainly not politicians.

In the name of “I am for the Poor”, politicians, have tried to control and break down the spirit of Entrepreneurs by creating a plethora of rules, License and Inspection-raj. So, businesses have now become Foxes. Instead of creating wealth for themselves and the nation, they now create wealth for political animals who control them and want them to remain foxes.

2. Bharatiya Worlview – We’ve to ask a basic question: Who is responsible for giving me employment? I and you alone must take responsibility and be accountable – me, myself, my family, my community, my teachers and mentors! Employment is not an isolated issue. It encompasses all aspects of human life. Employment and Education cannot be centrally planned for everyone.

2.1 An Employee is not really a Bharati: For a Bharati, work is worship, work is life and living, work is karma-yoga and a Bharati understands that our daily work is an integral part of living and, the only way to grow towards Perfection, in and thru ‘earning’ and enjoying’, if we offer our best for good of society, not for profits, which are just a bye-product for satisfying a need of samaaja, which implies ‘God’. Look at common Indians like dhobi, darzi, paanwallah, chanajor garam, shikanji, etc. They stand on own feet, bribe and face a host of educated-Inspectors and yet, they can satisfy customers without any top-management over them!

2.2 In contrast, in a way an employee is a glorified ‘slave’, with fixed salary, labour contract, holidays, etc. A ‘slave’ has no love for master, no love for work, no ownership of work. He works for money. He does what you tell him. He doesn’t do anything or least possible, if he can get by, as most govt. employees get away!

3. Action Plan: Decentralise affairs to Block Levels & Create Panchayat as Mentors: If at all the govt. wants to help it should create a series of “Panchayat”, a Mentoring place where I and you can go and seek career guidance. They can suggest whether I should go for higher studies or learn a vocation needed by the community, etc. The Panchayats should be well aware of all SWOT factors of the Block or region/District, their “Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats”. They should be aware of employment opportunities and of our educational-abilities, family background, Vocation, Occupation, caste, the agriculture produces, local skills and expertise and the problems and the needs of the region and society, since all these are closely interlinked.

The Panchayat should not be ‘elected’ or ‘govt. servants’; but should be those ’Volunteers’ who people respect, because they are apparently Fair, Just, Truthful, Impartial, working in a spirit of worship for good of society. The Mentors should be elders of samaaja with heart, compassion, seva-bhava. They must be men of wisdom, who belong to the Block, have permanent stake and roots in the Block.

They can advise and discover who is fit for which vocation so that schooling can train and make them fit for an appropriate vocation suited to their inherent capabilities and tendencies, engaged in which they can excel and work for good of society. We need schooling that will produce employable and enlightened citizens and guide them towards living and meaningful occupation (not necessarily employed as a wage earner) in a vocation appropriate to their inherent character and competence.

4. Where do we look for such Mentors? It was the responsibility of education to make good, responsible citizens; now that they focus only on 3R’s and IQ, we’ve no option but to seek help of Volunteers from NGO’s working in the community. We could stop the idea of full-time students in meaningless courses like BA, MA, etc. except for those who are brilliant in studies and wish to go for research, design, specialist courses, academia, etc.

4.1 Sandwich-Courses: All Schools and colleges should spend only 2/3 days on theory in schools and colleges. The other 2/3 days must be used for learning and working in the field, in industry, in farms, agriculture, in cottage units, with Army, in villages, in taking on problem solving projects of the region, surveys, under guidance of Panchayats, in finding an appropriate occupation to serve society.

4.2 Time for Reflection on the Higher: Daily one-hour time must be provided in all schools and colleges for Self-study and Self-development practices to know about our culture, awaken Viveka and build powers of mind, will-powers, self-control, to know and walk the talk to the highest “Perfection” in and thru’ our daily work and living.
