India to Bharat · Essay #101

As a global village we do need a universal idea & ideal of our common goal – God

Our ancient civilizational culture stands on three universal and fundamental pillars which are common to all religions, viz. “God, rebirth and the Vedas/Revealed-Scriptures”, which alone can reveal the truth of things beyond life-death, beyond our intellect, and beyond what our sense organs can perceive or reveal. Our global forefathers, the Shrestha, believed in a healthy worldview of this Creation as derived therefrom, and the purpose of education was to inspire us to be on the path to the best.

However, today as a global-academic-society, we’ve miserably failed to undertake any academic, scientific, intellectual study for understanding and fine tuning these 3 fundamental pillars towards which we could all together strive to converge.

Without any knowledge and conviction on our common “North” and the Path to it, we’ve somehow allowed narrow minded preachers to spread religious fanaticism, extremism, radicalism etc. based merely on religious-dogmas and belief. With the result that the best men of today, have thrown away religions from mainstream educational and governance processes and we have allowed petty, narrow-minded preachers to polarize and divide societies.

Modern society is thus growing up without any idea of the aim and purpose of life and its relationship to God or an entity or a super intelligence, which may be held responsible for all that for which we are not responsible for; and who we may term as the designer, planner, Creator of this universe.

As the Shrestha, elite men of wisdom, irrespective of religious beliefs, we need to identify and highlight the universal aspects of religions so that as a global society we have a healthy worldview of reality as our “Best” goal. As a global scientific society, we need to arrive at a common understanding of the similarities as well as dissimilarities on the idea and ideal of God, Rebirth and Vedas/Revealed-Scriptures.

Evolving a universal idea of God

Of course, many of us are atheists and even those who believe in God have just blind belief without any intellectual conviction and clarity on the god-idea. Let’s investigate and explore.

We all can believe as a religious hypothesis that there is but one Creator of this universe who is infinite, omnipotent, omnipresent, omniscient, consciousness. Hinduism calls it Brahman and Brahman literally mean ‘Greater than …; i.e., the greatest’; mathematically we may understand it as infinite. We may call it Creator God and understand it as one and only One God, Paramatma, Brahman, or simply as God, Father, Super-intelligence, or by whatever name we may refer to it as.

As the Creator God, He alone is. He has no equal. He has no partners. He has no form and cannot be represented by any idol. He is formless. He is all pervasive. He alone is deserving of our highest worship. We all are his children.

He alone is pure Consciousness. He is infinite, greater than all and the greatest. He alone is exclusively deserving of the highest worship because he alone is the Creator; and everything else is his creation and so no one else can be fit for the highest worship.

He knows all our weaknesses and has the capacity to forgive and help us and we surrender to him and seek his forgiveness and mercy for our sins done knowingly or unknowingly and will strive not to repeat them. We can accept that we will always worship the Creator God and never consider anyone equal to him.

We can have reverence and respect for others, the Son, the Prophets, Saints, Sages, etc. who are virtuous and are of righteous conduct and remind us of the Creator God; but the highest worship belongs to the One-Creator-God alone. There is no one equal to the one Creator God, who is the king, controller of this creation and of life and death. All praise and worship belong to the Creator God; and there is no one like him. He always is and is eternally alive and everlasting and for all time to come and is beyond life and death. He alone was, is, and will be in command of all happenings for eternity.

While we may agree that besides Him none is fit for worship, and we will always worship the One Creator God alone, and never consider anyone equal to him; we may also consider our mother, father, elders, teachers, as walking-talking representatives of God and offer them all our respect, reverence and worshipfully offer our sincere and devoted services.

Some questions? Why does God create us? What material is used to create us and from where does it come? Why and on what basis are we all created different? If God is our Creator, we are all brothers and sisters, and all creation is our family. How then can we, and especially those who consider themselves as religious and followers of this or that religion, go around hating, killing, treating anyone disrespectfully, whether they agree or not agree with us or belong to this or that religion and try to see the infinite, formless God, in this or that symbol or idol, or angels or messengers of the One God!

Our challenge is to bring out that essential aspect of all religions into academia that can inspire mankind to strive to build character.

We will continue to explore the pillars of God, rebirth and Vedas/Revealed scriptures.
