India to Bharat · Essay #036

Education is really self-development

1. There is a dire need in the present-day society and system of education to inspire and educate people in practice of self-development skills to move towards ‘higher’ side of personality. This ‘higher’ dimension can be harnessed by tapping the ‘inner’, ‘divine’ and ‘infinite’, ‘intellectual’ ‘emotional’ and spiritual resource of mind and body.

Self-development is a life-long and continuous learning process of understanding life and the higher dimensions of personality and becoming ‘learning-working-giving’ individuals. Towards this end, children need to be made aware of their ‘infinite’ potential.” It suggests ideas, ways and means thru’ which students, teachers, parents can be inspired to build powers of mind to walk-the-talk towards higher long-term goals for good of all.

2. “The present system of education is nothing but a perfect machine for turning out clerks; it is not a life-building, man-making, character-making assimilation of ideas. If you have assimilated five ideals in your life and character, you have more education than any man.

We want that education by which character is formed, strength of mind is increased, the intellect is expanded, and by which one can stand on one’s own feet. Western science coupled with the spirit of oneness, chastity in thought, word and deed. And Sraddha and faith in one’s own Self; divinity within.

The peasant, shoemaker, sweeper, etc. have much greater capacity for work and self-reliance than you (the educated). Silently working they produce the entire wealth, without complaint; uneducated but they are the backbone of the nation. And we dare regard them as low-class and ourselves as cultured!

A most selfish man becomes a hero and shows unselfishness when in the spotlight, with people cheering him; blessed indeed are the masses who are the source of power for the rulers, and yet show same unselfishness and devotion to duty in the smallest of work, unnoticed by all.

We the educated are responsible for their miseries. We are to blame. We have trod on the masses till they have become helpless. Thoughtful people now realize it but they have blamed the Hindu religion for it and to them the only way of improvement is by crushing this grandest religion!

Religion is not at fault. Our Religion teaches that every being is divine, only your Self multiplied. But it was the want of practical application – sympathy, love, care and concern for others. Buddha taught us how to feel, how to sympathise with the poor, the miserable, the sinner, but we heard him not. Our educated do not care for the poor, and yet it is they who help make us rich.

We have but one method of acquiring knowledge – concentration of the powers of mind – in making money, blacking shoes better, cooking or worship. Along with concentration we must develop powers of detachment. We must learn not only to attach the mind to one thing exclusively, but also to detach it at a moment’s notice and place it upon something else. Development of both these keeps us safe.

Every work that we do, every movement of the body, every thought we think, leaves such an impression on the mind-stuff … What we are every moment is determined by the sum total of these impressions on the mind … Each man’s character is determined by the sum total of these impressions. If good impressions prevail, the character becomes good; if bad, it becomes bad.

The only remedy for bad habits is counter habits; all the bad habits are to be controlled by good habits. Go on doing good, thinking holy thoughts continuously; that is the only way to suppress bad impressions.

The old system of education in India was very different from the modern system. The teacher must not teach with any ulterior selfish motive, for money, name, or fame; his work must be simply out of love, pure love for mankind at large (not limited to ‘me and mine’). Within man is all knowledge (Self, Consciousness, Kingdom of God within!), we can only facilitate, remove obstacles (Righteousness & Selflessness purifies the Mind), and knowledge comes out of (Consciousness) its own nature.” – Vivekananda

3. Besides giving knowledge, Education must work with parents to plan how to provide the inspiration so that during 20-25 years of schooling and growing-up, the students get into the habit of daily “Self-study/self-reflection (Swadhyaya)” & “Self-development practices (Sadhana)” to awaken Viveka-buddhi, discriminative-intellect, and build powers of mind to walk-the-talk of Righteousness.

This need not be confused with my vs your god or religion. As academia and devotees of any religion we must distinguish between “Religious-Mythology-Rituals- God ideas” Vs daily practices to be good and better towards “best”, irrespective of religion, nation, race, man or women”!

Our role as Teachers & Mentors of society is to guide the young towards Righteousness!
