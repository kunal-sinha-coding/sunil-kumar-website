India to Bharat · Essay #230

Who have failed us – leaders or the led?

The paradox: While our ‘composite culture’ promotes the spirit of oneness, harmony, excellence, commitment to duties, love for all equally, etc., our leaders have failed to set up educational or training plans to ensure that people are inspired to imbibe and institutionalize this spirit of our Fundamental Duties (Art. 51A). Can selfish, only-my-party-my-family-leaders, stop quarreling and work together, to set up governance plans to ensure that only those committed to working selflessly for good of society are allowed to assume positions of responsibility, in parties or in governance!!

Are Indians, businessmen and workers, lazy and inefficient, or have our leaders, the politicians, academia, administrators, the so-called educated, failed to tap the unlimited potential of our result-producers – the workforce whose entrepreneurship had once made India the wealthiest nation?

Look at the same educated Indians, around the world. They are making India proud in every field, given conducive governance systems. Or at common uneducated Indians at work, outside formal sector, or even plain housewives. Once they are given freedom, autonomy, responsibility, accountability, then these same Indians have the creativity to stand on their feet, identify and solve problems without formal education and training.

The problem is with our education and governance systems: They have created selfish leadership. The educated have knowledge, power, authority but because they have no love for India, they have failed to create an educational system or administrative machinery to solve problems of society or to make India once again a leader in world affairs.

What is our vision? Our Vocation itself is our only bridge to Perfection, Prosperity, Happiness. Each nation and race have their individuality, their own uniqueness and characteristics. Our USP is stated on the gate of our Parliament, i.e. “the whole world is our very own family”. Therefore, the educational vision of “India that is Bharat”, must be to ensure that this vision of ‘oneness’ of all people of all faiths, of Vedanta, Islam, Christianity, etc. is discussed and understood during schooling and then inspires each of us to strive to become better, in and thru’ our faith and our vocation.

We invite sincere suggestions as parents and teachers on how we can try and put together ideas on how this vision of ‘oneness’, can be included in schooling.
