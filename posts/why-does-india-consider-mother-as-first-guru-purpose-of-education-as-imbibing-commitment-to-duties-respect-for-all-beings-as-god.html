India to Bharat · Essay #026

Why does India consider ‘mother as first guru’ & ‘purpose of education as imbibing commitment to ‘duties’ & ‘respect for all beings as God?’

It is a scientifically accepted fact that, “existence can never go out of existence”. “And that which doesn’t exist can never come into existence”. As individuals we exist because of our respective mother and as creatures we exist because of mother Earth! Once we exist, fact is that we can never go out of existence – but our names-forms-nature may keep changing!

It is from this fact, that ancient wisdom defines that our Swaroopa, i.e., our eternal unchanging reality is “Sad-chid-ananda”, “existence-consciousness- bliss”, and we’re all ONE as “existence”! However, attached to our Body-mind we think we’re this changing-existence! This is our basic ignorance! And education means to recognize our ignorance and inculcate and imbibe the wisdom of our Oneness with all, “Whole world as our family”.

Indian wisdom suggests that education, religions, parenting and growing up must be a self-development process to know our Swaroopa and walk-the-talk thru’ Righteousness, goodness, kartavya-palan, etc. to realize our Swaroopa as “Sad-chid-ananda”.

From childhood we must learn to respect this subtle, invisible, principle and reality of our oneness with all, as “existence”. Worship of our mother and mother-Earth and the entire creation itself as God, can expand our Mind to think and recognize the reality that we all, you and I, “exist” only because of ‘mother’; and “existence” is our common, universal true eternal Identity, Swaroopa. As Existence we’re all One!

Logical commonsense analysis to know our inherent nature & form : India asserts that this ever-changing ‘body-mind-intellect’, BMI, is not our inherent “nature” and “form” – it isn’t our unchanging Swaroopa.

Our unchanging eternal reality, Swaroopa, is the singular ever existing Sad-chid-ananda, Consciousness that enlivens our Body-mind, that gets “born-Exists-dies”. Body-mind cannot be our real, ever-existing, changeless “Swa-roopa”. i.e., our “nature” and “form” that “Exists” eternally and is changeless!

The fact is that “consciousness” is our eternal, unchanging existence; but attached with our Mind we feel separated. We must learn that we’re all Individuals with ‘Body, Mind, Intellect’, enlivened by ‘consciousness’, aka Spirit, Life, that enlivens the Mind. Mind is full of memories of all our past ‘intentions-thoughts-deeds’, Samskaras; and it is our Mind that identifies and enslaves us! We’re all enslaved by our own past ideas, beliefs, worldview, etc. as impressed in our Mind, self-acquired habits – Samskara!

2. Understanding the idea of Swa-roopa , our inherent ‘nature’ & ‘form’: As an example, let’s determine the Swa-roopa of say, “sugar” or “salt” or “water” etc. etc. Their “nature, swabhava, characteristics” i.e., “dharma” is un-changing; they eternally ‘exist’ respectively as sweet, salty, H2O. However, while all of them ‘exist’, they appear and manifest in various different names and ever-changing “forms”.

3. So, what is our nature & form, inherent, changeless existence, Swaroopa? Let’s investigate and consider the wisdom and scientific reasoning, to analyse, understand and Know our “Swa-roopa” i.e., “the nature and form of our Unchanging Existence; that “Swaroopa” now “Existing” and appearing as our “Body-mind-intellect-memories” individuality; that manifest as our habits, behaviour, character, etc.

4. Logic of “existence”: From the ancient Vedic past, ‘India that is Bharat’ was aware of the Scientific reality that if “I, you, creation” Exist, we can never disappear; and Existence cannot come out of Nothing; as Big-bang suggests! So, they discussed, explored and logically concluded that our ‘existence’ as this body-mind ‘ Roopa ’, form, is only a temporary existence; appearing and manifesting a particular changing ‘nature (Dharma)’ and ‘form (Roopa)’.

Our present universal intrinsic, inherent human ‘Nature’ as different human beings, now appearing as ‘existing’ is not our real unchanging ‘Nature’ (Dharma). Each individual has its own ‘Nature’ (Swa-dharma), characteristics, behaviour. Universal human nature, is termed as our Dharma and our individual, respective Dharma in a particular role and responsibility, is termed our Swa-dharma. We need to become “aware” that our present changing, ‘Name-Form’ identity as Body-mind, now appearing as ‘existing’, is NOT our Swaroopa!

7. India has always proposed education as a self-reflection & self-development process to Purify our thoughts and direct our Thoughts to realize and re-gain our Swa-roopa thru’ work as worship and learning to see our mother, father, teachers, even ‘Atithi’, guest as God.

Self-development practices and striving to imbibe and follow the spirit of righteousness, selflessness, of Work as worship, of work as service to God in man and all creation, purifies the Mind and only a purified Mind can try to understand the idea of Swaroopa. This Wisdom, is a universal, fundamental truth that individuals must be taught, inculcate, learn and imbibe, during 20-25 years of ‘parenting, schooling, growing up’.

8. Universal “self-awareness” practices: Without practice, we are prejudiced, enslaved by mind and its past ideas. The more we learn to put aside the judgmental-mind, more light happens… truth fills us. The more we learn the art of being with our unchanging “SELF”, the more I can imbibe the art of being with ‘others’ in ‘Love’, considering them as one with us!

If we don’t know how to be with our “Self”, I cannot relate with “Self” of others, with love and oneness! Like it or not, we’re designed to act with our “limited-I-ego-self”. Practicing Righteousness in our External behaviour and Internal practice of Self-awareness exercises, supplement and complement each other, in Being and making Mentors!

If we reflect, we can ourselves realize and recognize that as awareness we’re all one with each other and with creation as a whole; as existence; consciousness, because that which exists can never go out of existence, though it may change its form e.g., ‘water, ice, steam, vapour, cloud’ with H2O as changeless Swaroopa; and that which doesn’t exist can never come into existence!
