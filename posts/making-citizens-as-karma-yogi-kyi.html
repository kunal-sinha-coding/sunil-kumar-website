India to Bharat · Essay #097

Making citizens as karma yogi, KYi

Why strive to be a KYi?

Once we reflect, study and discuss we will appreciate that karma is not just work and ‘action’ but a holistic and deeper understanding of actions along with its effect on our own selves. Thus, Karma includes all actions – thoughts, words, actions/work as well as the motives/intentions behind them, that we are engaged in 24x7x365 throughout working and living.

And it also includes the reaction of karma, that remains in our mind as samskara, memories, impressions, and enslaves and binds us to this limited body-mind-ego-individuality and consequent cycle of pain-pleasure, governed by the principle of cause-effect, termed as destiny and it is this concept of karma that explains and determines what and where we’re and why we’re different from each other.

Knowing this we will realize that our daily work and living can itself lead us either towards our highest Goal or away from it. So, from childhood we must learn, and practice building the ability to manage our thoughts and mind and perform karma in a way that leads towards the highest Goal and not away from it towards ‘pain-misery-pleasure’ cycle of body-mind.

Schooling must be a process of learning to perform karma in a way that keeps us on the path to the highest ‘Goal of Freedom’; away from the cycle of pain-pleasure towards a state of perpetual Peace, Freedom, Bliss, Ananda; towards Heaven away from Hell! And this is the secret of Action, Karma Yoga, commitment to Righteous and ethical conduct that Arjuna learnt that we as Citizens must learn and practice.

And the ideal of KY is that whether a lowest or highest worker or Ruler, simply and merely by fulfilment of our respective duties, kartavya-palan, KY, we attain purity of mind, chitta-suddhi, and can attain That highest and best Goal of mankind, just as we attain thru’ worship, prayers, meditation, tapasya , give in charity, as per our respective religious observance, etc.

Arjun is curious to know more about those KYi who are situated in divine consciousness and Krishna, therefore, describes how persons who practice being a KYi become peaceful, blissful, free from attachment, fear, pain, anger. They are undisturbed and equipoised in every situation. With their senses subdued, they keep their minds always absorbed in God. He also explains the progression of afflictions of the mind – such as greed, anger, lust, etc. and advises how these can be overcome.

Arjuna learns that a KYi gradually gets established in divine knowledge and sees the reality that those who seek to enjoy the fruits of their works suffer the consequences of actions, good or bad. One who prudently practices the science of working without attachment can get rid of both good and bad reactions in this life itself. Therefore, strive for Yoga, which is the art of working skillfully, established in evenness of mind. The wise endowed with equanimity of intellect, abandon attachment to the fruits of actions, which bind one to the cycle of life and death. By working in such consciousness, they attain the state beyond all suffering.

When our intellect crosses the quagmire of delusion, we then acquire indifference to what has been heard and what is yet to be heard, about enjoyments in this world and the next. When our intellect ceases to be allured by the fruit-fulfilling actions here or of heaven and hell and remains steadfast in divine consciousness of love for all, love for whole world as our family; with continuous practice and focus we attain the state of perfect Yoga, Unity with all Creation.

A question arises, what is the ideal KYi like? What is the disposition of one who is situated in even mindedness, divine consciousness? How does an enlightened person talk? How does he sit? How does he walk?

Arjuna learns that the signs of the ideal KYi are: He has discarded all selfish desires and cravings of the senses that torment the mind and becomes satisfied in the realization of the Self/is satisfied in the self by the self, such a person is said to be stable in wisdom/transcendentally situated.

How to reach that state? Two paths lead to enlightenment; the path of knowledge, for those inclined toward contemplation, and the path of ‘work’ for those inclined toward action.

One cannot achieve freedom from karmic reactions by merely abstaining from work, nor can one attain perfection of knowledge by mere physical renunciation. There is no one who can remain without action even for a moment. Indeed, all beings are compelled to act by their qualities born of material nature (the three qualities, gunas, of Sattva, Rajas, Tamas viz. calm, active, dull; all things and beings are composed of a mixture of these three qualities in varying proportions).

Simply restraining the external organs of action, while continuing to dwell on sense objects in the mind, is delusion and hypocrisy! The superior choice is to learn to practice controlling the senses by the mind – will power, self-control, intellect and then learn to work and fulfil the duties, without attachment to results. Performing our prescribed duties is to be done, since action is superior to inaction. By ceasing activity, even our bodily maintenance will not be possible!

Attitude at Work: Work must be done in the spirit of Yajna, i.e., as an offering, work as service and our highest worship of God in man and in all Creation. Only such work leads to enlightenment, Freedom, all other work for ‘me and mine’ leads to and causes bondage in and to this material world. Therefore, let us practice working for the satisfaction of God; performing our prescribed duties, without being attached to the results!
