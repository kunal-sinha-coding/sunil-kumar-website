India to Bharat · Essay #175

Imbibing the truth of truths: Nuggets for self-introspection – 2

1. Search for Truth : Who is responsible for helping us imbibe the virtues to reach the Truth? A preacher has to be a seeker of truth first and foremost; as a bye-product he may inspire us and show us the way to the highest truth, the truth of Truths. Instead of inspiring us to imbibe the highest ideals of our respective scriptures, religions seem to have lost the way and are no longer in fashion. In fact, they’ve been and have become more political than spiritual and are responsible for mass scale carnages, fanaticism, extremism. Both preachers and we the followers, like the blind leading the blind, have imprisoned ourselves into capsules of beliefs, teachings and words of our respective Masters, Prophets, God-men, etc.

Religions have failed to follow or imbibe in us, the followers, the ideal of ‘the whole world as our own family’, that all religions subscribe to. We’ve failed to imbibe the virtuous ideals of love, peace, unselfishness, e.g. ‘just as God loves us, so should we love each other; love your enemies, do good, lend, expecting no return; love the Lord your God with all your heart, and with all your soul, and with all your mind; love your neighbour as your-self’.

Modern science and religions have made us believe that we’re just a Body-Mind complex, which is matter, ignoring the Consciousness, the ‘Self’ within; it is only as Self that we’re all one. The question is: How do we learn to identify more and more with our-Self?

2. How can Education help? It is we the educated, we the scientists, professionals and political animals who now pride ourselves as truth seekers. Unfortunately, schooling is focused more and more on mundane issues, memory, information, marks; and the quantity of the taught rather than their quality. It has, for various obvious reasons, killed the scientific spirit of enquiry and the process of seeking, exploring, questioning truth for ourselves. The problem is that the more we know and discover, the more we realize that our mind and intellect thru’ which we seek to know, are extremely limited, and we’re not likely to ever discover the truth of Truths, in this way!

3. The Remedy – ‘Hearing-Reflecting-Imbibing’: To understand, appreciate and be intellectually convinced in higher Truths, a continuous, daily process of reflection is needed. It would include ‘repetition of the idea’; ‘discussions, case studies, stories as illustrations’; ‘to see the context and relevance to our short- and long-term goals’; ‘appeal to logic and reasoning’; ‘conviction on how will I benefit from it and how will it help knowing the unknown’. We have to reflect on such truths and meditate on them till the idea of truth and virtues is imbibed in our psyche and we can act accordingly. This has to be done continuously for whole life.

The many years of schooling is the ideal place to get established in this habit of daily self-introspection. Here ideas and ideals can be freely explored and discussed based on proper appreciation of our respective scriptures to imbibe the ideal of the whole word as our family; and, nurturing the sincere towards leadership.

The best that we can do during schooling is to provide sufficient time, space, conducive environment and inspirational ideals for daily reflection. After that it is up to the pupils; they will absorb as much as they can. And those who have the intelligence, curiosity, character and a passion for the truth of Truth will emerge as the elite, real leaders, scientists, and role models of society.

Religions ask us to first have sraddha, faith-in-Truth. Prophets and Rishis give us indications and pointers towards the highest spiritual truths of the unknowable entity, God. To imbibe the ideals, Schooling should provide daily time for knowing the Truth and imbibing Virtues. In this way we can combine the “logical and reasoning aspect of education available on a common platform” with the “faith and devotional aspect of religions , now available separately, leading to mine-versus-your”. We have to reflect on the highest truths, together:

  • God is the Father, the creator; Mother, who nourishes, nurtures, sustains us; the dispenser who judges the consequences of our thoughts-actions and delivers us the just fruits, no more and no less. He is the One to be sought after, because once He is known, everything becomes known.
  • While we have many goals, all our goals can be put into ‘striving for long efficient life’; ‘seeking after knowledge; knowing more and more’; ‘searching for more and more happiness’; all these are the characteristic of God. And thus, God becomes the common, highest, and ultimate goal for us all.

After the process of ‘Hearing-reflection-imbibing’, the convocation address, in ancient times, was: Righteousness, Truth, learning, and teaching are to be practiced. Social good conduct is to be adhered to. Truth is the thing. Speak the truth. Practice Righteous Conduct. There should be no negligence about truth. There should be no deviation from righteous conduct. Do not neglect duties that lead to one’s own good or general welfare. Do not be careless about learning and teaching.

In India that is Bharat, Schooling has to have a spiritual flavour that alone will fulfill our ideal of harmony of religions and the whole creation as our family.
