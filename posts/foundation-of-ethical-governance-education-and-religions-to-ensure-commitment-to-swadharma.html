India to Bharat · Essay #049

Foundation of Ethical Governance? Education and Religions to ensure Commitment to Swadharma

Who is responsible in modern systems of education, democracy and governance to guide and inspire us to be good and better towards an ideal of Best; and, to be committed to our respective Swadharma, Duties?

Now that science has replaced religion, and teachers have replaced preachers, which areas of science and education are exploring the idea of being the best human being? Don’t we need to know existential issues of life? Of who we are? Why are we here? Where we are going? How to prepare ourselves to go there? What is our connection with each other; with nature? Aren’t we interconnected and not separate entities?

Shouldn’t education, sciences, focus on letting us know, discuss, explore, discover how science and religions are grappling with these fundamental issues of who we’re and where should we be going; and, of how mankind’s ancient seers have thought on these profound issues and what were their findings? Shouldn’t our best people focus on these basic issues? Isn’t the very purpose of education and religions to inspire us to be good? Obviously, modern democratic and scientific society has failed to unravel the idea of ‘best’ and so we remain in a state of purposelessness!

Education focuses on teaching us various subjects but totally ignores building of character and educating us to be in a vocation in accordance with our inherent tendencies, our Swadharma. Religions focus on dividing and teaching us of our respective scriptures and Gods; but totally ignore the ideal that though following different religions and Gods, we must together strive to fulfill our Swadharma.

Science has been so busy denying God that they’ve failed to define the idea of God, “as that which is responsible for those things for which you and I are obviously not responsible”, e.g., I sow a seed and for some reasons mother earth becomes fertile and, the seed sprouts as a plant! And, like it or not, I am suddenly born somewhere! And, I am nurtured by someone! And, under whom the infinitely vast universe is running intelligently, in a cosmic harmony, Rtm? Scientists are so busy exploring the material and objective universe out there and finding no God there, that they’re totally ignorant of the “subjective” which is within, our own inner-universe, our infinite-mind illumined by ‘Life’, Consciousness, (God within?) that make us living-beings!

Why is religion leading to divisions in society and, to forcible conversions and in extreme cases even to terrorism and fanaticism? Isn’t it only because we allow self-centred petty narrow-minded preachers of a religion to teach ‘hatred’ for others in secret?

Action Plan: Vivekananda was aware that the world – politically, economically, socially, and culturally – was rapidly becoming a close-knit whole, which could be stable only if unity was also found among religions themselves – a unity not dogmatically imposed, but inherent in religions themselves. So, it was Vivekananda who first declared on a global world platform at Chicago in 1893 the idea of unity of all religions, and so he defined thoroughly and taught explicitly the simple idea that Religion is the manifestation of the divinity already in man.

He suggested that we need schools, colleges, universities as human values development centres, whose faculty would include spiritually evolved professors selected from all religions and faiths; where basics of all religions can be taught, openly from a common platform, and not in secret; which may serve to educate a superior kind of spiritual Mentors of society. Where people may go not to quarrel but to meet on the common plane between religions. Today, our Supreme Court and our Constitutional Fundamental Duties are echoing this action plan.

In this age, true sympathy with all men, must be a predominant motive of work felt Vivekananda. This largeness of spirit was perhaps wanting in Hinduism, lost in quest for Moksha , thereby ignoring active compassion and service to fellow beings. Having once explored and fixed the highest goal, our focus ought to be on the means, i.e., following our respective Swadharma; which shall certainly lead to the ideal!

Standing free from current dogmas, sects, ideologies of different religions and, in the true Vedantic spirit of India that is Bharat, we need our teachers, our universities and the academia, to blaze a path of reason; and focus on building the educated as Karma-yogi, committed to their respective Swadharma.

Vivekananda told his brother monks in India: If you want any good to come, just throw your ceremonials in the water of the Ganga and worship the visible God, He who wears all these various human forms. This world is His universal form. Worshipping Him means serving the world; this indeed is religion, not indulging in ceremonials.

The highest and the best Goal of any Religion is just this ideal: “I can secure my own good only by doing your good. There is no other way, none whatever. The only way of getting our divine nature manifested is by helping others do the same”. This is the purpose and the heart of all Religions, be it Islam, Christianity, various sects of Hinduism, Vedas, Buddhism. This Science and Practice of Spirituality is the essence of all religions and needs to be an integral part of education.

This spirit of service to others, of being a Karma yogi, of being committed to our Swadharma is the lesson Hinduism must learn from the West and from all modern religions and this should now be the focus of education and training in schools and colleges.
