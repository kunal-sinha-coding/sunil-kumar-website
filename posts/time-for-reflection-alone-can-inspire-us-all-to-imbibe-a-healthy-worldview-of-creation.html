India to Bharat · Essay #045

Time for reflection alone can inspire us all to imbibe a healthy worldview of creation

1. An Inspirational exhortation for all – especially for parents, teachers, students:

# Go placidly amid the noise & haste & remember what peace there may be in silence. As far as possible without surrender be on good terms with all persons. Speak your truth quietly and clearly; and listen to others, even the dull & ignorant; they too have their story.

# Avoid loud & aggressive persons, they are vexations to the spirit. If you compare yourself with others, you may become vain & bitter; for always there will be greater & lessor persons than yourself. Enjoy your achievements as well as your plans.

# Keep interested in your own career, however humble; it is a real possession in the changing fortunes of time. Exercise caution in your business affairs; for the world is full of trickery. But let this not blind you to what virtue there is; many persons strive for high ideals; and everywhere life is full of heroism.

# Be yourself. Especially, do not feign affection. Neither be cynical about love; for in the face of all aridity & disenchantment it is perennial as grass.

# Take kindly the counsel of the years, gracefully surrendering the things of the youth. Nurture strength of spirit to shield you in sudden misfortune. But do not distress yourself with imaginings. Many fears are born of fatigue & loneliness. Beyond a wholesome discipline, be gentle with yourself.

# You are a child of the universe, no less than the trees & the stars; you have a right to be here. And whether or not it is clear to you, no doubt the universe is unfolding as it should,

# Therefore, be at peace with God, whatever you conceive Him to be, and whatever your labors & aspirations, in the noisy confusion of life keep peace with your soul.

# With all its sham, drudgery & broken dreams, it is still a beautiful world.

# Be careful. Strive to be happy – Found in Old Saint Paul’s Church, Baltimore – 1692.

2. Questions for Reflection and Discussions: The best method to inspire the young and old in this era of logic and reasoning, of ‘nulius in verba’ , i.e., distaste for authority of faith and belief based religious ideologies, is to inspire students to study, explore, debate issues. For example, from the above we can raise following questions for discussions:

Is there peace and happiness in silence? How to remain calm and peaceful? Why should we be on good terms with all? How? Many persons strive for high ideals – What are high ideals? Unless we first fix the highest ideal how can we discuss good, better, best? What are some processes to nurture strength of spirit? What exactly do we understand by ‘spirit’? Where is it located? How can we access it? How can it shield us?

What if any is the comparison or relationship between us and the trees and stars? What is the plan of creation, of God, whatever you conceive Him to be, according to which the universe is unfolding as it should? What is happiness? Where is the source of happiness? How do we strive to be happy?

3. Time for Daily Reflection and Self-study: Mere literacy, 3R’s, teaching of this or that subject cannot, and hasn’t made us good! It is our duty as elders to give the young the inspiration, time, space, conducive environment to explore, enquire, question and acquire an Intellectual Conviction in the Principles and Plan of Creation and, evolve for themselves an action plan to realize our goals and fulfill our purpose in life. Let students reflect upon certain basic fundamental Principles, e.g.: –

Thru’ righteousness, ethics, fulfilling our respective duties, i.e., ‘Swadharma’, ‘earn’, ‘enjoy’ and remain on the critical path to Success, peace, joy, bliss, prosperity for all – our common, universal ‘North’, aka ‘Ananda’, ‘Fulfilment’.

Arise from the ignorance of our limited potential identifying ourselves only with our body! Awake to the knowledge of our unlimited potential identifying ourselves with the spirit, life, the consciousness, the kingdom of God, within each of us. Stop not till we have reached our Goal of experiencing our unlimited potential, of our unchanging inner nature of Ananda, the source of life.

Our duties, Swadharma, are to our parents, elders, youngsters, institutions, society, nation, all living beings, vegetation, nature and environment; because, as Consciousness, we’re all one, interconnected. As has been well said, ‘Whoever will be ready to serve God – no, no, not God but his children – the poor and downtrodden, the sinful and afflicted, the wicked and the virtuous, in them God will manifest himself’.

This unselfishness is the essence of all religions; the key to understanding the healthy worldview of Creation, now lost in the forest of words of scriptures thanks to narrow minded immature preachers fighting and killing over ‘my versus your’ ideologies. Today, no one is responsible and can be held accountable for the self-centered corrupt society, where science and money are the gods, led by political manipulators.

5. The Means to imbibe higher ideals: I must strive to know and be committed to my duties. I must strive for Quality and excellence in my vocation, chosen in accordance with our inherited inclinations and tendencies, where I can excel and strive for continuous improvements. I must gradually learn that working for good of samaaja (literally meaning, same as god) is the only way to prepare our mind thru daily self-development practices to: Improve Quality of our Thoughts. Reduce Quantity of unwanted Thoughts by keeping busy on planned Important activities. And, learn to Direct our Thoughts towards the higher and better by daily reviewing our short-term and long-term SMART goals.
