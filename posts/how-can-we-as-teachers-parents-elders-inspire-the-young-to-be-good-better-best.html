India to Bharat · Essay #184

How can we as teachers, parents, elders inspire the young to be good, better, best?

1. Dissatisfaction with the present vyavastha, state of Affairs: We’re all products of schooling based on Western ideas that 3R’s, science, technology will cure all our problems, physical, mental and spiritual; and so, we are busy creating schools and teaching a variety of subjects to all and sundry. We ignore the reality that different pupils need different types of education. We insist on giving the same education to everyone, irrespective of wide differences, needs, strengths and weaknesses of the pupils. We’ve not even bothered to see what is the quality of people that we have produced from this mechanical schooling over the last seventy years, and whether the schooling has any relevance to what individuals and society really need.

Are we really happy with the education that is creating the educated who have given us nothing but corrupt systems of schooling and governance? Don’t we realize that our education and governance have failed to curb galloping selfishness, greed, exploitation, consumerism, and wide spread corruption amongst the educated. Why is it that the best of our IIT’s, MBA’s, MP’s, Civil servants have failed to find creative and innovative ways to solve problems of India and make us a great nation? Shouldn’t we realize that the same best-people are able to attain great success overseas; and therefore, there is something very seriously wrong with our systems of education and governance.

2. Schooling and Governance based on ideals of Bharat: Perhaps, it is time that we discard the system of schooling and governance that Macaulay and his ilk imposed on India that was Bharat and relook, recreate, and re-engineer our systems based on the lofty vision of Bharat, of our own forefathers, who had a clear and holistic worldview of reality based on the experiences of countless rishis, seers, sages as conveyed to mankind thru’ Upanishads. Unlike the state of purposelessness and material covetousness of the West, the twin ideals of Bharat are very clear and very specific:

  • Firstly, only a man of character, a good and virtuous man striving for becoming good, better, best is really fit to be a teacher and a leader of men in politics, administration and business.
  • Secondly, schooling is the time to build character, getting committed to kartavya-palan, to human values and ethics, and to care and concern for others. Literacy and other secular knowledge needed for life and vocation is secondary and not the primary purpose of schooling.

It is obvious that character, discipline, ethical conduct has to be established when young, whereas knowledge and skills can be acquired later also, depending upon the ability and inherent samskaras. Moreover, we should now realize from our own experience, that knowledge without character has given us nothing but selfish and corrupt teachers and leaders.

3. Teachers for building Character not just Teaching: Success in life doesn’t come from IQ alone. More important are our ability to manage our thoughts and feelings, the negative emotions that arise in our minds and learn to build the ability to direct our progress towards good, better, ‘best’, the ideal of human Perfection. Powers of mind can be developed throughout life by inculcating the habits and disciplines of practicing time-tested self-development processes, available with faith groups.

Schooling must focus on establishing the youth in daily self-development practices to develop an intellectual conviction in the power of goodness, ethics, human values. A multitude of Faith groups and NGO’s are equipped to bring self-development activities to Academia. Teachers and Parents must realize and teach that our daily work and vocation are our only opportunity to attain the highest goal of life, provided we remain committed to ethics, human values, and fulfil all our duties.

3.1 Teachers as Mentors: Parents and academia can plan and choose the appropriate time-tested self-development practices for daily routine of prayers, self-introspection, meditation, yoga, goal setting, self-study, etc. to get inspiration to strive to be better.

3.2 It’s a Long-term, Life-long Process: Imbibing values, character, disciplines are not a short-term goal, nor is it just an academic issue. It needs changing our mindset, our inherent tendencies, worldview, beliefs, habits, etc., i.e. our samskara and replacing them with higher, better, samskaras. Continuous pouring of positive, holy thoughts in the mind is required. It needs life long struggle and a desire to be good and better, towards the ‘best’. But what is best, needs to be reflected upon, explored continuously. Therefore, the way is to establish children from a young age in good habits, values, disciplines, work ethos, quality consciousness, mindfulness, Quiet time.

3.3 We see strikes, dharna, violence, destruction of public properties, incompetency, inefficiency, everywhere. We need a meeting-point between Religion, Governance, Academia. Between the East and the West. Between Science and Religion. Between the past and future.

4. Excellence in our Vocation is the highest Worship: We must learn that not just all modes of worship alone, but equally all modes of work, all modes of struggle, all modes of creation, are paths to realization of the highest perfection.

No distinction, henceforth, between the sacred and secular. Our daily work, relationships and Life itself are the spiritual aspects of any religion. Quality in work, fulfilment of duties, leading people and nations, all these are as much a worship, as worship in temples. Our daily work, is not as divorced from, but as expressing knowledge and devotion. Our office, farm, business, work is the true and fit field for the meeting of God with man.

Schooling mut convince us that there is no difference between service of man and worship of God, between true righteousness and spirituality. Art, science, and religion are but three different ways of expressing a single truth.
