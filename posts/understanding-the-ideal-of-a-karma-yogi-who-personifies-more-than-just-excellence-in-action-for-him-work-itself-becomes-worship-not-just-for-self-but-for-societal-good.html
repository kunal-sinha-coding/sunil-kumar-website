India to Bharat · Essay #154

Understanding the ideal of a karma yogi, who personifies more than just excellence in action; for him work itself becomes worship, not just for self, but for societal good!

The Government plans to transform its officers and staff into karma yogis : The idea of being a Karma yogi is extremely relevant for everyone, irrespective of who and what we’re, and what our status or vocation is.

It is therefore essential that education in schools and colleges should now also institute workshops and training programs so that all Indian Citizens understand, appreciate and are inspired to be Karma Yogis. Education and Religions will have to join together and take responsibility and accountability to help and inspire us grow from just being employees working for wages, to learning to treat work as our highest worship, till we grow into becoming a Karma yogi who passionately works not just for self, but for good of society as a whole.

The best source to understand the ideal of a Karma Yogi is the series of lectures delivered by Vivekananda in UK and USA which have been compiled in a booklet “Karma Yoga”. Its essence will be included in the next series of blogs.

What is Karma? “To do”; all action is Karma. Each karma has an Effect both externally and internally by leaving impressions on our mind, which then become our habit, persona, character, and thus influence our present choices, actions, behavior patterns. Thus, inner consequences of our actions in form of impressions on our mind are also Karma. Karma often implies the present actions as effects, of which our past actions were the causes.

In Karma-Yoga we have simply to do with the word Karma as meaning work. We are all doing Karma all the time. I am talking to you: that is Karma. You are listening: that is Karma. We breathe: that is Karma. We walk: Karma. Everything we do, physical or mental, is Karma, and it leaves it’s marks on us (in our mind). Karma in its effect on character is the most tremendous power that man has to deal with.

No one can get anything unless he earns it. This is an eternal law. Our Karma determines what we deserve and what we can assimilate. We are responsible for what we are; and whatever we wish ourselves to be, we have the power to make ourselves. If what we are now has been the result of our own past actions, it certainly follows that whatever we wish to be in future can be produced by our present actions.

The purpose of all work , karma, is simply to bring out the power of the mind, soul, Kingdom of God, Spirit, which is already within us all. Karma Yoga tells us how to work so that we become united, yoga, with this colossal power within and can obtain the greatest results. Unlimited potential power is inside every man. So, it is extremely important to become aware of it, unite (yoga) with it, so that our work manifests its purity, goodness, power in our daily working and living. Different works are like blows, and when done in spirit of Yoga, they bring the goodness of the colossal power within, to come to our work.

Man works with various motives . There cannot be work without motive. A karma yogi struggles towards the ideal of learning to work for work’s sake without caring for name, or fame, or even to go to heaven. They work just because ‘good’ will come of it. There are others who do good to the poor and help mankind from still higher motives, because they believe in doing good and love good. If a man works without any selfish motive in view, does he not gain anything? Yes, he gains the highest. Unselfishness is more paying, only people have not the patience to practice it. It is more paying from the point of view of health also.

Love, truth, righteous conduct, are not merely moral figures of speech; they form our highest ideal, because they purify our mind and help union, yoga, with the divine power.

Essence of Karma Yoga: The public servants of today are akin to erstwhile ruler or ‘raja’. It was their duty, like parents, to love, nurture, protect, guide the praja, the ruled or citizens, towards the highest ideal of work and life! Do we ask anything from our children in return for what we have given them? It is our duty to work for them, and there the matter ends. In whatever you do for a particular person, a city, state, or organization, assume the same attitude towards it as you have towards your children — expect nothing in return.

If you can invariably take the position of a giver, in which everything given by you is a free offering to the world, without any thought of return, then will your work bring you no attachment. Attachment comes only where we expect a return. If working like slaves results in selfishness and attachment, working as master of our own mind gives rise to the bliss of non-attachment.

All thought of obtaining return for the work we do hinders our spiritual progress; nay, in the end it brings misery. There is another way in which this idea of mercy and selfless charity can be put into practice; that is, by looking upon work as “worship” where we give up all the fruits our work unto the Lord. The Lord Himself works incessantly and is ever without attachment. Karma-Yoga means to help anyone, without asking questions.

To be an ideal householder is a much more difficult task than to be a Sannyasin; the true life of duties is indeed as hard as, if not harder than, the equally true life of renunciation.
