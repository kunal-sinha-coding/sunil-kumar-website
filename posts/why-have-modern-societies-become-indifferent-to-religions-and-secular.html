India to Bharat · Essay #182

Why have modern societies become indifferent to religions and secular?

History of modern religions : Separation of religion and state is the idea of Secularism which grew in Europe and USA, because of growing intolerance between various sects of the church and the church with the rulers, all wanting supreme power. Finally, emerged the idea to keep the church away from interfering in governance.

Intolerance : The environment into which people, communities, institutions are born has a great impact on the type of religions they have. Tolerance can be innate and inherent or forced due to circumstances beyond our control. The two modern major religions, Christianity and Islam believe that only their God is the real and the only one true God, because they have arisen and grown from an environment of a tribal mindset in Arabia, Middle East. Tribes have only one Chief who demands strict obedience to their commandments. Tribes are taught not to forgive; take revenge! Therefore, these modern monotheist religions naturally have a tribal mindset and believe that their Chief, i.e. God, alone is to be followed. Thus, Allah of Muslims, God of Christians, Adonai of Jews are not the same person; they’re different Chiefs, though all of them were Arabs. Religions that are polytheist are innately tolerant whereas monotheist religions become intolerant of others.

Religions have a tendency to divide; they actually teach us that people are not equal! The idea of differentiation based on birth prevailed throughout the ancient worlds. There were always rulers, landholders, freemen, slaves and in fact just a hundred years back only landholders were considered fit to vote and women were excluded. On the other hand, Hinduism had a very well-organized, but much-criticised, idea of caste which kept people focused on their respective vocations and created a barrier preventing people rising beyond their levels of character and competence.

Christianity had the Clergy (priesthood), Nobility, Laity including traders, freemen, serfs. In Islamic countries, the Muslim are at the top, followed by the people of the book – Jews and Christians – and at the bottom are the Kaffirs, atheists, nonbelievers, people of other religions, such as Buddhists and Hindus and non-Muslim who had to pay Jazia, in lieu of being spared from execution for being kaffirs.

Religions apparently seem antisocial: Religions teach hatred for others and newer religions have to criticize the older religions, in order to convert them to their new ways of worship! It is ironical that even though Jews, Christians, Muslims are all children of Abrahm, they all hate each other. Jews were prosecuted and pogroms have been carried out by both the others. Religions tell their followers that the ‘others’ are evil, unclean, infidels, kaffirs, untouchables, etc. The Muslim conquest of India was usually ruthless. To the Muslims, Hinduism with its many gods, rituals, image-worship was the exact opposite of what Islam believed in. Hindu resistance suffered wholescale massacres, destruction of temples, conversions; the choice was between Allah or the sword. Of course, earlier Christianity was the most intolerant religion engaged in conversions, killings, persecution, pogroms, burning of witches, etc. Intolerance is connected with the quest for power, rulership, obedience, cruelty, arrogance lack of humility, etc. No wonder, modern generation with the benefit of schooling, is unhappy with religions; of course, also because they’ve never taken the trouble to appreciate the real spiritual essence of religions.

Religions are anti-progress: Religion is actually supposed to lead to the spiritual growth of its followers towards being virtuous, loving, caring! And, as we grow spiritually, we’re bound to grow towards becoming more scientific, logical, forgiving. In actual fact, history of modern religions is a saga of intolerance, cruelty, killings, loot; followers are ever ready to kill and do kill those who question their authority and preaching. Both the major religions teach forgiveness but their followers till today have built-in hatreds, for whatever reasons e.g. the persecution of Jews; destruction of idols in Afghanistan! Many scientists were executed in Europe because they questioned the biblical beliefs that the earth is flat, stationary, center of universe and the sun and moon revolve around it. That is why science grew in Europe in opposition to religions and the motto of Royal Society was “nullius in verba”, i.e. not to accept the authority (of the church!)”. The USA had a law declaring it illegal to teach Darwin’s Theory of Evolution, and teach only the Biblical theory of creation.

Secularism is a product of intolerance of modern religions: The Catholic church had established a dictatorship in western parts of Europe and heresy was a capital crime and Inquisition courts dealt out torture for dissent. People gradually revolted; they began to doubt whether corrupt popes, clergy, the church could be a guide to God! Many new leaders and sects were born, all believing in the Bible but in conflict with each other. The clergy, the rulers, noblemen, professionals were fighting for supremacy and power; the latter were getting literate and were more in numbers and they created the idea of parliament and ultimately the people who were the largest in numbers had a voice and democracy came into being, because of the constant fighting, rising aspirations, and lack of character and integrity in the clergy and the higher classes.

This is a picture of the Christian world who have emerged victorious after centuries of struggle with various classes, religious sects and their dogmas and wrong-beliefs. They’re the new secular, scientific, tolerant, global leaders. They are being challenged by a ‘clash of civilizations’ from the Islamic world, which has yet to get refined and become tolerant of dissent, by going thru’ the struggle which Christianity has faced.

Intolerance and secularism are not a product of India and the East where religious tolerance prevails and the Vedantic Hinduism easily accepts all religions as the path to one God, though called by many different names. Vedanta shows that God being all pervasive, omnipotent, omnipresent, omniscience, cannot be finite, but has to be infinite. Mind cannot appreciate the idea of infinite. So, Idols, images, symbols, temples help to point towards the One infinite God. The problem is that India allows Muslim and Christians to teach in their schools the idea that ‘my God, my Scripture, my Prophet’ alone is true, but doesn’t allow the teaching of this Vedantic idea that all religions are true, to everyone in mainstream schooling. How to teach people to be good, is our modern challenge.
