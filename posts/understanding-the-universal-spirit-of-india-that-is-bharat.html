India to Bharat · Essay #193

Understanding the universal spirit of India that is Bharat

1. Not ‘my versus your religion’: Modern religions are of a very recent origin whereas mankind has existed for thousands of centuries. So, for over 95% of human history, men and women have nourished their sense of spirituality without any formal separatist religious constructs. And, history acknowledges Bharat as the oldest civilization where a spiritual tradition, Sanatana-dharma of Vedic and Vedantic civilization, of becoming good, better, best thru’ a virtuous life, is still living, though with many encrustations. In modern times it is being called Hinduism, to differentiate it with modern religions.

2. Universal spirit of Bharatiya culture: Bharat literally means ‘those who are striving for illumination’, i.e. ‘highest human perfection’, irrespective of any differences of race, nationality, religion, gender, caste, etc.

It is well known that Mahatma Gandhi began his meetings with multi faith prayers, reciting portions from various religious texts. Gandhi was a firm believer in the idea of communal harmony. From childhood, as he cared for his father, he got an opportunity to listen and learn from his father’s friends, belonging to different religions, and appreciated that each religion was a path of virtuous conduct and practices, to attain a state of ‘perfection’.

He drew his inspiration from the Bharatiya samskriti, aptly called Sanatana-dharma, i.e. universal and eternal human and ethical values, obligatory duties. It has not originated from any one prophet or at any particular period of time and does not depend on the authority of any one holy book, person or persons. It is based on universal truths and eternal principles relating to man-nature-God, as revealed to a galaxy of pure, selfless men of wisdom, called Rishis, Masters, God-men, Prophets and saints, who have ‘realized God’ and therefore have the holistic vision to guide people towards the highest goal of human life.

3. Universal idea of religions: Simply put, the purpose of all religions is to inspire people to be virtuous and to provide an ideal of human ‘perfection’ to be attained through goodness! Religions create a mythology. They provide a God-like prophet and role models of goodness. They give clarity on the aim and purpose of human life and create prayers, ways of worship, rituals, self-development practices to build the powers of mind to walk-the-talk towards ‘perfection’. Human-values, virtues and ethical principles, dharma, can be understood as those universal and eternal values, e.g. peace, righteous conduct, love, ahimsa, which when acquired and inculcated in one’s character, take one on the critical path, from wherever one is now, to the ultimate goal of life.

Religion is derived from the two Latin words ‘re’ and ‘ligare’ which means ‘to reconnect’. Religions try to create a ‘connection’ between man and man, man and nature, and man and God. They focus on inspiring man to follow righteous and ethical conduct, so as to be ‘perfect’ and ‘to reconnect’ with God, an unknown but intelligent power, the ultimate ‘Cause’ behind this ‘Effect’, i.e. the universe and living beings.

The spirit of Bharat can be best described in the concept of the profound, yet simple message which asserts that get established in ethics, dharma, during schooling. Then thru’ ethics ‘earn’ and ‘enjoy’ so as to remain on the critical path of ethics, human values, Dharma, to attain the ultimate goal of human life, ‘perfection’, described variously by different religions.

4. The modern problem: Unfortunately, the modern ideas of democracy and religions have become divisive forces. Modern Religions propagate their narrow worldview of ‘my vs your religion’, which has led to a series of wars, crusades, between Christians from Europe and Muslims in the middle eastern Mediterranean Sea and is now called a clash of civilizations. It is now leading to communal clashes, forcible conversions, killings, terrorism, etc.

In fact, the situation because of religious persecution is so bad that UNESCO has asked that schooling must teach “How to live together”! And, it has brought a book for schools on learning the way to Peace! It has been very aptly said that if a religion becomes a cause of dislike, hatred and division, it was better to be without it, and to withdraw from such a religion would be truly religious! Because of the shenanigans of modern religious preachers and misguided followers, education has thrown away religion, and adopted ‘science’ as the new God, whereas earlier, religion was an integral part of schooling, focusing on character building and making good and virtuous men!

5. Conclusion: In the modern idea of democracy, religion and schooling, making good and virtuous men is no longer the responsibility of any one. Thus, we have corrupt teachers, corrupt politicians and administrators leading modern societies. It is time the academia takes responsibility to make ‘good men’ by establishing us in the time-tested self-development practices of religions, bereft of their divisive ideologies which cause divisions and hatred for the ‘other’. This is in accordance with the directions of the Supreme Court and knowing and respecting our composite culture, is also a part of our Fundamental Duties.
