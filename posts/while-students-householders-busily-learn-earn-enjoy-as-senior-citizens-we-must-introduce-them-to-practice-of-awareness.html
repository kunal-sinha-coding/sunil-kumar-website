India to Bharat · Essay #028

While ‘students-householders’ busily ‘learn-earn-enjoy’, as senior-citizens, we must introduce them to practice of awareness!

Problem: Modern 3R’s, IQ focused Education has created a society that functions from “head”, which leads to selfish thoughts for individual self-interests, because we identify with our limited mind. Education has no time for helping us Imbibe the habit of daily self-development practices for managing our Inner-Instrument, the Mind which controls all our desires and behaviours. Unless we learn to know and befriend our Mind, mere IQ, Degrees, status in society, cannot help us learn to grow from self-centeredness to selflessness.

In contrast, traditional societies learnt to function from “heart”, and easily lived together along with each other as ‘communities, tribes, villagers’ following the logical and commonsense principle of ‘love they neighbour as thyself’; even though they may have different Gods, Vocations, etc. Too much of IQ education has killed the natural, instinctive feelings of love, care, concern for each other, even though ‘love and joy’ are nearer to our reality as Self, where we’re one with each other as Consciousness, which we experience as “Awareness” as long as we don’t get identified with our self-centered body-mind!

Our Role? As Senior Citizens we can, and we must take responsibility to be, to identify, and to make Mentors, in our respective Colonies. Through small study-circles we can prepare brief write-ups, power-point presentations, on various Self-development / Life skills practices, which we can then share with ‘students and householders’ who’re busy otherwise. A Self-awareness exercise is discussed here:

2. Practicing being in Awareness: We must always remember that the gift of ‘awareness’ is a unique gift available only to Living-beings. Awareness is the manifestation of Life! However, we can’t stay in awareness per se, because we are born and designed to identify with our self-centered body-Mind, which I continuously create and reform, ‘refine or pollute’, by my own choices-actions.

If we reflect and Practice Self-awareness exercises, we can learn to stay as awareness per se, for longer periods; and over time we can learn to appreciate that as awareness, as the subject, observer, we’re One with all!

3. Self-awareness Practice: Stand apart and just practice being aware, just as a neutral, unconcerned witness:

# Of the sounds outside the room # Of the sounds Inside the room # Of your whole body # Of your face

Practice for say, 10-15 seconds, each stage or step. Each step or steps can be practiced independently, or all together.

Become aware of your breathing. Just be a witness, don’t control/change your breathing for say 2 minutes; gradually make it longer.

Become aware of the thoughts, feelings and emotions; Just be a witness; just be aware. Don’t control or try to change or judge or get attached or focus on the thoughts, etc.; let them, if any, come and go for say 2 minutes; gradually make it longer.

We may now mentally repeat and reflect on a holy name or brief prayer, repeating, reflecting trying to visualize the qualities, form, symbol or just light; 2 minutes approx. and offer our salutations, gratitude.

Now gradually reverse the process. Come back to the initial state gradually. End the exercise with offering a universal prayer for well-being of all. Time for each stage can be adjusted as per convenience.

4. Benefits and Learnings: This simple Self-Awareness exercise leads to the following kinds of learning: Becoming aware of our physical self and the sounds. Keeping our mind in the present. Becoming aware of our real self as Awareness.

The breath is the manifestation of mind. By watching the breath, the mind becomes calm and ‘tranquil’. This enhances our effectiveness, power of concentration, etc. We can learn to stand apart from our mind and look directly, without words, thoughts, prejudices, pre-judgment; start from neutral things, objects, situations, before known individuals and people!

By watching the flow of thoughts, we learn that thoughts are ‘objects’ and I am the ‘subject’. I am, therefore, not the thoughts but the ‘observer’ of thoughts. We can better manage our thoughts and become the master of the mind and not the slaves of our unwanted thoughts.

The more we pray for the good of others, the more we purify the mind and hence allow manifestation of our unlimited potential already within us all, beyond our mind, as Self that enlivens us. We move towards excellence. Mind is relieved of stress.

When our mind dwells on the divine perfection, it absorbs its quality of goodness and perfection. Therefore, besides righteousness, etc., repeating the holy name or brief prayer (Mantra) leads to purifying, improving the quality of the mind (Chitta Shuddhi), so that the unlimited potential comes to the fore.

5. Thought Management: Mind is a subtle, all pervasive inert field for flow of thoughts, feelings, etc., enlivened by the Self. It performs four functions at lightning speeds, viz. receives data from Sense-organs; sends it to the Chitta, storehouse of memories; Buddhi analyses and decides – refined Viveka-buddhi suggests long-term good; ego-appropriates and “I” Choose; as I Choose and act, “I” suffer the consequences, good or bad, law of karma! We’ve to learn to reduce Quantity by keeping busy on planned long-term good; improve Quality and Direct the Thoughts towards love and long-term good for all.

6. As Senior Citizens and as Mentors it is our responsibility and accountability to inspire “Students-householders” to get established in Self-awareness, Self-development practices, to purify and expand our mind so that we may learn to act out of ‘love’ of our blissful Self for the long-term good of all.
