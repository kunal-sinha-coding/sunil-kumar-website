India to Bharat · Essay #138

Knowing our mind and managing our thoughts

It is good and very grand to conquer external nature, but grander still to conquer our internal nature – our mind. It is grand and good to know the laws that govern the stars and planets; it is infinitely grander and better to know the laws that govern the passions, the feelings, the will of mankind’ – Vivekananda.

In Western thinking, brain is important and mind isn’t treated as an independent entity! Our ancient scientists of Bharat questioned, how does Body-brain which is just matter, become conscious and become a knower, a living-being, jiva-atman? And, they discovered and realized the status of mind as the subtlest matter which is both our friend and the villain. Let us understand our Mind:

i) Being subtlest, mind is formless, all pervasive and acts as a bridge, between ‘Matter’ and ‘Consciousness’ aka One, Infinite Spirit, Atman, aka Omniscient God.

ii) Being subtlest, mind is in closest proximity to the knowing-principle, i.e. Consciousness, and because of that immediacy, the mind, though matter, draws its consciousness from the Spirit; just as iron in proximity with fire glows red hot and becomes hot.

iii) Thus, our mind makes us conscious ‘living-beings’ and the mind becomes conscious knower and our instrument of perception.

iv) Our basic ignorance is that we think we’re conscious because of our mind; whereas in reality the mind itself is matter; it just borrows consciousness.

v) Linked to our minds we become separate, self-centered. Though the reality is that linked to Consciousness we’re all one Whole Creation!

vi) All religions try to teach us to be virtuous, cultivate humility, reduce our I-sense, ahamkara, be self-less, etc. so that we get united and regain our inherent Oneness with Pure infinite Consciousness because that alone gives us the peace and fulfilment we seek.

vii) Mind is really the subtle field for flow of Thoughts thru which arise all our – feelings, experiencing, perception, knowing, awareness, hearing, touching, seeing, tasting, smelling, growing, decaying etc.

viii) We can ‘observe’ the flow of thoughts-feelings; this implies that the thoughts-feelings must be different from us. Thus, ‘I am’ the ‘knower’, observer, of ‘my-thoughts’.

ix) I am independent of ‘my’ thoughts and, my thoughts are just ‘objects’ to be known, like any other objects!

x) Mind is formed out of the subtlest part of ‘food’; and therefore, purity of ‘food’, not only of what we ‘eat’ but what we ‘intake’ thru’ our five-senses, is considered important. The mind is being continuously formed by the impressions left in our mind as memories, of our ‘actions’. The mind is being ‘polluted and deformed’ or ‘refined and purified’, by the quality of “thoughts-words-actions”; the ‘good’ purify while the ‘bad’ pollute. Simply put, the ‘good’ are unselfish and selfless deeds; the ‘bad’ are the selfish and self-centered.

xi) The mind is not only our instrument of perception, but it also acts as a veil, which does not allow us to become aware of the fact that the real source of my being is not my limited mind; but the Consciousness behind it! Because of this basic ignorance we start identifying with our limited body-brain-mind complex, become selfish and self-centered; and are totally unaware of the infinite potential within each of us, our very source.

xii) Mankind’s goal and very purpose of this creation, the universe is to learn to identify with the pure Consciousness which is the reality, behind each of us and is the Cause of everything that exists! But the first thing is to befriend our Mind, purify it thru selflessness and then the pure mind will reveal the Consciousness within. Until we purify the mind thru’ ethical, righteous conduct, thru Swadharma, karma-yoga, we remain unfulfilled, and keep on looking for fulfillment outside in this or that.

xiii) Mind is a continuous Flow of thoughts – wanted / unwanted. By its very nature mind has a tendency to ‘flow down’ – be tempted, enjoying the experiences of the senses. Difficult to lift up and control – like reversing the flow of river. Gets attached easily to what it likes, but we need effort and struggle to attach it to ‘higher’ ideals and detach it from the pleasurable.

xiv) Mind performs four Functions at lightning speeds: as Manas it receives and coordinates the inputs from the sense-organs. These inputs are referred to the Chitta , the storehouse and record-keeper of all past ‘impressions, memories, etc.’, which sorts out and presents all this data to the Buddhi which then chooses, decides, the response. And then comes the I-sense, Ahamkara , which appropriates to itself the response.

xv) Managing the Mind really means managing the flow of Thoughts. It needs constant Practice and Focus on goals. Awareness of the highest Goal of Perfection, God-realization, Total-fulfilment, Poorna-Ananda, inspires the struggle to control the flow of unwanted thoughts. Continuous, regular, repetitive practice is needed to purify and improve the Quality of Thoughts, reduce the Quantity of Unwanted thoughts and focus the Thoughts towards our goals and the Goal.

Conclusion: The very fact that I am able to observe the ‘thoughts-feeling’ flowing thru’ our mind shows that I am separate from my Mind and so, I can control and conquer my own mind and not be enslaved by what the mind desires us to do.

All virtues and religions and their ways of worship are trying to inspire us to learn to manage our mind and thru’ practice, strive to control and direct the thoughts-feelings toward a higher direction; whereas the mind by default is built to follow whatever is ‘pleasurable’. To direct it towards the higher values, ideals, is difficult; it needs struggle and effort and only thru’ deliberate, conscious effort, continuous practice can we build powers of mind to control the flow of thoughts.
